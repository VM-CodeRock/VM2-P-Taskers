What Is Autoresearch? (Plain Language)

Think of the standard way a researcher or engineer improves something: they have an idea, they try it, they measure whether it worked, they keep the change if it helped or discard it if it didn't, then they start again with a new idea. This cycle — hypothesis, experiment, evaluate, iterate — is the fundamental engine of improvement in almost every knowledge-based field.

Autoresearch hands that entire cycle to an AI agent.

The Human Role Shifts Entirely

You stop writing the code. You start writing the research strategy — the goals, the constraints, the definition of "better." The AI handles everything else.

In Karpathy's implementation, this strategy lives in a plain-English file called program.md. The AI agent reads it, runs experiments against train.py, and iterates. By morning, you have a log of everything it tried and a better result.

"Karpathy calls it 'programming the research org in Markdown.' The durable artifact from an overnight run isn't the code changes the agent made — it's the instruction file that produced them."

— Data Science Dojo analysis, March 2026 · datasciencedojo.com

The Key Insight

The agent wasn't doing anything a careful human researcher couldn't do. It was just running experiments continuously, without cognitive fatigue or context-switching. A human researcher completes 8–10 cycles per day. The agent runs back-to-back overnight, compounding each improvement on the last.

As Fortune reported in March 2026, the project reached 41,000 GitHub stars and attracted attention from the CEO of Shopify to Google DeepMind engineers — not because the technique is new in principle, but because it works and anyone can run it today.

The Karpathy Loop: A Generalizable Pattern

Analyst Janakiram MSV coined the term "the Karpathy Loop" to describe the generalizable pattern autoresearch demonstrates. It has three components:

The Three Components

1. An agent with access to a modifiable asset (code, document, process, template)
2. A single, objectively measurable metric the agent is trying to improve
3. A fixed time limit per experiment, keeping cycles fast and failures cheap

Five Conditions for a Good Autonomous Loop

Not every process is ready for this level of automation. Early applications will be found where these conditions are met:

🎯
Objective Scoring
A measurable score that distinguishes better from worse without human judgment.
⚡
Fast, Cheap Iterations
Cycles measured in minutes, not months. Bad attempts waste minutes, not relationships.
🔲
Bounded Environment
A clearly defined scope — what the agent can and cannot modify.
🛡️
Low Cost of Failure
A bad iteration is easily reversed and doesn't cause downstream harm.
🔍
Traceability
The agent leaves a clear record of every decision and change it made.

"It's worth considering whether your problem falls into this category too."

— Andrej Karpathy, on X, March 2026

Beyond Machine Learning: Business Applications

The loop Karpathy built is specific to ML training. But the pattern — autonomous agent, modifiable asset, objective metric, fixed time budget, commit or revert — applies far beyond machine learning. Early applications are already emerging across business functions:

  • 📢
    Marketing Agents generating ad variations, running A/B tests against live audiences, iterating toward conversion rate targets — autonomously, overnight, without a team meeting to review 37 variants manually.
  • 📧
    Sales Outreach Loops running cold outreach experiments, testing subject lines, email lengths, and call-to-action phrasing against reply rate data — improving over time without human configuration of each test.
  • 💻
    Software Engineering Agents generating code changes, running acceptance tests, keeping what passes and discarding what doesn't — continuous improvement without manual code review for every micro-change.
  • 🛒
    Product Development An agent modifying product copy, pricing page structure, or onboarding flow against a conversion metric, iterating overnight and surfacing only what actually moved the number.
  • ⚙️
    Operations Any process that can be parameterized and scored — routing rules, scheduling algorithms, pricing logic — becomes a candidate for autonomous overnight optimization.
The One-Sentence Test

Any process where you can define "better," measure it automatically, and run experiments cheaply is a candidate for autonomous overnight improvement.

Implications for Changeis

Changeis operates in a domain — government contracting — defined by three simultaneous pressures: strict compliance requirements, high-stakes document production under tight deadlines, and the need to improve win rates across a portfolio of bids. The Karpathy Loop maps onto each of these with meaningful specificity.

Proposal Development: Iterative Optimization Against Evaluation Criteria

Federal proposals are evaluated against explicit criteria, often published in detail within the RFP. This is one of the cleaner scoring environments in business — the government tells you exactly what they're looking for and how they'll weight it.

The Karpathy Loop applied here: an AI agent with access to a draft proposal, a scoring rubric derived from the RFP evaluation criteria, and the ability to modify sections within defined constraints. The agent proposes edits, scores the result against the rubric, keeps improvements, and iterates. A proposal manager sets strategy and reviews the morning output rather than manually drafting every pass.

  • 📋
    Compliance Matrix Automation Automated generation and gap-checking of compliance matrices against RFP requirements — continuous, not periodic.
  • 🎯
    Technical Approach Refinement Iterative optimization of technical narratives scored against published evaluation criteria — the agent proposes, humans approve.
  • ⭐
    Past Performance Optimization Testing which accomplishments and narrative framing score highest against stated criteria before submission.
  • 💰
    Price-to-Win Modeling Price strategy iteration against known agency budget signals and competitive intelligence.

Compliance Monitoring: From Periodic Audit to Continuous Loop

Government contractors operate under layered compliance requirements — FAR clauses, CMMC cybersecurity frameworks, agency-specific reporting requirements, and contract-level obligations. Compliance gaps are expensive.

The Karpathy Loop converts compliance from a periodic audit function (expensive, human-intensive, retrospective) to a continuous monitoring function (systematic, agent-driven, prospective). An AI agent with access to compliance documentation, a checklist of requirements, and the ability to identify gaps and propose remediation runs continuously — flagging new gaps as requirements change or new contracts are added.

NIST Regulatory Signal

NIST launched its AI Agent Standards Initiative in February 2026, signaling autonomous AI is now within the federal governance and compliance domain. For government contractors, demonstrating mature AI governance — with audit logs, agent scope controls, and traceable outputs — becomes a competitive differentiator in bids, not just a compliance cost.

Process Optimization: Internal Operations as an Experimental System

Government contracting involves significant recurring process work: capture management, opportunity analysis, team staffing, subcontractor vetting, contract data reporting, deliverable production. Much of this work is systematic enough to be parameterized and measured.

  • 📈
    Capture Pipeline Optimization Agent analyzing historical bid decisions against win/loss outcomes — identifying which combination of factors (team composition, incumbency, set-asides, agency familiarity) predicts wins.
  • 📝
    Deliverable Quality Iteration For contract deliverables with defined quality criteria, an agent that reviews drafts, scores against criteria, and proposes improvements — shifting humans from first-draft writers to strategic reviewers.
  • 👥
    Resource Allocation Modeling Agent iterating staffing configurations for complex bids, optimizing cost-to-win ratios, key personnel availability, and labor category compliance.

The Bigger Picture

Autoresearch is a working demonstration of something the AI research community has theorized about for years: recursive self-improvement — the ability of an AI system to evaluate its own outputs, generate modifications, and improve without human supervision.

The current version is narrow and safe. It improves training code for small language models, operating within strict constraints, with a locked evaluation function that prevents the agent from gaming its own metric. But the architecture demonstrates what a broader version could look like.

"LLM labs will do this. It's the final boss battle. You spin up a swarm of agents, you have them collaborate to tune smaller models, you promote the most promising ideas to increasingly larger scales."

— Andrej Karpathy, on X, March 2026 · via Techstrong.ai

The Enterprise Adoption Signal

According to Gartner's 2026 projections, 40% of enterprise applications are expected to embed task-specific AI agents by 2026 — up from low single-digit adoption just a few years ago. The OneReach enterprise AI analysis frames the strategic imperative: "By 2026, winners will not be those who deploy agents fastest, but those who reimagine their enterprise as agent-native from the ground up."

The Operating Model Shift

Before: Humans execute and decide; AI assists.
After: AI executes and proposes; humans define strategy and review results.

This is a fundamentally different operating model — not an incremental improvement. The companies that transition to it earliest will compound advantages that are difficult to reverse.

What a CEO Should Watch For

Autoresearch is a research tool for ML engineers. CEOs do not need to run it. But the pattern it demonstrates — and the signals it sends — are directly relevant to strategic planning.

The Metric Definition Advantage
The companies that win with autonomous AI loops will be those that get good at defining measurable objectives. This is a strategic capability, not a technical one. The quality of the metric determines what the agent optimizes for. Poorly defined metrics produce agents that optimize for the wrong things brilliantly.
The Overnight Compounding Effect
Agents don't sleep. A firm deploying autonomous improvement loops on proposal development, compliance monitoring, or process optimization gains compounding advantage over time — each overnight run builds on the previous night's improvements. The gap between firms that deploy these loops and those that don't widens continuously.
The Competitive Shift in GovCon
Other firms will apply these tools to proposal development. Deltek's analysis of AI in government proposals documents accelerating adoption. When autonomous optimization becomes standard among large primes, the manual proposal process becomes a structural disadvantage.
The Governance Compliance Obligation
NIST's AI Agent Standards Initiative (February 2026) signals that autonomous AI is now within the federal regulatory domain. Security controls around agent authentication, activity logging, and scope limitation will transition from best practices to compliance obligations. Government contractors should assume sector-specific requirements will follow.
The Talent Model Shift
Junior staff at firms deploying autonomous loops spend less time on mechanical work and more time on strategic judgment — reviewing agent outputs, refining objectives, identifying where the loop misses nuance. This changes what hiring looks like and what skills create value. Firms that don't adapt will find themselves paying premium salaries for automatable work.
The "Program the Research Org" Mindset
Karpathy's phrase "programming the research org in Markdown" describes something real and transferable. The quality of your instruction document — the goals, constraints, and definitions of "better" you encode for your agents — determines how fast your organization improves. Organizations that invest in building well-structured instruction documents build a compounding institutional asset.
9

Summary: The Decision That Matters

Autoresearch is a proof of concept. The 41,000 GitHub stars it earned in days are not enthusiasm for a training script — they are recognition that the pattern it demonstrates is generalizable and that it works.

The question for any CEO is not whether autonomous improvement loops will become standard in business. The Fortune analysis, the Gartner projections, the NIST regulatory attention, and the speed of adoption all point in one direction. The question is whether your organization defines its metrics, builds its instruction documents, and deploys its first loops before or after your competitors do.

For Changeis: The Right First Steps

The opportunity is concrete. Proposal development is criteria-driven with publicly available rubrics. Compliance monitoring is systematic and parameterizable. The government is itself accelerating AI adoption, creating both regulatory requirements and competitive dynamics that favor early movers.

  • Pick one process with a clear, measurable objective — not the most complex one, the most measurable one
  • Write a precise instruction document defining what "better" means and what constraints the agent must respect
  • That document has value independent of any specific AI tool — it encodes your organization's judgment about quality
  • Start with proposal compliance gap-checking: clear criteria, binary outcomes, low cost of failure
  • Build the governance framework first: audit logs, scope controls, human review checkpoints
  • The overnight results from the first run define your baseline — everything after is compounding improvement
