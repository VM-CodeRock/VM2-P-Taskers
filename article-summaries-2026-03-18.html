5. AI for Boring Businesses (Every.to)

Source: Every / Thesis | Published: March 10, 2026 | Author: Sam Gerstenzang

What it is: Lessons from applying AI to operationally complex, "boring" businesses (funeral homes, medspas) — arguing these sectors, not flashy SaaS, are where AI thrives.

Key Points:
- AI cut market research timelines from 3 months to 3 weeks (using ChatGPT, Clay, Claude, Granola)
- "Reward outcomes, not AI usage" — avoid making AI adoption a checkbox; hold teams accountable for results
- AI lead gen (Clay via LinkedIn) mirrors SEO — initially gameable but becoming pay-to-play
- Humans are harder to replace than expected: partners resist change, customers forgive human errors more than AI's, AI receptionists at Moxie medspa increased churn
- AI excels in pilots but struggles with edge cases, integration, and maintenance

Direct Relevance to Changeis (government contracting = "boring business"):
- Speed wins: Use AI to accelerate RFPs, market analysis, compliance research — cut bid cycles
- Outcome-focused: Tie bonuses to win rates, not AI tool adoption
- Cautious acquisition: Test AI lead gen but expect paywalls/spam fatigue — stick to proven channels like SAM.gov
- Human-AI hybrid: Automate rote tasks where possible but expect agency/partner resistance; retain humans for client interactions and edge cases
- Bottom line: AI boosts efficiency but won't eliminate bureaucracy — focus on resilience, not hype
