Contextual Factors Affecting Task Orders

DOT IT Centralization — HIGH IMPACT Secretary Duffy's April 2025 memos require CIO approval for ALL DOT IT acquisitions. Every TORP now flows through OCIO. The OCIO relationship is more critical than ever — it's now the gateway to every OA.
DOGE / Workforce Reductions — MEDIUM-HIGH FMCSA zeroed out its Office of the CTO (23 FTEs → 0) in FY2026. Federal IT staff reductions historically create surge demand for contractor IT governance and PMO support — a tailwind for IPSS3 activity.
IT Spending Trajectory — MEDIUM Civilian agency IT spending down 11% YoY in FY2025. DOT centralizing IT budgets into Working Capital Fund. Target WCF-funded TORPs and OCIO-directed work; avoid OA-specific discretionary programs at risk of cuts.
DOT HVA/Cybersecurity Gaps — HIGH OPPORTUNITY OIG Oct 2023: DOT HVA governance "managed in ad hoc, inconsistent manner" — 7 open recommendations. FISMA/HVA was among the highest-volume IPSS2 task categories. High-probability recurring TORPs.
FAA-Specific Advantage — Changeis Unique IPSS3 covers DOT OCIO-level work; FAA AIT work flows through ITIPSS. However, DOT OCIO IPSS3 TORPs can support FAA policy/governance. Use CLMRS relationships to build OCIO bridge.
AI & Digital Transformation — EMERGING DOT IT strategy emphasizes digital factories, AI integration, and data-driven operations. Changeis's CLMRS Digital Factory narrative (4 Pillars) maps directly to what DOT OCIO is buying. Position AI-augmented PMO as differentiator.
