OPENAI ChatGPT & API
OpenAI Privacy Filter: Open-Source, On-Device PII Redaction — Free, Apache 2.0
OpenAI released Privacy Filter today — a 1.5-billion-parameter open-weight model that detects and redacts personally identifiable information from text entirely on-device. Available on Hugging Face under Apache 2.0, it handles names, addresses, phone numbers, emails, URLs, account numbers, dates, and secrets (API keys, credentials). It runs locally on a standard laptop or in-browser, meaning unredacted data never reaches a cloud server. Organizations can fine-tune the model on their own data and privacy policies.
Why It Matters for Changeis

Federal contractors face strict data residency and PII handling requirements. Privacy Filter enables Changeis to safely pass government documents through AI workflows without exposing sensitive data to cloud providers — directly addressing a key compliance bottleneck that has blocked broader AI adoption in federal programs.

Implementation Ideas
  • Deploy Privacy Filter as a pre-processing step in any AI pipeline touching employee data, contractor records, or sensitive client documents — sanitize inputs before sending to cloud LLMs.
  • Fine-tune on Changeis-specific identifiers (contract numbers, employee IDs, clearance data) for higher accuracy in internal workflows.
