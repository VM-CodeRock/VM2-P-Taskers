03 · Notable Posts & Announcements
Top Items This Week (May 4–11, 2026)
MAS Program 📅 April 2, 2026 (acceptance ongoing) Source: GSA MAS Modification Guide — April 2026

MAS Refresh 31 Mass Mod A914 — TDR Mandatory; Acceptance Window Closing

GSA released MAS Solicitation Refresh 31 on April 2, 2026, mandating Transactional Data Reporting (TDR) for all 277 MAS SINs. The accompanying Mass Mod A914 ("Participate in TDR") was issued to all non-TDR contractors. The Price Reductions Clause (PRC) is eliminated; monthly transactional reporting at the order level is now the standard. GSA's April 2026 Modification Guide (updated April 28) incorporates Refresh 31 and Class Deviation 2026-02 for TDR expansion and OLM changes. Contractors who accept the Mass Mod in Q2 2026 will have TDR effective July 1, 2026; those who delay to Q3 will see it start October 1. Non-acceptance risks contract cancellation.

Why it matters for Changeis: Changeis holds SINs 54151S (IT Services), 54151HACS (HACS), and 541611 (Management Consulting). All three transition to TDR. Accepting Mass Mod A914 immediately is critical — the next reporting quarter after acceptance determines when TDR enforcement begins. Internal transactional data capture workflows (order-level pricing, labor categories) must be operational by July 1, 2026. The elimination of PRC simplifies pricing compliance but requires new monthly reporting infrastructure. Non-TDR proposals are now rejected by default.
FAS Reorg 📅 May 1, 2026 Source: GSA Blog — FAS Reorganization (May 1, 2026)

FAS Reorganizes into 5 Portfolios + New AI "Transform" Office — Effective May 1, 2026

GSA's Federal Acquisition Service restructured its operations into five new portfolios: Office of Assisted Acquisition Services (Assist), Office of Centralized Acquisition Services (Centralize), Office of Acquisition Solutions Development (Create), Office of Shared Services Delivery (Deliver), and Office of Business Optimization (Optimize). A sixth dedicated office — Transform — was established to accelerate automation and implement AI solutions across FAS. GSA confirmed existing programs (MAS, GWACs, Fleet, CityPairs) continue without immediate disruption. The reorganization directly supports Trump Administration EOs on Workforce Optimization and Procurement Consolidation.

Why it matters for Changeis: The Centralize portfolio will handle agency procurement consolidation — the primary mechanism by which agency-direct contracts are being redirected to GSA vehicles. Changeis should track Centralize portfolio announcements for new task-order flows under SINs it holds. The Transform office signals GSA will issue AI-related task orders and may use AI in acquisition decisions — Changeis's AI capability positioning on MAS becomes more strategically important. The Procurement Consolidation Open Office Hours on May 12 are a direct forum to understand the Centralize workstream.

Alliant 3 📅 March 10, 2026 (NTP); ongoing Source: GSA Alliant 3 Program Page

Alliant 3 GWAC: Live and Accepting Orders — Phase 1 Awardees Include Major Integrators

Alliant 3 is now fully operational. GSA issued the Notice to Proceed on March 10, 2026, with 43 Phase 1 awardees covering the full-and-open unrestricted pool. The 5-year base period runs to March 9, 2031 (option through 2036; task orders may extend to 2041). Phase 1 awardees include Booz Allen Hamilton, SAIC, Leidos, Deloitte Consulting, Accenture Federal Services, GDIT, Mantech, CACI, Peraton, and others. GSA hosts a "Discover Alliant 3" webinar on May 20 (2:00 PM ET) for the federal acquisition workforce. Task orders flow through eBuy; BPA toolkit available for establishing blanket purchase agreements. A companion Alliant 3 Community of Interest exists on GSA Interact.

Why it matters for Changeis: Changeis is not a direct Alliant 3 awardee (unrestricted pool), but can access Alliant 3 task orders as a subcontractor to the 43 primes. Given that EO 14240 is pushing IT spend to Alliant 3, proactively engaging teaming discussions with Alliant 3 holders (particularly those in IT management consulting and program support) is a near-term BD priority. The May 20 webinar provides intelligence on where agencies are directing early task orders.
Polaris 📅 Ongoing — last update Jan/Mar 2026 Source: Washington Technology — Polaris WOSB Update (Mar 31, 2026)

Polaris: WOSB Pool Grows to 51 Awardees; Small Business Pool Protests Continue at COFC

GSA added 4 more WOSB awardees (Digital Decypher, Fed Tec, Open San Consulting, The Prospective Group) to Polaris in late March, growing that pool to 51 companies. GSA states more WOSB awards are expected. The HUBZone pool has 30 final winners and the SDVOSB pool has 23. All three active pools are live for task orders (base period 5 years, option 5 years). The Polaris Small Business general pool remains blocked at the U.S. Court of Federal Claims — 6 companies are challenging evaluation scores. SBA previously denied three post-award SDVOSB size protests (January 2026). No resolution expected imminently on the SB pool.

Why it matters for Changeis: Changeis may have teaming partners in the HUBZone, SDVOSB, or WOSB pools. Ensure those partners are DPA-trained (Polaris DPA Training: May 13, May 27) and have SRP reporting active. The WOSB pool expansion is an opportunity to identify new Polaris-holding teaming partners with complementary IT service offerings. The SB pool protest resolution timeline remains uncertain — do not count on SB pool for FY2026 task order capture.
OASIS+ 📅 Phase II open since Jan 12, 2026 Source: Trident Proposals — OASIS+ Phase II Guide (Apr 2026)

OASIS+ Phase II: 13 Domains Continuously Open — New Human Capital and Management Advisory Domains Relevant to Changeis

OASIS+ Phase II continuous on-ramp has been live since January 12, 2026, with all 6 contract pools accepting submissions. The expansion from 8 to 13 domains adds Business Administration, Financial Services, Human Capital, Marketing and Public Relations, and Social Services. The Human Capital and Management and Advisory domains are directly relevant to Changeis's consulting practice. Self-scoring requires qualifying projects at $1M (unrestricted pools) or $500K (small business pools). Domain Qualification Matrices (scorecards) are published. The OASIS+ Submission Portal now requires authenticator app authentication (SMS discontinued April 30). OASIS+ SRP training for current holders is scheduled May 13, 1:00 PM ET.

Why it matters for Changeis: If Changeis is evaluating an OASIS+ Phase II submission in the Management & Advisory or Human Capital domains (both relevant to 541611 SIN capabilities), the continuous on-ramp means no deadline pressure — but domain scorecard review and qualifying project selection should be underway. Phase II participation would diversify Changeis's vehicle portfolio beyond MAS. Current OASIS+ holders should attend May 13 SRP training to ensure reporting compliance.
