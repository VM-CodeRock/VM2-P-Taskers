That bridge award (70Z02326FMSC00001) was executed on March 20, 2026.

APFS record F2026072744 is the bridge forecast supporting this transition. Simultaneously, the larger recompete forecast (F2025071629, $2M–$5M, 5-year PoP) signals a full competition is planned for an award around April 2026. Changeis should treat this as a two-track opportunity: (1) monitor and potentially sub to Tetra Tech on the bridge, and (2) compete aggressively on the recompete with a teaming strategy targeting maritime/homeland security specialists.

Requirement Description

The purpose of this requirement is to obtain contractor technical services in support of evaluating Vessel Security Plans (VSP) and Piracy Annexes (PA), and validating participation in Alternative Security Programs (ASP) for the U.S. Coast Guard Marine Safety Center (MSC) Vessel Security Division.

The Maritime Transportation Security Act of 2002 (MTSA), under 33 CFR Subchapter H, requires owners/operators of approximately 11,000 vessels to submit VSPs to USCG for approval or indicate participation in a USCG-approved ASP. Contractors must review VSPs and PAs to verify compliance with all legal and regulatory elements, and prepare correspondence to submitters. Work volume: approximately 60 submissions/month (~720 annually), including amendments and notifications.
