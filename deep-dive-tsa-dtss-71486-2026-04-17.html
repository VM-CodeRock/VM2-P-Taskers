Executive Summary

TSA's Developmental Testing Support Services (DTSS) contract is a follow-on recompete of the incumbent Blanket Purchase Agreement held by Battelle Memorial Institute (BPA #70T04021A7672N002, awarded September 2021, worth up to $34.7M, expiring September 2026). The new IDC vehicle is estimated at $50M–$100M over 5 years (August 2026–August 2031), representing a significant scale-up. Work is performed at the TSA Systems Integration Facility (TSIF) in Alexandria, VA, and supports Developmental and Qualification Test and Evaluation of Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) — explosives detection systems, checkpoint screening, and baggage screening technologies — mandated by the 2001 Aviation and Transportation Security Act (ATSA).


This is a Full & Open competition — no small business set-aside — which means Changeis will compete against Battelle and potentially large primes. The RAG match score of 0.7451 indicates strong alignment on engineering/testing skills (primarily via FAA CLMRS and Volpe work), though direct DHS/TSA T&E experience is absent from the Changeis portfolio. The recommended posture is GO — with a teaming strategy, pairing Changeis program management and systems engineering pedigree with a specialized DHS/TSE testing subcontractor.

Program Context: TSA Systems Integration Facility (TSIF)

The TSA Systems Integration Facility — located in Arlington/Alexandria, VA — is TSA's primary test bed for evaluating Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) before field deployment. It is a globally unique government laboratory that directly enables TSA's compliance with ATSA (2001) statutory mandates.


TSIF Mission: Discover, develop, and deliver breakthrough security technologies at the national level. The facility houses test lanes, baggage handling systems, explosives detection systems (EDS), checkpoint screening equipment, and credential authentication technologies — all in an environment that replicates real airport conditions.


Key Testing Activities at TSIF (relevant to DTSS scope):

  • Developmental & Qualification Testing (DT/QT): Rigorous lab-based testing of candidate TSE to verify conformance to TSA functional specifications (e.g., probability of detection, false alarm rates) — prior to Operational Testing (OT) at airports
  • Baggage Handling System (BHS) Integration: EDS-to-BHS integration testing for inline checked baggage screening
  • Checkpoint Screening Equipment: Advanced imaging, millimeter wave, credential authentication technology (CAT) evaluation
  • Explosives Detection Systems (EDS): Automated screening systems for checked baggage; DHS Certified (DHS TSL certification testing)
  • Engineering Change Reviews: Impact assessment for system modifications
  • T&E Planning, Execution, Data Capture, Analysis & Reporting
  • Test Article Development & Verification: Building/preparing test articles (surrogates, threat simulants, test loads)
  • Specialized Equipment: Robot arms, mass spectrometers (Agilent), GCMS triple-quad systems, mass spectrometry for explosives/narcotics detection testing

ATSA Statutory Context: The 2001 Aviation and Transportation Security Act mandates that TSA screen 100% of checked baggage and all persons accessing sterile areas. DTSS enables TSA to certify and qualify equipment used in this mission. The contractor is directly supporting Congressional mandates — making this a mission-critical, non-discretionary procurement.

Innovation Test Bed Facility: DLR Group (architects) recently designed an Innovation Test Bed Facility within TSIF that serves as a real-world proving ground for new equipment, procedures, and scenarios. This signals TSA's commitment to long-term facility investment and growing scope of DTSS-type work.
