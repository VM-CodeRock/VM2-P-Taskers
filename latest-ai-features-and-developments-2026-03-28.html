3 · AI Policy & Regulation
Major Development — Federal Contractors Must Act

GSA MAS AI Clause (Refresh 31) — expected late March / early April 2026. Requires: (1) disclose ALL AI tools used in contract performance within 30 days of award, (2) irrevocable "any lawful use" government license, (3) American-made AI systems only, (4) no AI content moderation on government data, (5) data segregation and prohibition on training government data for other purposes. Flows down to all subcontractors and vendors. Non-compliance can mean contract termination. (Gibson Dunn)

  • White House National AI Legislative Framework (Mar 20, 2026): Trump administration released a four-page legislative blueprint calling on Congress to: (a) preempt state AI laws that impose "undue burdens," (b) bar new federal AI regulatory agencies, (c) protect children, IP, and free speech, (d) create regulatory sandboxes, (e) open federal datasets for AI training, (f) combat AI-enabled scams. Not yet law — a legislative recommendation. Sets up a major congressional fight over state preemption. (White House, Politico)
  • Federal Judge Blocks Pentagon's Anthropic "Supply Chain Risk" Designation (Mar 26–27): U.S. District Judge Rita Lin issued a temporary injunction blocking the Pentagon from labeling Anthropic a supply chain risk and blocking Trump's executive directive ordering all federal agencies to stop using Anthropic/Claude. Ruling: "Punishing Anthropic for bringing public scrutiny to the government's contracting position is classic illegal First Amendment retaliation." Order delayed 7 days for government appeal. (ABC News, KSAT)
  • GAO Report — AI Privacy Gaps (Mar 2026): GAO published Artificial Intelligence: OMB Action Needed to Address Privacy-Related Gaps in Federal Guidance — identifying 13 practical challenges in AI privacy protection and urging OMB to issue more specific guardrails. Particularly relevant for agencies handling sensitive citizen data. (Fed Contract Pros)
  • EU AI Act Enforcement Status: As of March 2026, only 8 of 27 EU member states have designated enforcement single points of contact — uneven enforcement expected across Europe. The EU is also considering centralizing AI Act enforcement further via a "Digital Omnibus" proposal. (European Parliament)
  • State AI Law Activity: Washington Governor signed a digital replica law (Mar 16). Chatbot safety bills moving in Arizona, Georgia, Idaho, and Oklahoma. US AI policy faces growing congressional divisions on child safety, copyright, and data center siting. (Transparency Coalition)
  • US AI Advisory Council Formed: New US science and technology council includes Meta, Nvidia, Oracle, Google, and AMD executives to shape national AI strategy and respond to China competition. Signals continued industry-government alignment under the Trump administration. (MarketingProfs)
