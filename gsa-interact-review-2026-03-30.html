Executive Summary

This week's GSA Interact review surfaces three high-priority developments for Changeis. The most time-sensitive: the comment deadline for GSA's proposed AI procurement clause (GSAR 552.239-7001) is April 3, 2026 — less than four days away. This clause will directly govern how Changeis delivers AI-integrated services under GSA Schedule contracts and imposes significant compliance requirements around data handling, American-AI mandates, and IP ownership.

Separately, OASIS+ Phase I is fully resolved (all protests cleared as of March 17), and Phase II rolling awards begin in Q3 FY 2026. MAS Refresh 31 — which mandates Transactional Data Reporting (TDR) for all SINs including IT — is imminent at March/April 2026, requiring Changeis to accept a mass modification within 90 days of issuance. A new FAR overhaul (Revolutionary FAR Overhaul / RFO) is also reshaping the acquisition landscape, increasing contracting officer discretion and simplifying the regulatory framework.

Policy & Regulatory Updates

1. GSA AI Clause — GSAR 552.239-7001 (Comment Deadline: April 3)

Published March 6 as part of MAS Refresh 31 advance notice, then decoupled on March 19 due to industry pushback. The clause will not appear in Refresh 31 but will be issued in a later refresh. Key requirements Changeis must understand:

RequirementImpact on Changeis
American AI Systems only Prohibits "foreign AI systems" in task order performance; "American AI System" not yet defined — Changeis should seek clarity in comments
Government owns all outputs All data inputs, outputs, and "Custom Developments" vest in Government; Changeis retains "background IP" only
Data localization + segregation Government Data cannot leave "agreed premises"; "eyes off" handling restrictions apply; requires separate storage from commercial systems
No model training on gov data Changeis cannot use Government Data to fine-tune or improve any AI system used in delivery
72-hour incident reporting to CISA Requires cybersecurity incident reporting pipeline; FedRAMP-covered AI systems must report within 1 hour
"Unbiased AI" principles AI must be "neutral, nonpartisan" — consistent with OMB guidance and Executive Orders; no DEI-aligned AI filtering
Changeis Comment Strategy Key issues for Changeis to raise: (1) Define "American AI System" with clear criteria to avoid ambiguity. (2) Clarify "Custom Developments" scope — narrow the definition so Changeis's pre-existing methodologies and frameworks are protected. (3) Address data localization — current cloud environments (AWS GovCloud, Azure Government) should qualify as "agreed premises." (4) Ensure FedRAMP-authorized commercial AI tools (e.g., Microsoft Copilot in M365 GCC) are not inadvertently captured.

2. MAS Refresh 31 — Transactional Data Reporting (TDR) Becomes Mandatory

GSA released Refresh 31 draft on March 9, 2026. Expected finalization: March/April 2026. Key changes:

  • TDR mandatory for all 112 previously-exempt SINs, including all IT Category SINs (541511, 541512, 541513, 541519, 518210). Changeis must report detailed quarterly sales transaction data.
  • Open-market items restricted: GSA replaces ad-hoc open-market items with a structured Order-Level Materials (OLM) SIN — requires process changes for how Changeis handles ancillary products/services in task orders.
  • New IT Rapid Response Vehicle: Refresh 31 introduces a fast-track modification path for IT contractors tied to eligible federal IT initiatives — potential opportunity for Changeis to access streamlined task orders.
  • Mass modification acceptance window: 90 days for TDR; 60 days for other changes. Clock starts at Refresh 31 issuance.

3. Revolutionary FAR Overhaul (RFO) — In Progress

Under Executive Order "Restoring Common Sense to Federal Procurement", the FAR is undergoing its first comprehensive rewrite. FAR Part 4 reduced ~50%; Part 40 (new, security-focused) created; Part 48 reduced ~70%. Contracting Officer discretion significantly increased — good news for Changeis's ability to negotiate flexible task order terms. The March 12 GSA office hours session on FAR Overhaul Updates was cancelled; expect rescheduling in April.

4. FedRAMP 20x — AI Authorization Streamlined

GSA launched FedRAMP 20x to eliminate agency-sponsorship requirement for AI cloud providers entering the FedRAMP pipeline. First three AI Prioritization 20x Low authorizations targeted for completion in early 2026. Changeis's cloud migration and DevSecOps practices should incorporate FedRAMP 20x timelines when advising agencies on AI adoption.
