E-IDS Program Overview

The Enterprise Information Display System (E-IDS) is one of the FAA's flagship modernization programs, designed to replace multiple legacy Information Display Systems across the National Airspace System.

Program Scale

Deployment Target: 400+ FAA facilities, 5,000+ users

Scope: Replacing legacy IDS systems — some dating back to the 1980s — with a single, NAS-wide, cross-domain information display

Prime Contractor Support: Mosaic ATM leads AT-Workstation design, development, integration, and testing

E-IDS Capabilities

  • Displays supplemental static and real-time data supporting safe, efficient ATC operations
  • Filtering, sorting, and searching to organize and access information quickly
  • Cross-domain access ensuring consistent information across all facility types
  • NAS-wide on-demand access to operational data
  • Customizable configurations for diverse facility requirements (towers, TRACONs, ARTCCs)
  • Replaces legacy systems: IDS4, ACE-IDS, ERIDS, and facility-specific paper-based systems

Strategic Importance to FAA

The May 2025 DOT "Brand New Air Traffic Control System" plan identifies legacy IDS replacement as a critical priority:

  • Legacy IDS systems use "obsolete hardware such as floppy disks" and face cybersecurity concerns
  • E-IDS consolidates multiple legacy systems into a single platform, reducing maintenance costs and compensating controls
  • Part of a broader three-year framework to transform U.S. air traffic control from "antiquated state to a modern system"
