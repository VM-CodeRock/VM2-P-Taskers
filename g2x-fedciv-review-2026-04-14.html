DOGE Winding Down by July 4, 2026 — but Oversight Framework Permanent
Regulatory High Relevance Amerifusion (Feb 2026) · Multiple Sources
Summary: DOGE is scheduled to conclude operations by July 4, 2026, but its oversight functions are being absorbed into OMB and OPM permanently. Key lasting impacts: (1) IT consulting contracts over $10M and advisory services contracts over $1M require DOGE/OMB approval; (2) every contract payment requires written justification; (3) fixed-price contracting is preferred over cost-type. IT modernization spending continues to grow (tracking toward ~$50B/quarter in Q4 FY2025). Agencies that lost workforce (~9% reduction) are increasing contractor reliance.
Changeis Angle: The DOGE scrutiny era has permanently shifted procurement toward outcome-based, fixed-price, publicly-justified contracts. Changeis proposals must lead with measurable outcomes, cost-benefit narratives, and value documentation. The increased contractor reliance post-workforce reduction is a net positive for Changeis's service lines.
