What Is SWIM

FAA SWIM (System Wide Information Management) is the FAA's enterprise data-sharing backbone for the National Airspace System (NAS). Launched in 2007 as part of NextGen, it replaces hundreds of point-to-point data connections with a single publish-once, subscribe-anywhere service bus.

Rather than each system building custom feeds, NAS producers (TFMS, STDDS, SFDPS, TBFM, weather systems, etc.) publish data once to SWIM's messaging infrastructure. External consumers — airlines, airports, government agencies, researchers, contractors — subscribe to only the feeds they need in near real-time via JMS messaging.

How to Get Access

Standard path via the SWIFT Portal — publicly accessible, no FAA affiliation required.

1
Register on SWIFT Portal
Go to portal.swim.faa.gov and create a free account. Any organization or individual can register.
2
Browse & Subscribe to Feeds
Use Service Discovery to find feeds. Subscribe to data products via the portal. Feeds delivered over Solace JMS messaging.
3
Sign the Service Access Agreement (SAA)
Each subscription requires an SAA. Free for standard use. High-volume users (>200 GB/day or >2 TB/month) must request an exemption.
4
Download Jump Start Kit
Self-service provisioning kit for JMS client setup. Available directly in the SWIFT Portal.
5
Special feeds via email request
CDM data, TFMS Request/Reply, and TFMS International Data require separate approval: Data-To-Industry@faa.gov

Architecture & Stack
Transport: NAS Enterprise Messaging Service (NEMS) → SWIM Cloud Distribution Service (SCDS) via one-way VPN → subscribers over Solace JMS pub/sub messaging

Service Oriented Architecture (SOA) — producers publish once; consumers subscribe to what they need. No polling. Near real-time push delivery.

NSRR (NAS Service Registry and Repository) — catalog of all published SWIM services and technical specs. Requires 2FA login. Used by developers to discover endpoints and download WSDL/schema docs.

Data format: XML/FIXM (Flight Information Exchange Model) — ICAO-standard message schemas. Industry-standard, parseable with off-the-shelf aviation libraries.

LADD Filter: Private aircraft owners can opt out of public data distribution. SWIM subscribers who display data publicly must apply the weekly LADD filter file (updated Tuesdays).

Contractor & Industry Use Cases
✈️
Flight Operations Analytics
Combine TFMS flight plan/routing data with TBFM metering info to build delay prediction models, on-time performance dashboards, or EDCT advisory tools for airline/airport clients.
🛬
Surface Movement Monitoring
STDDS surface data (ASDE-X/ASSC) enables real-time runway occupancy tracking, pushback sequencing optimization, and ground delay analysis for airport authority clients.
🌩️
Weather Impact Assessment
Layer ITWS/CSS-Wx storm cell and windshear data against TFMS traffic flow data to quantify weather-related NAS disruptions — useful for FAA program evaluation or airport resilience work.
🔒
Airspace Security / SAA Monitoring
AIM SAA feed provides real-time TFR and Special Activity Airspace status — relevant for DoD/DHS clients managing restricted airspace compliance or UAS corridor planning.
📊
NextGen Program Evaluation
SWIM is the data backbone for measuring NextGen outcomes. Government contractors evaluating TFDM, TBFM, or CDM program effectiveness use SWIM feeds as the ground truth data source.
🤖
AI / ML Training Datasets
Near real-time NAS data from SWIM is used to train predictive models for pushback time optimization, taxi time prediction, en route delay estimation, and arrival sequencing — active research area per FAA ATIEC 2021.
Restrictions & Compliance Notes
LADD Compliance: If you publicly display SWIM data, you must apply the weekly LADD (Limiting Aircraft Data Displayed) filter — private aircraft owners who have opted out must be scrubbed from any public-facing product. Weekly file distributed every Tuesday. Contact: ladd@faa.gov
Non-operational use only: SCDS data is provided for non-operational purposes (research, business analysis, analytics). It cannot be used as the sole source for operational air traffic control decisions. The FAA's operational NAS systems are authoritative.
Account hygiene: SWIFT Portal accounts inactive for 245+ days are flagged; accounts inactive 275+ days are deleted. Subscriptions that don't renew SAAs are disabled. Plan for periodic re-authentication if building a long-running data pipeline.
Recommended Next Steps
1
Register on portal.swim.faa.gov with a company email — free, no approval wait time for standard feeds. Get the Jump Start Kit and confirm JMS connectivity.
2
Clarify the "special project" context — which feeds are relevant depends heavily on whether the project is surface ops (STDDS), en route flow (TFMS/SFDPS), weather (ITWS), or airspace management (AIM SAA). Different feeds, different schemas.
3
Browse the SWIM Users Forum archives (quarterly meetings) — they publish slide decks covering new capabilities, policy changes, and real-world integration examples from the community.
4
If targeting CDM or TFMS Request/Reply feeds, email Data-To-Industry@faa.gov early — those require FAA review and take longer to provision than standard SWIFT Portal subscriptions.
