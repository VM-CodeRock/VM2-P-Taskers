ODRA-25-965  ·  Docket: 25-ODRA-00976  ·  Served: March 21, 2025
Protest of Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC)
Protester: SAIC  |  Respondent: FAA Product Team  |  Solicitation: 693KA9-24-R-00005
Dismissed — Moot

BID PROTEST IT / TECH SYSTEMS

Decision Summary

SAIC filed a bid protest challenging an FAA procurement under Solicitation 693KA9-24-R-00005. On March 20, 2025 — one day before the Final Order — the FAA Product Team issued a voluntary corrective action notice fully addressing SAIC's protest grounds. Both protester's and intervenor's counsel concurred that the corrective action constituted an adequate remedy. ODRA Director John Dietrich dismissed the protest as moot under 14 C.F.R. § 17.19(c).

Key Reasoning

ODRA dismissed without reaching the merits. The corrective action's timing — the eve of the Final Order — suggests the FAA Product Team recognized evaluation vulnerabilities in SAIC's grounds and chose self-correction over adjudication risk. Under ODRA precedent, corrective action need not cure every ground; it must only provide a remedy that makes the protest moot as a practical matter.

Changeis Implication (ATEPS-II / General FAA BD): SAIC's use of ODRA as a lever — without needing a sustained ruling — is a model Changeis should have in its bid protest playbook. If Changeis is displaced on any ATEPS-II IDIQ task order or CLMRS recompete, a timely, well-grounded ODRA filing often triggers corrective action within the protest window. The corrective action here likely triggered a re-evaluation — creating a second-bite opportunity. Monitor Solicitation 693KA9-24-R-00005 on SAM.gov for re-award actions.

Claimant: Diversified Management Solutions  |  Respondent: FAA  |  Contracts: 6973GH-23-R-00057 & -00060

CONTRACT DISPUTE IT / PROFESSIONAL SERVICES

Decision Summary

DMS filed two contract disputes against the FAA arising from contracts 6973GH-23-R-00057 and -00060. Both parties filed a joint motion to dismiss both disputes with prejudice, reflecting a negotiated resolution. ODRA granted the joint motion under 14 C.F.R. § 17.31(d). Dismissal with prejudice bars DMS from re-filing these specific claims.

Key Reasoning

Joint dismissal with prejudice in a contract dispute context almost invariably reflects a settlement — typically a contract modification, additional payment, or scope adjustment that satisfied DMS's monetary claims. The dual-docket structure (two disputes consolidated) suggests DMS had layered contract claims, possibly spanning different performance periods or CLINs on related IT or professional services contracts.

Changeis Implication (CLMRS / MAPS Program Delivery): DMS's pattern — filing, then settling — demonstrates the value of ODRA as a negotiation forum rather than a pure litigation venue. For Changeis on CLMRS and MAPS, where scope-change and equitable adjustment risk is present: document every change, every CO direction, and every performance impact contemporaneously. A well-documented REA (Request for Equitable Adjustment) submitted proactively is far more powerful than a post-dispute ODRA filing. DMS's two disputes filed from a single contract portfolio is a cautionary signal — consolidate claims strategically rather than filing piecemeal.

Decision Summary

Mythics, Inc. (Oracle's authorized reseller) and Oracle America, Inc. jointly protested FAA SAM Notice 692M15-25-R-00009. All parties filed a joint motion to dismiss without prejudice on December 23, 2024. ODRA granted the motion under 14 C.F.R. § 17.19(c). The SAM prefix 692M15 (FAA Mike Monroney Aeronautical Center, Oklahoma City) indicates an enterprise IT/database procurement — plausibly an Oracle database or enterprise software license competition. Without-prejudice preservation is strategically important for Oracle, which faces sustained competition from AWS/Azure on FAA cloud migrations.

ODRA-24-961  ·  Docket: 23-ODRA-00948  ·  Served: December 3, 2024
Contract Dispute of Prow'ess Construction Corporation
Claimant: Prow'ess Construction Corp.  |  FAA Counterclaim  |  Contract: 697DCK-20-C-00150 (Indianapolis ARTCC Roof Replacement)
Both Sides Denied (Nominal $1K Award)

CONTRACT DISPUTE CONSTRUCTION / FACILITIES

Decision Summary

Prow'ess Construction claimed $13,029.02 for roof board damage during an FAA-directed suspension of work on the Indianapolis ARTCC roof replacement contract. Prow'ess argued the suspension notice constituted a constructive change triggering equitable adjustment under the Changes clause. The FAA countered with a $113,395 claim alleging failure to install specified "Kato Lite" coping and deficient workmanship. ODRA denied Prow'ess's claim in full. ODRA denied the FAA's counterclaim in all but $1,000 — reducing it from $113,395 — because the agency failed to present "reliable and probative evidence" supporting the damage valuation.

Key Reasoning

On Prow'ess's claim: the suspension period did not constitute a "change" because the CO's suspension notice did not direct different work — it merely halted work. Under the Changes clause, a suspension without a direction to perform differently does not trigger REA rights for incidental storage damage. On the FAA's counterclaim: ODRA acknowledged workmanship deficiencies but held the agency's $113K damage estimate was unsupported by independent professional assessment or documented cost basis. This is a significant evidentiary holding — the agency bore the burden of proof on its counterclaim and failed it.

Changeis Implication (CLMRS / MAPS Delivery Risk): This is the most doctrinally important decision on the docket for Changeis program delivery. Three lessons: (1) Suspension ≠ Change: A CO stop-work order does not automatically entitle equitable adjustment for collateral damages — the direction must alter the scope of work. Changeis should not rely solely on oral or implied direction. (2) Document Everything: ODRA slashed the FAA's $113K claim to $1K purely for evidentiary failure. The same standard applies to Changeis claims — every REA must be backed by contemporaneous records, invoices, and expert support. (3) Counterclaim Risk: The FAA will file counterclaims. Changeis should ensure QA/QC documentation on CLMRS and MAPS is bulletproof — deficiency claims with documented, costed remediation are an FAA litigation tactic.
3. Key Themes & Patterns
▶
ADR / Corrective Action Dominates Resolution

60% of ODRA decisions on the current docket resolved via voluntary corrective action or joint dismissal — no merits adjudication. ODRA's ADR success rate (est. 65–90%) means the docket understates the true volume of FAA acquisition disputes. Changeis should use this as leverage: a timely, specific ODRA protest filing creates legal jeopardy that typically compels FAA self-correction.

⚠
Evidentiary Rigor = Existential for Claims

The Prow'ess decision reduced a $113K government counterclaim to $1K for lack of documented evidence. ODRA's "reliable and probative evidence" standard applies symmetrically to contractors. Changeis claims — whether REAs, change orders, or delay impacts — must be built on contemporaneous cost records from day one.

◆
IT / Enterprise Software Protests Rising

3 of 5 docket cases involve IT or enterprise software/GIS acquisitions. SAIC, Spatial Front, and Oracle/Mythics are all IT-focused. This reflects FAA's accelerating digital modernization spend and the corresponding competitive intensity in the IT services space — directly relevant to Changeis's MAPS, ATEPS-II, and CLMRS program areas.

■
Publication Lag — Active Cases Invisible

The ODRA registry has shown no new postings since March 2025 (14+ months). Given FAA's active procurement pipeline in FY2026, disputes almost certainly exist in the ODRA docket that are pending ADR or not yet published. Changeis should not interpret a quiet registry as an absence of competitive dispute activity.

●
Repeat Players Use ODRA Strategically

Spatial Front (prior sustained protest + EAJA fees), DMS (parallel dual disputes), and Oracle/Mythics (joint protest) are all sophisticated ODRA operators. They file with precision and extract value through corrective action or settlement. Changeis's BD team should track these companies' ODRA activity as a leading indicator of FAA procurement vulnerabilities.

✔
No Automatic Stays — Performance Continues

Unlike GAO, ODRA does not grant automatic stays. All five docket cases proceeded with contract performance during the dispute period. For Changeis, this means: if a competitor protests a Changeis award, Changeis can continue performance. Conversely, if Changeis protests, it cannot rely on a stay to halt the awardee's performance unless it meets ODRA's "compelling reason" standard — an extremely high bar.
