What is MAPS?

MAPS consolidates two of the Army's largest professional services vehicles — RS3 ($37.4B ceiling, $10.6B obligated) and ITES-3S ($12.1B ceiling, $4.4B obligated) — into a single unified IDIQ for knowledge-based professional services and IT services. This is the Army's primary acquisition pathway for the next decade, replacing vehicles that expire in 2027.

Section 1 — Executive Summary

The original Deep Dive Assessment (Tab 1) concluded that Changeis, Inc. should Monitor the Army MAPS solicitation and pursue subcontracting opportunities rather than prime. That assessment was correct given Changeis's solo position — but it did not account for a material change in strategic posture: the ArchZen Mentor-Protégé Joint Venture.

ArchZen MPJV, formed under SBA regulations as a formal 8(a) Mentor-Protégé Joint Venture between Changeis, Inc. (Mentor, 49%) and Kaizen Solutions Group (Protégé/8(a) SDB, 51%), fundamentally alters the bid calculus. The JV qualifies as a Small Business for the MAPS Small Business lane, where 25 IDIQ awards are available per domain — as compared to only 15 for Large Business. Under SBA MPJV rules, the JV may leverage Changeis's past performance, business systems, ISO certifications, and CMMI infrastructure as if they were the JV's own. Changeis's $284.3M prime contract portfolio — led by contracts like the $35.3M ICE SEVP (NAICS 541512) and multiple $5M–$25M FAA eFAST vehicles under NAICS 541611 — provides a deep bench of Qualifying Projects that would otherwise be unavailable to a small business bidder.

Secondary target: Domain 4 (Emerging IT, NAICS 541512). The ICE SEVP contract (70CTD022FR0000005, $35.3M obligated) is a standout QP that would earn maximum dollar-value points and could be the highest-scoring QP in ArchZen's arsenal. Estimated Domain 4 scorecard: 72,000–86,500 points (55%–67%).

Three gaps must be closed within the 28-day sprint to May 1: (1) ArchZen MPJV SAM registration, (2) Secret Facility Clearance for the JV entity, and (3) CMMC Level 2 self-assessment and SPRS filing. All three are achievable — but each requires immediate action. This paper documents the gate analysis, scorecard model, risk assessment, and critical path to submission.

Section 2 — Joint Venture Structure

MPJV Rules (RFP L.1.1.3): Unpopulated JV Provisions

An unpopulated JV may use the mentor's business systems, ISO 27001, and CMMC certification if the JV uses the mentor's IT systems. The JV must have its own SAM.gov registration, UEID, and CAGE code. Qualifying Projects may come from the mentor's past performance history. Past performance ratings (CPARS) flow through the mentor to the JV for scoring purposes. The JV entity itself need not hold its own ISO 9001 if the CO accepts the mentor's certification — a question to submit to the CO via the Q&A process before submission.
