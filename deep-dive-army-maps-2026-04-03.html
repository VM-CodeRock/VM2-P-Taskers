What is MAPS?

MAPS consolidates two of the Army's largest professional services vehicles — RS3 ($37.4B ceiling, $10.6B obligated) and ITES-3S ($12.1B ceiling, $4.4B obligated) — into a single unified IDIQ for knowledge-based professional services and IT services. This is the Army's primary acquisition pathway for the next decade, replacing vehicles that expire in 2027.

Section 2 — Joint Venture Structure

MPJV Rules (RFP L.1.1.3): Unpopulated JV Provisions

An unpopulated JV may use the mentor's business systems, ISO 27001, and CMMC certification if the JV uses the mentor's IT systems. The JV must have its own SAM.gov registration, UEID, and CAGE code. Qualifying Projects may come from the mentor's past performance history. Past performance ratings (CPARS) flow through the mentor to the JV for scoring purposes. The JV entity itself need not hold its own ISO 9001 if the CO accepts the mentor's certification — a question to submit to the CO via the Q&A process before submission.
