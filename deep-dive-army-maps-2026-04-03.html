What is MAPS?

MAPS consolidates two of the Army's largest professional services vehicles — RS3 ($37.4B ceiling, $10.6B obligated) and ITES-3S ($12.1B ceiling, $4.4B obligated) — into a single unified IDIQ for knowledge-based professional services and IT services. This is the Army's primary acquisition pathway for the next decade, replacing vehicles that expire in 2027.

Evaluation Framework

Self-Scoring Scorecard System

MAPS uses a unique self-scoring evaluation model. Offerors claim their own points, which are then verified downward by the Government. Scores can only go down during verification, never up. The top verified scores per domain win.

Qualifying Project (QP) Requirements

Go / No-Go Assessment

⚠ RECOMMENDATION: MONITOR — Pursue as Subcontractor / Teaming Partner
  • Do not pursue as a prime offeror at this time. Gate criteria (Secret facility clearance, ISO 9001, CMMC L2, $2.5M+ QPs with matching Army NAICS) and the self-scoring scorecard heavily favor firms with large-scale Army IT and professional services portfolios.
  • Actively pursue subcontracting opportunities with Tier 1 and Tier 2 primes competing in Domain 3 (Management & Advisory) and Domain 4 (Emerging IT). Changeis's management consulting, BPR, data analytics, and program support capabilities are valuable to primes needing small business subcontracting credit.
  • Build toward future prime eligibility by (1) securing Army-direct contracts in 541611/541512 NAICS, (2) obtaining ISO 9001 and CMMC L2 certifications, (3) growing individual contract values above $2.5M, and (4) establishing CPARS history with Army customers.

Strengths / Pursue Factors

  • Strong management consulting and program support pedigree with federal agencies
  • Active BPR, data science, and digital transformation capabilities
  • Growing AI/ML practice aligned with Army modernization priorities
  • FAA/DOT past performance demonstrates federal credibility
  • 350 total awards across domains creates wide opportunity for subcontracting
  • 10-year vehicle lifespan — time to grow into prime position for re-compete
  • Small Business reserves (25 per domain) may offer path if SB-eligible

Gaps / Risk Factors

  • No Army-direct contract past performance in portfolio
  • No Qualifying Projects meeting $2.5M minimum with matching NAICS
  • Gate criteria: Secret facility clearance, ISO 9001, CMMC L2 — status unknown
  • Self-scoring favors $50M+ contracts, Exceptional CPARS, low vacancy rates
  • All RAG matches are DOT/Volpe — no DoD/Army customer overlap
  • Intense competition: 135+ incumbents from ITES-3S alone will re-compete
  • Proposal cost is high — one-step submission with no early-stage gate filtering
