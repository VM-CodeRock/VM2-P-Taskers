POST 3 — Practical Tips / How-To

Type: Tactical tips | Image: "5 Warning Signs" infographic

5 warning signs your AI program has an adoption problem (not a technology problem):

  1. Employees use AI for basic tasks but default to manual processes for anything important
  2. AI tools are deployed but only a small percentage of eligible staff use them regularly
  3. Mid-level managers are uncertain about how AI fits their team's workflows
  4. No one tracks time-to-adoption, utilization rates, or AI output quality
  5. Governance documentation exists but no one is accountable for enforcing it

Sound familiar?

These aren't signs of a bad AI vendor. They're signs of a change management gap.

The fix:
✓ Build a real governance architecture before you scale
✓ Train managers first — they're the adoption multiplier
✓ Create safe experimentation spaces (no-penalty AI sandboxes)
✓ Measure adoption, not just deployment
✓ Appoint AI Ambassadors who convert skeptics to advocates

This is what we mean by "aligning people, processes, and technology."

The technology is there. The framework is missing.

POST 6 — Human Interest / Employee Perspective

Type: Human-centered | Image: People-at-work illustration with AI elements

The employee at the center of your AI transformation isn't afraid of AI.

They're afraid of:
- Not being consulted about changes to their own work
- Being trained once and then left to figure it out
- Having their job quietly redefined while leadership talks about "augmentation"
- Not understanding if their expertise still matters

Prosci's research identifies the top 5 reasons employees resist AI:
1. Lack of awareness about why the change is happening
2. Perceived change in their job role
3. Fear of the unknown
4. Lack of trust in leadership
5. Being excluded from decisions

None of these are about the technology.

All of them are about communication, trust, and inclusion.

The organizations achieving real AI adoption aren't those with the best tools. They're the ones who answered the question every employee is asking: "What does this mean for me?"

That's the most important question in AI transformation. It deserves a real answer.
