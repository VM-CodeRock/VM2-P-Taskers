Section 1 · ELB Track — Bid Decision Analysis

What Is the Emerging Large Business (ELB) Category?

The Emerging Large Business (ELB) is a distinct award tier introduced in the MAPS final solicitation (Amendment 0001, released April 1, 2026). Per RFP Section H.1, an ELB is defined as a company that has graduated from the applicable NAICS size standard within the last five years and has an average annual revenue for the last three fiscal years below $50 million. Out of 70 awards per domain, 15 are reserved exclusively for ELBs — carved out from within the 30 Large Business slots.

The ELB tier was created to bridge the gap between competitive small businesses that recently grew out of the SB size standard and established large-business incumbents, giving recently-graduated firms a more level competitive playing field with relaxed CPARS thresholds.
