02 · Client Context

DHS ICE SEVP — Mission, Posture, and Pain Points

Mission and Regulatory Environment

SEVP is the bridge between the U.S. Department of Homeland Security and the Department of State, authorized under the Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act (IIRIRA) of 1996 and Section 641 of IIRIRA. It operates SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System), the web-based system that maintains data on nonimmigrant students (F and M visa) and exchange visitors (J visa). SEVP sits organizationally within HSI, the investigative arm of ICE, meaning all knowledge assets are subject to Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) handling requirements, Privacy Act provisions, and DHS 4300A security policies.

The regulatory context imposes strict data handling requirements: documents containing student PII, school certification data, and enforcement-sensitive information must enforce least-privilege access, maintain audit trails, and reside on infrastructure with a valid Authority to Operate (ATO) traceable to FedRAMP High or DoD Impact Level controls.

Current SharePoint Pain Points

SEVP's knowledge ecosystem is currently anchored in Microsoft SharePoint (SEVPAMS Documents), with content also dispersed across Confluence wiki pages, JIRA project histories, and the Study in the State public portal. Field feedback consistently surfaces the following friction points:

  • Fragmented libraries: Multiple SP libraries with inconsistent folder taxonomies, no unified content model, and redundant copies of the same policy documents at different versions.
  • Weak search: SharePoint's out-of-box search does not traverse Confluence or JIRA, forcing analysts to context-switch between 3–4 systems per query.
  • Metadata gaps: Fewer than 30% of documents carry consistent classification tags (program area, document type, effective date, RBAC sensitivity tier), limiting filter-driven retrieval.
  • No AI readiness: The existing KM stack has no vector embedding infrastructure, no LLM interface, and no structured chunking pipeline — preventing any near-term generative AI integration.
  • RBAC gaps: Access control is applied at the SP library level rather than document level, creating over-provisioned access to sensitive enforcement documents.

AI Platform Under Development

SEVP leadership has signaled intent to procure or build an AI-driven analytics platform to support adjudication workflows, trend analysis, and decision support. The KM platform described in this proposal is explicitly designed to serve as the knowledge layer for that future AI platform — ensuring clean, tagged, chunked, and rights-managed documents are available as context for downstream AI agents.

SharePoint Connector Note

Amazon Bedrock natively supports a SharePoint Online data source connector for Bedrock Knowledge Bases, supporting incremental sync, inclusion/exclusion filters, and App-Only OAuth2 authentication. In AWS GovCloud, this connector pairs with OpenSearch Serverless as the vector store. For Confluence and JIRA, Changeis will deploy Lambda-based custom connectors using the respective REST APIs, normalizing outputs to the same chunked Markdown format before embedding generation.

Visio + Complex Document Handling

Visio (.vsdx) files present a parsing challenge not addressed by Textract alone. Changeis will use a two-pass approach: (1) export Visio diagrams to PNG via LibreOffice Draw in a Lambda container layer, then pass through Textract's AnalyzeDocument for text extraction and Claude's vision capabilities for diagram semantic description; (2) extract embedded XML shape data directly from the VSDX ZIP container for structured metadata. This produces a hybrid text+semantic representation suitable for embedding.

05 · Model Strategy

The Claude-Centered Model Stack — Justified for DHS

Primary Model: Claude Sonnet (Bedrock GovCloud)

Claude Sonnet serves as the primary reasoning and agentic model for the KM platform. Key capabilities directly relevant to SEVP use cases include:

  • Tool use and agentic orchestration: Claude's tool-use API enables structured function calling against SEVP knowledge base tools, SEVIS lookup hooks, and metadata APIs — enabling true multi-step retrieval workflows rather than single-shot RAG responses.
  • Citation grounding: Claude's native citation API returns source document references with character-level attribution, satisfying federal accuracy requirements and enabling traceability for policy and enforcement decisions.
  • Extended thinking: For complex adjudication-support queries requiring multi-step reasoning across multiple policy documents, Claude's extended thinking mode produces verifiable reasoning chains before generating a final answer.
  • Context window: 200K token context supports ingestion of large policy documents, regulation compilations, and multi-document synthesis in a single inference call.
  • FedRAMP / IL4/5 authorization: Claude models are approved for FedRAMP High and DoD IL4/5 workloads through Amazon Bedrock in AWS GovCloud (US) as of June 2025 — making this the only major frontier model family with this authorization level on AWS at launch.

Classification and Tagging: Claude Haiku

Claude Haiku runs the high-throughput metadata classification pipeline — processing every ingested document to assign program area tags, sensitivity tier, document type, effective date extraction, and RBAC access class. At roughly 10x the throughput and 5x lower cost per token than Sonnet, Haiku is optimized for batch classification workloads that do not require complex reasoning. Haiku outputs feed a human-in-loop review queue for edge cases before committing to the metadata store.

Embeddings: Amazon Titan Text Embeddings v2

Titan Text Embeddings v2 generates 1,536-dimensional dense vector embeddings for all document chunks. Titan v2 supports variable output dimensions (256, 512, 1536) and binary quantization, enabling cost/performance optimization. Its native integration with Bedrock Knowledge Bases eliminates custom embedding pipeline code and supports in-region GovCloud deployment without data egress.

Reranking: Cohere Rerank 3.5

Cohere Rerank 3.5, available on Amazon Bedrock, is a cross-encoder reranker that takes the top-40 candidates from the initial hybrid retrieval pass and produces a reranked top-5 based on fine-grained query-document relevance scoring. This two-stage retrieval pattern (high-recall initial retrieval + high-precision reranking) consistently outperforms single-stage vector search on precision metrics — research shows hybrid+rerank systems achieve precision of ~0.80 vs. ~0.70 for pure vector search on complex document corpora.

Why AWS Bedrock + Claude over Microsoft 365 Copilot?

The honest answer: MS 365 Copilot is a strong option if SEVP's primary goal is summarizing SharePoint content within the M365 ecosystem. However, for SEVP's specific requirements — multi-source ingestion (Confluence, JIRA, Study in the State), custom RBAC enforcement at the document level, agentic multi-step retrieval, and a path to custom AI models for adjudication support — custom Bedrock delivers more flexibility. Additionally, Copilot for Government requires Azure Government Cloud and a separate ATO track; SEVP's existing AWS infrastructure footprint makes Bedrock the lower-friction path. Copilot licensing costs $30/user/month on top of M365 E3/E5; AWS Bedrock usage is consumption-based and significantly cheaper at SEVP's expected query volumes.

Build Bedrock Agent with three tools: (1) Knowledge Base search tool, (2) Document metadata lookup tool, (3) Source URL citation tool. Configure Claude Sonnet as the agent's underlying model. End-to-end test: natural language query in Teams channel → Bedrock Agent → Knowledge Base → cited response returned in Teams. Implement session context (user Entra ID token passed as agent session attribute for RBAC enforcement). Deliverable: Working Teams bot demo + agent architecture documentation.

Wks 9–10
RBAC Enforcement + Guardrails + Evaluation Harness

Implement full RBAC: Entra ID group claims → Cognito → session attributes → OpenSearch metadata filter (sensitivity_tier and owner_group pre-filter applied before vector search). Deploy Bedrock Guardrails: PII entity blocking (SSN, SEVIS ID patterns, email), denied topics (classified material categories), grounding score threshold (0.75 minimum). Run RBAC penetration test: verify restricted documents not returned to unauthorized users. Run expanded RAGAS evaluation (100 questions): target Faithfulness >0.85, Answer Relevancy >0.80. Deliverable: Security test report + RAGAS Evaluation Report v2 + Guardrails configuration documentation.

Structured UAT with 15–20 SEVP end users (program officers, analysts, administrators). Collect satisfaction scores (SUS scale), search success rate, time-to-answer measurements. Remediate top UAT-identified issues. Performance hardening: OpenSearch index optimization, Lambda cold-start mitigation, Bedrock provisioned throughput assessment. Finalize operational runbook: monitoring dashboards (CloudWatch), alert thresholds, daily sync health checks. 08 · Innovation Lab Pitch

The Changeis Innovation Lab — AWS GovCloud AI Sandbox for DHS

A dedicated, secure experimentation environment where SEVP and Changeis co-develop, test, and harden AI capabilities before committing to production — compressing the gap between idea and deployed capability from months to weeks.

What is the Changeis Innovation Lab?

The Innovation Lab is a persistent AWS GovCloud sandbox environment operated by Changeis as a shared service for DHS program clients. It is not a skunkworks — it is a structured, security-compliant, sprint-driven environment where new AI capabilities are developed, evaluated, and graduated to production. Think of it as a staging runway with full FedRAMP instrumentation.

The Lab comprises three components:

  • The Sandbox: A dedicated AWS GovCloud account (separate from production) with pre-configured Bedrock access, OpenSearch Serverless collections, S3 buckets with appropriate SCPs, CloudTrail audit logging, and a RAGAS evaluation harness. New capabilities can be stood up, tested with synthetic or anonymized data, and torn down without impacting production workloads.
  • The Playground: A Claude/Bedrock experimentation interface (custom React UI) where SEVP program SMEs can directly interact with prototype AI capabilities, provide feedback, and co-design prompt templates — without requiring technical expertise. Guardrails and PII protections are always active.
  • The Agile Pod: A standing cross-functional team of 5–7 Changeis staff dedicated to DHS AI experimentation, available on a retainer or labor-hour basis.

12 · Appendix

A. Claude Model Card Highlights (FedRAMP Context)

As documented in Anthropic's June 2025 announcement, Claude models on Amazon Bedrock in AWS GovCloud (US) are approved for FedRAMP High and DoD Impact Level 4/5 workloads — the first frontier AI model family to achieve this authorization on AWS. Key safety properties relevant to DHS use:

  • Constitutional AI training: Claude is trained with Constitutional AI principles, reducing harmful output risk without relying solely on RLHF reward hacking artifacts.
  • Citation grounding: Claude natively returns source citations with character-level attribution when operating in RAG mode via Bedrock Knowledge Bases, supporting SEVP's traceability requirements.
  • No training on customer data: Amazon Bedrock does not use customer prompts or responses to train foundation models. All data remains within the customer's AWS account boundary.
  • Model versions: Claude 3.5 Sonnet v1 and Claude 3 Haiku are currently authorized in GovCloud; additional Claude 4 models are expected to receive GovCloud authorization in 2026 per Anthropic's roadmap.

B. AWS GovCloud Region Notes

AWS GovCloud (US) consists of two isolated regions — us-gov-east-1 (Ohio) and us-gov-west-1 (Oregon) — physically and logically separated from AWS commercial regions. Access requires a separate GovCloud account linked to a commercial root account. The regions are operated exclusively by U.S. persons on U.S. soil, satisfying ITAR and EAR requirements. Amazon Bedrock is available in both GovCloud regions. OpenSearch Serverless is available in GovCloud. Lambda, Step Functions, S3, Textract, Cognito, IAM, CloudTrail, and CloudWatch are all available in GovCloud.
