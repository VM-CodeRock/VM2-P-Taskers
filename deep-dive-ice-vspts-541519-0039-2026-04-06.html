Program Background: What is VSPTS?

The Visa Security Program Tracking System (VSPTS), also designated VSPTS-NET, is ICE's official system of record for the Visa Security Program (VSP) — the HSI-led counterterrorism mission that screens and vets visa applicants to prevent terrorists, criminals, and other inadmissible individuals from obtaining U.S. visas.


The VSP traces to Section 428 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 and deploys ICE/HSI special agents to U.S. embassies and consulates worldwide ("consular posts") in high-risk areas. As of the most recent OIG audit, the program operates at 45 consular posts across 29 countries.


What VSPTS does operationally:

  • Provides a globally-accessible web-based application for HSI agents at consular posts to record, track, and manage all visa security reviews
  • Serves as the repository for visa adjudication recommendations (issuance or non-issuance) sent electronically to the State Department
  • Supports the Security Advisory Opinion (SAO) Unit — processing SAOs for applicants with terrorism or proliferation flags
  • Integrates with CBP's Automated Targeting System — Passenger (ATS-P), which transmits analyst notes into VSPTS
  • Supports MANTIS SAO processing (targeting technology export control violations) — NSID screens 100% of MANTIS SAOs worldwide, inputting results into VSPTS
  • Interfaces with PATRIOT (Pre-Adjudicated Threat Recognition Intelligence Operations Team), TECS, and the State Department's Consular Consolidated Database (CCD)

System evolution noted in OIG-22-70 (September 2022): The DHS OIG found data transfer issues between ATS-P and VSPTS including a 4,000-character note limit. ICE resolved the character limit on July 22, 2022 and committed to redesigning the VSPTS interface. This procurement is likely the vehicle to fund that continued evolution plus steady-state O&M.

Key distinction: This APFS item is labeled New Requirement, No Contract — meaning VSPTS O&M is being carved out as a standalone contract for the first time. Previously, VSPTS support was bundled into the broader "Visa Security Program & International Operations Support Services" contract (70CMSD19FR0000029). This separation is significant: it narrows scope and creates a cleaner competitive field.

Forecast Notes & Strategic Intelligence
  • New Requirement carve-out: This is the first time VSPTS has been acquired as a standalone O&M contract, separate from broader VSP international operations support. This means there is no direct incumbent — an unusually level playing field (if Changeis had the vehicle).
  • CIO-SP3 vehicle headwinds: CIO-SP4 was cancelled (Feb 2026) and CIO-SP3 extended to April 2027. NITAAC has signaled future IT acquisitions may shift to GSA vehicles (aligned with EO 14240). If a follow-on VSPTS recompete occurs after April 2027, it may land on a GSA vehicle where Changeis IS positioned (MAS IT, OASIS+, STARS III).
  • National security vetting is a high-priority mission: The current administration's emphasis on immigration enforcement and border security makes VSPTS a protected program. Budget risk is low. Continuity of this system is a non-negotiable mission requirement for HSI.
  • VSPTS interface redesign: OIG-22-70 recommended and ICE concurred with redesigning the VSPTS interface to fix data transfer issues. The O&M contractor will own this ongoing modernization work — a richer scope than pure maintenance.
  • Clearance requirements: VSPTS handles law enforcement sensitive (LES) data including terrorism nexus records with 75-year retention. Personnel will require at minimum a Secret clearance; expect TS/SCI for some roles. This is a barrier to entry that favors cleared small businesses.
  • FFP pricing: At $2–5M over 4 years, this averages $500K–$1.25M per year. For a team of 3–6 FTEs doing O&M, this is a market-rate FFP. ICE uses FFP to push risk to the contractor — stable scope means this is manageable.
