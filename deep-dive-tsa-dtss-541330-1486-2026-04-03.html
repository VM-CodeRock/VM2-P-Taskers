TSA's Developmental Testing Support Services (DTSS) contract is the highest-scoring opportunity in Changeis's APFS forecast pipeline, with a RAG composite score of 0.745 — driven primarily by deep alignment with Changeis's FAA development test and evaluation past performance (FAA CLMRS engagement). The contract funds testing and qualification of Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) at the TSA Systems Integration Facility (TSIF) in Alexandria, VA — a 120,000-square-foot globally unique test bed for passenger screening technology — and is a critical statutory obligation under the 2001 Aviation and Transportation Security Act (ATSA).

Requirement Description

The DTSS contract provides essential testing support services and materials at the TSA Systems Integration Facility (TSIF). Work centers on Developmental and Qualification Test and Evaluation (DT&QT&E) — the process of rigorously testing and evaluating candidate Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) being acquired to ensure it meets TSA-specific requirements and maintains TSA's compliance with the statutory mandates of the 2001 Aviation and Transportation Security Act (ATSA).

The TSIF is a 120,000-square-foot globally unique test bed located in Alexandria/Arlington, VA, operated by TSA test engineers and scientists. It houses inline baggage screening systems, explosive detection labs, and next-generation checkpoint screening prototypes. The DTSS contractor provides T&E labor, specialized test equipment, data collection systems, and technical support across the full testing lifecycle: DT planning → execution → data collection, analysis, and reporting.

06 Competitive Landscape

The DTSS recompete is a specialized, relationship-intensive competition. Battelle has held this work for at least 5 years. Likely competitors include national labs, defense testing firms, and DHS-aligned T&E shops.

HIGH THREAT
Battelle Memorial Institute
Incumbent with dominant position. Battelle holds the existing DTSS BPA (70T04021A7672N002, $34.7M), won the 2023 DHS S&T TSL Certification Test contract ($19.2M), and has deep TSIF-specific knowledge including specialized equipment (robot arms, AGILENT mass spectrometers, GCMS). The 2021 award had only 1 bidder. Battelle is a nonprofit research organization with extensive DHS and national security lab relationships. Primary risk: if TSA structures the recompete as a competitive BPA or IDC, Battelle's institutional knowledge creates a strong technical proposal advantage. Strategy to compete: price competitiveness + teaming for program management, data analytics, and reporting functions where Battelle may be over-resourced.
HIGH THREAT
Leidos Holdings
TSA's largest T&E and sustainment contractor. Leidos holds an 8-year TSA sustainment contract covering 12,000+ screening units across 430+ airports. Their DHS footprint (SCI, logistics, engineering) makes them a formidable competitor if they decide to expand into developmental testing. However, their focus is operational testing and sustainment — less developmental. Likely a bidder on the consolidated TSA Testing recompetes initiative. Mordor Intelligence Airport Security Market identifies Leidos as a top-5 airport security systems player.
MED THREAT
MITRE Corporation
FFRDC with TSA advisory relationships. MITRE operates multiple DHS/TSA FFRDCs and holds deep institutional knowledge of TSA's test and evaluation framework. While FFRDCs cannot typically compete for contracts on which they provide advisory support, MITRE's subsidiary entities or teaming could surface. MITRE's Aviation Security division specifically supports TSA's requirements definition — creating conflict-of-interest constraints but also adjacency.
MED THREAT
Booz Allen Hamilton
DHS/TSA systems integrator with T&E capabilities. BAH holds broad DHS contracts and has engineering services capabilities under NAICS 541330. Their T&E practice for DHS and DoD programs could translate to TSA TSIF work. They would likely pursue as prime with physical test engineering subcontractors. Less specialized than Battelle in physical security equipment testing but strong in program management and data analytics overlays.
LOW THREAT
PAXON Group / Engility / SAIC
DHS mid-tier T&E support firms. Several mid-tier firms (PAXON, SAIC, ManTech) hold DHS/TSA support contracts in adjacent spaces. Less likely to have the specialized equipment T&E background for TSIF's explosive detection and CT scanner testing regime, but could surface as subcontractors or niche prime competitors if TSA segments the work.
Changeis Teaming Strategy
The highest-probability path is teaming with a hardware/physical T&E firm as prime or as a strong subcontractor. Target: firms with DHS/TSA TSIF experience but weaker program management and analytics bench. Changeis contributes: T&E program management, test documentation (CMTP, VRTM, test reports), data analysis, and reporting. Partner contributes: EDS-specific test engineering, TSIF facility access, specialized instrumentation expertise.
