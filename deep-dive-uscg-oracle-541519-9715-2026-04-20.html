1. Executive Summary

The U.S. Coast Guard C5I Service Center (C5ISC) is renewing its annual Oracle Enterprise Software License Maintenance for FY26. The requirement covers Oracle database and application software across desktops, servers, and enterprise USCG systems — estimated at $10–20M, procured as a Firm Fixed Price order via NASA SEWP V (contract NNG15SC71B) under NAICS 541519. The period of performance runs August 1, 2026 – July 31, 2027, with award anticipated June 1, 2026 and solicitation release targeted April 6, 2026.

The prior-year award (FY25, Award ID 70Z07925CALD00002) went to Akira Technologies Inc. at $10.68M with a single bidder, strongly signaling incumbent advantage. A separate FY25 brand-name justification (70Z07925QALD00006) documents 24 specific Oracle CSI numbers with an estimated value of $5.28M, indicating the total award spans multiple line items across Oracle's product portfolio.

This is a follow-on to an existing contract and is designated as a Small Business set-aside. Changeis's RAG system returned a MODERATE composite score of 0.6253, driven primarily by prior task order work involving Oracle database maintenance in DOT/Volpe center contracts. The opportunity is highly defensible for Akira, but navigable if Changeis can establish a teaming relationship with an authorized Oracle reseller holding SEWP V access.

Bottom line: HIGH barrier-to-entry due to brand-name sole-source precedent and incumbent lock; however, the Small Business set-aside and SEWP V fair-opportunity requirement create a procurement window. Recommend a Conditional Pursue — only viable if teamed with an Oracle-authorized SEWP V reseller and competitive pricing strategy is confirmed prior to solicitation release.

Conditional Pursue — Team or Pass
This is a recurring Oracle enterprise license renewal with a strong confirmed incumbent (Akira Technologies, single-bidder $10.68M FY25 award). The procurement structure — brand-name justification plus follow-on designation — creates significant barriers. However, the SEWP V vehicle and Small Business set-aside introduce structural openings that could be exploited with the right teaming partner.
