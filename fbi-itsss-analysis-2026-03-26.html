01 Contract Vehicle Overview

What Is ITSSS-2?

The Information Technology Supplies and Support Services 2nd Generation (ITSSS-2) is a multi-award Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA) established by the FBI's Finance and Facilities Division, Procurement Section. It is the largest IT contract vehicle ever established by the FBI, with an estimated $8 billion ceiling over eight years — effectively the successor to the original ITSSS BPA (2010–2018) under which the FBI spent over $2 billion.

Strategic Context

ITSSS-2 replaced what was once a $2B+ FBI IT spend vehicle with an $8B follow-on — a 4× increase — reflecting the FBI's massive IT modernization ambitions. It is now the FBI's primary acquisition vehicle for all IT services, and also accessible by all DOJ components, making it one of the most consequential federal IT vehicles active today.

02 Industry Day Intelligence

June 13–14, 2023 Industry Day

The FBI held its ITSSS-2 Industry Day on June 13–14, 2023 at the Davidson Center for Space Exploration, National Geographic Theater in Huntsville, Alabama. Three identical sessions were offered: Morning (June 13), Afternoon (June 13), and Morning (June 14). Registration closed May 14, 2023 and was conducted via biz.fbi.gov.

Key Industry Day Takeaways

Based on publicly available post-event analysis, here are the critical intelligence points from the Industry Day:

Evaluation Structure — Four-Phase Process

Phase I
General Info, Security & LCAT Crosswalk
Pass/Fail. Must map 110 of 123 LCATs to GSA Schedule to score "High Confidence"
Phase II
Corporate Experience Certification
Max 19 examples. SBs need 60% coverage; LBs need 75% of 23 sub-categories
Phase III
Corporate Experience Elements
Contract details for Phase II examples; self-scoring worksheet submitted
Phase IV
Price
Evaluated as complete and reasonable; Best Value Trade-Off for final awardees

Scoring: High / Medium / Low Confidence

High Confidence in each phase advances automatically. Low Confidence = effectively disqualified. Medium Confidence = 2-business-day window to decide whether to continue.

Market Intelligence

Task orders are competed among the 95 primes via SCORs (Standardized Call Order Requests). Small businesses compete only against other small businesses. The FBI is issuing regular SCORs across all seven service areas — the pipeline is active and growing. Early winners like Accenture, ECS, and Zolon PCS are establishing beachhead positions.

05 Go / No-Go Analysis for Changeis

Should Changeis Pursue ITSSS-2?

Go Factors ✓
  • WOSB/EDWOSB certification creates demand from large prime subcontracting goals
  • CMMI Level 3 + ISO certifications match FBI quality expectations
  • FAA/DOT IT modernization past performance is directly analogous to FBI's needs
  • $8B over 8 years = sustained pipeline of task order opportunities
  • Vehicle available to all DOJ components — expands market beyond just FBI
  • Active task order competition ongoing; early mover advantage with the right prime
  • 64 small business primes create broad teaming partner ecosystem
  • Emerging tech (ITS/CAV) maps to FBI Category 6 differentiation
  • Strategic comms and training map well to Cat 1 (Workplace) and Cat 7 (Other)
Risk Factors ✗
  • Changeis is not a prime on ITSSS-2 — must operate as subcontractor for now
  • Top-Secret Facility Clearance required; must verify Changeis's current clearance level
  • Limited FBI/DOJ-specific past performance compared to incumbent primes
  • Highly competitive — 95 primes all competing for each task order
  • Large firms (Booz Allen, GDIT, Leidos, Accenture) dominate large task orders
  • ITSSS-2 recompete expected ~2032; may be too late to get on next iteration as prime without FBI-specific performance
  • NAICS 541519 (primary ITSSS-2 code) size standard: $34M — monitor revenue to maintain SB eligibility
BD Recommendation
GO — via strategic subcontracting with 2–3 targeted ITSSS-2 primes
ITSSS-2 is the right vehicle for Changeis to pursue, but as a subcontractor in the near term. The highest-value play is to establish 2–3 teaming agreements with aligned small-business primes (priority: JCS Solutions, ZemiTek, NDi) within 60 days. Position Changeis as the WOSB/EDWOSB quality-certified consulting and IT modernization partner on specific task orders in Categories 1, 2, and 7. Document FBI/DOJ-adjacent performance on current task orders to build past performance for a prime spot on the ITSSS-3 recompete (~2031). The $8B, 8-year vehicle represents a generational revenue opportunity for a firm with Changeis's profile.
