1 · Executive Summary

The Transportation Security Administration's Office of Information Technology (TSA/OIT) is renewing its Ultimate Kronos Group (UKG) suite of software licenses and maintenance — the commercial workforce management platform underpinning eTAS (electronic Time, Attendance, and Scheduling), the automated system that processes bi-weekly payroll for over 50,000 Transportation Security Officers (TSOs) across 400+ federalized airports. The current contract, 70T03024F7667N054, was awarded in FY2024 to Immix Technology, Inc. (McLean, VA) — Arrow Electronics' federal IT distribution subsidiary and a NASA SEWP V prime holder — via SEWP V contract NNG15SC39B.

This is a straightforward brand-name software license renewal pass-through. TSA is purchasing UKG/Kronos subscription licenses and maintenance directly from an authorized SEWP V reseller. There is no professional services component. The procurement strategy — NASA SEWP V, FFP, no set-aside specified — is the same commercial software acquisition model used across DHS for enterprise software renewals. Solicitation releases June 8, 2026; award expected July 20, 2026; period of performance runs August 1, 2026 through July 31, 2027 (one-year base, Springfield, VA place of performance).

Immediate No-Go: Changeis is a professional services firm — not a NASA SEWP V prime contract holder, not a UKG/Kronos authorized reseller, and not a software license VAR. Three hard eligibility disqualifiers, each independently fatal. Additionally, Immix Technology (Arrow Electronics subsidiary) holds the incumbent contract with embedded UKG OEM distribution rights and SEWP V vehicle. There is no professional services component on which Changeis could add value through teaming. Archive and move on.

Scope Summary — eTAS System: TSA's eTAS (electronic Time, Attendance, and Scheduling) is the backbone of workforce management for the entire TSO workforce. Deployed at over 400 federalized airports and used by more than 50,000 Transportation Security Officers, eTAS processes timecards for bi-weekly payroll submission (26 payroll cycles per year) to the National Finance Center (NFC), a DHS HQ financial processing node. The UKG/Kronos product suite provides the full functional layer:

  • Time & Attendance: Automated timecard capture, approval workflows, bi-weekly payroll submission to GovTA (DHS HQ / NFC)
  • Workforce Scheduling: Centralized scheduling platform for Scheduling Officers managing federalized airport operations across all shift configurations
  • Employee Self-Service: TSO portal for schedule viewing, timecard access, time-off requests, accrual balances, and shift-trade submissions
  • Custom Interface Layer: Proprietary integration packaging timecard data for secure transmission from TSA to DHS/NFC; UKG products enable interoperability with the GovTA system

Product Context — UKG (Ultimate Kronos Group): UKG is the merged entity of Ultimate Software and Kronos Incorporated (merged 2020), headquartered in Lowell, MA. UKG is the dominant federal workforce management platform, used extensively across DHS, DoD, and civilian agencies for time-and-attendance, scheduling, and HR service delivery. In the federal market, UKG distributes through authorized channel partners including immixGroup — which operates as a high-value distributor for the SLED and federal public sector. Immix Technology, Inc. is the rebranded operating entity of immixGroup following Arrow Electronics' 2015 acquisition.

Prior Contract Context: Contract 70T03024F7667N054 was awarded in FY2024 to Immix Technology, Inc. (UEI L6REMB5VKLL9, CAGE 3CA29) through NASA SEWP V contract NNG15SC39B. Immix is a large business (Arrow Electronics subsidiary) and a well-established UKG federal channel partner, making them the structural incumbent with OEM distribution rights and an existing agency relationship across the TSA/OIT eTAS program.

Key Structural Point: This is a brand-name UKG/Kronos software license and maintenance renewal. TSA is not selecting a service integrator — it is renewing commercial software subscriptions through the authorized SEWP V distributor who holds a UKG channel partner agreement. The custom GovTA/NFC interface work embedded in the eTAS system was likely delivered under a separate professional services contract; this procurement covers only the UKG software licensing layer. Evaluation factors are price, vehicle eligibility, and UKG reseller authorization — not IT services capability.
