Requirement Summary

FAA's Acquisition and Contracting (AAQ) organizations require qualified contractor personnel to support a growing acquisition workload across the agency. Requested skill set is substantially similar to civil service OPM 1102 (Contracting), 0500 (Financial Management), and 0343 (Management Analyst) series, at competence levels equivalent to GS-5 through GS-14.

Core Disciplines

  • Contract Specialist support — solicitation development, source selection support, evaluation, award, modifications, closeouts
  • Financial Management support — funding execution, obligations tracking, cost analysis, budget reconciliation
  • Contract Administrator support — performance monitoring, deliverable review, COR support, file management

Key Requirements & Constraints

  • Non-personal services; no inherently governmental work (no fund obligation, no contract execution on behalf of FAA)
  • Decentralized ordering: each AAQ division places orders independently at varying times
  • Primarily Firm-Fixed Price for FTE personnel; up to 5% Labor Hours for transitional/surge needs
  • FAA seeking feedback on cost-of-living indexed pricing (geographic/remote work adjustments calculated, not renegotiated)
  • Governed by FAA AMS (Acquisition Management System), not FAR

Place of Performance

Multi-site per PWS §2.16 — primary AAQ HQ (Washington, DC area), Aeronautical Center (Oklahoma City, OK), and remote work permitted with geographic pricing adjustments under consideration.
