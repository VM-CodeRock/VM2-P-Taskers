1 · Executive Summary

TSA's Test and Evaluation Division (TED) is re-competing its flagship Operational Test & Evaluation Support Services (OTSS) contract — a multi-year vehicle currently held by Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC) under a $150M Blanket Purchase Agreement awarded in September 2022. The recompete is structured as an Indefinite Delivery Contract (IDC), with solicitation expected March 1, 2027 and award in Q4 FY2027 for a period of performance through September 2032.

The RAG engine scores this opportunity 0.7441 (STRONG), driven primarily by Changeis's FAA/DOT test & evaluation past performance on CLMRS (composite 0.7441) and multiple Volpe Center task orders covering engineering support, data analysis, and program evaluation. The domain overlap is genuine: field demonstrations, data collection, T&E program management, and multi-modal transportation security technology assessment.

Recommendation: Conditional GO
Changeis has credible T&E past performance in the FAA/DOT/DHS ecosystem, and the RAG match is the strongest in the pipeline at 0.74. However, SAIC has a decade-long entrenched position in this specific scope with deep TSA TED relationships, and the new contract is classified as a "follow-on to existing contract." The optimal path is a teaming strategy — pursue as a subcontractor or prime on a team with a DHS/TSA security T&E specialist (e.g., GST, Booz Allen, or Leidos) rather than competing as a standalone prime. Begin engagement immediately given the ~11-month runway to solicitation. Conditional on identifying a teaming partner with TSA-specific clearances and security screening T&E credentials by Q3 2026.
