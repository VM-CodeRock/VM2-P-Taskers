TSA's Office of Security Technology (OST) is recompeting its Acceptance Testing Support Services (ATSS) contract — currently held by Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC) under BPA 70T04021A7672N004. ATSS is one of four testing pillars TSA uses to qualify and field Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) at airports nationwide. The recompete will transition from a BPA structure to an Indefinite Delivery Contract (IDC), reflecting TSA's broader effort to consolidate and modernize its testing procurement architecture.

The contract covers Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT), Site Acceptance Testing (SAT), Operational Readiness Testing (ORT), and Verification Testing (VT) at both factory and airport locations, as well as test article development and end-to-end logistics support. Work is performed across the U.S. airport network, placing of performance Alexandria, VA, with nationwide field execution.

The RAG capability match of 0.7211 (STRONG) is driven primarily by Changeis's FAA CLMRS test and evaluation experience, which demonstrates directly relevant T&E program management, test planning, execution, and production readiness testing capabilities. This is a competitive — not sole-source — recompete under full and open competition, creating a genuine pathway for a well-positioned challenger team.

⚡ Conditional GO — Teaming Required

Changeis has DHS incumbency, T&E-adjacent experience, and strong RAG alignment, but lacks direct TSA security equipment testing credentials. A strategic teaming arrangement with a firm holding direct ATSS/TSE experience is the key condition for a viable bid. Pursue immediately given the March 2026 solicitation timeline.

Scope of Work

The Contractor shall provide the following acceptance testing activities for Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) at airport and factory locations:

  • Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) — validates TSE at manufacturer facilities prior to deployment
  • Site Acceptance Testing (SAT) — confirms TSE performance after installation at airport sites
  • Operational Readiness Testing (ORT) — verifies TSE is operationally ready for sustained use
  • Verification Testing (VT) — confirms equipment meets required specifications after modification or repair
  • Test Article Development & Logistics Support — development, procurement, management, and maintenance of test articles and related materials
  • Acceptance Test System Acceptance (ATSA) — comprehensive program management, technical support and execution, labor, personnel, expertise, logistics, and facilities
