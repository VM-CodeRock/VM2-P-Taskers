Initiative Background — What Is the Acquisition Superhighway?

The Acquisition Superhighway is a multi-year USCG digital transformation initiative targeting the end-to-end modernization of its contracting and acquisition (C&A) enterprise. The concept surfaced publicly in November 2025 when the Coast Guard issued a Request for Information seeking market input on commercial AI and COTS solutions to enhance procurement efficiency. The initiative is a direct outgrowth of Force Design 2028 (FD28), the Coast Guard's sweeping transformation roadmap launched in January 2025, which earmarked contracting and acquisitions as a named campaign area.

The core problem statement: USCG contracting is currently heavily reliant on manual workflows, with minimal end-to-end visibility, fragmented knowledge management, limited use of AI, and no single system of record for the acquisition lifecycle. According to MeriTalk's coverage, the agency seeks to move from "disparate and redundant business process workflows" to a unified, AI-driven platform.

The initiative has progressed from RFI (November 2025) → APFS Forecast (March 23, 2026) → Solicitation (April 6, 2026) → Award (May 1, 2026) — an unusually compressed 6-month timeline from concept to award, reflecting the urgency under Force Design 2028. As Washington Technology noted, the $5M–$10M ceiling may appear modest but carries outsized strategic weight: the Coast Guard obligated $3.6 billion in contracts in FY2025, meaning the Acquisition Superhighway contractor will sit at the center of how all of that spend flows.

BPA Vehicle Analysis — Who Can Compete?

The contract is structured as a BPA Call under FAR Part 8 or Part 13. This is the most important gating factor: only companies holding BPAs already established with the USCG CG-HCA (or DHS at large) can receive a call against those BPAs. The agency does not need to re-open the market — it issues calls to pre-qualified BPA holders.
