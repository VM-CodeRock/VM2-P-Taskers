Industry News & Policy
  • OMB Memo M-26-10: CIOs Must Report All IT Contracts Monthly Starting May 2026
    Policy OMB · All CFO Act Agencies Source: FedScoop / JD Supra
    OMB Director Vought signed M-26-10 requiring CFO Act agency CIOs to report monthly on all IT contracts they approved, starting May 2026. Agencies must also collect vendor pricing/utilization data and share with OMB and GSA. Policy sunsets October 2026 unless extended.
    Why it matters: Increases CIO oversight of IT contracting — expect slower awards and greater scrutiny on IT service contracts. Changeis proposals need clear cost justification and alignment to CIO-approved priorities.
  • GSA Draft AI Procurement Clause (GSAR 552.239-7001) — "American AI" Requirement
    Policy / AI GSA · All MAS Contracts Source: Fox Rothschild
    GSA's proposed GSAR clause requires contractors to: use only US-made AI systems, give government ownership of all data inputs/outputs, prohibit training AI on government data, report security incidents within 72 hours, and certify compliance with "Unbiased AI Principles" (no DEI programming). Clause expected in Refresh 32.
    Why it matters: Any Changeis AI-enabled proposal on GSA MAS must comply. Review current AI tool stack for non-US components. Update proposal boilerplate with AI safeguarding language before next submission cycle.
  • FY27 Budget: Civilian Agencies Face 10% Non-Defense Cuts
    Budget OMB · All Civilian Agencies Source: GovWin Recon
    Trump's FY27 budget proposes $2.2T total, with 10% ($73B) cuts to non-defense agencies. CISA, NIST, and IRS hit hardest. White House IT topline set at $75.7B — lower than FY26. Procurement consolidation expected.
    Why it matters: Budget pressure will push agencies toward IDIQ/GWAC vehicles and away from standalone contracts. Changeis should prioritize OASIS+, STARS III, and EIS positioning. Agencies will also pursue more sole-source and bridge contracts as acquisition offices thin out.
  • OPM Procurement Operations Fully Halted After Layoffs
    Disruption OPM Source: NextGov/FCW
    Sweeping OPM Office of Procurement Operations terminations have fully halted OPM contracting. Internal email confirms operational risk. Existing OPM contractors face contract disruption; new solicitations on hold.
    Why it matters: Any Changeis work at OPM is at risk. Flag for capture team to assess exposure. Watch for work migrating from OPM to GSA or shared services providers.
  • GSA EIS Networx/WITS Expiration — May 31, 2026 Final Deadline
    Contract Expiry GSA · DOJ · DHS Source: GSA EIS
    May 31, 2026 marks the final expiration of all Networx and Local Telecommunications contracts under EIS. DOJ and DHS received extensions to this date. All work must migrate to EIS contract by May 31.
    Why it matters: DOJ and DHS are Changeis target agencies. EIS transition creates ancillary IT services opportunities (migration support, implementation, testing). Watch for bridge or T&M task orders at both agencies in the next 30 days.
  • Army Issues $50B MAPS Contract Vehicle RFP — IT & Professional Services
    Army / Civilian Cross-Use NAICS 541330 Major Vehicle Deadline: May 8, 2026  ·  Full & Open
    Army released the RFP for the Marketplace for Acquisition of Professional Services (MAPS) — a $50B IDIQ for IT and professional services (NAICS 541330). May 8, 2026 deadline. Full and Open.
