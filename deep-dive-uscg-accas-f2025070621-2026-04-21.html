Scope — What This Contract Actually Requires

The ACCAS program supports the USCG C5I Service Center's electronics repair mission at Portsmouth, VA. The full requirement as stated in the APFS forecast:

"Incumbent must be able to provide engineer level Pinpoint Alpha/IIR services in test routine development and technical support to Repair facilities within the Coast Guard. Must be qualified IAW with Miniature Microminiature Module Test Repair (2MMTR) Navy program for Gold Disk routine development and technical assistance, must be able to recertify as 2MMTR technicians IAW 2MMTR Navy program requirements. Must be able to adapt to supply policies and procedures IAW USCG/DOD policy. Must comply with Gold Disk developer procedures and communicate with Naval Underwater Warfare Center (NUWC) regarding technical questions or signature analysis for routines and provide Logistic data required for sustainment and allowable parts list development."

Key technical elements unpacked:

  • Pinpoint Alpha / IIR: The Astronics/Diagnosys PinPoint IIR is a specialized benchtop circuit card diagnostic and in-circuit functional tester used by DoD maintenance facilities. The "Pinpoint Alpha" is an earlier-generation model. Operating this equipment requires trained electronics technicians, not IT consultants.
  • 2MMTR (Miniature Microminiature Module Test Repair): A NAVSEA-managed program that provides fault-isolation and circuit card repair capability across more than 300 Navy and Coast Guard vessels and shore locations. Certification requires hands-on training, proficiency testing, and site certification by 2MMTR inspectors — not a credential any consulting firm can acquire without dedicated electronics technicians.
  • Gold Disk: A DoD standardized Gold Disk DVD containing nearly 8,000 computer-aided test routines for fault-isolating specific circuit cards. Routine development (not just use) requires deep hardware-level electronics expertise, reverse engineering capability, and NUWC validation coordination.
  • NUWC Coordination: Developers must interface directly with the Naval Underwater Warfare Center (NUWC) in Newport, RI for technical questions and signature analysis — requiring an established Navy research lab relationship that takes years to build.
  • Allowable Parts List / Logistics Data Sustainment: Supporting parts provisioning and logistics data for circuit card assemblies under USCG/DoD supply policies — a logistics engineering function, not program management.
