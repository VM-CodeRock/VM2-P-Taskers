Industry & Policy Items
Fixed-Price EO — 90-Day Clock Running (Due ~Late July 2026)
President Trump’s April 30 EO mandates fixed-price contracts as the default for all federal procurement. Civilian agencies have 90 days to renegotiate their 10 largest non-fixed-price contracts; thresholds are $10M (most agencies), $25M (DHS), $35M (NASA). OMB must issue guidance within 45 days; OFPP must coordinate FAR revisions within 120 days. Legal experts caution that unilateral restructuring is impossible — contractors must negotiate, and refusal could result in termination for convenience. Changeis implication: Position on new pursuits with FFP/performance-based pricing; watch for recompetes of large cost-plus advisory vehicles. (FedScoop, Washington Technology, ExecutiveGov)
FY27 IT Budget — $75.7B Proposed (Record High)
The White House FY27 budget proposes a record $75.7B for government-wide IT. Key civilian allocations: VA EHR $4.2B, VA total IT $6.3B, DOE AI $1.2B (7 AI supercomputers), Treasury Cyber $15.2M infusion, NNSA $32.8B (+12%). CISA cut $707M (critical — downstream impact on civilian cybersecurity work). NIST cut $993M, eliminating MEP. IRS cut $1.4B but retains tech modernization language. Overall civilian non-discretionary spending proposed –23% vs. FY26. (Nextgov/FCW)
FAA Modernization — Multiple Active Procurements
FAA has three active acquisition efforts with near-term windows: (1) Tower Simulation System Enhancement 2 (ChBA framework, multi-phase, concept papers submitted); (2) APEX — Air Traffic Controller Academy Performance & Excellence System (platform analytics, concept papers due April 17); (3) Post-Quantum Cryptography for NAS systems (RFI closed April 24). FAA also paused the SAVES IT procurement (personal computing, network infra) pending solicitation amendments — no revised RFP date announced. SAM SAVES colocation special notice (NAICS 518210) deadline May 20 is most immediate action item. (GovCon Wire — DOT/FAA)
ECS / Everforth Rebrand — Competitive Landscape Shift
ECS has rebranded as Everforth ECS following ASGN’s corporate rebrand. This signals continued integration of the ASGN federal portfolio and may affect teaming dynamics on upcoming GSA, DHS, and VA vehicles where ECS has incumbent positions.
