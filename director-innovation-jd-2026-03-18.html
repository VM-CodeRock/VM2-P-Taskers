Director of Innovation

Changeis, Inc. | Arlington, VA (Potomac/DC Metro Area)

About Changeis

Changeis, Inc. is an award-winning, woman-owned small business headquartered in Arlington, Virginia, that provides management consulting and engineering services to the public sector. Founded on the belief that lasting organizational change requires the alignment of people, processes, and technology, Changeis has built a track record of delivering enterprise-scale transformations across some of the federal government's most mission-critical programs.

Changeis delivers unparalleled expertise across four core practice areas:

  • Enterprise Technology Solutions — Large-scale IT modernization, Agile/SAFe transformation, cloud computing, legacy system replacement, and emerging technologies
  • Mission Support — Aviation software analysis, program management, technical analysis, and National Airspace System (NAS) support
  • Strategic Communication and Training — Change management, stakeholder engagement, research dissemination, and workforce development
  • Emerging Disciplines — Cutting-edge capabilities at the intersection of technology, data, and federal mission

Changeis is a trusted long-term partner to the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), holding multiple major contract awards including a $103M ATAPS II contract and a $115M ATEPS II contract to support the FAA Air Traffic Organization. The firm also holds the GSA OASIS+ contract vehicle, enabling delivery of complex professional services across management consulting, technical and engineering, research and development, logistics, and financial services to federal agencies government-wide.

As Changeis enters its next phase of growth, the company is building an Innovation Lab to drive the adoption of artificial intelligence and emerging technologies across its client portfolio — and is seeking a visionary, hands-on Director of Innovation to lead this effort.

The Opportunity

This is a rare, ground-floor opportunity to build something. As the inaugural Director of Innovation, you will establish and lead the Changeis Innovation Lab — designing its structure, culture, methodology, and technical capabilities from scratch. This role sits at the intersection of technology, strategy, and client delivery: you will be equal parts practitioner, strategist, and evangelist.

The Director of Innovation reports directly to the President of Changeis and serves as a member of the senior leadership team. This is not a purely advisory role — we are looking for someone who writes code, builds prototypes, and rolls up their sleeves alongside junior teammates and client stakeholders alike.

Key Responsibilities

Innovation Lab Leadership

  • Design, build, and operationalize the Changeis Innovation Lab — defining its charter, governance model, processes, tooling stack, and success metrics
  • Recruit, develop, and manage a multidisciplinary team of AI engineers, data scientists, and innovation practitioners
  • Establish a rapid-prototyping and proof-of-concept (PoC) pipeline that takes ideas from problem framing to working demos in weeks, not months
  • Create and maintain a portfolio of reusable AI assets, accelerators, and solution frameworks that can be deployed across Changeis client engagements
  • Build and manage a lab environment that supports experimentation with generative AI, large language models (LLMs), agentic AI, intelligent automation, and data analytics

AI Implementation and Client Solutions

  • Serve as the primary hands-on technical leader for AI/ML implementations across federal client engagements — this means building, not just advising
  • Identify, assess, and prioritize AI use cases across Changeis's client portfolio, with a particular focus on FAA, federal acquisition, and IT modernization programs
  • Lead applied AI projects end-to-end: scoping, data assessment, model development or integration, testing, ATO (Authority to Operate) support, deployment, and performance monitoring
  • Develop and apply LLM-based solutions (RAG architectures, agentic workflows, prompt engineering, fine-tuning) to solve real federal mission problems
  • Build intelligent automation tools that reduce manual effort in acquisition, data analysis, technical documentation, reporting, and program management workflows
  • Ensure all AI implementations are responsible, explainable, and compliant with applicable federal AI policies (OMB M-25-21, M-25-22, NIST AI RMF)

Strategy and Thought Leadership

  • Develop and execute the Changeis AI/innovation strategy in partnership with the President and senior leadership
  • Monitor and synthesize developments in federal AI policy, emerging technology, and GovCon best practices — translating them into actionable opportunities for Changeis
  • Represent Changeis at industry forums, conferences, and client executive briefings as a recognized expert in federal AI and innovation
  • Author white papers, blog posts, and case studies that establish Changeis as a credible voice in federal AI and GovCon innovation
  • Cultivate relationships with academia, federal research bodies, and technology vendors to keep Changeis at the forefront of emerging capabilities

Business Development Support

  • Partner with Changeis business development and capture teams to identify AI-led service opportunities and develop compelling technical approaches for proposals
  • Contribute to proposal writing, orals preparation, and client presentations with hands-on demonstrations of AI capabilities
  • Develop innovation lab showcase materials and client briefings that demonstrate concrete return on investment from AI adoption
  • Support the identification and development of new contract vehicles and teaming partnerships focused on AI and emerging technology

Internal Capability Building

  • Establish an AI literacy and upskilling program for Changeis staff, enabling consultants across the firm to identify and apply AI tools in their daily work
  • Develop and institutionalize standard methodologies, playbooks, and governance frameworks for AI project delivery within Changeis
  • Create an internal community of practice around AI/emerging technology, fostering a firm-wide culture of curiosity and experimentation
  • Coordinate with program delivery leads to embed AI solutions into active contract work where appropriate

Required Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Data Science, Information Technology, Engineering, or a related technical field (Master's degree strongly preferred)
  • 10+ years of progressive experience in AI/ML, technology consulting, or innovation roles, with a demonstrated track record of building and deploying AI solutions — not merely advising on them
  • Hands-on technical proficiency in one or more of the following:
  • Python (scripting, automation, data manipulation, model prototyping)
  • Large language models and generative AI (OpenAI, Anthropic, Llama, Gemini, or equivalents)
  • RAG architectures, vector databases, and semantic search
  • Agentic AI frameworks (LangChain, LangGraph, AutoGen, Crew AI, or equivalents)
  • Machine learning model development and MLOps pipelines
  • Cloud platforms (AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, or equivalent)
  • 3+ years of experience in federal government contracting — with fluency in the GovCon environment including FAR, ATO processes, federal acquisition, and the unique operational constraints of serving federal clients
  • Demonstrated experience leading innovation programs, labs, or R&D teams — including standing up new capabilities, managing cross-functional teams, and delivering PoCs that progress to production
  • Strong client engagement and executive communication skills — ability to explain complex AI concepts to non-technical government stakeholders and to navigate the "compliance-innovation paradox" inherent in federal work
  • Experience with AI ethics, responsible AI, and federal AI governance — including working knowledge of NIST AI RMF, OMB AI memoranda, and FedRAMP/cloud security requirements
  • U.S. Citizenship required; ability to obtain and maintain a federal security clearance

Preferred Qualifications

  • Active Secret or higher security clearance (Top Secret with SCI eligibility a plus)
  • Direct experience supporting FAA, DOT, or aviation domain programs — including familiarity with NAS systems, acquisition management, and ATO mission support
  • Experience supporting federal acquisition or change management programs with AI and automation tools
  • Prior experience standing up or running an innovation lab within a government contracting, consulting, or technology firm
  • Master's degree or Ph.D. in Computer Science, Data Science, or a related STEM discipline
  • Certifications in cloud (AWS, Azure), AI/ML (e.g., Google Professional ML Engineer, AWS Machine Learning Specialty), or project management (PMP, SAFe)
  • Experience with intelligent document processing, digital forms automation, or NLP applications in government administration workflows
  • Published thought leadership — articles, white papers, presentations, or conference contributions on AI in government or enterprise settings
  • Experience with DARPA, IARPA, SBIR/STTR, or other federal R&D funding mechanisms
  • Familiarity with the GSA OASIS+ or similar IDIQ contract vehicles and their role in delivering professional services to federal agencies

Why This Role, Why Now

The federal government is at an inflection point. OMB's AI memoranda (M-25-21 and M-25-22) are accelerating AI adoption across agencies, and GovCon firms that can credibly deliver hands-on AI solutions — not just slide decks — are rapidly differentiating themselves. Changeis holds deep, decade-long relationships with the FAA and other federal clients. The Innovation Lab will transform those relationships by bringing demonstrable AI value to every engagement.

This is a genuine opportunity to shape an organization's trajectory. You will have the executive support, the client relationships, the contract vehicles, and the budget to build something that matters. If you are a practitioner-leader who is energized by the challenge of making AI work in the real world of government — navigating compliance, legacy systems, and risk-averse organizations — this role was designed for you.
