4 · Supported Systems Analysis

Six TSD programs/systems are in scope. All are located across four TSA classified/sensitive facilities. The HW/SW renewal cycle is annual, primarily expiring in March — creating a predictable, repeating workload.

Secure Flight (SF)

TSA's passenger prescreening program that vets travelers against terrorist watchlists before boarding. Mission-critical DHS/TSA function. IT services provided separately by Accenture Federal Services ($199M, 10-year contract, 2022). HW/SW maintenance for SF infrastructure is a distinct sustainment track.

Security Threat Assessment Mission Platform (STAMP)

Platform supporting TSA's threat assessment vetting operations. Used for identity verification and background checks on transportation workers. Classified/sensitive operations at CSOC and AJOC. HW/SW licenses and maintenance renewals are time-critical for ATO continuity.

Vetting & Credentialing System (VCS)

Supports TSA credentialing programs including TWIC, HME, and other transportation worker background checks. ManTech was the prior IT O&M incumbent for SF/VCS (sole-source extension Nov 2024), supporting the transition to Cape Fox. VCS renewals are ATO-gating.

Mission Scheduling Notification System (MSNS)

Internal TSA scheduling/notification system. Dev Tech Group (DEV TECHNOLOGY GROUP INC) received a $4.1M BPA call for FAMS 2.5 MSNS Modernization in Sept 2025 — suggests active investment in this platform requiring maintained infrastructure.

Vulnerability Assessment Laptop Unclassified (Val-U)

Mobile vulnerability assessment platform used in the field. Laptop-based, unclassified — renewals likely include endpoint security, AV, and hardware warranty agreements. Straightforward annual renewal cycle.

Enterprise Analysis System (EAS)

TSA analytics platform. A separate APFS record (F2025071837 — "Enterprise Analysis System Hardware and Maintenance Support") was posted on 04/20/2026, suggesting EAS hardware/maintenance is being separately procured. Confirming scope overlap should be part of proposal strategy.

Critical Renewal Timing: The requirement notes "the majority of existing hardware and software vendor maintenance agreements expires annually in March." This means a new contractor must be ready to execute renewals immediately at contract start (09/30/2026) — the transition period is extremely tight and the government will heavily weight past performance on similar annual renewal cycles and transition risk mitigation plans.
