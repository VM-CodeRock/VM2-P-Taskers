Why This Opportunity Exists (OIG Audit Context)

A July 2024 DOT OIG audit (Report FS2024030) found that DOT's WCF lacks effective controls to determine whether billing-rate methodologies achieve cost recovery for IT services. OCIO could not validate 26 of 36 sampled transactions (~$194M+), and OFM lacked written policies for reserve management. This contract is the direct remediation. The FY25 IT consolidation across 11 OAs amplified these problems, and the planned FY27 HR/Procurement expansion makes modernization urgent. Every task area in the SOW maps directly to a specific OIG finding.

1. Executive Summary

Tiered Communications Plan

5. Task Area 4 — Cost Allocation, Billing & Financial Integration

The July 2024 OIG audit found that OCIO could not provide sufficient documentation for billing rates in 26 of 36 sampled transactions — totaling over $194 million — and that Delphi did not contain all valid WCF transactions, contrary to DOT Order 2300.3B requirements. Task Area 4 exists to eliminate these deficiencies permanently. EY leads this task area, with Changeis contributing stakeholder engagement and portal user experience.

Unified Billing Integration Layer (UBIL)

EY's federal financial management practice will design the Unified Billing Integration Layer — a multi-source data ingestion architecture that connects AWS/Azure/GCP cloud usage meters, ServiceNow IT service management records, telecommunications billing systems, and Delphi (DOT's accounting system of record) into a single, reconcilable data environment. The UBIL will produce a billing record for each OA charge that is traceable from source system to Delphi entry — directly addressing the documentation gaps the OIG identified.

The Billing Logic Engine will implement DOT's service catalog in code — translating each WCF service offering's billing methodology into an automated calculation that eliminates the manual reconciliation steps that currently produce errors. Rate cards will be maintained as version-controlled artifacts with effective dates, ensuring that the OIG's finding of inadequately documented billing rates becomes structurally impossible to replicate.

G-Invoicing Compliance & Delphi Integration

Treasury's G-Invoicing mandate requires all Intra-Agency Agreements to be executed and managed through the G-Invoicing platform using standardized 7600A (General Terms and Conditions) and 7600B (Order) documents. EY has implemented G-Invoicing across multiple federal agency environments and will ensure that every WCF IAA is compliant with the mandate, automated where possible, and documented with the bilateral modification process the OIG identified as lacking.

Delphi integration will be addressed through flat-file and API-based data exchange, ensuring that all WCF billing transactions appear in Delphi as required by DOT Order 2300.3B and DOT Order 1200.9A. The $13.8 million in transactions that OCIO excluded from OFM's monthly charges report — identified in the OIG audit — represents exactly the type of gap our Delphi integration architecture will structurally prevent.

Audit-Ready Compliance Framework

All billing outputs will conform to GAAP, FASAB Statement of Federal Financial Accounting Standards (SFFAS) No. 4 (Managerial Cost Accounting), FAR Part 31 (Contract Cost Principles), and OMB Circular A-123 (Management's Responsibility for Enterprise Risk Management and Internal Control). Changeis's compliance monitoring experience across multiple DOT programs — spanning grant management, information system governance, and financial program support — reinforces EY's technical compliance framework with operational accountability at the program level.1,6

OA Self-Service Portal & Executive Dashboard

Transparency is the OIG's primary finding for OFM — customers cannot understand what they are being billed for. The OA Self-Service Portal addresses this directly: each Operating Administration will have a real-time view of its WCF budget burn rate, service usage by category, and outstanding IAA balance, with drill-down capability to the individual transaction level. Changeis's portal and web development experience — across multiple DOT client environments — ensures that the Portal is not a technically accurate but operationally unused system: it will be designed with the OA financial staff as the primary user, not the architect.3,4
