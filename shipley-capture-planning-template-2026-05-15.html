1. Executive Summary

Purpose: This template adapts the Shipley Capture Guide v3.0 seven-phase Business Development Lifecycle to Changeis capture operations in federal IT services — specifically FAA, DHS, and USCG pursuits in the $1M–$100M range. Use it to plan, execute, and govern each capture from market identification through proposal award.

Shipley Associates codified the industry-standard GovCon Business Development Lifecycle in the late 1990s. Research shows that 40–60% of customers already have a preferred vendor in mind before RFP release (GovEagle, 2026). Teams that begin capture 6–12 months pre-RFP can shape requirements, build agency relationships, and set win themes before competitors react.

2. Shipley Framework Overview

The Shipley BD Lifecycle is a phase-based process model giving GovCon teams a repeatable, measurable framework for winning federal contracts. Each phase has defined tasks, inputs, outputs, and decision milestones. The framework is customizable — a small task order against an existing vehicle warrants a lighter touch; a major IDIQ recompete warrants all 96 steps.

5. PWin Scoring Model

What PWin Measures: PWin (Probability of Win) is NOT a statistical probability — it is a structured self-assessment of how well you have optimized your position on a specific opportunity. A 75% PWin means you have 75% executed the actions within your defined rubric for winning. It calibrates resource allocation, not guarantee of outcome (LinkedIn, Veit 2025).

5.1 Changeis PWin Factor Model

Formula: PWin = SUMPRODUCT(Weight × Rating/5) / SUM(Weights). Rate each factor 1–5 (1 = weak, 5 = outstanding). Weighted sum normalized to 0–100%.

Customer Relationship
Weight: 12% — PM/CO access, hot button knowledge, SETA influence
Competitive Positioning
Weight: 12% — Incumbent advantage, known bidder landscape
Technical Fit
Weight: 15% — Solution alignment, innovation, risk retirement
Past Performance
Weight: 12% — Recency, scope match, CPARS ratings
Key Personnel
Weight: 10% — Named resumes, availability, labor mix
Price-to-Win / Cost Realism
Weight: 15% — Target price vs. budget ceiling; wrap rate competitiveness
Teaming & SB Strategy
Weight: 8% — Partner capability gaps filled; socioeconomic strategy
Compliance & Proposal Risk
Weight: 8% — RFP compliance confidence; L/M traceability
Capture Maturity
Weight: 8% — Gate artifacts complete; Black Hat done; customer meetings
