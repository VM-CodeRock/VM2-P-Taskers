2 · Federal Contractor Onboarding — Key Milestones

For federal IT consulting firms like Changeis, the following milestones are critical to track per new hire. Each has a distinct deadline dependency that can block billable start dates.

A. Security Clearance Status Tracking

MilestoneTypical TimelineOwnerNotes
SF-86 / eQIP submission Day 1–5 New hire + FSO Must be initiated immediately on offer acceptance for cleared roles
Interim clearance granted 2–8 weeks DCSA Enables work to begin on contract; not permanent
Full adjudication (Secret) 3–6 months DCSA Background investigation includes 10-yr employment, credit, criminal review
Full adjudication (TS/SCI) 6–18 months DCSA Polygraph may be required for intelligence-adjacent contracts
Clearance transfer (reciprocity) 1–2 weeks FSO-to-FSO via DISS For hires with active clearances from prior employer — fastest path to billability
Best Practice

Use NSIPS verification for cleared candidates before extending offers. Verify via your FSO through DCSA. Pre-employment clearance verification eliminates costly delays after start date. Source: USFCR Federal Contractor Blog.

B. SAM.gov Registration — Staff & Entity Updates

  • Annual renewal deadline: SAM.gov registrations expire every 12 months — lapses block contract payments and awards. Assign someone to track all expiration dates and renew 90 days early. Source: USFCR SAM Registration.
  • New staff additions: While individual employees don't register personally on SAM.gov, adding new key personnel to contract-specific certifications (size status, ownership certs) may require updates to the company's SAM profile if they trigger changes to NAICS representations.
  • Banking/POC changes: If new HR or finance staff assume Points of Contact roles in SAM.gov, update those fields promptly to avoid disruption to payment processing.
  • Subcontractor registration: If Changeis brings on new subcontractors for any contract, ensure they have active SAM.gov UEIs — large primes strongly prefer or require this. Source: Procura Federal.

C. Contract-Specific Onboarding Requirements

RequirementWhenWhy It Matters
DD Form 254 (Contract Security Specification) review Before day 1 on cleared contracts Defines exactly what clearance level and access is required per task order
Agency-specific security awareness training Before system access granted Mandatory at FAA, DHS CBP, FMCSA; completion must be certified (e.g., Form 14616 at IRS)
COR/COTR introduction & badging Week 1 Physical and logical (PIV card) access to agency facilities and systems
NDA / NCA execution Day 1 Contract-specific NDAs beyond the company NDA may be required for classified programs
CMMC compliance verification Before handling CUI Required for any contracts involving Controlled Unclassified Information

D. Timecard & Labor Category Assignment

  • SOW alignment: Labor categories must match roles defined in the contract Statement of Work. Misclassification (e.g., billing a Mid-Level Engineer at Senior rates) can trigger audit findings and rejected invoices. Source: RedstoneGCI / Unanet Guide.
  • Default assignment: Set each new hire's default labor category in the timecard system (Unanet, Deltek, etc.) at the person-project assignment level before their first timesheet period closes.
  • T&M vs. Cost-Type: T&M contracts require exact labor category mapping to bill correctly. CPFF contracts use labor categories primarily for cost tracking and budgeting.
  • Rate structure: Confirm that the new hire's negotiated rate aligns with the contract's approved labor rate schedule — overrides must be documented before billing.
