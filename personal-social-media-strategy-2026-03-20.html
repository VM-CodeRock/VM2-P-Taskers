Optimal Posting Frequency & Timing

Recommended Cadence

Target: 3 posts per week — This aligns with HubSpot's benchmark that drives 5.6× more follower growth than irregular posting, without crossing into content fatigue territory. Posts must be separated by 24+ hours. Quality over quantity: one strong post outperforms five generic ones.

Day Optimal Time (ET) Rationale Recommended Post Type
Tuesday 8:00–9:00 AM ET Highest platform-wide engagement. Professionals are focused, low digital fatigue, not yet overwhelmed by the week. 40%+ engagement lift over baseline per HubSpot research. Thought leadership / industry insight (Pillar 1)
Wednesday 8:00 AM or 12:00 PM ET Two strong peaks: morning start-of-day and midday break. Strong among senior/executive audiences and decision-makers. Less saturated than Tuesday AM. Company milestone / win announcement (Pillar 2)
Thursday 8:00 AM or 12:00 PM ET Consistent engagement window. Strong for in-depth, reflective content. Captures attentive audience before end-of-week wind-down. Leadership / personal brand story (Pillar 3 or 4)

Government Audience Timing: Sprout Social's 2025 data specifically identifies the best times to post for government audiences as 10 AM–2 PM on weekdays, with Wednesdays and Thursdays showing peak engagement windows. Since Changeis's primary customers are federal employees, morning posts that federal workers see as they arrive at their desks (8–10 AM ET) are ideal.

What the Algorithm Rewards (2026)

SignalWhat This Means Practically
Dwell Time Write posts long enough to keep people reading. Open with a hook that compels scrolling. Posts with strong openings and 150–300 word bodies outperform short one-liners.
Comment Quality Reply to every comment within the first hour of posting. The algorithm scores reply threads higher than one-way comments. Ask a question at the end of posts to trigger discussion.
Topic Consistency Stay within your 2–3 core topics (federal IT + organizational transformation + GovCon leadership). The algorithm routes off-topic posts to a less relevant audience.
Saves (Bookmarks) Signals long-term value. Posts with frameworks, checklists, and "save this for later" content extend reach for weeks after posting.

What to Avoid (Dead Tactics in 2026)

Based on LinkedIn's 360Brew algorithm update in Q4 2025, these tactics actively hurt reach:

  • Engagement pods — detected as inauthentic, results in shadowban
  • Mass-tagging irrelevant people — kills reach; only tag people directly relevant to the post
  • External links in first comment — penalized; put links in the post body (ideally at the end) or nowhere
  • Editing posts immediately after publishing — wait 5+ minutes or the spam filter triggers
  • Hashtag stuffing — hashtags haven't influenced distribution since 2023; 1–3 relevant tags max
  • Generic motivational content — erodes your authority signature and routes to wrong audience
