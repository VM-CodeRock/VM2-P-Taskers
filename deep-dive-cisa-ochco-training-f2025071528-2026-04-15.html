Requirement Summary

The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) Office of the Chief Human Capital Officer (OCHCO) requires contractor support services to: (1) monitor the performance of education and training programs; (2) manage the distribution and allocation of educational and training resources across the agency in a timely and cost-effective manner; and (3) minimize personnel required for system maintenance and operations of the Training and Education System (TES).

This is a follow-on to CISA's current CTES (CISA Training and Education System) contract held by KnowledgeBank, Inc. under HCaTS-SB. The recompete moves to the OASIS+ vehicle and shifts the NAICS code from 541612 (HR Consulting) to 541611 (Administrative Management Consulting), with a small business set-aside maintained.

Agency Context

CISA OCHCO is responsible for the full spectrum of human capital management for a workforce of approximately 3,200 federal employees. The Training and Education System supports OCHCO's mandate to track, deliver, and report on employee training — a function that gained increased scrutiny following a September 2025 DHS OIG audit that found CISA OCHCO failed to maintain adequate records for the Cybersecurity Retention Incentive Program ($138M in FY2020–2024 spending). This audit underscores the urgency of robust system maintenance and performance monitoring — directly aligned with this contract's scope.

CISA also launched CISA Learning in late 2024, a new LMS replacing FedVTE and multiple legacy platforms, making this contract critical during a period of active system migration and consolidation.
