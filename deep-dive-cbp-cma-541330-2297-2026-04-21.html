1 · Executive Summary
Bottom Line This is a high-priority, full-and-open competition opportunity tied directly to the 2025 Congressional Reconciliation Bill's projected $51.6B border security investment. CBP needs a Construction Management as Advisor (CMa) to oversee an estimated 30–40 capital construction projects at the southwest border and nationally. The BPA vehicle, 5-year PoP, and ~$100M ceiling (with $3.6B project pipeline) make this a landmark win for any qualified engineering firm. RAG score of 0.7046 (STRONG) indicates strong portfolio alignment.

CBP's Office of Facilities and Asset Management (OFAM) — specifically the Administration and Facilities Requirements Office (AFRO) and contracting division (AFCD) — is establishing a Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA) under FAR Part 8 (GSA Schedule 541330ENG) for independent, advisory-only construction management oversight. The scope is unprecedented in size for a CBP CMa requirement: projects valued at up to $5 billion drawn from Reconciliation Bill authorization, predominantly at the southwest border.

This is a new requirement with no incumbent — CBP's prior CMa work was executed through separate task orders (Parsons/AECOM for the border wall program, Planate for port-of-entry CMES). The solicitation was originally expected January 5, 2026; as of April 2026, the procurement is in late pre-solicitation/RFP stage with award targeted for Q3 FY2026. Full and open competition means the field is wide open to both large and small businesses through the GSA MAS.

Key Signals — Why This Matters

  • Directly tied to "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" $46.5B border barrier investment
  • CBP concurrently procuring Design-Build IDIQ (70B01C26R00000007) — CMa is the independent oversight layer
  • AECOM ($594M) and Parsons ($609M) already won DHS border wall PM contracts — creating conflict of interest openings for independent CMa firms
  • Changeis has active OFAM footprint via HCPS contract ($20M, 2024)
  • No set-aside — full field, but GSA Schedule 541330ENG required

Key Risks

  • Solicitation delay: Original Jan 2026 release not yet published as of Apr 2026
  • Construction management execution (not just advisory) could require licensed PE/CM credentials beyond current portfolio
  • Large ODC/subcontracting required — 30–40 active projects simultaneously
  • Protest risk is elevated given size and political visibility
  • Reconciliation Bill passage uncertainty — could affect obligated funding

Scope Summary CBP seeks a highly qualified CMa to provide expert, independent oversight and guidance across design and construction phases of capital projects funded through the 2025 Congressional Reconciliation Bill (One Big Beautiful Bill Act). The CMa does not design or construct — it advises, reviews, and independently validates quality/cost/schedule. Specific services include: A&E advisory (planning, design reviews, RFI responses, cost estimates, proposal reviews, submittal reviews, SOW development, technical oversight, sustainable building), and construction phase advisory (timeline oversight, budget management, quality assurance, risk mitigation, advanced construction technology integration). Scope covers approximately 30–40 capital construction design-build projects, primarily at the southwest border with smaller projects nationwide. Contractor must provide all labor, management, travel, and equipment/supplies.
Funding Context — Reconciliation Bill The 2025 House Homeland Security Committee reconciliation package includes $46.5B for the border barrier system (wall construction, river barriers, secondary barriers), $8.3B for CBP agent/facility improvements, and $6.3B for border inspection technology. CBP estimates up to $5B in capital projects requiring CMa oversight through this vehicle. This funding is designated outside normal appropriations and is specifically characterized as "not impacted by government shutdowns." The funding authorization created an urgent, large-scale construction pipeline requiring an independent advisor before the current administration's timeline ends.
