Executive Summary

CBP's Office of Finance is preparing to recompete its $16M–$50M financial operations support contract, currently held by Dextera (Centreville, VA; WOSB) under delivery order 70B01C21F00000644. The contract provides accounting clerks and program analysts supporting CBP's Office of Finance in Indianapolis, IN, and expires August 7, 2026. HigherGov forecasts the recompete at up to $50M — a 2.6× growth from the original award — indicating an anticipated scope expansion.

A parallel sources sought notice (PR20156685, deadline March 30, 2026) signals CBP is actively modernizing its financial close process with a COTS SAP-integrated tool. This is a separate procurement (NAICS 541511, no set-aside) but directly informs the operational direction of the finance ops recompete: the workforce that currently executes manual close procedures will transition to supporting an automated, AI-enhanced ERP environment.

Changeis holds active CBP past performance — including a $20M OFAM Human Capital Program Support contract awarded November 2024 — and possesses SBA-certified EDWOSB status. Changeis is contract-vehicle ready via MAS (GS-10F-054BA, SIN 541611) and OASIS+. The primary gap is direct CBP Office of Finance financial management experience; however, Changeis's DOT/FAA/Volpe financial management task orders provide functional equivalence that can be argued in a competitive proposal. The recommendation is a conditional GO with immediate teaming outreach to GAP Solutions (Dextera's own subcontractor with CBP National Finance Center experience) and early WOSB set-aside confirmation.

Scope Description

The contractor provides financial operations support services to CBP's Office of Finance — specifically deploying accounting clerks and program analysts across various functional areas. Based on the current contract labor data, this represents approximately 44 FTEs (avg. 91,600 hours/year) with a blended billing rate that grew from $27.00/hr (2022) to $34.57/hr (2024), reflecting annual SCA wage determination increases and scope growth. The August 2023 modification adding 3 personnel for Forfeiture Fund work and the April 2025 addition of a Program Analyst indicate ongoing scope evolution.
