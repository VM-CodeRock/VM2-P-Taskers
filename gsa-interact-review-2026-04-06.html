BD Pipeline Highlights

Two major items with direct Changeis opportunity signal this week:

FAA IT Modernization Challenge — Multi-Phase Competition (10-Year Contract) HIGH PRIORITY
FAA launched a challenge-based acquisition to modernize its mission support portfolio: ~200 applications and 3,000 databases. The multi-phase structure: Phase 1 self-scoring (due March 10 — now complete), Phase 2 concept papers (top 10 scorers), Phase 3 "modernization factory" live demonstration (top 5 finalists), Phase 4 award target September 30, 2026. Award value undisclosed but matrix scores contracts above $25M, $50M, and $100M/year. AI analysis of legacy code, automated security monitoring, and cloud-native migration are the stated focus areas — directly matching Changeis capabilities (NAICS 541511, 541512, 541519, 518210). Multiple awards possible.

Changeis angle: If Phase 1 was missed, monitor for consortium/teaming opportunities with Phase 2 finalists. Alternatively, watch for SOW fragments and sub-contracting from prime awardees post-September.
OASIS+ Phase II — Rolling On-Ramp Open Continuously (No Deadline)
GSA completed all Phase I protests and finalized Phase I awards. Phase II awards expected to begin rolling out in Q3 FY2026 (April–June). 13 domains, continuously open solicitations, no award cap. For Changeis, the relevant domains are Management & Advisory, Enterprise Solutions, and Technical & Engineering — all aligned to NAICS 541511/12/19 and 541611. If Changeis is not yet on OASIS+, this is the window to submit.

Changeis angle: Submit or expand domains now. QPP (Qualified Past Performance) can be reused across multiple domains in a single submission, substantially reducing proposal burden.

Policy Changes Affecting GSA Schedule Holders
Change What It Means Changeis Impact
MAS Refresh #31 — TDR Mandatory All MAS contractors must transition to monthly transaction-level reporting. Price Reduction Clause (PRC) and Commercial Sales Practices (CSP-1) disclosures eliminated. New AI provisions added. Expected April 2026 release, 60-day acceptance window. Contract compliance action required. Adjust reporting workflow from quarterly aggregate to monthly transactional. Engage GSA contracting officer promptly.
Revolutionary FAR Overhaul (RFO) FAR is being stripped to statutory requirements. Part 8 (ordering procedures) and Part 12 (commercial items) heavily impacted. SAM.gov Type 2 and Type 3 representations moving out of SAM and into individual solicitations. Interim deviations already in effect. Monitor solicitations carefully — requirements now in each RFP rather than SAM. Ensure proposal teams are reading the full solicitation rather than relying on SAM data alone.
Procurement Consolidation (EO 14240) GSA now executive agent for all IT GWACs. Agencies must use GSA-approved vehicles for common goods/services. Consolidating spend toward MAS, OASIS+, and GSS vehicles. Agency-specific IDIQs being cancelled or consolidated. Strong tailwind for Changeis MAS and OASIS+ positioning. FAA, DHS, and DOT acquisitions increasingly routed through GSA vehicles. Being on the right vehicles is now more critical than ever.
AI Procurement Requirements GSA negotiating AI tool deals (Perplexity, OpenAI, SAP) for agency use. MAS Refresh #31 includes AI clauses. Agencies evaluating vendors' AI experience in acquisition criteria (e.g., FAA challenge self-scoring matrix rewards AI deployment history). Changeis should document AI project history in past performance database now. AI capability narratives are becoming differentiators in self-scoring evaluations.
