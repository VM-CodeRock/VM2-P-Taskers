Top Stories — Biggest AI Developments

Claude Opus 4.7 & the LlamaCon Week — Historic Model Convergence

Model Release

Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 launched in the same week as GPT-6 (OpenAI) and Meta's LlamaCon releases, marking arguably the densest frontier release window in AI history. Opus 4.7 targets complex multi-step reasoning and long-running agentic workflows, competing directly with OpenAI's latest. Anthropic's annualized revenue is now approaching $19B, with the company having doubled compute capacity to enable this release.

Sources: Mean CEO Blog · State of AI Newsletter

Anthropic Conway: Always-On Autonomous Agent Enters Testing

Product Launch

Anthropic is internally testing "Conway," an always-on background AI agent that accepts goals rather than prompts — browsing the web, executing multi-step workflows, and delivering results without user involvement. Users assign objectives and Conway runs continuously, representing a fundamental shift from chatbot to autonomous operator. Raises significant questions around reliability, privacy, and oversight for enterprise deployment.

Source: MarketingProfs AI Update

OpenAI Surpasses $25B ARR, Eyes Late-2026 IPO

Market

OpenAI has crossed $25B in annualized revenue and is reportedly taking early steps toward a public listing as soon as late 2026. The company raised $110B at an $840B valuation in Q1 2026 alone. GPT-5.4 (released March 5) now leads computer-use benchmarks with a 1M-token context window, scoring 75% on OSWorld. GPT-4o is now fully retired from all ChatGPT plans as of April 3, 2026.

Sources: State of AI Newsletter · Mean CEO Blog

Google Gemma 4 Open-Weight Family — Apache 2.0 for Enterprise

Product Launch

Google released Gemma 4 on April 1 under Apache 2.0 licensing — four model variants from edge-device to data-center scale, each purpose-built for advanced reasoning and agentic workflows. Google is competing simultaneously on both the open and closed model fronts, with Gemini 3.1 Pro leading closed benchmarks (94.3% on GPQA Diamond) while Gemma 4 targets on-premise and sovereign deployments.

Sources: WhatLLM · Radical Data Science

Microsoft MAI Foundational Models — Speech, Voice, Image at Scale

Product Launch

Microsoft's MAI Superintelligence initiative released three production-ready foundational models: MAI-Transcribe-1 ($0.36/hr), MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2 — all available through Microsoft Foundry. Simultaneously, Microsoft Copilot now supports multi-model workflows where GPT and Claude collaborate in a single pipeline using a "Critique" quality-verification feature. These developments reinforce Microsoft's dual strategy: OpenAI partnership plus in-house model independence.

Sources: Radical Data Science · MarketingProfs
