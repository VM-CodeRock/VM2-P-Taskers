Priority 1 — Exclusivity Scope & Auto-Release Triggers

Article II § 2.3 contains the exclusivity clause. As drafted it is dangerously broad: it prohibits Changeis from supporting any other entity for the Solicitation and — critically — extends to support of other awardees under a multiple-award vehicle. There are no auto-release triggers if BAH fails to submit or is eliminated.

Art. II § 2.3 Preamble Exclusivity Scope — Narrow to ATLAS ChBA Only Critical
Current BAH Language
Team Member agrees that neither the Team Member, nor any of its divisions, affiliates or subsidiaries shall…
Proposed Redline
Team Member agrees that, solely with respect to the FAA ATLAS Solicitation (Solicitation TBD) and for no other FAA procurement or opportunity, neither the Team Member, nor any of its divisions, affiliates or subsidiaries shall… This exclusivity applies only to the ATLAS Solicitation and does not restrict Team Member from pursuing, bidding on, or performing under any other FAA contract, task order, or procurement, including without limitation ATAPS II, ATEPS II, CLMRS, and any follow-on or re-compete thereof.
Why: Changeis holds $218M in active FAA ATO contracts (ATAPS II, ATEPS II). Without an explicit carve-out, BAH could argue this clause blocks Changeis from performing on those vehicles. The carve-out is standard in federal teaming practice where the sub has incumbent positions.
Track-Changes Comment to BAH: Changeis has active prime contracts with FAA ATO (ATAPS II, ATEPS II). This exclusivity language as drafted could be read to restrict performance on those existing contracts. We require a specific carve-out limited to the ATLAS Solicitation only. This is consistent with FAR 9.601(2) contractor team arrangements.

Proposed Redline

Why: "Any manner competitive to this Agreement" is undefined and overbroad. It could theoretically prohibit Changeis from bidding on any FAA IT modernization work. Replace with a narrowly scoped prohibition limited to the specific Attachment A work on the ATLAS Solicitation.
Track-Changes Comment to BAH: The phrase "efforts that in any manner are competitive to this Agreement" is undefined and overbroad. Please confirm this is limited to direct competition for the ATLAS Solicitation and Attachment A work scope only. We propose the revised language above.

Priority 2 — Work Share Guarantee (Firm Commitment)

Article VIII § 8.1 contains only "good faith negotiation" language for the subcontract. Attachment A's SOW is vague and contains no minimum work share percentage. This is the second most critical gap — Changeis could contribute its FAA relationships and past performance to BAH's win, and then receive zero work.

Art. VIII § 8.1 "Good Faith Negotiation" → Firm Subcontract Commitment Critical
Current BAH Language
8.1 In the event Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract and the Prime Contract includes the Work, the Parties agree to enter into good faith negotiation of a subcontract for the Work (hereafter the "Subcontract").
Proposed Redline
8.1 In the event Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract and the Prime Contract includes the Work, Team Leader shall award Team Member a subcontract for the Work (hereafter the "Subcontract"), with Team Member's work share comprising not less than [___]% of the total Prime Contract value, consistent with Team Member's role as identified in Attachment A and as represented to the Client in the Proposal. "Good faith negotiation" alone shall not satisfy Team Leader's obligation under this section.
Why: "Good faith negotiation" with no minimum percentage and no issuance deadline is legally unenforceable as a work share commitment. A federal court reviewing this language would find no binding obligation on BAH's part. Changeis needs a minimum percentage (recommend 15–20% given ATLAS scale) and a 90-day deadline locked into the agreement.
Track-Changes Comment to BAH: Section 8.1 as drafted imposes no binding obligation on Team Leader. "Good faith negotiation" language does not constitute a subcontract commitment. Please confirm Team Leader will replace with language committing to award a subcontract with a minimum work share percentage and a specific issuance timeline. What work share percentage is BAH proposing for Changeis?

Priority 3 — IP Ownership Protection

Articles VI and VII address IP. Article VI requires Changeis to cooperate in resolving IP rights disputes on the Client's behalf — but provides no reciprocal protection. Article VII's joint invention clause defaults to negotiation with no protective floor for Changeis's background IP or past performance data.

Art. VI § 6.1 Client IP Rights — Protect Changeis Background IP High
Current BAH Language
6.1 The Client may require written justification for any restrictions placed on its rights in any technical data, software, reports and other intellectual property to be delivered under the Prime Contract. Team Member agrees to provide such written justification, if requested, for any such restriction asserted by Team Member in relation to the Program. Such justification shall be provided at no cost to Team Leader.
Proposed Redline
6.1 The Client may require written justification for any restrictions placed on its rights in any technical data, software, reports and other intellectual property to be delivered under the Prime Contract. Team Member agrees to provide such written justification, if requested, for any such restriction asserted by Team Member in relation to the Program. Such justification shall be provided at no cost to Team Leader. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Team Member retains all rights in Background IP (as defined in § 6.2 below) and shall not be required to provide any justification for restrictions on Background IP beyond asserting its pre-existing proprietary rights therein. "Background IP" means all intellectual property, technical data, software, methodologies, tools, processes, and know-how developed by Team Member prior to, or independently of, this Agreement, including without limitation all data rights, software licenses, and methodologies used in Team Member's existing FAA ATO contracts. Background IP shall not become deliverables under the Prime Contract without Team Member's express written consent in the Subcontract.
Why: Without a Background IP definition, the Client's broad data rights in federal IT contracts (under FAR Part 27 and DFARS 252.227 where applicable) could be construed to cover Changeis's pre-existing tools and methodologies developed under ATAPS II/ATEPS II. The Background IP carve-out is standard and essential.
Track-Changes Comment to BAH: Article VI as drafted does not distinguish between Background IP and foreground IP developed specifically for ATLAS. We require the Background IP definition and carve-out above. This is standard in federal IT services teaming agreements. Our tools, methodologies, and ATO-developed assets must remain our property.

Priority 4 — Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation Scope

The TA does not contain an explicit non-solicitation clause, but the exclusivity provisions in § 2.3 function as a de facto non-compete. Article III's conflict of interest provisions also impose ongoing restrictions. Several termination provisions in Article IX are triggered unilaterally by BAH with minimal notice. These require tightening.

Current BAH Language
3.1 Team Member must immediately notify Team Leader if, at any time during the term of this Agreement, Team Member becomes aware that it has an actual or potential conflict of interest, including without limitation a relationship of any nature which may affect or which may reasonably appear to affect Team Member's objectivity or ability to perform the Work ("Conflict of Interest").

3.2 As a material obligation hereunder, Team Member agrees that it will not, during the term of this Agreement, form a relationship that results in a Conflict of Interest.
Proposed Redline
3.1 Team Member must immediately notify Team Leader if, at any time during the term of this Agreement, Team Member becomes aware that it has an actual or potential conflict of interest, including without limitation a relationship of any nature which may affect or which may reasonably appear to affect Team Member's objectivity or ability to perform the Work that would be recognized as an Organizational Conflict of Interest (OCI) under FAR Subpart 9.5 with respect to the ATLAS Solicitation ("Conflict of Interest").

Tying the standard to FAR 9.5 OCI provides a defined, legally established threshold.
Track-Changes Comment to BAH: The COI definition in § 3.1 is broader than the FAR 9.5 OCI standard and could encompass Changeis's legitimate incumbent contractor activities. Please narrow to the FAR 9.5 OCI standard, which is the appropriate threshold for federal contracting purposes.
