Top 10 Must-Fix Items
ℹ️

Items 1–5 are CRITICAL — Changeis should not sign the TA without resolving these. Items 6–10 are HIGH priority and should be addressed in the same negotiation round.

#1 Critical

Exclusivity: No Release Trigger

Art. 2.3 locks Changeis out of ALL ATLAS work — including future task orders — with no auto-release if BAH fails to award a subcontract. Add 90-day post-award auto-release and a firm commitment trigger.

#2 Critical

Work Share: "Good Faith" Only

Art. 8.1 is just a good-faith negotiation obligation — BAH can walk away with no penalty. Add minimum guaranteed work share percentage (e.g., ≥15% of prime contract value) as a firm contractual commitment.

#3 Critical

Attachment A: Work Share Defined as "Best Qualified Staff"

Attachment A states work allocation is based on "best qualified staff at the price that meets the needs of the Client." This lets BAH zero out Changeis's work share at any time. Must include a floor percentage.

#4 Critical

IP: No Pre-Existing IP Carve-Out

Art. 7.1 covers inventions during performance but does not explicitly protect Changeis's pre-existing methodologies, tools, frameworks, and IP brought into the engagement. Add explicit pre-existing IP ownership and license-back language.

#5 Critical

Unilateral Termination Rights: BAH-Only

Arts. 9.3–9.9 give BAH 7 unilateral termination triggers with minimal cure periods. Changeis has none equivalent. Add symmetric termination for cause rights and require 30-day cure period (not 7 days).

#6 High

Exclusivity: No Carve-Out for Existing Clients

Art. 2.3(A) restricts any ATLAS-related services — but Changeis may have existing FAA relationships. Add a carve-out for pre-existing client relationships and contracts predating this TA.

#7 High

No Pricing Protections for Changeis

Art. 9.4 lets BAH terminate if Changeis pricing doesn't meet BAH's requirements — with no obligation to disclose what "competitive" means or share target rates. Add reciprocal transparency on rate requirements.

#8 High

Assignment: M&A Deemed Breach

Art. 12.1 treats any acquisition/merger of Changeis as automatic assignment requiring BAH approval. This creates a change-of-control veto right that could block legitimate M&A for Changeis. Narrow to actual material adverse change.

#9 High

Missing: Payment/Teaming Cost Reimbursement

No provision for reimbursement of Changeis proposal costs if BAH wins and then fails to execute a subcontract. Add a break-up fee or cost-sharing clause for proposal development investment.

#10 High

Conflict of Interest: One-Sided Notification

Art. 3.1–3.2 requires Changeis to notify BAH of any COI and prohibits forming new COI relationships. No reciprocal obligation on BAH. Add symmetric COI disclosure requirements for Team Leader.

Article I Proposal Effort
High ▶
High §1.4 — Failure to Respond = Material Breach, But No Reciprocal Obligation
Current Language
"Failure of Team Member to respond to Team Leader's reasonable request for assistance and information by such date required by Team Leader…constitutes a material breach of this Agreement."
Issue

Changeis bears material breach risk for missing proposal deadlines — which BAH sets unilaterally. BAH has no reciprocal obligation to respond to Changeis's requests or provide timely information needed for Changeis to fulfill its proposal obligations. BAH could set unreasonably short deadlines and then invoke this clause.

Recommended Redline
Failure of Team Member to respond to Team Leader's reasonable request for assistance and information by such date required by Team Leader mutually agreed by the Parties, or if no date is agreed, within five (5) business days of Team Leader's written request, constitutes a material breach of this Agreement. Team Leader shall provide Team Member with sufficient advance notice (no less than five (5) business days except in cases of urgent Client-imposed deadlines) and shall promptly provide Team Member with all information necessary for Team Member to fulfill its obligations hereunder.
Rationale

This converts a unilaterally-imposed timeline into a mutual agreement while still maintaining the obligation. It also creates a reciprocal information-sharing duty on BAH.

Medium §1.5 — "Ultimate Responsibility" Gives BAH Total Proposal Control
Current Language
"The ultimate responsibility for Proposal content and team composition shall be with Team Leader."
Issue

This gives BAH absolute discretion to modify Changeis's proposal contributions, change Changeis's staffing, or reduce Changeis's proposed work scope without notice or consent. BAH could change the proposed work allocation for Changeis downward in the proposal before submission.

Recommended Redline
The ultimate responsibility for Proposal content and team composition shall be with Team Leader, provided that Team Leader shall not, without Team Member's prior written consent, (i) materially reduce Team Member's proposed scope of work below that described in Attachment A, (ii) remove Team Member from the Proposal, or (iii) change Team Member's role in the Proposal in a manner material adverse to Team Member.
High §1.6 — BAH Has Sole Option to Amend or Terminate; Changeis Has None
Current Language
"Team Leader reserves the right, at its sole option, to either negotiate an amendment of Attachment A with Team Member, or terminate this Agreement in its entirety if, during the term of this Agreement: Client substantially alters or eliminates: (i) the Program; (ii) Team Member's portion of the Program…"
Issue

Only BAH gets this option. If the Client changes the work scope in a way that eliminates Changeis's work area, BAH can terminate — but Changeis has no equivalent right. Also, "substantially alters" is undefined and could be triggered by minor scope changes.

Recommended Redline
Either Party Team Leader reserves the right , at its sole option, to request either negotiate an amendment of Attachment A with Team Member, or terminate this Agreement in its entirety if the Client substantially alters or eliminates (as defined as a reduction of greater than 25% of the value or scope of the affected work area) : (i) the Program; (ii) Team Member's portion of the Program; (iii) Team Leader's portion of the Program; or (iv) if the Parties' understanding as set forth in Attachment A regarding Team Member's socioeconomic status or NAICS code changes. In the event of any dispute as to whether a change is "substantial," the Parties shall negotiate in good faith for no less than fifteen (15) days before either Party may invoke termination rights.
Article II Relationship of the Parties — EXCLUSIVITY
Critical ▶
🔴

This is the highest-risk article in the TA. Article II contains the exclusivity obligations that lock Changeis out of competing on ATLAS with any other team. The current language is extremely broad, has no auto-release trigger, and extends potentially indefinitely through the protest period. This must be substantially redlined before execution.

Critical §2.3(A)–(D) — Exclusivity Too Broad; No Release Trigger; Extends to All ATLAS Task Orders
Current Language
"Team Member agrees that neither the Team Member, nor any of its divisions, affiliates or subsidiaries shall: (A) provide the data or perform the services described in paragraph 1.1 and Attachment A to any entity other than Team Leader, (B) collaborate with any entity other than Team Leader for the purposes of the Solicitation, (C) independently submit a proposal for the Work, (D) undertake efforts that in any manner are competitive to this Agreement…"
Issue

Scope is dangerously broad:

  • Clause (D) "efforts that in any manner are competitive" could sweep in any FAA IT modernization work Changeis does for other clients.
  • No auto-release if BAH fails to award a subcontract within a defined period post-award.
  • Clause (E) extends exclusivity to any multiple-award IDIQ task orders where BAH is an awardee — meaning Changeis could be locked out of competitors' task orders on a multi-award vehicle indefinitely.
  • No carve-out for Changeis's existing FAA client relationships or pre-existing contracts.
  • No carve-out for BAH's failure to include Changeis in a subcontract after award.
Recommended Redline
Team Member agrees that neither the Team Member, nor any of its divisions, affiliates or subsidiaries shall: (A) provide the data or perform the services described in paragraph 1.1 and in Attachment A specifically to any entity other than Team Leader for the purpose of responding to this specific Solicitation, (B) collaborate with any entity other than Team Leader for the purposes of submitting a proposal in response to the Solicitation, (C) independently submit a proposal for the Work as described in Attachment A, (D) undertake efforts that in any manner are competitive to this Agreement, or (D) directly solicit or accept work from any other prime contractor bidding on this specific Solicitation for work that is identical to the Work described in Attachment A, or (E) in the event the Solicitation will lead to a multiple-award prime contract in which Team Leader is an awardee, support the efforts of any other awardee on task orders for which Team Leader is also competing and for which Team Member is a proposed subcontractor, or its subcontractors, in relation to its resultant prime contract without Team Leader's written consent. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the exclusivity obligations in this Section 2.3 shall automatically terminate and be of no further force or effect upon the occurrence of any of the following: (i) Team Leader fails to be awarded the Prime Contract; (ii) Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract but fails to execute a Subcontract with Team Member within ninety (90) days of Prime Contract award; (iii) this Agreement expires or is terminated pursuant to Article IX; or (iv) Team Leader materially breaches any obligation under this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of written notice from Team Member. Nothing in this Section 2.3 shall restrict Team Member from: (a) performing work under any contract or relationship existing as of the Effective Date of this Agreement; (b) performing work for FAA or any other federal agency that is not directly responsive to this specific Solicitation; or (c) responding to unsolicited requests for information or capability statements that do not constitute a proposal for the Work.
Priority Rationale

Without the auto-release trigger at item (ii) above, BAH could win ATLAS, refuse to subcontract Changeis, and Changeis would still be bound by exclusivity during the 60-day subcontract negotiation window (Art. 9.1(D)). The existing carve-out for "off-the-shelf products" (§2.3 end) is grossly insufficient — Changeis is a services firm, not a product vendor.

High §2.4 — All Client Communications Controlled by BAH
Current Language
"Team Leader shall conduct all contacts with the Client relative to the Solicitation and its subject matter unless Team Leader directs Team Member otherwise in writing."
Issue

BAH controls all FAA relationship management. Changeis's "strong relationships within the FAA" (cited in Attachment A as the reason for including Changeis) are neutered — those relationships must now be channeled through BAH. If BAH mismanages the FAA relationship, Changeis has no recourse and cannot directly advocate for itself.

Recommended Redline
Team Leader shall conduct all formal contacts with the Client relative to the Solicitation procurement process and its subject matter unless Team Leader directs Team Member otherwise in writing. Nothing herein shall prevent Team Member from maintaining its existing professional relationships with FAA personnel in the ordinary course of business, provided that Team Member shall not discuss the specific Solicitation or Proposal without Team Leader's prior written consent.
Article III Conflict of Interest
High ▶
High §3.1–3.2 — COI Obligations Entirely One-Sided
Current Language
"Team Member must immediately notify Team Leader if…Team Member becomes aware that it has an actual or potential conflict of interest… Team Member agrees that it will not, during the term of this Agreement, form a relationship that results in a Conflict of Interest."
Issue

All COI disclosure and avoidance obligations run only to Changeis. BAH has no reciprocal obligation. BAH — as the prime and a much larger firm — is statistically more likely to have COIs on a federal IT modernization program. If BAH has a COI that surfaces post-award, Changeis (as subcontractor) could be implicated and lose work.

Recommended Redline
3.1 Each Party Team Member must immediately notify the other Party Team Leader if, at any time during the term of this Agreement, such Party Team Member becomes aware that it has an actual or potential conflict of interest…

3.2 As a material obligation hereunder, each Party Team Member agrees that it will not, during the term of this Agreement, form a relationship that results in a Conflict of Interest.
Medium §3.3 — Former Government Employee Restriction Too Broad
Current Language
"Team Member represents and warrants that…it will not use in the proposal preparation effort for this Opportunity any former Federal government employees who had access to competitively useful nonpublic information relevant to this Opportunity."
Issue

This is a legitimate FAR-based concern, but "competitively useful nonpublic information" is vague and subjective. Given that Changeis employs former FAA personnel (as noted in Attachment A — "FAA Badged staff"), this restriction could inadvertently exclude Changeis's most valuable staff from proposal work.

Recommended Redline
Team Member represents and warrants that…it will not use in the proposal preparation effort for this Opportunity any former Federal government employees who had access to competitively useful nonpublic information relevant to this Opportunity that has not been publicly released or disclosed, as determined in accordance with applicable post-employment restrictions under 18 U.S.C. § 207 and FAR 3.104.
Article IV Costs / Liability Limitation
Medium ▶
Medium §4.1 — Each Party Bears Its Own Costs; No Proposal Investment Reimbursement
Current Language
"Each Party shall bear its own costs, expenses, and liabilities caused by or arising out of this Agreement, its performance, amendment, or expansion and neither Party shall be liable for any such costs…"
Issue

If BAH wins ATLAS and then refuses to subcontract Changeis (citing "good faith failure" under Art. 8.1), Changeis has invested significant proposal costs with no recovery path. This is standard in TAs but warrants a specific carve-out for break-up fee scenarios.

Recommended Redline
Each Party shall bear its own costs, expenses, and liabilities caused by or arising out of this Agreement, its performance, amendment, or expansion and neither Party shall be liable for any such costs, expenses, or liabilities incurred or other obligations undertaken by the other Party. Notwithstanding the foregoing, in the event Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract and thereafter either (a) declines to award a subcontract to Team Member without cause or (b) fails to execute a Subcontract within ninety (90) days of Prime Contract award, Team Leader shall reimburse Team Member for its reasonable, documented, out-of-pocket proposal preparation costs, not to exceed [INSERT AMOUNT OR 1% of Team Member's proposed subcontract value], within thirty (30) days of written demand.
Low §4.2 — Liability Cap Tied to "Out-of-Pocket Costs" Is Commercially Unusual
Current Language
"…the aggregate liability of a Party…shall in no event exceed the actual out-of-pocket costs of the injured Party incurred in the performance of this Agreement."
Issue

The cap is tied to the injured party's own out-of-pocket costs — not the breaching party's fees or contract value. During the pre-award proposal phase, Changeis's out-of-pocket costs may be quite small (e.g., $20K–50K in staff time), making this cap extremely low. In a breach scenario where BAH wrongly excludes Changeis post-award, actual damages could be in the millions.

Recommended Redline
…the aggregate liability of a Party…shall in no event exceed the actual out-of-pocket costs of the injured Party incurred in the performance of this Agreement the greater of: (i) the actual out-of-pocket costs of the injured Party incurred in the performance of this Agreement, or (ii) in the event of a claim arising after award of the Prime Contract, [XX]% of Team Member's total proposed subcontract value as set forth in the Subcontract or, if no Subcontract has been executed, as proposed in Team Leader's Proposal.
Article V Proprietary Information
Medium ▶
Medium §5.3 — Confidentiality of Agreement Is One-Sided
Current Language
"This Agreement is confidential and proprietary, and Team Member may not disclose its contents without the prior written consent of Team Leader."
Issue

Only Team Member is prohibited from disclosing the TA contents. BAH is free to disclose the terms of the TA — including Changeis's proposed work scope and pricing — to anyone without restriction. This is asymmetric and unfair. In practice, this could allow BAH to share Changeis's proposed approach with BAH's internal teams or other subcontractors.

Recommended Redline
This Agreement is confidential and proprietary, and Team Member neither Party may not disclose its contents without the prior written consent of Team Leader the other Party.
Medium §5.2 — No Control Over How Changeis IP Appears in Proposal
Current Language
"Proprietary Information disclosed by Team Member may be included in the Proposal to be submitted to the Client, with appropriate restrictive legends as specifically requested by Team Member and permitted by the Client's regulations."
Issue

BAH gets to include Changeis's proprietary information in the proposal with Changeis having only the right to "request" restrictive legends. If the Client doesn't permit certain legends, Changeis's IP is exposed. No provision requires BAH to notify Changeis before including Changeis IP, or to limit use to only what is necessary for the Proposal.

Recommended Redline
Proprietary Information disclosed by Team Member may be included in the Proposal to be submitted to the Client, with appropriate restrictive legends as specifically requested by Team Member and permitted by the Client's regulations. Team Leader shall (i) provide Team Member with a copy of any Proposal section incorporating Team Member's Proprietary Information for Team Member's review and comment no less than three (3) business days prior to Proposal submission, (ii) include only the minimum amount of Team Member's Proprietary Information reasonably necessary to demonstrate Team Member's qualifications, and (iii) immediately notify Team Member if the Client objects to or rejects any requested restrictive legend, so that Team Member may seek alternative protective measures.
Articles VI & VII Intellectual Property — CLIENT RIGHTS & INVENTIONS
Critical ▶
🔴

IP provisions are dangerously thin. The TA has only two IP articles, neither of which protects Changeis's pre-existing methodologies, toolkits, frameworks, or proprietary processes. Article VII covers only "inventions" (a narrow term) and only for the TA period. Article VI requires Changeis to cooperate with BAH on FAA IP rights with no protection for Changeis's own IP asserted in the Prime Contract.

Critical §6.1 — Changeis Must Justify IP Restrictions at Its Own Cost, With No Guarantee of Protection
Current Language
"Team Member agrees to provide such written justification [for IP restrictions], if requested, for any such restriction asserted by Team Member in relation to the Program. Such justification shall be provided at no cost to Team Leader. Team Member shall cooperate with Team Leader in every reasonable manner to resolve the Client's questions or assertions regarding the restrictions and validity of Team Member's assertion of intellectual property rights."
Issue

This clause requires Changeis to: (1) justify its IP restrictions to FAA at its own cost, and (2) "cooperate" with BAH — which could mean accepting BAH's legal position on what constitutes Changeis IP vs. government-owned data. There is no obligation on BAH to support Changeis's IP assertions or challenge FAA's position if BAH disagrees. BAH could "cooperate with FAA" by undermining Changeis's IP claims.

Recommended Redline
6.1 The Client may require written justification for any restrictions placed on its rights in any technical data, software, reports and other intellectual property to be delivered under the Prime Contract. Team Member agrees to provide such written justification, if requested, for any such restriction asserted by Team Member in relation to the Program. Such justification shall be provided at no cost to Team Leader. Team Member shall cooperate with Team Leader in every reasonable manner to resolve the Client's questions or assertions regarding the restrictions and validity of Team Member's assertion of intellectual property rights. Team Leader shall: (i) not take any position adverse to Team Member's asserted IP rights in discussions with the Client; (ii) promptly notify Team Member of any Client challenge to Team Member's asserted IP rights; (iii) include in the Prime Contract (and any Subcontract) such flow-down provisions as are necessary to preserve Team Member's IP rights as asserted in accordance with applicable law; and (iv) not require Team Member to grant rights in Team Member's pre-existing IP beyond those required by law or the terms of the Prime Contract.
Critical §7.1 — No Pre-Existing IP Protection; "Inventions" Scope Undefined
Current Language
"Inventions conceived in the course of performance of this Agreement shall remain the property of the originating Party. Inventions conceived jointly by both Parties shall belong jointly to both Parties with their specific rights and obligations to be negotiated in good faith."
Issue

Critical deficiencies:

  • No pre-existing IP clause: The TA says nothing about methodologies, frameworks, tools, processes, training materials, or software that Changeis brings into the engagement. Under certain FAR clauses that flow down (FAR 52.227-14, FAR 52.227-17), pre-existing IP used in performance can be subject to government license rights if not explicitly carved out.
  • "Inventions conceived in the course of performance" is ambiguous — does "performance of this Agreement" mean only the proposal phase, or the entire subcontract? Needs to align with the Subcontract.
  • Joint IP "negotiated in good faith" is unenforceable as written — no default rule, no deadlock mechanism. If Parties can't agree, who owns joint IP?
Recommended Redline — Add Pre-Existing IP Section
7.0 Pre-Existing Intellectual Property. Each Party shall retain ownership of all intellectual property, including without limitation patents, copyrights, trade secrets, methodologies, frameworks, tools, software, training materials, and other works of authorship, that were developed, conceived, or reduced to practice prior to the Effective Date of this Agreement or that are developed independently of this Agreement without use of the other Party's Confidential Information ("Background IP"). No license to a Party's Background IP is granted under this Agreement except as expressly stated herein. Team Member's Background IP shall not be subject to any claim of ownership or work-for-hire by Team Leader or the Client, except as expressly required by applicable law or as separately agreed in writing. 7.1 Inventions conceived in the course of performance of this Agreement shall remain the property of the originating Party. For the avoidance of doubt, improvements or derivatives of a Party's Background IP shall remain the property of that Party even if developed in the course of performance of this Agreement. Inventions conceived jointly by both Parties shall belong jointly to both Parties, with each Party having the right to exploit such jointly-owned inventions without accounting to the other Party, with their specific rights and obligations to be negotiated in good faith any licensing or commercialization restrictions to be agreed in a separate written instrument. In the event the Parties cannot agree on terms for jointly-owned inventions within thirty (30) days of a written request by either Party, either Party may exercise its independent exploitation rights without further obligation to the other, subject only to mutual non-disparagement.
Missing Clause — Recommend Adding

License-Back Clause (Absent from TA): There is no clause addressing the license BAH needs to use Changeis's Background IP in the Proposal or Prime Contract deliverables, nor the license Changeis would need to use any BAH Background IP in performing its subcontract work. Add a mutual, limited, non-exclusive, royalty-free license for the purpose of proposal preparation and subcontract performance only, with automatic termination upon TA expiration or subcontract termination.

Article VIII Subcontract Negotiation — WORK SHARE
Critical ▶
🔴

This is the weakest article in the TA for Changeis. The entire work share commitment is just a "good faith negotiation" obligation. BAH can win ATLAS, thank Changeis for proposal contributions, and decline to award any subcontract — suffering no consequences except the expiration of this TA under Art. 9.1(D). There is no floor, no minimum, no penalty, and no enforcement mechanism.

Critical §8.1 — Good Faith Negotiation Only; No Minimum Work Share Guarantee
Current Language
"In the event Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract and the Prime Contract includes the Work, the Parties agree to enter into good faith negotiation of a subcontract for the Work (hereafter the 'Subcontract')."
Issue

This creates no firm obligation. "Good faith negotiation" has been repeatedly held by courts to be unenforceable as a standalone obligation absent an agreed minimum term or floor. BAH can negotiate in apparent good faith, reach impasse, and walk away with zero liability under Art. 9.1(D). Meanwhile, Changeis was locked out of competing on ATLAS with anyone else.

Recommended Redline
8.1 In the event Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract and the Prime Contract includes the Work, the Parties agree to enter into good faith negotiation of a subcontract for the Work (hereafter the "Subcontract") Team Leader shall award to Team Member a Subcontract for the Work (hereafter the "Subcontract") representing no less than [XX]% of the total estimated Prime Contract value (or [XX]% of each task order value under a multiple-award arrangement), provided that Team Member is qualified, cleared, and capable of performing the Work. The specific terms of the Subcontract shall be negotiated in good faith and executed within ninety (90) days of Prime Contract award. In the event Team Leader determines it has a legitimate, documented basis for awarding less than the minimum work share percentage to Team Member, Team Leader shall provide written notice to Team Member with full justification, and the Parties shall negotiate in good faith for a period of thirty (30) additional days before either Party may invoke termination rights. 8.1(a) Work Share Floor. The Parties acknowledge that Changeis's unique capabilities (FAA relationships, badged staff, institutional knowledge) are a material basis for this teaming arrangement. Accordingly, the minimum work share percentage set forth in Section 8.1 is a firm contractual commitment and not merely an aspirational goal. Failure by Team Leader to award a Subcontract meeting the minimum work share percentage (absent documented cause) shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement and shall entitle Team Member to damages as set forth in Article IV.
High §8.3 — "Fair and Reasonable Price" Defined by BAH/Client, Not Objectively
Current Language
"The Subcontract shall be negotiated at a fair and reasonable price to be established after cost or price analysis in accordance with the requirements and regulations of the Client."
Issue

The cost/price analysis is conducted "in accordance with requirements and regulations of the Client" — which means BAH uses FAR Part 15 cost analysis principles that BAH controls. BAH could establish a "fair and reasonable" price well below market rates for Changeis's services, and Changeis has no independent audit right or right to reject.

Recommended Redline
The Subcontract shall be negotiated at a fair and reasonable price to be established after cost or price analysis in accordance with the requirements and regulations of the Client. Team Member shall have the right to provide independent cost or price data to support its proposed rates, and Team Leader shall provide Team Member with the basis for any cost or price analysis conclusions that affect Team Member's proposed rates within five (5) business days of Team Member's written request. If the Parties are unable to agree on pricing within the negotiation period specified in Section 8.1, either Party may invoke the dispute resolution procedures specified in Article [ADD].
Article IX Expiration & Termination — ASYMMETRIC RIGHTS
Critical ▶
Critical §§9.3–9.9 — BAH Has 7 Unilateral Termination Rights; Changeis Has Essentially None
Issue

Count of BAH-only unilateral termination triggers:

  • §9.3: COI determination (BAH sole judge, 7-day cure)
  • §9.4: Changeis pricing doesn't meet BAH's requirements
  • §9.6: Assignment/merger without approval
  • §9.7: BAH decides not to submit proposal
  • §9.8: CMMC certification issue
  • §9.9(A)-(C): Due diligence, anti-bribery

Changeis's only termination right is the mutual termination under §9.5 (both Parties for cause, 7-day cure) and §9.2 (mutual written agreement). Changeis cannot terminate if BAH fails to include it in the proposal, reduces its work scope below Attachment A, or fails to negotiate the subcontract in good faith.

Recommended Redline — Add Changeis Termination Rights
9.10 Team Member Termination Rights. Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, Team Member may unilaterally terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice to Team Leader if any of the following occur: (A) Team Leader materially reduces Team Member's proposed work scope below the level described in Attachment A without Team Member's written consent; (B) Team Leader fails to identify Team Member as a proposed subcontractor in the Proposal as required by Section 1.5; (C) Team Leader is awarded the Prime Contract and fails to execute a Subcontract with Team Member meeting the minimum work share described in Section 8.1 within ninety (90) days of Prime Contract award; (D) Team Leader materially breaches any other obligation under this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of written notice from Team Member; or (E) Team Leader experiences a change of control, merger, acquisition, or bankruptcy proceeding that materially affects Team Leader's ability to perform its obligations under this Agreement. 9.3 In the event that, during the term of this Agreement, Team Leader determines that Team Member has a Conflict of Interest, Team Leader may unilaterally terminate this Agreement, so long as Team Leader has notified Team Member of its intent to terminate due to the Conflict of Interest and allowed Team Member no fewer than seven (7) thirty (30) calendar days, or fewer as Client proposal deadlines dictate, prior to the effective termination date to cure the Conflict of Interest to Team Leader's reasonable satisfaction. Any dispute as to whether a Conflict of Interest exists shall be subject to expedited dispute resolution before a mutually agreed neutral.
High §9.4 — Pricing Termination Right Has No Transparency Standard
Current Language
"Team Leader may unilaterally terminate this Agreement for failure of Team Member to provide pricing which meets Team Leader's requirements for submitting a competitive Proposal."
Issue

BAH's "requirements" for competitive pricing are undefined. BAH could demand below-cost pricing and use this termination right as leverage. There is no requirement for BAH to disclose its target rates, share competitive intelligence about competitor pricing, or prove that Changeis's rates are actually uncompetitive in the market.

Recommended Redline
Team Leader may unilaterally terminate this Agreement for failure of Team Member to provide pricing which meets Team Leader's requirements documented, objectively justifiable requirements, as communicated in writing to Team Member no less than ten (10) business days prior to the deadline for pricing submission, for submitting a competitive Proposal. Prior to invoking this termination right, Team Leader shall: (i) disclose to Team Member the basis for its pricing requirements, including any market rate benchmarks or competitive pricing data available to Team Leader; (ii) provide Team Member with at least ten (10) business days to revise its pricing; and (iii) certify in writing that Team Member's pricing is genuinely non-competitive and not merely below Team Leader's profit target. However, as time allows, and as otherwise practicable, prior to such termination, Team Leader agrees to work with Team Member to allow Team Member a reasonable opportunity to provide revised pricing.
Medium §9.1(D) — 60-Day Subcontract Negotiation Window Is Too Short
Current Language
"Failure of the Parties, despite their good faith efforts, to reach an agreement on the terms and conditions of a subcontract within sixty (60) calendar days from the commencement of active negotiations…"
Issue

60 days is insufficient for complex federal subcontract negotiations, particularly for ATLAS which involves FAA-specific flow-down clauses, cybersecurity requirements (CMMC), and potentially complex pricing structures. BAH could slow-walk negotiations past 60 days and let the TA expire automatically, eliminating its obligation to subcontract Changeis without technically breaching the agreement.

Recommended Redline
Failure of the Parties, despite their good faith efforts, to reach an agreement on the terms and conditions of a subcontract within sixty (60) one hundred and twenty (120) calendar days from the commencement of active negotiations, or such period greater than (60) (120) calendar days as agreed to by the Parties…provided that active negotiations shall be deemed to have commenced no later than thirty (30) days following Prime Contract award to Team Leader, regardless of Team Leader's internal readiness to negotiate.

High §12.1 — M&A Change-of-Control Creates BAH Veto Over Changeis Corporate Transactions
