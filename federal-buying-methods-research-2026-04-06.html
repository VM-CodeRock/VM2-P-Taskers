Executive Summary — Key Findings

This report maps every major federal procurement vehicle and method against Changeis Inc.'s current position as an other-than-small Emerging Large Business (ELB) with a GSA MAS schedule, active OASIS+ pursuit, DOT/Volpe concentration, Secret FCL, and an approved 8(a) Mentor-Protégé agreement with Kaizen Solutions Group.

  • Channel concentration risk is real. Changeis derives the overwhelming share of revenue through a single channel: GSA MAS task orders at DOT/Volpe. The IPSS-3 BPA provides stability through 2031, but any Volpe restructuring or DOT budget reduction would create an outsized impact.
  • ELB transition closed some doors, opened others. Losing small business size status eliminates direct access to STARS III, Polaris, VETS 2, 8(a) sole-source primes, and SB set-asides — but the Kaizen MP JV preserves access to most of these via protégé eligibility. MAPS ELB lane is the immediate priority.
  • CIO-SP3 gap is manageable now. CIO-SP4 was cancelled (Feb 2026); CIO-SP3 extended to April 2027 and transitioning to GSA. The blocker for ICE VSPTS-type work still matters, but Alliant 3 and OASIS+ fill significant coverage.
  • OASIS+ is the single highest-leverage vehicle to maximize. Phase II opened 13 domains with continuous on-ramps. Changeis should hold this as an unrestricted prime and actively pursue task orders across all eligible domains — especially Management & Advisory, Program Support, and Engineering.
  • The Kaizen MP JV is underutilized. This JV can be used as a small business on STARS III, Polaris (SB pool), and 8(a) set-asides where Kaizen individually qualifies. It should be aggressively deployed as a second pursuit lane for every small-business-set-aside opportunity in Changeis's core NAICS codes.
  • OTAs are nearly untapped. DOT/FAA have OTA authority (49 U.S.C. § 106(l)). DoD OTAs are exploding — a March 2025 EO mandates OTAs as the default software acquisition pathway. Changeis's IT modernization and Agile capabilities are a natural fit for OTA consortia (NSTXL, DIU, etc.).
  • Army MAPS is the most urgent new pursuit. The final RFP went live April 1, 2026. ELB lane has 15 reserved slots per domain × 5 domains = 75 ELB award slots. Changeis's NAICS alignment (541512, 541519, 541330, 541715, 561110) covers all five domains.

2.2 Government-Wide Acquisition Contracts (GWACs)

GWACs are pre-competed IDIQ contracts for IT services managed by specific executive agents (GSA, NASA, NIH/NITAAC). Any federal agency can use a GWAC by requesting a Delegation of Procurement Authority (DPA). GWACs compress task order competition timelines and are increasingly the preferred channel for complex IT work.

Part 6 — Critical Strategic Analysis
Q1: Is Changeis Over-Reliant on GSA MAS / DOT-Volpe?
Yes — this is Changeis's most significant strategic risk. Approximately 30+ task orders at Volpe through a single BPA at a single agency means that any DOT reorganization, DOGE-related program cuts, Volpe mission change, or budget reduction would have an outsized impact on Changeis's revenue. The IPSS-3 BPA runs to December 2031, providing a 5-year runway — but that runway is also a false sense of security if no diversification occurs in parallel.

Diversification targets:

  • DHS cluster (CBP, TSA, CISA, ICE, FEMA): Monitored via APFS — convert monitoring into active pursuit. Target 2–3 DHS task orders via open market or MAS by end of FY2026.
  • Army via MAPS: ELB lane is a purpose-built diversification path. Winning even 1–2 MAPS task orders would materially reduce DOT concentration.
  • OASIS+ at non-DOT agencies: Treasury, HHS, VA — agencies that need program management and systems engineering.
  • Direct SAM.gov monitoring for DHS/Army open market solicitations in NAICS 541512, 541611, 541519.

Q3: CIO-SP3 Gap — How Critical Is It?
The CIO-SP4 cancellation (Feb 2026) has effectively leveled the playing field. All competitors who had CIO-SP4 positions no longer have a path to that vehicle. CIO-SP3 extensions through April 2027 remain useful but are a legacy vehicle with no new on-ramps. The ICE VSPTS blocker was real — but the market has moved. GSA's Alliant 3 and OASIS+ will absorb most CIO-SP3 work as agencies migrate.

Action: Do not invest resources trying to get onto CIO-SP3 (no on-ramp). Instead, position for Alliant 3 Phase 2 on-ramp and ensure OASIS+ is active. For agencies that still require CIO-SP3 for specific task orders, team with a CIO-SP3 holder as a significant subcontractor — building the relationship now for the eventual Alliant 3 transition.

Q4: How to Maximize the Kaizen Mentor-Protégé JV?
The Kaizen MP agreement is Changeis's most strategically underutilized asset. A mentor-protégé JV can compete as a small business on ANY set-aside where the protégé (Kaizen) individually qualifies — including 8(a), SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone, and general SB set-asides.
