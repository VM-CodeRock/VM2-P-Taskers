GSAR 552.239-7001 AI Clause Delayed — Comment Period Closed April 3
GSA's proposed AI clause (GSAR 552.239-7001, "Basic Safeguarding of Artificial Intelligence Systems") was originally planned for Refresh 31 but was deferred to Refresh 32 following strong industry pushback. → Prepare internal AI systems inventory; review all AI vendor agreements for government rights compliance ahead of Refresh 32

MAS Program 📅 April 2, 2026 (Mass Mod acceptance ongoing) Source: GSA Interact MAS Community

MAS Refresh 31 Released — TDR Now Mandatory for All SINs Including 54151S, 54151HACS, 541611

GSA released MAS Solicitation Refresh 31 (Mass Mod A914) on April 2, 2026, marking the most significant MAS structural change in nearly a decade. Transactional Data Reporting (TDR) is now mandatory for all 277 MAS SINs — including the 112 services SINs (like 54151S, 54151HACS, and 541611) previously on Commercial Sales Practices. The Price Reductions Clause is eliminated, and CSP disclosures end. Additional reporting elements previously optional are now required. The AI clause (GSAR 552.239-7001) was deferred to Refresh 32. Startup Springboard restricted to FASt Lane-eligible IT companies only. Monthly TDR office hours continue: next session May 11, 2026 at 3 p.m. ET.

Why it matters for Changeis: Changeis holds SINs 54151S, 54151HACS, and 541611. Accepting Mass Mod A914 is mandatory. TDR begin date is July 1, 2026 if accepted immediately — internal reporting workflows must be updated. The elimination of PRC simplifies pricing compliance but requires new transactional data collection infrastructure. Non-acceptance risks contract cancellation.
FAS Reorg 📅 May 1, 2026 Source: GSA Blog

FAS Restructures into 5 Portfolios + New AI "Transform" Office — Effective May 1, 2026

GSA's Federal Acquisition Service implemented a new organizational structure effective May 1, 2026, consolidating operations into five new portfolios: Office of Assisted Acquisition Services (Assist), Office of Centralized Acquisition Services (Centralize), Office of Acquisition Solutions Development (Create), Office of Shared Services Delivery (Deliver), and Office of Business Optimization (Optimize). A new "Transform" office was created to accelerate AI automation and implementation. GSA stated current programs — MAS, GWACs, OASIS+, Fleet, CityPairs — will continue without immediate operational disruption. This restructuring directly supports Trump Administration EOs on Workforce Optimization and Procurement Consolidation.

Why it matters for Changeis: The Centralize portfolio will handle centralized contract consolidation — directly affecting where agencies buy IT and consulting services. The new Transform office signals GSA will itself become a significant AI adopter and may issue new AI-related task orders through MAS and GWACs. Changeis should position with both the Centralize and Transform portfolios to capture consolidation-driven task order flow.

AI Clause 📅 March 6 – April 3, 2026 (comment period closed) Source: Venable LLP Analysis · Draft Clause (PDF)

GSA AI Clause (GSAR 552.239-7001) — Comment Period Closed, Targeting Refresh 32

The proposed GSAR 552.239-7001 "Basic Safeguarding of Artificial Intelligence Systems" clause saw its comment period close April 3, 2026. Key requirements in the draft: (1) disclose all AI systems in performance within 30 days of award; (2) use only "American AI Systems" — no non-U.S. AI entities; (3) provide human oversight capabilities (reasoning transparency); (4) report CISA security incidents within 72 hours; (5) notify government before material AI system changes; (6) AI must be "neutral, nonpartisan" and comply with "Unbiased AI Principles." The clause applies to prime contractors AND to all AI subcontractors/vendors used in performance. Government receives irrevocable license for any fine-tuned AI models built for the contract. The clause overrides commercial AI vendor EULAs in case of conflict.

Why it matters for Changeis: Any Changeis IT or consulting engagement using AI tools in performance (under 54151S or 541611) will be subject to this clause once in Refresh 32. Changeis must: inventory all AI tools used in delivery, review vendor agreements for government rights compatibility, and assess whether current AI providers (OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, etc.) meet "American AI Systems" requirements. Non-compliance after Refresh 32 may require removing AI tools from contract performance or renegotiating subcontractor agreements.
