Pete Leibman / Stronger Cycle Quick Brief

Pete Leibman is a Washington, DC-area speaker and author who runs Stronger Talent, a firm that helps professional services firms—consulting, law, and financial advisory—sustain high performance under demanding conditions. His latest book, Stronger Advisor: High-Performance Habits for Consultants, Lawyers, and Advisors (published April 14, 2026), is a practical guide to building the habits, mindset, and systems required for sustained high performance in advisory roles. The central framework is The Stronger Cycle—a habit-formation model first introduced in his earlier book Work Stronger (Skyhorse, 2018)—which focuses on four performance levers: Focus, Renew, Fuel, and Sweat. Leibman argues that counterproductive habits gradually erode focus, energy, engagement, and execution, and that a simple, structured system of high-performance habits—applied consistently—allows leaders to sustain peak output without burnout. He has written three books and more than 300 articles on high performance and has been featured on Fox News, CBS Radio, and CNNMoney. The WashingtonExec session specifically covered: a simple system to sustain performance without burnout; the subtle habits that erode focus, energy, and execution; and practical strategies to strengthen resilience and presence in high-stakes situations. For Varun’s follow-up conversations, a natural opener is the shared experience of the session and asking how other executives are applying the habit-stacking framework in their own leadership practice.
