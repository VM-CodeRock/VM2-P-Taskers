C · Industry News & Policy Developments

OMB M-26-10: CIO Authority Expanded, IT Contract Reporting Begins May 2026

OMB · March 31, 2026 · HSToday · Crowell & Moring Analysis
OMB issued Memorandum M-26-10 requiring CFO Act agency CIOs to approve all IT contracts and submit monthly reports to OMB beginning May 2026. Agencies must also eliminate IT acquisition data silos — all pricing and utilization data from vendors must be shared in machine-readable formats across the federal government. Contracts executed after March 31 must include provisions requiring vendors to disclose pricing and utilization data.
Changeis relevance: Every new IT contract Changeis executes post-March 31 will require pricing/utilization disclosure clauses. More importantly, this centralized oversight wave creates a market for IT governance, program management, and change management support services as agencies struggle to comply. Position these capabilities proactively in Q2/Q3 pipeline.

Federal AI Risk Management Deadline Passes — Gaps Remain Across Agencies

SmallGovCon Week in Review · April 10, 2026 · SmallGovCon
A key federal AI risk management deadline passed in early April 2026, and not all agencies were ready. Gaps persist in agency-level AI governance, inventory tracking, and responsible AI frameworks. The White House has indicated continued oversight and potential additional deadlines, while lawmakers are calling for labor-centric AI policy updates.
Changeis relevance: Agencies that missed the deadline will need contractor support for AI risk management implementation, policy development, and change management. A strong near-term opportunity for Changeis to offer AI governance support services alongside IT modernization engagements.

GSA MAS Refresh 31 — Expanded TDR + New AI Requirements

GovTribe / GovCon Trends · April 7, 2026 · LinkedIn/GovTribe · Executive Gov
GSA's MAS Refresh 31 would expand transactional data reporting (TDR) requirements and introduce new AI-specific provisions into the Multiple Award Schedule. Industry groups are pushing back on draft AI procurement guidance that would grant the government broad rights to AI outputs and data — raising IP and liability concerns for vendors offering AI-powered services.
Changeis relevance: If Changeis is on MAS or plans to add AI-related SINs, review draft guidance now. Ensure AI/ML service delivery contracts include appropriate IP protections. This battle will shape AI contracting terms for the next 3–5 years.

SolarWinds Acquires Monalytic — Public Sector IT Management Consolidation

G2X Daily Brief · April 1, 2026 · G2X/LinkedIn
SolarWinds acquired Monalytic, a federal-focused IT monitoring and management partner, expanding its public sector footprint in automated IT operations and observability tools. This follows a broader trend of M&A in federal IT tooling as vendors consolidate to offer more integrated platforms.

Federal AI Spending Forecast: $2.7B in FY2026, Growing to $3.1B by FY2028

Deltek GovWin IQ · August 2025 · GovWin IQ
Deltek forecasts federal AI and AI-related technology spending will grow from $2.7B in FY2026 to $3.1B in FY2028 — a 15% increase over three years. Spending is concentrated among select departments, OTAs are a favored acquisition mechanism, and the AI maturity gap between leading and lagging agencies creates contractor opportunity.
Changeis relevance: $2.7B in AI-related federal spending in FY2026 is distributed across prime contracts, OTAs, and SBIR/STTR. Changeis's AI/ML advisory and implementation capabilities should be explicitly marketed under OTA mechanisms and as sub-tier partners to AI platform primes (Palantir, IBM, Booz Allen).

DOGE Transition: Contract Terminations Stabilizing, IT Modernization Spending Growing

AmeriFusion · February 2026 · AmeriFusion
DOGE is scheduled to sunset July 4, 2026, but its oversight functions are being absorbed into OMB and OPM. Federal IT contract spending was on pace to hit ~$50B in Q4 FY2025. Congress rejected most DOGE cuts; FY2026 budget bills increased spending in most categories. The distribution is shifting: defense, cybersecurity, AI, and IT modernization are growing while discretionary civilian programs face continued pressure. Agencies that shed ~9% of their workforce under DOGE will increasingly rely on contractors for mission-critical work.
Changeis relevance: The post-DOGE workforce gap creates demand for contractor-provided program management, OCM, and IT support services. Agencies that cut federal employees still need the work done — position Changeis as a cost-efficient, outcome-focused partner in the new fixed-price/performance-based contracting environment.
