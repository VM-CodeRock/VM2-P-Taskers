Contractors File Lawsuit to Block Federal DEI Executive Order (EO 14398)

A coalition of higher education associations and minority trade groups filed suit on Apr 20 in the U.S. District Court of Maryland, challenging EO 14398 ("Addressing DEI Discrimination by Federal Contractors," signed Mar 26, 2026). The order requires a new anti-DEI clause in all federal contracts by April 25, 2026, mandates subcontractor monitoring, and invokes FCA materiality certification — meaning non-compliance could expose contractors to False Claims Act liability. The lawsuit alleges First Amendment violations and ultra vires executive action. FAR amendment process underway; interim deviation expected imminently. Changeis action required: ensure internal policies and subcontract flow-down clauses are reviewed before April 25 deadline.

Sources: National Law Review · Government Executive · Dechert LLP
📋
OMB Issues M-26-12: Agencies Must Report Non-Commercial Contracting by May 4

OMB Director Russ Vought issued Memorandum M-26-12 (dated Apr 17, 2026) requiring all executive agencies to report by May 4, 2026 every non-commercial contract awarded April–September 2025, plus detailed justifications for awards over $10M. The memo notes $130B+ in non-commercial IT/professional services spending in FY2024 and signals aggressive scrutiny of cost-reimbursement contracts. Agencies seeking non-commercial buys must now get political-appointee approval (CAO-level) before going to OMB. Implication for Changeis: commercial-first contracting push may restructure how agencies structure professional services buys — shift toward FAR Part 12 commercial item vehicles favors firms that can price on FFP.

Sources: OMB M-26-12 (Whitehouse.gov) · GovExec / Washington Technology
