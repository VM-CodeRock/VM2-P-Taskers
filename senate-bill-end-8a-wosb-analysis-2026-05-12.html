Senate Bill S. 4390 — Ending Discrimination in Government Contracting Act

Threat Assessment for Changeis Inc. — 8(a) & WOSB Program Elimination Analysis
Created: 2026-05-12  ·  Prepared for Varun Malhotra, President, Changeis Inc.
Strategic Threat: High
1 · Executive Summary
Bottom Line for Changeis

S. 4390 would eliminate both the 8(a) Business Development Program and the Women-Owned Small Business (WOSB) set-aside program — removing the two statutory pillars on which Changeis's competitive advantage in federal contracting is built. While full passage is unlikely in the near term, the bill signals an accelerating policy environment hostile to set-aside programs, with material effects already occurring through regulatory and administrative channels regardless of the bill's legislative fate.

On April 27, 2026, S. 4390, the Ending Discrimination in Government Contracting Act, was introduced in the U.S. Senate by Sen. Mike Lee (R-UT), with companion legislation H.R. 8511 introduced simultaneously in the House by Rep. Glenn Grothman (R-WI). (GovInfo — S. 4390, Rep. Grothman press release)

The bill would completely repeal the statutory foundations of both programs, impose a government-wide blanket ban on considering race, ethnicity, or sex in contract awards, and require agencies to rescind conforming regulations within 60–180 days of enactment. As of May 12, 2026, S. 4390 has been referred to the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs. No co-sponsors have been identified in either chamber. (Washington Technology)

Bottom line: The bill is unlikely to become law in its current form. However, the hostile administrative environment — SBA terminating 628 firms from 8(a), the FAR Overhaul weakening set-aside protections, the SDB goal slashed to 5%, and 1,091 firms suspended — means Changeis faces real revenue and competitive risk now, independent of S. 4390's passage. (SBA.gov)

What the bill would do to 8(a): S. 4390 strips nearly every reference to "socially and economically disadvantaged individuals" from the Small Business Act (15 U.S.C.), including the eligibility and participation criteria that form the 8(a) program's legal foundation. The practical effect is the deletion of the entire statutory basis for 8(a) set-asides, sole-source awards, and the 8(a) subcontracting goal category. (Morrison & Foerster GovCon Insights)

What the bill would do to WOSB: The bill repeals 15 U.S.C. §637(m) — the core statutory provision authorizing WOSB set-aside solicitations and sole-source awards. It eliminates the 5% WOSB prime contracting goal (by striking clauses (iv) and (v) from 15 U.S.C. §644(g)(1)(A)), and removes the requirement that large prime contractors include WOSBs in subcontracting plans. (Steven Koprince analysis via LinkedIn)

Blanket Prohibition

The bill's most sweeping provision imposes a government-wide ban: "The head of an executive agency may not — (1) consider the race, ethnicity, or sex of business owners when awarding contracts or grants; and (2) require or encourage contractors to consider those factors when awarding subcontracts." This goes beyond mere program elimination — it would make WOSB-status consideration in any procurement decision unlawful.

What survives: HUBZone, SDVOSB (Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business), and general small business set-aside authority are explicitly retained. Rural business preferences are also preserved. Agencies would have 60 days to propose conforming rule changes and 180 days to finalize them. (Pub K Government Contracts News)

4 · Impact on Changeis Inc.
Direct Material Impact

Changeis holds both WOSB certification and has participated in 8(a) in past years. If S. 4390 is enacted, Changeis would lose set-aside eligibility on both dimensions simultaneously — WOSB and 8(a) status would carry zero contracting advantage. Even short of enactment, the chilling effect on agency behavior creates pipeline risk now.

Impact AreaCurrent Risk LevelAnalysis
WOSB Set-Aside Eligibility Critical WOSB set-aside solicitations would disappear; Changeis competes against all small businesses and large primes without set-aside protection. 5% WOSB agency goal removed eliminates incentive for agencies to seek out WOSBs.
8(a) Legacy Contracts Elevated Existing 8(a) contracts must still be completed; options may still be exercised. However, follow-on procurements under "Once 8(a), Always 8(a)" rule are already weakened by the FAR Overhaul (RFO), which removed SBA's mandatory approval authority. (SmallGovCon)
OASIS+ WOSB Pool High Risk GSA's OASIS+ has a dedicated Women-Owned Small Business IDIQ contract. If WOSB program is eliminated, WOSB pool task orders would disappear, and Changeis would need to compete through Total Small Business or Unrestricted pools — losing competitive differentiation. (GSA OASIS+)
OASIS+ 8(a) Pool High Risk If Changeis holds an OASIS+ 8(a) contract vehicle, that pool becomes defunct under the bill. No new task orders could be issued as 8(a) set-asides; existing orders could be completed but no new 8(a) task orders issued.
Subcontracting Revenue Moderate Large prime contractors currently required to include WOSBs in subcontracting plans. Bill eliminates this requirement. Changeis may see fewer inbound subcontracting solicitations from large primes who previously included WOSB targets.
Agency Chilling Effect (Near-Term) Occurring Now MoFo explicitly warns that even without enactment, "agencies may be more cautious about awarding contracts to 8(a) participants and WOSBs" given the congressional signal. This is already a material risk to Changeis's near-term pipeline.
GSA Schedule Positioning Monitor GSA Multiple Award Schedule status unaffected by S. 4390 — GSA Schedules are not set-aside vehicles. Changeis's MAS presence remains intact. This is a key resilience asset.
Transition Timeline (If Enacted) 60–180 Days Bill requires agencies to propose conforming rule changes within 60 days and finalize within 180 days of enactment. Gives limited but real runway to adjust capture strategy.

OASIS+ Contract Vehicle Risk — Details: OASIS+ currently operates six distinct IDIQ contracts: Total Small Business, Women-Owned Small Business, Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned, HUBZone, 8(a), and Unrestricted. S. 4390 would effectively invalidate the WOSB and 8(a) pools as set-aside vehicles. GSA would likely have to restructure or dissolve these pools, and existing contract holders would need to migrate to alternative vehicles. Changeis should immediately assess which pools it holds and model the revenue impact of losing WOSB and 8(a) task order eligibility. (GSA OASIS+)

Survivability Path — HUBZone & SDVOSB

The bill explicitly preserves HUBZone and SDVOSB preferences. If any Changeis principal or key employee has veteran status, or if Changeis qualifies for HUBZone certification based on office location and employee residence, these represent the most durable set-aside positioning available in the post-S.4390 environment. Additionally, remaining a "small business" (size standard qualification) is essential — Total Small Business set-asides survive the bill.

Observed Industry Sentiment

Industry commentary across legal, advocacy, and trade channels is overwhelmingly critical of the bill — particularly WOSB elimination. The only political support comes from the bill's sponsors and aligned ideological organizations. The disabled veteran community, while generally supportive of the Trump DEI EO, is notably cautious given that early drafts appeared to also target SDVOSB language (though the final bill preserves it). The bipartisan WOSB program origin (enacted under a Republican Congress in 2011) makes this a politically complex vote for Republican senators in competitive states.

6 · Strategic Recommendations for Changeis

These recommendations address the threat both from S. 4390 and from the broader administrative erosion of 8(a)/WOSB programs that is already occurring regardless of the bill's fate.

1
Audit & Harden Your OASIS+ and GSA Vehicle Posture
Immediately identify which OASIS+ pools Changeis holds (specifically WOSB and 8(a) pools) and model the revenue at risk if those pools are dissolved or restricted. If Changeis qualifies for the OASIS+ Total Small Business pool or Unrestricted pool, pursue those on-ramps now — before competition intensifies as other WOSB/8(a) firms scramble for alternative vehicles. GSA Schedule (MAS) is your most durable vehicle — ensure all relevant SINs are active and pricing is competitive for open-market competition.
30-Day Priority
2
Diversify Competitive Posture Beyond WOSB/8(a) Designations
Conduct a capability-based competitive analysis to identify which Changeis service areas can win on full-and-open or small business (not WOSB-restricted) competition. Invest in CPARS/past performance documentation, technical differentiators, and price-to-win analysis for unrestricted competitions. Begin building a win rate baseline for non-set-aside opportunities so Changeis is not dependent on the continuation of WOSB/8(a) preferences. Assess HUBZone eligibility — this is the single most strategically valuable surviving set-aside category if S. 4390 or elements of it move forward.
30-Day Priority
3
Engage Advocacy Channels — WIPP, SBA ASBDC, Congressional Offices
Contact WIPP directly (wipp.org) and request placement on their S. 4390 response working group or opposition coalition. As a Maryland/DC-area WOSB, reach out to your House and Senate representatives (MD Senators Van Hollen and Cardin's successor; VA Senators Warner and Kaine) to share Changeis's economic impact story. A 1-page fact sheet showing Changeis's federal contract revenue, employees, and WOSB-attributed contract wins is the ideal tool. Coordinate with the SBA's Office of Women's Business Ownership. Your direct testimony as a named WOSB federal contractor carries significant weight in a hearing environment.
60-Day Priority
4
Pipeline Rebalancing — Accelerate Pursuit of Non-Set-Aside Opportunities
Conduct a 90-day pipeline review with a filter: for each active pursuit, model two scenarios — (A) WOSB/8(a) preference intact; (B) full-and-open competition. Reprioritize captures where Changeis's technical strength, past performance, and pricing can win independently of set-aside status. Identify 3–5 strategic teaming opportunities with large primes who value Changeis's expertise and past performance as a subcontractor — independent of the WOSB subcontracting plan requirement that the bill would eliminate. Lock in teaming arrangements before agencies update their procurement strategies.
60-Day Priority
5
Monitor NDAA FY2027 and FAR Rulemaking — Trigger-Based Response Protocol
Establish a 90-day monitoring cadence: (1) Track S. 4390 and H.R. 8511 for any markup scheduling, floor votes, or NDAA rider attachment; (2) Monitor FAR rulemaking dockets for conforming rule changes to the Trump DEI EO; (3) Set Google Alerts for "S.4390," "WOSB elimination," and "8(a) legislative." Define specific triggers that elevate Changeis's response: a committee markup, a co-sponsor addition, or an NDAA rider attachment each require immediate escalation of strategic response. Engage a government contracts law firm on retainer to advise on compliance as the regulatory environment shifts — PilieroMazza, Morrison & Foerster, or Alston & Bird are tracking this legislation in real time.
