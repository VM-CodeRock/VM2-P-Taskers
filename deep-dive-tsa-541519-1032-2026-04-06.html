Requirement Description
What TSA is buying: Continued colocation space, power, and connectivity at DHS's Data Center 1 (DC1) and Data Center 15 (DC15) via the DCCO IDIQ. The fees cover physical infrastructure — raised floor space, power, cooling, and physical security — for TSA's production, test, and development environments. DHS HQ controls and owns the facilities; TSA pays O&M fees through an IDIQ task order. The work is not IT services delivery per se — it is the facility-level colocation subscription that keeps TSA's mission-critical systems running at DC1/DC15.

DC1 background: Data Center One is located at the National Center for Critical Information Processing and Storage (NCCIPS) at NASA's Stennis Space Center in Bay St. Louis, Mississippi. DHS occupies approximately 35,000 sq ft of raised floor in a NASA-owned facility. DC1 historically housed over 2,000 physical servers, 1,660 VMs, and 765 TB of provisioned storage across DHS components including TSA, OBIM, and others.

DC15 context: DC15 is a DHS HQ colocation/annex data center also served under the DCCO umbrella. Recent FPDS records show Peraton task orders explicitly covering "DC 1 and DC 15" colocation services for DHS components including TSA and OBIM. The TSA IMPACT II contract (Peraton, $342.7M, 2022–2027) covers TSA's enterprise IT management, while this separate task order specifically covers the colocation O&M fees owed to the DCCO facility manager.

DCCO Vehicle — Structure & Access
Critical Finding: The DCCO IDIQ (Contract No. 70RTAC21D00000006) is a single-award IDIQ held exclusively by Peraton Technology Services (formerly Perspecta Engineering Inc.). Peraton was awarded this $2.685B, 10-year contract in September 2021. Only Peraton — as prime — can receive task orders directly. All other companies, including Changeis, can only participate as subcontractors to Peraton.

Incumbent & Competitive Landscape

The incumbent on this task order is Peraton Engineering (a Peraton subsidiary). Peraton holds a dominant position across TSA IT — it is simultaneously:

  • DCCO prime — the only company that can receive task orders for DC1/DC15 colocation fees
  • TSA IMPACT II prime — $342.7M, 5-year contract (2022–2027) for TSA's entire enterprise IT management covering ~85,000 employees/contractors at all domestic and select international locations (Peraton press release, Dec 2022)
  • DC1 onsite management legacy — Prior to Peraton, General Dynamics IT (GDIT) and CSRA held DC1 onsite roles going back to at least 2013, with ETC Solutions serving as a sub for TSA IMPACT I/II work at DC1
