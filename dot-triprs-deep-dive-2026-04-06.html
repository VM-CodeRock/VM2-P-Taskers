What Is TRIPRS?

TRIPRS — the Traffic Records Improvement Program Reporting System — is NHTSA's central web-based IT platform for managing the federal Traffic Records Program. It serves as the authoritative portal through which all 50 states, territories, and tribal nations interact with NHTSA's traffic safety data collection infrastructure.

Core Functions

Module 1

STRAP

State Traffic Records Assessment Program module — states enter responses to assessment questions, upload documentation, and peer assessors review. Drives §405(c) Safety Data grant eligibility.

Module 2

CDIP Tracking

Crash Data Improvement Program tracking — manages workshops, technical advisory team visits, state-identified issues, and actionable recommendations for improving crash data quality.

Module 3

Performance Measures

Tracks state progress on six core traffic records data quality dimensions: timeliness, accuracy, completeness, uniformity, integration, and accessibility.

Strategic Context: NHTSA awarded $171M in grants to 19 states (Dec 2024) under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law's SEDC program, with $350M total available. This significantly increases state demand on TRIPRS as states must document their upgrades. The system is entering a high-growth, high-activity phase.

TRIPRS Users & Stakeholders

  • State Traffic Records Coordinators — primary portal users; submit assessment data, track performance measures
  • State Traffic Records Coordinating Committees (TRCCs) — strategic planning bodies in each state
  • NHTSA Regional Offices (10 regions) — oversight and facilitation of state assessments
  • NHTSA Traffic Records Program Office — system owner; manages contractor relationship
  • Peer Assessors — subject matter experts who review STRAP submissions
  • Tribal Nations — expanded scope for tribal crash reporting toolkit

Changeis needs to either hire/retain a traffic records SME or partner with VHB/academic institution with this expertise before proposal submission.
