Anthropic

Anthropic Hits $30B Revenue Run Rate After "Crazy" 80x Q1 Growth — IPO Weighing Begins, $900B Valuation on Horizon

In a candid interview this week, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei revealed the company achieved 80x annualized growth in Q1 2026 — versus a plan for 10x per year — describing the pace as "just crazy" and "too hard to handle." The company has now crossed a $30 billion annualized revenue run rate, having gone from $87 million in January 2024, to $1 billion by December 2024, $9 billion by end-2025, and $30 billion in April 2026. Salesforce took ~20 years to reach $30 billion in annual revenue; Anthropic did it in under three years. Enterprise demand is the primary driver: Anthropic now has over 1,000 enterprise customers spending more than $1 million/year, a figure that doubled since February. Claude Code alone — the company's fastest-growing product — hit $1 billion in annualized revenue within six months of launch and was generating $2.5 billion run-rate revenue by February 2026, with weekly active users doubling since January 1. Bloomberg reports Anthropic is now weighing an IPO as early as October 2026, with secondary market shares implying a near-$1 trillion valuation. The backdrop: Anthropic remains barred from DoD contracts following the Pentagon's supply chain risk designation, with an appeals court siding with the government in April — but a San Francisco district court's preliminary injunction allows it to serve other federal agencies.

Executive Use Case (GovCon) The Anthropic story this week has a dual edge for GovCon executives. On one hand, Anthropic's explosive growth signals that Claude is the enterprise AI tool of record — GovCon firms using it for proposal development, BD research, and delivery have a powerful asset. On the other, the DoD supply-chain-risk designation means any defense contract work must verify Claude is not in the stack. Firms should audit AI tool usage across business lines now, before a contracting officer raises it in a post-award audit.

Google's Secret "Remy" Agent Leaks — A 24/7 Personal AI That Acts, Not Just Answers, as I/O Countdown Begins

Business Insider obtained internal documents revealing Google has been testing a Gemini-powered AI agent codenamed "Remy" among employees ahead of Google I/O 2026 (May 19–20). Unlike current Gemini, Remy is designed to monitor things that matter, handle complex tasks proactively, learn preferences over time, and coordinate across Gmail, Calendar, Docs, Drive, Android, and third-party apps. Google I/O 2026 opens May 19 with the traditional keynote at the Shoreline Amphitheater — where a new Gemini 4.0 or major 3.x overhaul is expected alongside Remy's potential debut, Android 17, the Aluminium OS (Android/ChromeOS merge), and Android XR smart glasses partnerships with Warby Parker and Gentle Monster.

Executive Use Case Remy represents Google's direct answer to OpenAI's Operator and Anthropic's Computer — an ambient AI that runs errands autonomously across the entire Google stack. GovCon / Policy

White House Drafts Executive Order to Strip AI Contractors of Usage Control — Federal AI Governance Power Shift

The federal government is circulating draft policy documents — potentially leading to an executive order — that would assert the government's authority to use AI technology it purchases without outside stipulations from contractors, according to sources speaking to Nextgov/FCW and Politico. The language directly targets the standoff with Anthropic over "all lawful use" terms, but the policy would apply broadly across AI vendors. One source described the goal as clarifying that "it is for that democratically elected government to determine what is a lawful and appropriate use of a particular technology, not solely a company." The White House is also mulling a pre-deployment review working group that would evaluate AI models before they go to market — a pharmaceutical-style approval process. Simultaneously, the policy addresses cybersecurity threats posed by advanced models like Anthropic's Mythos Preview and GPT-5.5. Pushback is emerging from major tech players: Daniel Castro of ITIF (funded by Anthropic, Microsoft, Meta) warned that pre-market approval "could be withheld" giving competitors critical windows of advantage. The White House told Nextgov that "any policy announcement will come directly from the President" — confirming active deliberation.

Executive Use Case (GovCon) This is a watershed regulatory moment for the federal AI market. If the EO passes, every AI vendor's government contract language will need to permit all lawful operational uses — including autonomous and surveillance applications. GovCon firms should brief their legal and BD teams on the pending shift, review teaming agreement terms with AI subcontractors, and begin preparing for potential contract re-negotiations. The draft policy also creates pre-award risk if an AI partner's compliance posture is uncertain.

Meta Builds "Hatch" — Its First Consumer Agentic AI — With Instagram Shopping Bot Targeting End-2026 Launch

The Information reports Meta is building a consumer AI agent internally named "Hatch" — a system designed to live inside Instagram and other Meta apps, performing tasks on users' behalf rather than just serving content. Inspired by OpenClaw, Hatch is currently powered by Anthropic's models while Meta's own Muse Spark LLM matures in the background. Separately, Meta is building a dedicated AI shopping bot for Instagram that lets users tap on products in Reels or posts, with the AI autonomously handling the purchasing process — targeted for end-of-year rollout. The agentic shopping tool would directly compete with OpenAI's Codex/Operator and Google Shopping. Meta is also working on integrating agentic features into all major Meta apps (WhatsApp, Facebook, Messenger), powered by Muse Spark and a multi-agent orchestration system that spawns subagents for complex task segments.

xAI Retires Grok 3, Grok 4, and Grok 4.1 API Models on May 15 — Grok 4.3 Becomes the New Standard

xAI published an official migration notice on May 6, 2026, announcing that eight Grok API models will be retired on May 15, 2026 at 12:00 PM PT. Retiring models include: grok-3, grok-4-0709, grok-4-fast-reasoning, grok-4-fast-non-reasoning, grok-4-1-fast-reasoning, grok-4-1-fast-non-reasoning, grok-code-fast-1, and grok-imagine-image-pro. Developers are instructed to migrate immediately: reasoning workloads → grok-4.3; non-reasoning workloads → grok-4.20-non-reasoning; image generation → grok-imagine-image. The notice teases "exciting model releases planned in the coming weeks" — widely interpreted as signaling Grok 4.4 or 4.5, which Reddit sources suggested were in development as of late April. This is not a soft deprecation; requests to retired model IDs will return errors. The forced migration to Grok 4.3 consolidates xAI's API around its most recent generation ahead of a likely new flagship announcement.

Executive Use Case Enterprise and startup teams using the xAI API for X/Twitter-integrated intelligence workflows have a hard four-day deadline to migrate. Engineering leads should audit all API calls referencing legacy Grok model IDs before May 15. The "exciting releases" teaser suggests xAI may be positioning a next-generation Grok model at or just after Google I/O week — timing that would allow xAI to steal narrative share from the AI calendar.

Pentagon: "Never Again Single-Threaded" on AI — DoD Under Secretary Doubles Down on Multi-Vendor Strategy

Pentagon Under Secretary for Research and Engineering Emil Michael spoke publicly at the Special Competitive Studies Project's AI+ Expo in Washington D.C. last week, explicitly stating the DoD will "never again be single-threaded with any one model." Referencing the Anthropic dispute, Michael said: "We were single-threaded on one vendor, one AI vendor at the Department of War — and integrating into classified systems is not just putting your software on a public cloud." Executive Use Case (GovCon) The DoD's multi-vendor mandate is a structural opportunity for GovCon firms: any prime or sub with access to two or more of the cleared AI providers can position themselves as "AI-diversified" — a procurement differentiator in an environment explicitly rewarding non-concentration. OpenAI

OpenAI Rolls Out "Trusted Contact" Safety Feature in ChatGPT — Crisis Intervention Layer for At-Risk Users

OpenAI rolled out a "Trusted Contact" feature in ChatGPT, allowing users to designate a nominated trusted person to be contacted by the system during moments of emotional crisis. The feature is designed as a supplementary safety layer — not a replacement for crisis hotlines or professional care. If a user discusses self-harm in a manner indicating a serious safety concern, ChatGPT can initiate contact with the designated person while simultaneously directing the user to emergency services. This follows legal exposure: OpenAI was sued by families of a Canadian school shooting victims, and the company faces growing scrutiny over AI's role in mental health contexts. OpenAI stated the trusted contact feature is "one of several layers of safeguards" and would not contact the trusted person for routine distress, only serious safety signals.

Executive Use Case For enterprise and government HR departments deploying ChatGPT for workforce productivity, the Trusted Contact feature introduces new data privacy and duty-of-care considerations. Enterprise policy teams should review whether the feature is enabled by default in their tenant, whether employees need to be notified, and whether HIPAA/privacy regulations intersect with the feature's design. For GovCon firms serving health agencies or VA, this is a preview of the AI safety standards that health-focused contracts will require.

Five AI Moves for GovCon Leaders This Week

1. Audit Your AI Stack for DoD Compliance — Before a Contracting Officer Does It For You
Anthropic remains barred from DoD contracts despite its explosive commercial growth. 2. Shape for DHS NOSC 2.0 — $100M AI-Security Vehicle Drops Late May
The DHS Network Ops Security Center 2.0 RFP, with a Q4 FY2026 award and five-year $100M ceiling, is one of the most actionable AI-security opportunities of the year. Firms with cleared SOC talent, AI-augmented monitoring platforms (SIEM + AI threat hunting), and existing DHS relationships should begin teaming conversations now. 3. Position as "AI-Diversified" in Every Proposal — The Pentagon Mandate Creates a Differentiator
The DoD's explicit policy of never again being "single-threaded on AI" means evaluators will reward proposals demonstrating multi-model, multi-vendor AI architectures. Stop treating AI as a single-tool feature in your technical approach. Document your ability to deploy, orchestrate, and govern at least two-to-three AI provider stacks (e.g., OpenAI + Microsoft Copilot + Perplexity Computer) with governance controls, fallback logic, and human oversight built in.

4. Monitor the White House AI Contractor EO — It Will Rewrite Every AI Subcontract

5. Start a DOE Compute Opportunity Pipeline — $1.2B AI Supercomputer Program Creates Task Order Ladders
