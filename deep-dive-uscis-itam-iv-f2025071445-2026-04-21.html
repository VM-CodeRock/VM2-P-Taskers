1 · Executive Summary

USCIS/OIT is recompeting its Information Technology Asset Management (ITAM) contract — a follow-on to the current KCI-Acuity award (contract 70SBUR21F00000099, ~$8.4M obligated, PoP 6/14/21–6/13/26) covering Software Asset Management (SAM) functions: MEGABYTE/FITARA compliance, IT commodity spending reporting, software request fulfillment, license delivery, and license waste avoidance. Estimated value is $5M–$10M FFP over approximately four years (PoP: 6/14/2026–6/13/2030), placed on NITAAC CIO-SP3 SB with a SDVOSB set-aside.

The solicitation release date per the March 27, 2026 APFS update was 03/27/2026 — meaning the RFP may already be posted to NITAAC's e-GOS ordering system as of this writing. Award is targeted for May 14, 2026. The window to influence or respond is narrow.

Critical eligibility constraint: Changeis is not SDVOSB-certified. This is a hard set-aside — Changeis cannot serve as prime contractor. The only viable path is as a subcontractor to a qualified CIO-SP3 SDVOSB holder that has relevant ITAM expertise and is pursuing this opportunity.

NO-GO as prime. Changeis lacks SDVOSB status required for this NITAAC CIO-SP3 SB SDVOSB set-aside. Pursuit as a subcontractor to a qualified SDVOSB prime is theoretically possible but requires immediate identification of an eligible partner given the solicitation is likely already released. This is a low-priority monitoring opportunity.

Scope Summary: ITAM IV covers critical Software Asset Management (SAM) functions within USCIS: MEGABYTE Act and FITARA compliance reporting, IT commodity spending tracking, software license request fulfillment, license delivery and deployment, and active license waste avoidance. This is an IT governance/compliance function tied directly to OMB mandates, not pure software development. The work requires ITAM domain expertise, familiarity with USCIS's IT portfolio, and experience with federal license management tools and reporting frameworks.
