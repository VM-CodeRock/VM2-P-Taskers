Industry News & Policy
Fixed-Price EO — 90-Day Restructuring Deadline Approaching (Due ~Late July 2026)
President Trump's April 30 EO "Promoting Efficiency, Accountability, and Performance in Federal Contracting" makes fixed-price the default procurement type. OMB must issue agency implementation guidance by June 14; FAR Council must propose amendments by August 28. Each agency head has 90 days (~late July) to review and seek to convert the 10 largest non-FFP contracts. Written justification + senior approval required above: $10M (most civilian agencies), $25M (DHS), $35M (NASA), $100M (DoD). R&D and pre-production development are exempt. Agencies must also submit semi-annual reports to OMB on compliance. (Mayer Brown, Gibson Dunn, Holland & Knight)
FAR DEI Clause 52.222-90 — July 27 Modification Deadline for Existing Contracts
FAR Council issued model deviation clause FAR 52.222-90 (Apr 2026 Deviation) effective April 24. New solicitations must include it now. All existing contracts extending past December 31, 2026 must be modified by July 27, 2026. Contractors must certify no "racially discriminatory DEI activities," allow records access, and flow-down to all subcontract tiers including commercial items. Agencies must confirm implementation to the White House Domestic Policy Council by July 24. (Fisher Phillips, Miller Chevalier)
DHS Funding Runs Through May 22 — Process Friction Expected
DHS appropriations authority expires May 22, 2026. TSA has lost significant personnel; FEMA is compressed heading into hurricane season. If Congress does not act, procurement velocity at DHS sub-agencies (CBP, USCG, TSA, FEMA, CISA) may slow materially. CISA has already sustained a $707M budget cut in FY27 proposals — contractors dependent on CISA-funded work should monitor recompete timelines and scope contraction. (Small Business GovCon Brief May 2026)
SBA 8(a) Program — ~800 Firms (20%) Facing Termination; Sole Source Volume Declining
SBA has initiated termination proceedings against nearly 800 8(a) firms (~20% of program) for failure to provide financial data. Sole-source 8(a) volume has dropped sharply. SBA also reduced the SDB contracting goal from 15% back to statutory 5%. Firms built on 8(a) sole-source pipelines need competitive vehicle strategies in place by FY27. Also: House Small Business Committee considers H.R. 2804 (Protecting Small Business Competitions Act) on May 20 — would codify the Rule of Two. (SBA.gov, PilieroMazza May 14)
FY27 IT Budget — Record $75.7B Civilian IT Proposed; Key Allocations
OMB's FY27 budget request sets a new high at $75.7B government-wide IT. Key civilian allocations: VA EHR $4.2B, VA total IT $6.3B, DOE AI/supercomputing $1.2B, Treasury Cyber $15.2M additional. Notable cuts: CISA −$707M, NIST −$993M (MEP eliminated), IRS −$1.4B. Overall civilian non-discretionary proposed at −23% vs. FY26. DOT SBIR Phase I closes May 29. (GovDash May 2026 Intel Brief)
GSA MAS Rightsizing — Non-Compliant / Low-Sales Schedules Being Pulled
GSA is removing MAS contracts that fail to meet sales thresholds, addressing non-compliance, and eliminating low-demand SINs. Contractors have a short window to audit schedule offerings. MAS Refresh #31 (expected earlier this spring) modernizes ordering procedures under GSAR 538.71, now the single source of guidance. Vendors must accept the associated mass modification or risk contract expiration. (GSA.gov)
