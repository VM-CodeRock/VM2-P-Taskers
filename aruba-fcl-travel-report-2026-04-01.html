1. Regulatory Basis & Why This Applies to You

Varun Malhotra holds an interim Top Secret (TS) clearance as President of Changeis Inc., which is undergoing the Facility Clearance Level (FCL) process. Under the NISPOM rule codified at 32 CFR Part 117 (effective February 24, 2021), reporting requirements from Security Executive Agent Directive 3 (SEAD 3) apply to all "covered individuals" — meaning anyone with eligibility for access to classified information, including those in the process of being cleared.

Key rule: Reporting is based on eligibility level, not just current access. Because Varun holds interim TS eligibility, the most stringent SEAD 3 reporting tier applies.

4. Step-by-Step: What Varun Needs to Do
1
Notify the FSO — Immediately (at least 30 days before travel, no less than 10 business days)

Email or notify Changeis's FSO of the planned trip. Include destination (Aruba), approximate dates, and purpose (personal vacation). The FSO will send you a pre-travel form — you can use Section 3 of this document as your initial notification.

2
Complete this Pre-Travel Form (Section 3 above)

Fill in all TBD fields. Provide passport information, full itinerary (airline, hotel, dates), names of travel companions, and identify any planned contacts with foreign nationals. Return the completed form to the FSO.

3
Receive Pre-Travel Security Briefing from FSO

The FSO is required to provide you with: (a) the NCSC "Safe Travels" resource (DNI Safe Travels PDF), and (b) State Department travel advisory for Aruba (currently Level 1 — Normal Precautions). Because Aruba is not on the DNI high-threat country list, a DCSA CISA special briefing is not required — but confirm with FSO.

4
FSO submits report in DISS (FSO action — not Varun's)

Prior to your departure, the FSO will enter the foreign travel report into DISS (Defense Information System for Security) using the Foreign Travel Module (Subject Summary → Subject Details → Foreign Travel Tab). Travel is considered approved for SEAD 3 purposes once the FSO completes this DISS entry. Varun does not need system access to DISS.

5
Travel to Aruba — Keep a Journal

During travel, note: any deviations from your submitted itinerary, any foreign nationals met (name, affiliation, nature of contact), any suspicious incidents, and any contact with foreign government representatives. Keep your US passport secure at all times. Report deviations from itinerary to the FSO as soon as possible.

6
Post-Travel: Contact FSO within 5 business days of return

This is Required. Contact the FSO immediately upon return to schedule the post-travel debrief. SEAD 3 requires the FSO to debrief you within 5 business days. Report any itinerary deviations, new foreign contacts, suspicious incidents, lost/stolen items, or anything unusual that occurred during the trip.

7
Sign and return the Post-Travel Debrief Form

The FSO will use a debrief form (see CDSE sample debrief form with standard questions). Sign and return it. The FSO will then update the DISS record and mark the travel report as "Debriefed." If you answer "yes" to any debrief questions (suspicious contact, unusual incidents, etc.), the FSO may coordinate with a DCSA Counterintelligence Special Agent (CISA) for follow-up.
