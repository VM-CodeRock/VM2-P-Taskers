1 · Executive Summary

U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP/OIT) is renewing its Harness.io software licenses — a brand-name commercial platform providing continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipeline orchestration and cloud cost management (CCM) analytics on a Kubernetes infrastructure. The renewal follows existing contract 70B04C23F00000606, awarded June 2023 to incumbent Swish Data Corp (Springfield, VA) — a Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned, HUBZone-certified IT VAR and NASA SEWP V prime contract holder — worth up to $8.5M over three years.

This is not a professional services contract. It is a commercial software license renewal pass-through: CBP purchases Harness.io subscriptions through a NASA SEWP V small business reseller at a price-competitive GWAC rate. The acquisition strategy is explicitly 100% Small Business set-aside on NASA SEWP V. Solicitation releases May 10, 2026; award expected June 20, 2026; new period of performance June 23, 2026 through June 22, 2029 (three years).

Scope Summary: CBP's Office of Information and Technology (OIT) requires renewal of its enterprise Harness.io software licenses covering two product modules: (1) CI/CD Pipelines — a developer platform automating build, test, and deployment workflows across CBP's multi-cloud Kubernetes environment, enabling portability across AWS, Azure, GCP, and on-premises nodes; and (2) CCM (Cloud Cost Management) — a data analytics module providing spend visibility, anomaly detection, and rightsizing recommendations across cloud service providers. CBP explicitly states this capability is "critical for CBP to have flexibility to move workloads to any Cloud Service Provider or run on multiple cloud providers."

Product Context — Harness.io: Harness is a commercial DevSecOps platform headquartered in San Francisco, offering modular software delivery, feature flags, CI/CD, and cloud cost management. It is widely used in federal civilian and defense DevSecOps programs, typically deployed on Kubernetes and integrated with service meshes. Harness has an active federal public sector practice and distributes through authorized federal resellers. The product is not GSA Schedule-listed as a standalone item in most vehicles — procurement typically runs through SEWP V, GSA IT Schedule 70, or agency-specific BPAs via authorized VARs.

Prior Contract Context: Contract 70B04C23F00000606 was awarded June 2023 to Swish Data Corp through NASA SEWP V (NNG15SC91B), valued at up to $8,512,675 over 3 years, set aside as Small Business Total, with 5 bids received. The new renewal (F2025072013) is structured as a follow-on (REC status) on the same SEWP V vehicle, suggesting CBP is moving toward a streamlined competition or sole-source justification among SEWP V SB holders.

Key Structural Point: This is a brand-name Harness.io license renewal. CBP is not selecting a service integrator — it is purchasing commercial software subscriptions through an authorized SEWP V reseller who holds a Harness.io partner/reseller relationship. Technical evaluation is minimal; price and vehicle/set-aside eligibility dominate. Changeis has no place in this transaction.
