DOL “Make America AI-Ready” initiative

What it is, why it matters, and how Changeis can use it as a baseline for AI adoption programs (2026-03-25).

Enroll: text “READY” to 20202
Format: SMS microlearning
Time: 7 days · ~10 min/day
Audience: every American worker (no laptop needed)

What it is (plain English)

The U.S. Department of Labor launched “Make America AI-Ready”, a free AI literacy course delivered entirely by text message. The simplest way to start is to text “READY” to 20202.

DOL positions this as a lightweight on-ramp: 7 days, about 10 minutes/day, with “bite-sized” content and daily challenges that work even for participants with limited internet access.

Why this is interesting
It’s a federal, mass-market “AI 101” baseline you can reuse as prerequisite training.
In client programs, this helps you stop re-teaching fundamentals and focus on mission-specific workflows + governance.

The 5 pillars (what they’re teaching)

  • Understand AI principles — core concepts, capabilities, and limitations.
  • Explore AI uses — practical tools and use cases; AI as a complement to human expertise.
  • Direct AI effectively — providing context and creating clear prompts.
  • Evaluate AI outputs — accuracy, relevance, and iteration.
  • Use AI responsibly — ethical and secure use; accountability for outcomes.

How Changeis can use this immediately

1) Make it “baseline pre-work” for internal or client cohorts

For any AI adoption effort (internal enablement or client delivery), treat AI-Ready as a prerequisite. It standardizes vocabulary and reduces time spent covering basics in workshops.

2) Turn the 5 pillars into a client-facing readiness rubric

Use the pillars as headings for a one-page rubric to assess maturity across:

  • People: who can prompt, validate, and apply security guidance?
  • Process: where do AI outputs get reviewed/approved?
  • Technology: which tools are approved and with what data policies?

3) Your differentiator: a govcon “Day 8–14” extension

The DOL course is intentionally generic. A Changeis follow-on module can be tailored to federal contracting execution:

  • Capture/proposal prompt patterns: RFI drafts, outline creation, compliance matrices.
  • Safe-use guardrails: what counts as sensitive procurement/client information; redaction patterns.
  • Validation checklists: hallucination checks, citation discipline, version control.

4) Copy the format: SMS microlearning as change management

The delivery mechanism is arguably the “product insight”: daily, low-friction behavior change. Mirror it with a 7–14 day “AI habit sprint” (daily prompt challenge + short reflection + optional office hours).

Suggested positioning copy

Short line

“We combine baseline AI literacy with role-based, mission-specific AI workflows for the federal market.”

Trust line

“Our approach emphasizes prompting + validation + responsible use—not tool hype.”

Client outcome framing

“Move from AI curiosity to repeatable execution: faster drafting, better quality control, safer handling of sensitive information.”

Quick actions

  1. Enroll a pilot cohort (5–10 people) and ask them to complete the 7-day course.
  2. Create a 1-page readiness rubric using the 5 pillars.
  3. Design a govcon “Day 8–14” add-on: prompts + validation + safe-use policy.
