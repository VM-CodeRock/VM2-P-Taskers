Scope Summary: The contractor will support ICE/ERO's Public Safety Partnership (PSP) Division in managing and analyzing data for all 287(g) Program models: Jail Enforcement Model (JEM), Task Force Model (TFM), Tribal Task Force Model (TTFM), and Warrant Service Officer (WSO) program. Work includes data management, reporting, analysis, coordination with 287(g) partner agencies and ERO Field Offices, process improvement, tasking support, and strategic and budget planning support.

Program Context: The 287(g) program deputizes state and local law enforcement officers to perform immigration enforcement. Under the second Trump administration, 287(g) participation has grown from roughly 150 agencies to over 1,169 agencies with 1,372 agreements — a >900% increase. As of January 2026, over 13,800–15,800 local officers are deputized, and the program is projected to generate $1.4–$2B in 2026 federal transfers to local agencies. This growth is driving a fundamental expansion of data management and analytics requirements — the contractor must handle significantly more partners, jurisdictions, data flows, and performance tracking than the prior contract period.
