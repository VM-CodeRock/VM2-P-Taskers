1 · Executive Summary

The Aviation Innovation Coalition (AIC) is a newly forming, Virginia-incorporated 501(c)(6) nonprofit trade association dedicated to transforming the U.S. National Airspace System (NAS). It is organized and led by Gene Hayman, CEO of Stratify Aerospace, a credible and well-regarded aviation modernization firm founded in 2023.

The AIC's central thesis is timely and well-grounded: the tragic January 2025 DCA crash accelerated White House and Congressional attention to NAS modernization, resulting in a historic $12.5 billion initial investment in the Brand New Air Traffic Control Systems (BNATCS) initiative, with an additional $19.5 billion needed to complete it. AIC positions itself as the coordinated U.S.-industry voice that will shape how those dollars are spent and which technologies get adopted.

Mission & Strategic Focus

AIC's declared mission is NAS transformation through coordinated public-private collaboration. The prospectus articulates seven specific purposes, with the most politically significant being: facilitating industry engagement with Congress, DOT, and the FAA; and supporting establishment of a U.S. Industrial Base Policy for Aviation (a direct counter to foreign-based vendors competing for BNATCS dollars).

Why the 501(c)(6) structure matters: A 501(c)(6) allows AIC to engage in lobbying and advocacy — including Congressional outreach — in ways a 501(c)(3) cannot. The current framing as "educational only" is a conservative starting posture that can expand. Members' dues are typically deductible as ordinary business expenses.
3 · Bylaws Key Points

Varun's Obligations as a Board Member

  • Board attendance: Minimum quarterly meetings (in person or electronic). No explicit attendance threshold stated — an absence policy is missing.
  • No substitution or delegation: The Overview Deck explicitly states "No substitutions or delegates will be allowed" for Board meetings. Varun must attend personally.
  • 2-year minimum term: Once designated, Varun's individual serves a minimum 2-year term. This is per the Overview Deck; the Bylaws say "two-year terms and may be reappointed" without a resignation mechanism specified.
  • Annual dues: $10,000/year. No grace period, payment timeline, or late fee specified.
  • Mission alignment: Conduct "inconsistent with the mission" is grounds for termination — a broad standard that needs clarification.

Voting Rights

  • Each Director has one vote — equal voting regardless of company size (Changeis has equal footing with Google)
  • Board actions require majority vote of Directors present (not majority of all 9 Directors)
  • Quorum = majority (5 of 9 Directors)
  • Bylaws amendments require two-thirds vote of the full Board — higher bar protects founding structure

Board Structure — Missing Specifics

Pending items per Gene's email: Board member assignments have not yet been confirmed. Varun needs to formally submit Changeis's designated Board representative. The Overview Deck clarifies this must be a single named individual who commits to a 2-year term.

Financial Provisions

  • Dues are established and reviewed by the Board — Founding Member Board can vote to change dues levels
  • Funds may only be used to advance AIC's mission — no financial benefit to individual members
  • Annual budget requires Board approval
  • No assessment or special fees language present beyond dues
  • Fiscal year TBD by the Board

IP Provisions

Notable gap: The Bylaws contain no explicit intellectual property provisions. Working Groups will produce policy frameworks, technical architectures, and implementation roadmaps — there is no language governing who owns, licenses, or can commercialize these outputs. This is a meaningful gap that should be addressed before Changeis contributes proprietary methodologies to any working group.

Exit / Termination

  • Membership may be terminated for: non-payment of dues, mission-inconsistent conduct, bylaws violations, or "unethical" behavior — all require Board approval
  • No voluntary withdrawal procedure is specified. There is no "how to resign" clause. This needs to be added or clarified.
  • Upon dissolution, assets distributed per Virginia nonprofit law — no member gets assets back

Liability & Indemnification

Favorable: Article IX provides full indemnification for Directors, officers, employees, and agents "to the fullest extent permitted under the laws of the Commonwealth of Virginia." Virginia's nonprofit code provides robust D&O protections. As a Board member, Varun is personally protected from AIC-related claims arising from good-faith governance decisions.

5 · Prospectus Analysis

Market Opportunity

The prospectus makes a compelling case grounded in real policy developments. Congress has already allocated $12.5 billion through the "One Big Beautiful Bill" for BNATCS, with Secretary Duffy advocating for a total of $31+ billion. The FAA's stated goal is implementation by end of 2028 — an aggressive timeline that creates urgency for coordinated industry input now.

The prospectus astutely identifies the strategic gap: "The FAA is being approached by numerous individual vendors — many foreign-based — each promoting proprietary solutions." AIC positions itself as the U.S.-based industry counter-force that can present a unified architecture to the FAA and Congress, rather than letting foreign vendors fill the vacuum.

Funding model is membership dues only — no grants, no government contracts, no product revenue. This is a strength (independence) and a constraint (limited operational capacity).

9 · Red Flags & Concerns

Overall risk level for AIC participation is Low-Moderate — the LOI is non-binding and the Bylaws are well-structured. The concerns below are manageable, not disqualifying.

  • No IP / Intellectual Property Provisions in Bylaws Working Groups will produce technical frameworks and architectures. Bylaws are silent on IP ownership, licensing, or commercialization rights. If Changeis contributes proprietary methodologies to a WG, there is currently no protection. Require resolution before the binding Membership Agreement.
  • No Voluntary Withdrawal Clause The Bylaws specify how members can be terminated by the organization but contain no procedure for a member to voluntarily resign or withdraw. Varun could be "trapped" in membership — unable to exit gracefully if AIC's direction changes. Request a withdrawal clause.
  • Dues Are Board-Controlled and Uncapped Section 3.5 states dues are "established and periodically reviewed by the Board of Directors." Since the Board consists of Founding Members, this is a self-governing check — but technically the Board could vote to raise Founding Member dues. Request a cap or founding member protection clause.
  • No Attendance / Absentee Policy for Board Members The Bylaws require quarterly Board meetings but don't specify consequences for non-attendance. The Overview Deck says "no substitutions or delegates" — if Varun's designated rep misses meetings, what happens to the Board seat? Needs clarification.
  • Gene Hayman / Stratify Aerospace — New Company, Limited Track Record Stratify Aerospace was founded in 2023 — it is 2-3 years old. While Gene Hayman has 30+ years of industry experience (Boeing, L-3, Collins Aerospace, CACI) and Stratify recently acquired Metron Aviation (a credible ATM firm), the company itself is an early-stage entity organizing a coalition that aims to influence $20B+ in federal spending. Monitor whether AIC gains traction; the April Congressional launch will be a key credibility test. Stratify's profile and Metron acquisition are positive signals.
  • Conflict of Interest Potential with ASRC Federal ASRC Federal just won a $437M FAA Second Level Engineering Services contract and competes in the same NAS support space as Changeis. Working closely in AIC Working Groups requires careful management of competitive information. Avoid sharing sensitive Changeis BD or proposal information in WG sessions.
  • Revenue Model Sustainability Risk $181K Year 1 revenue is a modest budget for an organization aiming to engage Congress, DOT, and FAA at a senior level. Crossroads Strategies (the congressional engagement firm) likely requires a separate retainer. If membership recruitment stalls below projections, AIC's operational capacity could be constrained. Watch whether Year 1 membership goals are met.
  • "Unethical Conduct" Termination Standard Section 3.6 allows termination for "operating in an unethical manner" — a subjective standard with no definition. Termination requires Board approval (majority vote), but the standard is vague. Request a more specific definition or a hearing/appeal process be added to the Bylaws.

  • Bylaws should be reviewed by Virginia nonprofit counsel to confirm 501(c)(6) compliance and member rights
  • Confirm that AIC's Congressional engagement approach (through Crossroads Strategies) does not trigger any lobbying disclosure requirements for Changeis as a member
