02 Opportunity Details — What This Actually Is

ICE's Tactical Communications (TACCOM) program underpins the agency's field operations across HSI (Homeland Security Investigations) and ERO (Enforcement and Removal Operations) divisions. Every ICE special agent, deportation officer, and field supervisor carries or uses a radio tied to the TACCOM network — a mission-critical system that must be interoperable with CBP, USCG, and state/local public safety networks.

Key Technical Terminology
TermDefinitionRelevance
TACCOM Tactical Communications — ICE's dedicated P25-standard encrypted radio network for law enforcement field operations The program this BPA supports
P25 (APCO-25) APCO Project 25 — North American standard for interoperable digital two-way radio communications used by public safety and federal law enforcement agencies All radios must be P25 Phase I/II compliant; DHS P25 CAP certified
Consolette A desktop or rack-mounted base-station radio unit that connects a dispatcher or supervisor to the radio network without carrying a handheld device — essentially a desk-based radio transceiver Core item in scope alongside handheld portables and vehicular mobiles
APX NEXT / APX Series Motorola Solutions' flagship law enforcement P25 radio line — the APX NEXT All-Band Radio is the current-generation model used extensively by DHS components Prior related forecast (F2025069804) explicitly named APX NEXT; this BPA likely covers the same product family
EOL Replacement Clause The BPA includes explicit language allowing newer radio models to replace end-of-life products during the 5-year ordering period as technology evolves Ensures flexibility for Motorola/L3Harris product refreshes over the contract period
BPA Structure A Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA) against NASA SEWP V allows ICE to place recurring orders over 5 years without re-competing each purchase, streamlining equipment procurement Explains the SEWP V vehicle choice — a hardware procurement GWAC
Interoperability ICE TACCOM must communicate with CBP, USCG, FBI, and state/local partner agencies — P25 compliance is mandatory for this cross-agency radio communication Restricts procurement to P25-certified hardware from a small set of OEMs
Full Scope Description (APFS)

"This acquisition is for the purchase of radios, consolette's and other radio accessories to support its mission. The Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA) will facilitate the recurring procurement of these critical communication tools, ensuring law enforcement personnel have reliable access to secure tactical wireless communication. Given the rapid advancement of IT and radio technology, certain products may become obsolete or reach their End of Life (EOL) over the contract period. To maintain operational effectiveness, newer models will replace EOL products, and emerging technologies that meet ICE's technical and operational requirements may be integrated into the BPA."

Source: APFS Record F2026073286

Relationship to Prior Forecast F2025069804 A closely related prior forecast (F2025069804, published April 2025) covered a "TACCOM Radio BPA" via the TacCom II IDIQ vehicle explicitly for "Motorola APX Next All-Band smart radios and accessories" with simultaneous deliveries to ICE CONUS office locations. The current F2026073286 uses NASA SEWP V instead of TacCom II — likely reflecting a strategic vehicle shift — but scope is substantively identical. This is a hardware resupply program, not a new capability build.
