Today’s prompt
Write a short vignette (250–400 words) about a moment when you chose to slow down and “work the process” instead of rushing to a quick answer—especially in a federal procurement or program delivery situation where ambiguity was high. Focus on the specific decision you made, the signals you noticed (or missed), and how you communicated confidence without overpromising.
Follow-up question
What is one process checkpoint you could institutionalize so your team makes that same high-quality decision more often—without you in the room?
Tip: If you’re short on time, write just the “decision moment” + the one sentence you used to align the room.
