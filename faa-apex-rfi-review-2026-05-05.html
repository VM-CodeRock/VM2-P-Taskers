RFI Response — Executive Overview

What Changeis Proposed

The submission is titled a "Concept Paper" — not just an RFI response — which is a deliberate strategic positioning choice. It advances the narrative that APEX is a transformation, not a modernization: Changeis explicitly distinguishes between "getting from the past to the present" (modernization) and what APEX actually is — a foundational enterprise intelligence backbone that positions the Academy for what comes next.

Architecture: Five Layers, Six Corpora, Four Feedback Loops

The architecture is described at non-proprietary level and is specifically designed to evolve. Models are accessed through an abstraction layer (not hardwired), enabling technology refresh without re-engineering. Three architectural principles enforced throughout: Hierarchical Agency (bounded-role components), Human-over-the-Loop (human validation before outputs affect student outcomes), and Data Sovereignty (FAA owns every corpus and model artifact).

Industry Day Q&A — Five Questions Tuned to Our RFI Submission

These questions are calibrated to what Changeis actually submitted — they signal technical continuity, show genuine RFI engagement, and probe the specific gaps our submission has. Bring these in priority order; if Q&A time is constrained, submit in writing to Stephen Shaffer as a follow-up.

Question 1 · Priority — High
Will the solicitation require ATC Academy-specific past performance as a separate evaluation factor, or will FAA credit analogous federal training performance intelligence or LMS administration experience?
Rationale: Our RFI cited DOT training programs and 3,500 FAA field personnel trained — neither is directly at the Academy. This answer determines whether we can compete as prime or need to team with an Academy incumbent.
Question 2 · Priority — High
The SOO identifies high-fidelity simulator grading data as a key source (§A). Are the Academy's current simulator systems — the Adacel-based and legacy units — already capable of one-way data export, or will the contractor need to develop custom integration firmware alongside the FAA IT and simulator vendor?
Rationale: Our submission proposed a one-way pipeline for simulator data and specifically noted the constraint that simulator units are not designed for bidirectional external analytics connectivity. If FAA has already worked this out with the simulator vendor, it simplifies our technical approach — and signals Phase 0 scope.
Question 3 · Priority — Medium
Will APEX be acquired as a new standalone task order, an add-on to an existing IDIQ vehicle (e.g., NavTac II), a GSA MAS order, or a new multi-year IDIQ? Is there a preference for small-business or full-and-open competition?
Rationale: We submitted as a small business under NAICS 541330 (ATC exception). Vehicle and competition type will determine whether our SB status is an advantage, and whether we need to team for scale. This is the single highest-value intelligence question at Industry Day if FAA will answer it.
Question 4 · Priority — Medium
SOO §E describes predictive analytics including early-to-downstream correlation between Academy performance and field controller outcomes. Does FAA currently have a mechanism for field-performance data to flow back to the Academy, or is closing that Safety Loop a contractor deliverable — and if so, what data-sharing agreements with field facilities and ARTCCs would the contractor need to support?
Rationale: Our submission described this as "the most ambitious element" of the predictive capability, maturing as longitudinal data accumulates. Knowing whether FAA has a data-sharing infrastructure in place changes the scope of Phase 3–4 significantly.
Question 5 · Priority — Medium
The SOO emphasizes that FAA does not seek a standalone reporting overlay — it seeks a foundational data backbone. Our concept paper proposed keeping existing LMS, assessment, and simulator systems in place and treating APEX as an integration layer above them. Is the FAA's intent to eventually consolidate those underlying systems into APEX, or to preserve the existing system landscape in perpetuity?
Rationale: Our "existing investments remain in place" framing is strategically safe for an RFI. But the long-term scope — and therefore cost and timeline — changes significantly depending on whether FAA sees APEX as a permanent overlay or a precursor to system consolidation.
