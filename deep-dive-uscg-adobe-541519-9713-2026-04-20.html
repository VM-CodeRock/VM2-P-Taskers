Bottom Line: This is a recompetition of a FY24 Adobe Enterprise License Renewal contract currently held by Emergent LLC (Virginia Beach, VA), a specialized Adobe federal reseller with established SEWP V access and prior USCG CG-C5I relationship. The requirement is for continued Adobe software maintenance and support — a transactional product-resale play with thin margin and a well-entrenched incumbent. Changeis's path to win requires either holding a SEWP V prime contract with Adobe authorization, or teaming as a subcontractor/pass-through under a SEWP V holder that can match Emergent's pricing and relationship depth.

The U.S. Coast Guard CG-C5I Service Center requires continued maintenance and support of Adobe software for FY26 (July 15, 2026 – July 14, 2027). The contract is a follow-on recompetition under NASA SEWP V, the government's dominant IT product and services GWAC. Emergent LLC, the incumbent, is an Adobe-certified federal reseller headquartered in Virginia Beach, VA with >1,500 employees and a broad software licensing portfolio spanning Adobe, Red Hat, and GitLab. The moderate RAG score (0.6414) reflects alignment at the "maintenance and support" task level from DOT/Volpe project work — not direct Adobe enterprise licensing experience from Changeis's past performance corpus.

7. Go / No-Go Recommendation
⚠ Conditional No-Go (Unless Teaming)

This opportunity is a product resale/license renewal vehicle — not a services engagement where Changeis's management consulting and IT engineering capabilities differentiate. The two critical gatekeeping requirements are:

  1. NASA SEWP V prime contract — mandatory contract vehicle. Without it, Changeis cannot receive a direct award.
  2. Adobe authorized reseller status — required to procure and resell Adobe enterprise licenses at government pricing. Without OEM authorization, Changeis cannot price competitively against Emergent or Carahsoft.

If Changeis holds a SEWP V prime and can obtain Adobe VAR authorization (or team with an authorized reseller): Pursue as a teaming vehicle to build CG-C5I relationship and diversify software licensing revenue. The USCG's willingness to use SEWP V for enterprise software renewals (VMware at $19.4M, Adobe at ~$3.3M) indicates a recurring procurement pattern worth pursuing.

If neither SEWP V nor Adobe authorization exists: Decline. The incumbent advantage (Emergent: active contract, Adobe partnership, SEWP V prime, 1,500+ employees, VA-based) combined with the transactional nature of the requirement makes a direct prime pursuit extremely low-probability without those credentials. The RAG score (0.64) reflects serviceable maintenance/support alignment — not license procurement capability — and would not sustain a competitive proposal without additional qualifications.
