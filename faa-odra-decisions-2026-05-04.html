ODRA-25-965  ·  25-ODRA-00976  ·  Served March 21, 2025
Protest of Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC)
Protester: SAIC  |  Agency: FAA  |  Solicitation: 693KA9-24-R-00005
Moot / Dismissed
SAIC filed a bid protest challenging an FAA procurement under Solicitation 693KA9-24-R-00005. Before adjudication on the merits, the FAA Product Team issued a voluntary notice of corrective action on March 20, 2025 — the day before the final order — that fully addressed SAIC's stated protest grounds. Both the protester's counsel and the intervenor's counsel concurred that the corrective action provided a sufficient remedy. ODRA Director John Dietrich dismissed the protest as moot under 14 C.F.R. § 17.19(c).
BD Lesson for Changeis: When Changeis competes on FAA acquisitions, voluntary corrective action by the Product Team can rapidly moot a protest — signaling the agency found merit in the concern without formal adjudication. Monitor for corrective actions as leading indicators that evaluation flaws were present. In BD strategy, flag procurements where corrective actions occur — the re-compete represents a second-chance opportunity.
ODRA-25-964  ·  24-ODRA-00972 & 00973  ·  Served March 19, 2025
Contract Disputes of Diversified Management Solutions, Inc.
Contractor: Diversified Management Solutions (DMS)  |  Agency: FAA  |  Contracts: 6973GH-23-R-00057 & -00060
Dismissed w/ Prejudice
DMS filed two contract disputes against the FAA related to contracts 6973GH-23-R-00057 and -00060. Both the Product Team and DMS jointly moved to dismiss both disputes with prejudice, indicating a negotiated settlement or mutual agreement to abandon the claims. ODRA granted the joint motion and dismissed both disputes under 14 C.F.R. § 17.31(d). Dismissal with prejudice means DMS cannot re-file these specific claims.
BD Lesson for Changeis: Joint dismissals with prejudice typically reflect negotiated resolutions — the contractor received something of value (contract modification, payment, scope change) in exchange for dropping claims. For Changeis, this reinforces the value of maintaining strong contractor-CO relationships to resolve disputes informally before ODRA involvement. DMS is a recurrent ODRA participant — track their active contracts for potential teaming or competition intelligence.
ODRA-25-963  ·  25-ODRA-00978  ·  Served March 4, 2025
Protest of Spatial Front, Inc.
Protester: Spatial Front, Inc.  |  Agency: FAA  |  Solicitation: 6973GH-24-R-00169
Moot / Dismissed
Spatial Front, Inc. filed a bid protest challenging an FAA procurement under Solicitation 6973GH-24-R-00169. On February 27, 2025, the FAA Product Team issued a voluntary corrective action notice that fully resolved Spatial Front's concerns. Spatial Front's counsel agreed the protest was moot, and ODRA dismissed the case under 14 C.F.R. § 17.19(c). Notably, Spatial Front is a recurrent ODRA filer — it has a history of successfully leveraging the ODRA process to extract corrective actions from the FAA.
BD Lesson for Changeis: Spatial Front's repeated use of ODRA — including a prior sustained protest (18-ODRA-00841) for which they were awarded EAJA attorney fees — shows that experienced ODRA protesters use the forum strategically. If Changeis contests FAA awards, understand ODRA's effectiveness: ~65% of protests resolve via ADR or corrective action before adjudication. Filing a timely, well-grounded protest often yields corrective action, giving a second evaluation chance without a full adjudication fight.
ODRA-24-962  ·  24-ODRA-00974  ·  Served December 23, 2024
Protest of Mythics, Inc. and Oracle America, Inc.
Protesters: Mythics, Inc. & Oracle America, Inc.  |  Agency: FAA  |  SAM Notice: 692M15-25-R-00009
Dismissed w/o Prejudice
Mythics, Inc. and Oracle America, Inc. jointly protested an FAA procurement under SAM Notice 692M15-25-R-00009. On December 23, 2024, all parties filed a joint motion to dismiss without prejudice. ODRA granted the motion under 14 C.F.R. § 17.19(c). Dismissal without prejudice means the protesters preserve the right to refile. This case is notable as it involves Oracle — a major enterprise software vendor — suggesting a potential FAA enterprise IT or software licensing procurement.
BD Lesson for Changeis: Oracle and Mythics (Oracle's authorized reseller) jointly protested, suggesting an enterprise software/IT incumbent challenge. FAA IT contracts in this space (database, cloud, enterprise software) can be teaming opportunities for Changeis if positioned as an integrator or implementer. Watch SAM.gov for re-solicitation of 692M15-25-R-00009 — without-prejudice dismissal may presage a restructured re-compete.
ODRA-24-961  ·  23-ODRA-00948  ·  Served December 3, 2024
Contract Dispute of Prow'ess Construction Corporation
Contractor: Prow'ess Construction Corp.  |  Agency: FAA  |  Contract: 697DCK-20-C-00150 (Indianapolis ARTCC Roof Replacement)
Both Sides Denied
Prow'ess Construction filed a contract dispute claiming $13,029.02 for roof boards damaged while stored on-site during a suspension period, arguing the CO's suspension notice constituted a change order triggering equitable adjustment. The FAA filed a counterclaim for $113,395 alleging Prow'ess failed to install "Kato Lite" coping and delivered substandard workmanship. ODRA denied Prow'ess's claim entirely and denied most of the FAA's counterclaim, awarding only a nominal $1,000 contract price reduction. This was one of the first merits-decided cases in the recent period.
BD Lesson for Changeis: This construction dispute illustrates ODRA's rigor on evidentiary standards — the FAA's $113K counterclaim was reduced to $1K because it lacked "reliable and probative evidence." For Changeis proposals on FAA construction or facilities contracts: (1) Document all suspension/stop-work periods meticulously; (2) ensure subcontractor quality is contractually enforceable and inspected; (3) never rely on informal CO communications — get changes in writing. The Changes clause interpretation here is conservative.

ODRA Reference — Key Facts for FAA Pursuit Teams
  • Exclusive jurisdiction: ODRA has sole authority over bid protests and contract disputes under FAA's Acquisition Management System (AMS) — GAO has no jurisdiction over FAA procurements
  • Filing deadlines: Pre-award protests before proposal deadline; post-award protests within 7 business days of award knowledge (or 5 days after debriefing)
  • Decision timeline: Typically within 65 business days of filing
  • ADR emphasis: ~65% of protests and ~90% of contract disputes resolved without adjudication
  • Sustain rate (historical): ~27% of adjudicated protests sustained (vs. ~14% at GAO)
  • Appeals: Final orders appealable to U.S. Court of Federal Claims or U.S. Court of Appeals for applicable circuit per 49 U.S.C. § 46110
  • EAJA fees: Prevailing protesters may recover attorney fees under the Equal Access to Justice Act
  • Source: FAA ODRA Recent Cases  ·  Bradley ODRA Process Guide (May 2025)
