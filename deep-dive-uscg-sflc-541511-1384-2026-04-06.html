Technical Domain: E-RCM and NLP in Naval Maintenance

Reliability-Centered Maintenance (RCM) is a systematic methodology — codified in SAE JA1011 — for determining the most effective maintenance approach for physical assets based on function, failure modes, and consequences. The "Enhanced" variant (E-RCM) integrates AI and digital technologies to overcome traditional RCM's resource-intensive limitations.

E-RCM Foundation

Why NLP is Central

  • Naval maintenance records are largely unstructured free-text (work orders, failure narratives, technician notes)
  • NLP extracts structured failure mode data from narrative reports automatically
  • LLMs enable semantic search across 5+ years of maintenance history
  • Reduces FMEA analysis time from 6–18 months to days
Navy Precedent

Active Programs

  • NAVSEA/Unstructured.io: Feb 2026 — AI-enabled data access for fleet decision-making in ASW & surface warfare; unstructured data to AI-ready formats
  • Charles River Analytics: $6.6M over 8.5 yr for NAVSEA predictive maintenance; probabilistic programming for fault prediction
  • SNAPPR (Navy STP): NLP on 2Kilo Logs for Aegis Weapons System; fuses unstructured logs with sensor data for maintenance optimization
  • Illumination Works: Navy edge data management for RCM/CBM — condition-based maintenance readiness
FedRAMP Requirement

Deployment Constraint

  • All AI solutions for government must be FedRAMP authorized (OMB M-24-10 mandate)
  • Commercial AI via cloud = FedRAMP required for agency use
  • AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, Google Cloud Gov are primary FedRAMP environments
  • Pre-trained naval domain models on FedRAMP = very narrow vendor set

The USCG SFLC requirement mirrors active Navy NAVSEA initiatives — the Unstructured.io NAVSEA award (Feb 2026) is the closest public analog, suggesting the government has already validated this technical approach and is now scaling it to Coast Guard surface forces.
