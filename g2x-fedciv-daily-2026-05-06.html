Policy Governmentwide High Impact

Trump EO: Fixed-Price Contracting Now the Default — Agencies Have 90 Days

President Trump signed an executive order on April 30, 2026 making fixed-price contracts the default for all federal procurement. Cost-reimbursement, T&M, and labor-hour contracts now require written justification to the agency head. Agency heads must review their 10 largest non-fixed-price contracts within 90 days (by July 29, 2026) and restructure them. OMB must issue implementation guidance within 45 days. OFPP must amend the FAR within 120 days. At DHS, non-fixed-price contracts exceeding $25M require agency head sign-off. FY2024 data shows ~$120B was obligated on cost-reimbursement vehicles — this EO directly targets that pool.

Changeis angle: T&M and cost-plus contracts common in management consulting are directly in scope. Changeis should anticipate contracting officers requesting conversion of existing vehicles and should prepare FFP pricing models for future bids. This also increases importance of well-defined SOWs and deliverable-based task orders — a negotiating advantage for disciplined firms.

Source: FedScoop — Trump Fixed-Price EO · Crowell & Moring — Legal Analysis · Blank Rome — Contractor Guidance

Policy GSA

GSA AI Procurement Clause (GSAR 552.239-7001) — Comment Period Closed, Final Rule Forthcoming

GSA's draft GSAR AI clause — requiring disclosure of AI systems used in contract performance, prohibition on foreign AI, NIST AI RMF compliance, and data rights protections — closed its public comment period April 3, 2026. GSA indicated the clause could appear in a GSA Schedule refresh as early as spring 2026. The clause imposes six core obligations on contractors using AI in performance, including a blanket prohibition on AI components "manufactured, developed, or controlled by non-U.S. entities."

Changeis angle: If Changeis uses AI tools in any deliverables (analysis, report drafting, data processing), these tools must be documented and vetted for the foreign AI prohibition. Prepare an internal AI inventory now — this will be a contract compliance requirement on GSA vehicles within months.

Source: Holland & Knight — GSA AI Clause Deep Dive · Fox Rothschild — GSA Schedule Impact

Policy GSA · All Agencies Deadline: May 31

GSA Networx & WITS3 Legacy Telecom Contracts Expire May 31, 2026

All GSA Networx and remaining WITS3 telecommunications contracts reach final expiration on May 31, 2026 — 26 days away. Agencies still on these contracts must complete transition to EIS (Enterprise Infrastructure Solutions) or other approved vehicles. GSA's Transactional Data Reporting (TDR) program, now fully implemented, will track post-transition spending. The transition has generated significant task order activity across federal civilian agencies.

Changeis angle: Agency transition planning and cutover support are active needs at impacted agencies this week. If Changeis supports any agencies still on Networx/WITS3, prioritize transition readiness assistance. This also signals broader infrastructure modernization spending headed to EIS contractors.

Source: GSA — Legacy IT Contracts End Dates · GSA — TDR Full Implementation

Legislative Congress · IT Procurement

House Panel Advances FIT Procurement Act — Raises Micro-Purchase to $25K, SAT to $500K

The Federal Improvement in Technology (FIT) Procurement Act (H.R. 4123) passed the House Oversight Committee 42-0, raising the micro-purchase threshold from $10K to $25K and the simplified acquisition threshold from $250K to $500K. The Technology Modernization Fund Reauthorization Act (H.R. 2985) also advanced, extending the TMF through 2032 and requiring full agency reimbursement of TMF loans. These bills are moving toward the House floor.

Changeis angle: Higher SAT thresholds benefit small businesses competing below full & open thresholds. TMF reauthorization means continued availability of modernization loan funding at agencies — Changeis should track agencies drawing TMF funds as a leading indicator of upcoming IT modernization task orders.

Source: FedScoop — Tech & Cyber Bills Advance · MeriTalk — House Panel
