Executive Summary

Today's Mission

  • Three high-value meetings with House Transportation & Infrastructure Committee members (both parties) and Senate Commerce Committee majority members — representing Changeis's highest-leverage congressional touchpoints in aviation IT modernization.
  • Post-DCA collision urgency: The January 29, 2025 Reagan National midair collision has galvanized Congress. Every member in today's meetings has taken action — new legislation, hearings, or public statements. ATC technology modernization is a rare bipartisan priority with real momentum and $12.5B+ already appropriated.
  • FAA Reauthorization Act 2024 is being implemented now. Congress is actively overseeing execution — and looking for qualified, proven federal IT contractors to deliver. Changeis's DHS/TSA portfolio is directly transferable.
  • Changeis positioning: Federal IT modernization firm with proven DHS/TSA delivery, FMCSA transportation safety systems expertise, cybersecurity credentials, and SBA small business certification. This is not a cold call — Changeis's existing federal IT work is directly analogous to what FAA needs.
  • Political landscape: Republican meetings (T&I Minority, Senate Commerce Majority) emphasize private sector innovation, counter-China technology competition, off-the-shelf solutions, and small business contracting. Democratic meeting (T&I Majority) emphasizes safety, worker protections, inclusive contracting, and bipartisan infrastructure delivery.
  • Key legislation to reference: ALERT Act (H.R. 7613), ROTOR Act, Aviation Innovation Act (H.R. 7553), SAFEGUARDS Act, Air Traffic Situational Awareness Act (S.3929).

What NOT to Say
⚠ Avoid These
  • Don't criticize specific agencies or officials by name — especially avoid criticism of FAA Administrator Bedford or DOGE/Trump administration actions. These are politically charged in different ways for each audience.
  • Don't promise specific contract scope or dollar amounts — you don't know what procurement vehicles will be used. Say "we're positioned to compete" not "we will win contract X."
  • Don't use partisan framing — avoid characterizing either party's approach negatively. Both sides have valid aviation modernization priorities.
  • Don't criticize Peraton by name to Republican members — some may be comfortable with the Peraton award. Stick to "as a small business, we offer a different model" rather than attacking a specific competitor.
  • Don't overstate DHS/TSA relevance — acknowledge the difference between TSA security IT and FAA ATC systems. Don't imply they're identical. Say "directly analogous" not "the same thing."
  • Don't make the meeting about Changeis — lead with questions about their priorities. Position Changeis as a solution to their stated problems, not a company seeking contracts.
