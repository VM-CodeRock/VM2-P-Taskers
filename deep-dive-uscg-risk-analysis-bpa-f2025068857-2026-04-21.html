Scope: What This BPA Funds

This BPA consolidates all strategic operational risk analysis work under the DCO into a single contracting vehicle. The two primary programs are:

  • MSRAM (Maritime Security Risk Analysis Model): A post-9/11 Microsoft Access-based risk model quantifying terrorist attack risk against Maritime Critical Infrastructure and Key Resources (MCIKR) — port infrastructure, waterways, vessels, petroleum facilities, LNG terminals, nuclear materials, and other CIKR. Risk = Threat × Consequence × Vulnerability. Data collected at sector/field level, aggregated nationally at classified level. Feeds DHS Port Security Grant Program (PSGP) allocations, USCG resource deployment decisions, and strategic planning at all levels. ABS Group (formerly ABS Consulting) has supported MSRAM since its 2005 development and has built 20 years of institutional knowledge in the model's architecture.
  • RBMSRO (Risk Based Maritime Security Response Operations): Optimization tool supporting Port, Waterways and Coastal Security (PWCS) missions — analyzes USCG law enforcement asset deployment, plans and reports Maritime Security Response Operations activities, and operationalizes MCIKR patrol through the Key Port Areas (KPA) patrol concept.
  • Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) Support: Additional operational risk assessments and ERM efforts across DCO leveraging the same BPA vehicle.
  • Risk Communication Enhancement: Using existing USCG and federal IT infrastructure to communicate risk data to internal and external stakeholders at local, state, and federal levels for resource allocation, policy, and budget decisions.

Organizational Context — Two Key Customer Offices:

DCO-81 — Office of Performance Management & Assessment

Primary BPA customer under the Deputy Commandant for Operations. DCO-81 owns enterprise risk management and strategic performance assessment across USCG operational programs. The BPA's strategic planning and program management scope (risk communication, enterprise risk integration, resource allocation modeling) maps directly here. Changeis's performance management and strategic planning capabilities are highly relevant to DCO-81 requirements.

CG-PSA-2 — Domestic Port Security Evaluation Division

Program manager for MSRAM and Port Security Resiliency Assessments (PSRAs) domestically. Under CG-5P (Assistant Commandant for Prevention Policy). Coordinates with DHS and other federal agencies on port security policy. This is the technically deepest dimension of the BPA — MSRAM database management, quantitative risk scoring, and maritime domain expertise. ABS Group's 20-year relationship with CG-PSA-2 is the primary incumbency risk.
