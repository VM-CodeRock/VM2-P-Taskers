CWMD Organizational Risk Assessment
FY2026 Budget: Complete CWMD Dissolution
The Trump-Vance Administration's FY2026 DHS Congressional Budget Justification proposes zeroing out all CWMD funding ($409.4M) and transferring all 286 positions to other components. As of January 2026, CWMD is down to ~140 federal employees (50% reduction via buyouts and reassignments). The House-passed DHS appropriations bill reflects the White House budget. Senate treatment is unresolved as of the FY2026 CR deadline.

Key Contracting Risk
The solicitation was estimated for March 6, 2026 — with Congress still resolving the FY2026 appropriations. If Congress approves the dissolution, the contracting office (70RCMD — CWMD Contracting Division) loses budget authority. The contract could be awarded then immediately novated to a different component's contracting office, or cancelled outright. The cloud infrastructure is real and will continue to operate — but who holds the contract vehicle and budget authority is highly uncertain.

Notably, the cloud engineering requirement (contractor base ~500 even as federal staff was halved) suggests the technical work itself persists. The CWMD cloud infrastructure supports real operational programs (BioWatch, Securing the Cities, classified detection networks) that will continue under receiving agencies. The risk is not mission cancellation — it is contracting vehicle disruption and customer loss.

Go / No-Go Recommendation
Recommendation
NO PURSUE — High Risk, Low Win Probability
Two independent fatal factors drive this verdict. First, the same organizational risk that killed the CWMD A&AS deep dive (F2024065852) applies here: CWMD is being dismantled by the Trump FY2026 budget, with full dissolution of the contracting office and redistribution of all programs. A 5-year cloud engineering contract awarded to a CWMD-branded contracting office that is simultaneously being dissolved creates enormous performance and continuity risk. Second, Changeis lacks the core technical differentiators required — classified cloud engineering, DHS ATO/RMF experience, and CWMD mission-specific past performance. The incumbent (CMCI) holds 5 years of deep institutional knowledge. The RAG score of 0.67 reflects capability adjacency, not direct relevance. With 9 prior bidders, no SB set-aside, and organizational chaos in the customer office, this is not a winnable opportunity for Changeis at this time.

This would require a teaming arrangement with a GSA Schedule holder who has DHS cloud credentials. Do not pursue as prime.
