1 — TL;DR · 60-Second Summary
Five Things Varun Needs to Know
  • GSA is consolidating federal procurement under OneGov and OASIS+. The Trump administration's Executive Order 14240 is routing trillions in federal spending through GSA-managed vehicles — OASIS+ is now the mandated Best-in-Class vehicle for professional services. If Changeis isn't on OASIS+, it is increasingly locked out of major task order competitions before evaluation even begins.
  • The FAR is being rewritten from scratch — first wave already live. The "Revolutionary FAR Overhaul" (EO 14275, April 2025) started taking effect February 1, 2026. Shorter cycles, outcomes over process, and commercial-practice norms are the new reality. Contractors who move fast and price precisely will win; those relying on compliance-heavy proposal machinery will fall behind.
  • AI in acquisition is moving from aspiration to infrastructure. GSA's USAi platform, OneGov AI deals (Microsoft, ServiceNow, Meta, Broadcom), and Laura Stanton's "buying guides for generative AI" signal that agencies are being equipped to buy and deploy AI right now. Demand for contractors who can deliver AI-enabled services — not just talk about them — is accelerating.
  • GSA is standing up a new Acquisition QSMO to standardize procurement systems across 229+ fragmented contract-writing systems. This creates a direct opportunity for acquisition modernization consulting and technology integration services — an emerging wedge for professional services firms with the right positioning.
  • Cultural change is the bottleneck. Both Johnson and Stanton emphasized that regulatory and technology changes are only as good as the workforce adopting them. Contractors who help agencies through the change — training, templates, adoption support — are better positioned than those who simply comply with the new rules.

3 — Key Points · Full Detail
OneGov Strategy — Unifying Government Buying ▶
Theme: Procurement Consolidation

GSA's OneGov strategy, launched April 2025, treats the entire federal government as a single customer to negotiate enterprise-level deals with commercial vendors. Rather than each agency independently contracting for the same software, GSA negotiates governmentwide pricing and terms — then makes those deals available to all agencies.

  • Over a dozen OneGov agreements have been executed: Microsoft, SAP, xAI, Meta (Llama models), ServiceNow (including agentic AI), Broadcom/VMware (up to 64% discounts), Cohesity (AI data security).
  • The initiative directly enables AI adoption — agencies that had "light contact" with AI tools can now experiment via pre-negotiated, discounted pathways without standing up their own acquisition processes.
  • Johnson's GDIT perspective: enterprise buying reduces complexity for agencies and frees integration partners to focus on deployment and customization rather than procurement mechanics.
  • Stanton's framing: OneGov is not just cost savings — it is building lasting vendor relationships and standardizing terms that improve predictability for both buyers and industry.
"We want to make sure that these technologies do not appear so distinct to normal federal procurement eyes that, 'Well, maybe we'll get to that later.' We want to make it available." — Zach Whitman, GSA Chief AI Officer (ACT-IAC summit, January 2026, related context)
FAR Overhaul — Most Significant Regulatory Change in Decades ▶
Theme: Regulatory Reform

The Revolutionary FAR Overhaul (EO 14275, April 2025) is the most comprehensive rewrite of federal acquisition rules since the FAR was introduced 40+ years ago. The first wave took effect February 1, 2026. The largest changes hit by June 30, 2026.

  • Core philosophy shift: Remove non-statutory requirements; emphasize outcomes over process; align with commercial practices; improve procurement predictability.
  • TINA threshold increase: Certified cost/pricing data requirement rises from $2M to $10M — major compliance burden relief for mid-size contractors.
  • Acquisition planning reform: Written acquisition plans no longer require specific elements; oral plans acceptable for many contract types. Planning becomes a living document updated to circumstances.
  • FSS ordering procedures moved from FAR Part 8 to GSAM Subpart 538.71 — significant administrative change for MAS-using contractors.
  • Approval threshold tiers restructured: Four tiers from $750K (CO authority) up to >$75M (senior procurement executive).
  • Required-use mandate: Agencies must use OFPP-designated "required use" contracts — including BIC vehicles like OASIS+ — unless HCA grants exception. This is a structural force-multiplier for OASIS+ task order volume.
  • Johnson emphasized: cultural change is the hardest part. COs trained for decades on old FAR will require leadership, training, templates, and institutional support.
"The FAR overhaul is both critical and far-reaching. Cultural change is essential — contracting officers have been trained for years to follow established methods." — Laura Stanton, Deputy Commissioner FAS (ACT-IAC ELC, January 2026)
OASIS+ Phase II — The New Professional Services Channel ▶
Theme: Contract Vehicle Strategy

OASIS+ Phase II launched December 2025, adding five new service domains (Business Administration, Financial Services, Human Capital, Marketing & PR, Social Services) for a total of 13 domains. Final Phase II amendments posted January 12, 2026 — the vehicle is now continuously open with no submission deadlines.

  • OASIS+ is now Best-in-Class (BIC) — under the FAR overhaul's required-use mandate, agencies must route professional services spending through BIC vehicles unless exempted.
  • Phase II was directly driven by EO 14240, consolidating redundant agency IDIQs onto OASIS+.
  • Self-scoring evaluation model — contractors calculate their own points across: Qualifying Projects (heaviest weight, $1M min for unrestricted), Past Performance, Federal Prime Experience, Business Systems, Clearances/Certifications.
  • Rolling on-ramps mean there is no deadline pressure — but early positioning is still strategic. The contractor pool expands continuously; agencies see newer entrants as they are added.
  • Task orders flow through GSA eBuy — firms on the vehicle compete for task orders across all 13 domains where they hold pool awards.

The consolidation effect is structural: billions are shifting from fragmented agency IDIQs onto OASIS+. Firms not on the vehicle miss the opportunity before evaluation even begins.

AI in Procurement — From Aspiration to Infrastructure ▶
Theme: AI Integration

The WashingtonExec panel explicitly addresses how AI is reshaping both how the government buys (acquisition tools) and what it buys (AI services). GSA has moved from talking about AI to deploying it operationally.

  • GSAi / USAi: GSA built an internal AI platform (GSAi) that looks like a chatbot but captures detailed telemetry — how users interact, how model components perform. USAi is the shared-service version for other agencies. Launched August 2025.
  • Buying guide for generative AI: Stanton highlighted GSA is building targeted buying guides specifically for generative AI — clear decision frameworks for when and how to procure these technologies.
  • AI as acquisition intelligence: By collecting expert feedback on AI responses across cost, speed, energy use, and quality, GSA becomes a more informed buyer — comparing model performance rather than relying on vendor claims.
  • Contractor implication: Agencies equipped with AI buying guides, USAi platforms, and OneGov AI deals will rapidly increase AI service task orders. Contractors able to deliver AI-enabled solutions on existing vehicles are in the strongest position.
  • Johnson's GDIT angle: standardization and enterprise-level purchasing accelerate technology adoption, allowing agencies to "focus more resources on mission-critical priorities" rather than procurement administration.
"Deeply discounted AI pricing allows agencies to experiment, test use cases, and decide how to scale in the future." — Laura Stanton
Acquisition QSMO — New Coordination Infrastructure ▶
Theme: Governance & Systems

In March 2026, OMB pre-designated GSA to establish the Governmentwide Acquisition Shared Services and Solutions QSMO — a central body to coordinate and standardize federal procurement systems across agencies. Stanton authored the GSA blog announcing this initiative.

  • The federal government currently operates 229+ known contract writing systems — causing siloed data, inconsistent processes, and higher costs.
  • The QSMO will define a marketplace-based operating model, promote common data standards, monitor provider performance, and consolidate demand toward vetted shared solutions.
  • FY26 focus: define operating model, establish evaluation criteria, identify service providers, initiate agency transition pathways.
  • Formal QSMO designation expected by end of 2026.
  • Opportunity: This creates explicit demand for acquisition system modernization, digital transformation of procurement workflows, shared service integration, and workforce transformation — exactly the type of consulting and IT delivery work professional services firms provide.
Cultural & Workforce Transformation — The Hidden Bottleneck ▶
Theme: Change Management

Both Johnson and Stanton returned to the same theme: the regulatory and technology infrastructure for acquisition modernization is being built, but cultural change in the contracting workforce is the binding constraint.

  • Contracting officers trained for decades on FAR compliance will not automatically adopt the new streamlined FAR — they need leadership, templates, training, and institutional backing from FAI and DAU.
  • Stanton explicitly cited practitioner handbooks and targeted buying guides as the enablers of workforce adaptation.
  • Johnson's industry framing: standardization (OneGov, OASIS+) removes complexity and lets agencies focus mission resources, but only if the workforce trusts and uses the new tools.
  • For contractors, this means the firms who help COs navigate the transition — providing templates, knowledge, co-consulting — gain soft power advantages that translate into task order wins.
4 — Changeis Strategy Relevance

Structured analysis of direct BD implications, strategic validation, and competitive dynamics for Changeis Inc. — a federal IT professional services firm focused on DHS (CBP, TSA, USCG, ICE), FAA/DOT, and adjacent civilian agencies.

Direct Opportunity

OASIS+ Position is Now Mission-Critical

  • OASIS+ is BIC — agencies must use it or get an exception. This is no longer optional for BD strategy.
  • Phase II continuous on-ramp: no deadline. Submit when your story is strongest.
  • Target pools: Management & Advisory, Program Support, Engineering, Technical & Analytical align directly with Changeis's FAA/DHS capabilities.
  • ATEPS-II and IPSS2 past performance are qualifying projects for OASIS+ scoring.
Direct Opportunity

AI-Enabled Services Demand Surge

  • OneGov AI deals (ServiceNow, Microsoft, etc.) mean agencies now have pre-negotiated access to AI tools — they need contractors to deploy and integrate them.
  • FAA/DHS are natural early adopters: aviation safety analytics, threat detection, logistics optimization all map to AI use cases.
  • Positioning Changeis as an AI integration partner (not just a staff augmentation firm) on existing vehicles is a high-value repositioning move.
Direct Opportunity

Acquisition QSMO / Modernization Consulting

  • GSA's Acquisition QSMO creates demand for acquisition systems modernization — helping agencies replace 229+ contract writing systems with standardized solutions.
  • This is IT delivery and change management work — Changeis's core competencies.
  • Adjacent agencies (DHS components, FAA) will need to participate in QSMO transition pathways — creating task order volume on existing vehicles.
Strategic Validation

OASIS+ BD Pursuit is Correctly Prioritized

  • The panel confirms that OASIS+ is the dominant channel for professional services — Changeis's active OASIS+ BD pursuit is the right call.
  • The required-use mandate under FAR overhaul strengthens the case: task order volume on OASIS+ will grow substantially as agencies retire legacy IDIQs.
  • Phase II's 13-domain scope means Changeis can position across more service lines than under Phase I.
Strategic Validation

DHS & FAA Focus Aligns With Consolidation Trajectory

  • EO 14240 consolidates common goods/services under GSA — but mission-specific IT at DHS/FAA will continue to need specialized contractors who know the domain.
  • Changeis's deep DHS/FAA domain expertise is a durable differentiator precisely because consolidated vehicles (OASIS+) reward domain depth in qualifying project scoring.
  • ATEPS-II as an active $114.7M contract is strong social proof for OASIS+ qualification.
Risk / Threat

FAR Overhaul Rewards Speed — Changeis Must Adapt

  • Shorter procurement cycles and simplified evaluations favor firms with streamlined proposal machinery and precise pricing. Firms relying on compliance-heavy BD processes face disadvantage.
  • Required-use mandate for BIC contracts (OASIS+) means agencies may no longer be able to use agency-specific IDIQs where Changeis has incumbent positions — forcing competition onto OASIS+ vehicles where new entrants compete equally.
  • TINA threshold rise to $10M reduces some compliance burden but also removes barriers for smaller competitors on task orders below that threshold.
Risk / Threat

Large Integrators (GDIT) Have Structural Advantages

  • GDIT (Johnson's firm) has $500M+ civilian portfolios, deep OASIS+ presence, and the scale to invest in AI capabilities proactively. They are a direct competitor at large task order thresholds.
  • As OASIS+ consolidates spending, large integrators with broad vehicle presence can outbid boutique firms on full-service task orders by bundling capabilities.
  • Changeis's counterstrategy must be domain specialization + teaming — not breadth competition against GDIT/Leidos/Booz Allen.
Competitive Implication

Market Structure Is Bifurcating

  • Procurement consolidation bifurcates the market: large integrators dominate full-vehicle, multi-domain OASIS+ primes; niche firms win on domain expertise, speed, and cultural fit with specific agencies.
  • Mid-market firms without strong OASIS+ positioning or deep domain moats risk being squeezed out of both tiers.
  • Changeis is correctly positioned in the domain-specialist tier — but needs OASIS+ vehicle access to compete for the task orders that will flow through it.
5 — Validate / Critique Current Changeis Efforts
Area Assessment Detail
OASIS+ BD Pursuit Well-Positioned Active pursuit is the correct strategic call. BIC mandatory use and Phase II continuous on-ramp make this time-sensitive but not deadline-driven. The key question is whether Changeis's qualifying projects are being documented at the $1M+ threshold with OASIS+ scoring language. ATEPS-II and IPSS2 are strong anchors.
DHS / FAA Domain Focus Well-Positioned Consolidation policy does not eliminate mission-specialized IT work at DHS/FAA — it concentrates it on vehicles like OASIS+ where domain depth translates to higher scores. Changeis's customer intimacy at CBP, TSA, USCG, ICE, and FAA is a durable differentiator that large integrators cannot easily replicate at the program level.
AI Capability Development Gap The panel makes clear that AI demand is moving fast — agencies now have OneGov access to ServiceNow AI, Microsoft Copilot, Meta Llama, etc. and need contractors to deploy them. Changeis needs a credible, articulated AI service line — not just acknowledgment that AI is important, but specific use cases, delivery models, and past performance in AI-enabled IT delivery. This gap will widen quickly if not addressed in 2026.
FAR Overhaul Readiness Gap The first wave of FAR changes took effect February 1, 2026. Changeis's proposal and BD processes should be audited against the new FAR — specifically the streamlined acquisition planning requirements, the new FSS ordering procedures in GSAM, and the required-use mandate for BIC vehicles. Unknown whether this internal review has occurred.
Acquisition Workforce Change Positioning Gap The cultural change theme from both speakers identifies an underserved market: helping agency contracting officers adopt the new FAR, new buying tools, and AI acquisition guides. Changeis could develop an advisory offering around acquisition transformation that builds relationships with COs at DHS/FAA — a non-BD, relationship-building play that creates pipeline.
Teaming Strategy Gap As consolidation shifts spending to OASIS+ and large integrators win broad IDIQ slots, Changeis needs a proactive teaming strategy — identifying large prime relationships (including potentially GDIT) where Changeis's DHS/FAA expertise can serve as a key sub. This is not currently reflected in active programs as far as known.
Pricing Discipline Behind Trend FAR overhaul rewards pricing precision and speed. Shorter procurement cycles with simplified evaluations mean less time to build pricing models in capture. Changeis should invest in a price-to-win capability and labor rate benchmarking specific to OASIS+ task order competition, where eBuy awards happen faster than traditional IDIQ competitions.
