1 · Executive Summary

The U.S. Coast Guard's Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Cyber and IT Service Center (CS5ISC) is recompeting its Business Development Services contract — a five-year Firm Fixed Price effort on OASIS+, Small Business set-aside, valued at $2M–$5M (NAICS 541611). The contract supports the Service Center's Business Operations Division Acquisition Integration Branch (BOD-AIB), providing consulting, project management, strategic planning, metrics development, and process improvement services to one of the Coast Guard's largest IT enterprise organizations.

The current incumbent is TECHPRIDE-JPI JV, LLC (contract 47QRAA23D007F) — a Small Business Administration-approved mentor-protégé joint venture of TechPride, Inc. and Java Productions, Inc. (JPI). Critically, TechPride-JPI's referenced IDV is a GSA Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) contract, not OASIS+. Because the recompete specifies OASIS+ as the required vehicle, the incumbent must compete on OASIS+ — effectively neutralizing any vehicle-based advantage TechPride-JPI currently holds.

Changeis holds three OASIS+ contract awards covering the Management and Advisory domain — Unrestricted (47QRCA25DU072), Small Business (47QRCA25DS260), and WOSB (47QRCA24DW284). NAICS 541611 is explicitly listed under the Changeis OASIS+ Management and Advisory domain. This is a direct prime opportunity — no teaming or subcontracting required for vehicle eligibility.

Changeis's RAG corpus returns a 0.6551 composite score (top match: Volpe/DOT SDDC Safety Analysis Support V3132063), confirming strong past performance alignment in program analysis, process improvement, metrics development, and strategic consulting — the exact capability pillars requested by BOD-AIB.

What "Business Development Services" Means in This Context: Despite the name, this is not external sales or capture management. Within CS5ISC, "Business Development" refers to the internal capability planning and acquisition integration functions performed by BOD-AIB — helping the Service Center plan, manage, and improve the acquisition lifecycle for its extensive portfolio of IT contracts supporting Coast Guard C5I operations. The scope is fundamentally management consulting, project management, and business operations improvement delivered inside a large federal IT service organization.

CS5ISC and BOD-AIB Context: The C5I Service Center is the Coast Guard's central IT organization, operating multiple product lines (Command & Control, Communications, Intelligence Systems, Operations, Unified Capabilities) and shared services divisions across campuses in Virginia (Alexandria, Chesapeake, Portsmouth), West Virginia (Kearneysville), and California (Alameda). The Business Operations Division (BOD) provides business processes and services to enable these product lines. The Acquisition Integration Branch (AIB) specifically focuses on aligning acquisition planning, market research, and contract strategy with the Service Center's mission execution needs — acting as the internal BD and acquisition support arm.

Services Required: The requirement covers five pillars that align directly with Changeis core services:

  • Contractor Consulting — Expert advisory services supporting BOD-AIB decision-making and mission alignment
  • Project Management — PM support for BOD-AIB initiatives across the C5ISC acquisition portfolio
  • Strategic Planning — Developing and refining the Service Center's business and acquisition strategy
  • Metrics Development — Building performance measurement frameworks for BOD-AIB programs
  • Process Development/Improvement — Designing and optimizing acquisition and business operation workflows

Place of performance is "Several C5I Campuses, VA" — primarily Northern Virginia and Hampton Roads, consistent with Changeis's Arlington, VA base of operations.
