2. Opportunity Details

The CBP Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) encompasses Global Entry, NEXUS, SENTRI, and FAST — risk-based programs that provide expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers at more than 80 enrollment centers across the United States, Puerto Rico, Guam, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. Each enrollment center requires contractor staff to support CBP officers in processing applicants.

Core Requirement: Bilingual contractor staff at TTP Enrollment Centers perform: (1) applicant interviews in English and a second language, (2) biographical data collection and verification, (3) answering public questions about TTP programs, (4) appointment scheduling, and (5) document scanning and administrative support. This is a 100% labor-hour, on-site, face-to-face service delivery model.

Key policy note: Executive Order 14224 (Jan 2025) established English as the official federal language, but the GSA ordering guide for language services (updated Dec 2025) affirms that agencies are not required to stop bilingual services and that agency heads may make mission-based decisions. CBP's enrollment mission inherently requires bilingual staff to process non-English-speaking TTP applicants (Spanish, French, Chinese, Korean, etc.) — this contract's core function remains operationally necessary despite the EO.
