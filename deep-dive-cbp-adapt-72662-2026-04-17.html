What CBP Is Asking For

ADAPT is a three-pillar AI and program transformation contract supporting the Passenger Systems Program Directorate (PSPD), which oversees CBP's passenger processing technology including the TECS system. The work is split across three functional areas:

2.1 — AI-Driven Software Development Core Pillar

  • Develop software, reusable code, and DevOps capabilities using LLMs and CBP-approved AI tools — automation, scalability, CI/CD integration
  • Use AI tools to accelerate software delivery and automate unit test case creation and execution
  • Streamline trouble ticket disposition and closure
  • Implement consistent UX/UI across all device types using AI and LLMs
  • Support new development and UX/UI projects; systems integration under COR direction

2.2 — Program Support Management Layer

  • Respond to CBP documentation/reporting requirements including TECS acquisition program needs
  • Support acquisition milestones: business case reviews, operational analyses, PMRs, system health/risk reporting
  • Support DHS Cost Wise Readiness (CWR) initiative; track required metrics
  • Support GAO and DHS OIG audits; identify/document/track program risks per TECS and PSPD guidelines
  • Develop and automate standardized performance reports; collaborate with CBP Records and Information Management (RIM)

2.3 — Research & Analysis Advisory Layer

  • Cloud optimization research: architecture review, open-source alternatives, cost avoidance identification
  • Coordinate with Azure, AWS, and CBP MAGE (ModulAr GCP Environment) teams to resolve technical issues
  • Advise PSPD Program Manager on solutions; participate in change request reviews
  • Liaise across CTO, CDO, system integrators, and cloud account representatives
  • Provide independent assessments of cloud solutions and efficiency opportunities to PSPD leadership
Key Technical Environment

PSPD manages CBP's core passenger processing systems (TECS), deployed on a multi-cloud architecture spanning Microsoft Azure, AWS, and CBP's proprietary ModulAr GCP Environment (MAGE). The ADAPT contractor must operate fluently across all three platforms and interface directly with CBP's Cloud Infrastructure Management (CIM) team.

Market & Agency Context

CBP OIT AI Investment Trajectory. CBP's Office of Information and Technology has been aggressively building AI capabilities since FY2022. The agency's AI Center of Innovation (AI COI) leads strategic transformation with 57 active innovation projects as of FY2022, with nearly half focused on AI & Analytics. CBP's OIT Strategy 2024–2028 explicitly prioritizes AI/ML integration across mission systems.

PSPD Technology Context. The Passenger Systems Program Directorate oversees CBP's largest passenger-facing IT investments — including TECS (the backbone passenger processing system), biometric systems, and traveler vetting tools. In 2025, CBP's PSPD issued an RFP for a $100M+ Traveler Processing & Vetting Software (TPVS) 2.0 contract, signaling aggressive modernization spend in the directorate. ADAPT is a parallel initiative bringing AI acceleration to PSPD's software development lifecycle.

Competitive AI Awards at CBP. CBP has been actively awarding AI-adjacent contracts in 2025–2026, including a Clearview AI facial recognition contract for tactical targeting (15 licenses, NTC), and IT-Strat's recent win of the $600M CBP Application Development and O&M BPA (April 2026). The DHS/CBP ecosystem is one of the most active in federal IT for AI-augmented contracting. ADAPT represents a focused small-business set-aside within this larger wave.

No Incumbent — Green Field. ADAPT is a new requirement with no prior contract. This is a significant structural advantage: there is no incumbent to displace, no protest risk from a displaced awardee, and no legacy relationship to overcome. The field is open to any qualified SB.
