"Any update on the review and analysis of the FAA report? Let me know if you were able to access the attachments and connect it to the 45 systems as discussed."

On the attachments & 45 systems: The 5/2 brief confirmed the full analysis. The 45 systems are the same ATO systems re-categorized as high-impact in August 2021 (OIG IT2021033). Exhibit C — the specific system names — is SSI-redacted in the public report; the names cannot be confirmed without need-to-know access. The OIG groups them into four NAS operational capability buckets: Automation, Communication, Navigation & Weather, and Surveillance. Illustrative examples include STARS, ERAM, VSCS, and SWIM-class systems. The 5/2 brief is live at the link above.

What's new since 5/2: No new OIG or Congressional action has dropped in the last 3 days. However, the macro procurement environment has materially developed in the weeks around the report — specifically the FAA NAS Cyber market survey and a post-quantum cryptography RFI — both of which are strong BD signals addressed below.

What Has Moved Since the 5/2 Brief

No new OIG action since April 1. The following items represent the broader FAA cybersecurity procurement and policy environment confirmed since the report's publication.

  • May 2026
    Varun's Follow-Up — This Brief
    Status-check email received May 5. Attachments question resolved (Exhibit C is SSI-redacted; 45 systems confirmed from OIG text). This status update published.
  • Apr 24, 2026
    FAA Post-Quantum Cryptography RFI Closes New
    FAA issued an RFI for enterprise-wide PQC migration covering NAS and business IT systems — a direct downstream from the OIG's NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 findings. Vendors with PQC architecture capability were solicited. Acquisition strategy in development. Source: GovCon Wire, Apr 7, 2026.
  • Apr 10, 2026
    FDD Analysis: "FAA Delinquent in Cybersecurity Practices" New Coverage
    Foundation for Defense of Democracies published policy-level analysis recommending Congress require a status briefing from FAA on cyber threat management process implementation under the FAA Reauthorization Act of 2024. Amplifies legislative pressure. Source: FDD, Apr 10, 2026.
  • Apr 10, 2026
    FAA NAS Cyber Market Survey Closes Key BD Signal
    Sources-sought notice for "NAS Cyber Information Security and Operations" closed April 10. Scope: penetration testing, risk assessments, vulnerability evaluations, incident response, threat hunting, cybersecurity architecture, OT/ICS/SCADA. NAICS 541519. This is the procurement vehicle that will fund the remediation work flagged in FI2026023. Source: FAA Market Survey PDF, Mar 11, 2026; ExecutiveGov, Mar 12, 2026.
  • Apr 1, 2026
    OIG FI2026023 Published — All 4 Recs, FAA Concurred
    Baseline event. FAA concurred with all recommendations; Dec 31, 2026 implementation target. Full analysis in 5/2 brief.
  • Mar 11, 2026
    FAA NAS Cyber Market Survey Published
    Pre-RFP market survey signaling NAS cybersecurity work coming to contract. Predates the OIG report by 3 weeks — FAA was already preparing to procure this work.
  • Feb 10, 2026
    FAA ATC Overhaul: Peraton $1.5B Contract Disclosed
    FAA's $12.5B ATC modernization program structure disclosed. Program Manager Peraton (Veritas Capital) at $1.5B. "No Excuses" performance clauses. 612 radar systems by June 2028 at $1.1B. RTX $420M. Cybersecurity is embedded as a continuous requirement across all modernization phases. Source: Reuters, Feb 10, 2026.
  • Aug 2021
    IT2021033 — Original OIG Accountability Finding
    FAA recategorized 45 systems as high-impact. OIG found FAA not enforcing remediation. FI2026023 (2026) is the 4.5-year follow-up finding insufficient progress.
4.5-Year Accountability Gap Confirmed: The 2026 OIG report is a direct follow-up to IT2021033 (Aug 2021). FAA was given years to fix these gaps. As of April 1, 2026: 15 of 45 systems still on NIST Rev 4, 1,836 of 16,245 controls not implemented, 38 of 45 SSPs outdated, and vulnerabilities tracked in SMART (internal) rather than CSAM (DOT system of record). The FDD analysis explicitly asks whether Congress has assigned cybersecurity authority to an agency equipped to exercise it.

Changeis Positioning — OIG-Flagged Systems vs. Our Capabilities

The OIG identified four remediation areas. ATEPS II (SDB1 vehicle 693KA925D00004, $114.7M, AJM-4 Surveillance Services) is the primary task order vehicle. The NAS Cyber market survey creates an additional independent contract path.
