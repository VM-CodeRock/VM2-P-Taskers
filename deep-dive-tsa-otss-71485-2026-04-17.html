Executive Summary

TSA's Operational Test and Evaluation Support Services (OTSS) contract is a follow-on recompete of the incumbent Blanket Purchase Agreement held by Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC) (BPA #70T04022A7672N001, awarded September 2022, ceiling $150M, ordering period through September 2027). The new Indefinite Delivery Contract (IDC) is estimated at over $100M over 5 years (September 2027–September 2032), with an anticipated solicitation release of March 1, 2027.


OTSS is the most operationally intensive of the four TSA T&E cluster contracts — it deploys test personnel to real airport locations nationwide, conducts agency-wide field demonstrations, and produces the independent OT&E assessments that gate procurement decisions for every major Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) acquisition. The program spans checkpoint screening, checked baggage, credential authentication, explosives detection, cybersecurity testing, and human factors evaluation across the full TSA enterprise.


SAIC's incumbent position is formidable — consecutive awards since 2020 ($40M BPA), 2022 ($150M BPA), and strong performance data — but the shift to an open IDC vehicle and the cluster-wide recompete dynamic create a genuine competitive opening. The RAG match score of 0.7097 confirms strong alignment on T&E methodology, data collection/analysis, and systems engineering via Changeis's FAA/Volpe portfolio. The critical gap is direct operational airport testing and cyber resilience / human factors SME depth — addressable through teaming.


This is a Full & Open competition — no set-aside. The recommended posture is GO — with a teaming strategy, positioning Changeis as prime for program management, data analytics, and systems engineering, with specialized subcontractors covering airport field testing operations and cyber/HSI evaluation.

Program Context: TSA Test & Evaluation Division (TED) and OT&E Mission

The TSA Test and Evaluation Division (TED) is TSA's independent test agency, responsible for conducting operationally realistic testing of Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) before deployment at airports nationwide. TED's OT&E mission has four pillars:


Operational Testing (OT) — OTSS Scope: Validates that TSE systems operate effectively, safely, and suitably under real-world conditions at actual airport locations. This is TSA's final vetting gate before full procurement decisions. Unlike Developmental Testing (DTSS) or Acceptance Testing (ATSS), Operational Testing deploys to live airports with real TSOs and actual passenger flows.


Key Technology Areas Evaluated by OTSS:

  • Credential Authentication Technology (CAT): Biometric and ID verification systems at checkpoints — a high-growth area as TSA expands CAT-2 deployment
  • Explosives Detection Systems (EDS): CT-based checked baggage screening; real-world Pd/Pfa performance validation
  • Checkpoint Screening: Advanced Imaging Technology (AIT), millimeter wave, automated screening lanes, prohibited items detection
  • Cyber Resilience Testing: OT cybersecurity assessments of checkpoint systems at multiple domestic airport locations — a growing OTSS requirement distinct from DTSS
  • Human Systems Integration (HSI): Human factors evaluation of TSO interactions with new equipment — usability, alarm resolution, cognitive workload assessment
  • Alarm Resolution: Checkpoint and checked baggage alarm resolution performance under operational conditions

OTSS vs. DTSS Distinction (Critical for Strategy): DTSS operates primarily at the TSA Systems Integration Facility (TSIF) in a controlled lab environment. OTSS deploys to real airports — requiring test personnel with airport access authorizations, TSO coordination protocols, airport authority liaisons, and the operational security posture needed to work in live sterile areas. This is a fundamentally more logistically complex and field-intensive contract than DTSS. A credible OTSS proposal must demonstrate airport operations experience.

TSA Qualification Process — OT Phase (Step 7): In TSA's 8-step TSE Qualification Process, the OTSS contractor executes Step 7: Initial OT&E (IOT&E) at field sites. This is preceded by DTSS qualification testing (TSIF) and followed by DHS Letter of Assessment and Full Rate Production decision. OTSS sits at the critical go/no-go gate for every TSE procurement decision.


Scope Expansion Signals:

  • Cybersecurity testing at airports — SAIC has been conducting this since 2022; scope expected to grow given rising cyber threats to airport infrastructure
  • Detection dog evaluation logistics at 60+ airports (CONUS, Hawaii, Puerto Rico, Alaska) — SAIC manages logistics; scope likely to continue
  • Cloud-based enterprise system testing — TSA enterprise IT OT&E in coordination with DHS OCIO and NOSC
  • DHS-wide OT&E support — contract is described as "agency-wide" suggesting scope beyond TSA to other DHS components
