Model & Product Releases

OpenAI GPT-5.5 ("Spud") — Now Shipping, API Rollout Ongoing

OpenAI Model Release

Released April 23 and continuing to ramp through April 30, GPT-5.5 (codenamed "Spud") is OpenAI's first fully retrained base model since GPT-4.5. The model is purpose-built for agentic coding and long-horizon, multi-step workflows. OpenAI co-founder Greg Brockman described it as "a new class of intelligence" that executes complex tasks with less user instruction. On OpenAI's proprietary GDPVal benchmark, GPT-5.5 outperformed or tied human workers on ~85% of benchmarked tasks. It is available in ChatGPT and Codex for paid subscribers; API access is being staged to allow implementation of additional cybersecurity guardrails. NVIDIA confirmed its newest chips reduce the cost of running models like GPT-5.5 by up to 35× per token.

Microsoft Copilot Hits 20M Paid Seats; AI Run Rate $37B/yr

Microsoft Product

Microsoft's Q3 FY2026 earnings (quarter ended March 31) disclosed that Microsoft 365 Copilot now exceeds 20 million paid seats, up from 15 million in January 2026 — representing approximately 4.4% of the 450 million commercial user base. Microsoft's AI annual revenue run rate reached $37 billion, a 123% increase year-over-year. The company also disclosed that token throughput per GPU for GPT-4.1 and GPT-5 improved by 30%+ in the quarter through hardware-software co-optimization. Microsoft simultaneously ended its exclusive cloud arrangement with OpenAI — any cloud provider can now use OpenAI models, ending years of Azure exclusivity.

Anthropic Claude Mythos Preview — Classified, Glasswing-Only Access

Anthropic Security

Anthropic's highest-profile model release remains the Claude Mythos Preview (announced April 7), which identified thousands of previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser. Access remains restricted to the 40+ organizations in Project Glasswing (a consortium including AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorgan, Microsoft, NVIDIA). Public release is indefinitely delayed due to offensive risk — Anthropic's policy team met with the Trump administration on April 13 to discuss Mythos deployment constraints. This is the most capable AI security tool ever demonstrated; its restricted availability is itself a strategic market dynamic.

Enterprise & Infrastructure

Alphabet Q1: Google Cloud Surpasses $20B for First Time, Backlog Hits $460B

Alphabet / Google Enterprise

Alphabet reported Q1 2026 revenue of $109.9 billion, beating estimates of $107.2 billion, driven by a 63% surge in Google Cloud revenue to $20.03 billion — the first quarter above $20B. Google Cloud's operating margin expanded to 32.9%, tripling year-over-year operating income. Critically, Alphabet disclosed that its Google Cloud backlog nearly doubled quarter-over-quarter, reaching over $460 billion — the largest contracted AI pipeline of any hyperscaler. Revenue from products built on Google's generative AI models grew nearly 800% year-over-year. Alphabet's capex was $35.7 billion for the quarter. The company raised its quarterly dividend by 5% and issued $31 billion in senior notes to fund further AI infrastructure.

Microsoft Azure Grows 40%, AI Capex Projected at $190B for 2026

Microsoft Infrastructure

Microsoft's quarterly earnings exceeded expectations across all segments, with Azure growing 40% year-over-year — ahead of the company's own guidance. CFO Amy Hood projected full-year 2026 capex at $190 billion, a 61% increase from 2025, noting that a global squeeze on AI memory components will add ~$25B to infrastructure costs alone. Microsoft's remaining performance obligations (contracted backlog) reached $627 billion. The company disclosed that Azure is running at near-full capacity and demand continues to exceed supply. Microsoft ended its exclusive partnership with OpenAI: any cloud provider can now run OpenAI models, though Microsoft retains an IP license through 2032. Azure's AI annual run rate hit $37 billion, up 123%.

Meta Raises 2026 AI CapEx Forecast to $125–$145 Billion

Meta Infrastructure

Meta's Q1 2026 results showed revenue of $56.3 billion (+33% YoY) with capital expenditures of $19.84 billion for the quarter. More significantly, Meta raised its full-year 2026 CapEx forecast to $125–145 billion, up from the prior $115–135 billion range, citing higher component pricing and additional data center costs. Total 2026 expenses guidance remains $162–169 billion. Meta's Family of Apps segment saw operating income of $26.9 billion at a 45%+ margin, while Reality Labs continued to lose $4 billion per quarter. The stock initially declined after-hours on the elevated spending forecast despite the strong revenue beat.

OpenAI Stargate Faces Headwinds: 50% of 2026 Data Center Capacity Delayed

OpenAI Infrastructure

According to a Financial Times report highlighted April 29, nearly 50% of AI data centers planned for 2026 have been delayed or scrapped, including core components of OpenAI's $500 billion Stargate project. OpenAI is reportedly rethinking its infrastructure strategy — moving toward a more flexible, partnership-based model rather than pure owned capacity. The Abilene, Texas flagship remains on track, but European Stargate projects in the UK and Norway were previously withdrawn. Industry analysis from Omdia projects 30–50% of planned 2026 data center capacity will slip to 2028 due to structural shortages of electricity, copper, and cooling gases. OpenAI also published a blog on April 29 detailing continued Stargate scaling with new data center capacity additions.

Scout AI Raises $100M to Build Military Foundation Model "Fury"

Defense AI Funding

Scout AI, a defense-focused AI startup founded in 2024 by Colby Adcock and Collin Otis, announced a $100M Series A — described as the largest defense-tech Series A in U.S. history — led by Align Ventures and Draper Associates. The company is developing "Fury," a Vision Language Action (VLA) foundation model purpose-built for unmanned warfare. Scout has $11 million in DARPA and DoD contracts and is one of 20 autonomy companies used by the U.S. Army's 1st Cavalry Division at Fort Hood. The company declines to identify which hyperscaler LLMs underpin Fury. Deployment in the field is targeted for 2027. The raise signals the emergence of a distinct defense AI vertical with different technical requirements from commercial AI — real-world sensor grounding, adversarial robustness, and autonomous weapons command chains.

Nvidia Sustains $5 Trillion Market Cap; Q1 Hyperscaler Capex Tops $650B

Chips Infrastructure

Nvidia remains the world's most valuable company, sustaining its $5 trillion market cap milestone achieved April 24. The company's Vera Rubin NVL72 rack-scale systems, deployed in partnership with Google (A5X bare-metal instances), deliver up to 10× lower inference cost per token and 10× higher throughput per megawatt than prior-generation systems. With Big Tech collectively committing ~$650B to AI infrastructure in 2026, Nvidia remains the primary beneficiary of the compute supercycle. A global squeeze on AI memory components — cited by Microsoft CFO Amy Hood — reflects the magnitude of demand pressure on the semiconductor supply chain.

Apple at ICLR 2026: ParaRNN Achieves 665× Speedup for Large-Scale RNN Training

Apple's research at ICLR 2026 (presentations ongoing this week) includes ParaRNN, a framework that enables parallel training of nonlinear RNNs with a 665× speedup over sequential approaches — enabling the first 7-billion-parameter classical RNNs competitive with transformers. This matters because RNNs offer dramatically more efficient inference than attention-based architectures, which could enable high-capability models on resource-constrained devices. Apple also presented MANZANO, a unified multimodal model reducing the performance trade-off between image understanding and generation.

Research Trend to Watch Multiple papers this week converge on the same theme: reducing the cost and latency of AI inference without sacrificing capability. Enterprises that invest now in inference-efficient architectures will benefit disproportionately as these techniques mature.

Regulatory & Policy

Musk v. OpenAI Trial — Musk Returns to Witness Stand Day 2 (April 30)

Legal OpenAI

The landmark civil trial pitting Elon Musk against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman entered its second day of testimony on April 30 in Oakland, California. Musk returned to the witness stand, continuing to argue that his ~$38 million in charitable donations established a trust requiring OpenAI to remain a nonprofit. Musk is seeking up to $134 billion in damages and requesting Altman's removal. On day 1, Musk testified that OpenAI was created as a nonprofit counterbalance to Google, and that Larry Page called him "a speciesist for being pro-human." OpenAI's defense maintains no specific promises were made regarding its structure. The jury of nine will decide liability; the judge will determine remedies. OpenAI is currently valued at $852 billion and has completed its for-profit restructuring. The case will determine whether AI lab founders can be held to early-stage nonprofit commitments as organizations scale commercially — a precedent with broad implications for the sector.

EU AI Act Delay: High-Risk AI Deadline Pushed to December 2027

EU Regulation

As of April 2026, the EU AI Act's full enforcement for high-risk AI systems has been pushed back to December 2, 2027 — from the original August 2026 date — after the European Parliament voted 569 to approve the Digital Omnibus proposal. Existing enforceable obligations remain in place: prohibited AI practices (since February 2025), GPAI model obligations (since August 2025), and AI literacy requirements for staff are all currently active. Colorado's SB24-205, the first U.S. state law targeting algorithmic discrimination in consequential AI decisions (housing, employment, healthcare), takes effect June 30, 2026 — now 61 days away.

White House AI Framework and State-Level Patchwork

U.S. Policy

The White House National Policy Framework for AI (released March 20) recommends Congress preempt state AI laws that impose "undue burdens," aiming for a unified federal standard. 45 states have already introduced 1,561 AI-related bills. California Governor Newsom issued Executive Order N-5-26 (March 30) directing state agencies to draft AI safety requirements for companies doing business with state government. Colorado's law remains on track for June 30, requiring risk management policies and impact assessments for high-risk AI deployers. Enterprises operating in multiple states face a rapidly fragmenting compliance landscape — with or without federal preemption.

Strategic Implications for Changeis

1. Hyperscaler Earnings Validate Enterprise AI Adoption — Changeis Is in the Right Window

Alphabet's report that gen AI product revenue grew 800% YoY and that Google Cloud's backlog nearly doubled confirms the enterprise adoption wave is accelerating, not decelerating. Changeis clients who delay AI integration risk a widening capability gap against peers who are already seeing productivity gains. This is the moment to accelerate delivery of AI transformation engagements, not build additional business cases.

2. Microsoft's OpenAI Exclusivity End Opens Multi-Cloud AI Strategies

Microsoft's decision to end exclusive OpenAI model access means enterprises can now access GPT-5.5 and future OpenAI models via any cloud. This is immediately actionable for Changeis: cloud-agnostic AI architecture recommendations become more valuable, and vendor lock-in risk assessments for clients using Azure-only AI stacks are now warranted. Multi-model, multi-cloud AI strategies are now the defensible approach.

3. GPT-5.5 Enterprise Readiness — Monitor API Rollout for Codex Integration

GPT-5.5's 60% hallucination reduction and 85% human-task parity on GDPVal make it immediately relevant for Changeis engagements involving knowledge work automation and agentic workflows. However, doubled API pricing requires TCO recalculation against GPT-5.4 Pro and Claude Opus 4.7 for each use case. Recommend establishing benchmark comparisons for Changeis' primary client workflows before broad adoption.

4. Defense AI Vertical Emerging — Assess Relevant Client Exposure

Scout AI's $100M raise (largest defense-tech Series A in U.S. history) and its validation by DARPA, Army Applications Laboratory, and the 1st Cavalry Division signal that defense AI is now an institutionalized investment category, not a pilot phase. For Changeis clients with defense or national security adjacencies, AI governance frameworks and responsible AI policies for autonomous systems are now relevant advisory opportunities.

5. Colorado AI Law — June 30 Compliance Deadline Requires Client Action Now

With 61 days until Colorado SB24-205 takes effect, Changeis clients deploying AI in consequential decisions (hiring, lending, healthcare) in Colorado need immediate risk assessment and compliance planning. The law requires risk management policies, initial and annual impact assessments, and consumer adverse-decision notices. An affirmative defense is available for organizations that adopt the NIST AI Risk Management Framework — Changeis should accelerate NIST RMF advisory offerings.

6. Inference Cost Compression Ahead — Revisit AI Build/Buy Economics

NVIDIA's Vera Rubin systems (delivering 10× lower inference cost per token) and Apple's ParaRNN framework (665× training speedup for RNNs) are converging signals: AI inference will continue to get cheaper at the compute layer. Changeis should update client AI economics models to reflect declining per-token costs in 2026–2027, which changes the ROI calculus for high-volume AI applications that may have previously appeared marginal.
