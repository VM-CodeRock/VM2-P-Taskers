1 · Executive Summary

USCIS is renewing its annual GovDelivery software subscription — a brand-name Granicus cloud platform that powers automated email distribution for two USCIS mission systems: the Customer Service Web Portal (CSWP) and E-Verify. The renewal is a direct, sole-source-style follow-on to existing contract 70SBUR25F00000112, held by incumbent DH Technologies Inc., a HUBZone-certified IT value-added reseller (VAR) based in Leesburg, VA.

This is not a professional services contract. It is a commercial software subscription resale — a pass-through buy where a NASA SEWP V contract holder procures a Granicus SaaS license on behalf of USCIS. The contract vehicle is NASA SEWP V (IDIQ for IT products and product-based services), and the set-aside is HUBZone Small Business. Solicitation is expected June 10, 2026, with award by June 30, 2026 and a period of performance July 1, 2026 through June 30, 2027.

The RAG score of 0.663 (STRONG) is a false positive. All top matches surface because Changeis's DOT/Volpe task orders referenced GovDelivery/Granicus as a tool used (e-blast newsletter platforms), not because Changeis is a GovDelivery reseller or software subscription vendor. The capability match is a corpus keyword overlap, not a business model fit.

Bottom line: Changeis is NOT a NASA SEWP V contract holder, is NOT a software/SaaS reseller, and does NOT have a Granicus authorized reseller relationship. There is no path to prime this contract. This opportunity should be archived as a NO-GO and flagged as a RAG false-positive for future calibration.

Scope Summary: USCIS requires renewal of its annual software subscription to GovDelivery (a Granicus product) to support automated email distribution for the Customer Service Web Portal (CSWP) and E-Verify programs. GovDelivery is a FedRAMP-authorized cloud communications platform used by federal agencies to send subscriber email notifications at scale — it is a commercial SaaS product, not a custom-developed system. The USCIS requirement is estimated at 2–5 million dollars annually, reflecting the volume of email subscribers across CSWP and E-Verify, likely hundreds of thousands to millions of contacts.

Product Context: Granicus and GovDelivery merged in 2016 under Vista Equity Partners backing, creating the largest cloud solutions provider to government in North America. GovDelivery is now marketed as Granicus's federal communications suite. Granicus explicitly lists NASA SEWP and GSA IT Schedule 70 as its primary federal procurement vehicles, which explains why USCIS is using SEWP V for this buy. A HUBZone SEWP V reseller holds the license on behalf of the agency — a standard indirect software procurement model.

Key Structural Point: This is a brand-name software subscription renewal. USCIS is not selecting a service provider — it is purchasing a Granicus product license through an authorized SEWP V reseller. The award will go to whichever HUBZone SEWP V contract holder can offer the Granicus GovDelivery subscription at the best price. Technical evaluation criteria are minimal or zero; price is the primary (often only) evaluation factor for a brand-name reseller buy.

4 · Competitive Landscape — SEWP V Reseller Market

Granicus markets GovDelivery specifically through SEWP V and GSA Schedule 70 — meaning any authorized SEWP V contract holder with a Granicus/GovDelivery reseller relationship can compete for this renewal.

Incumbent — DH Technologies Inc.: The current contract holder is DH Technologies Inc. (CAGE 6XKC9, UEI NKC2AB3ESFP5), a HUBZone-certified IT VAR based in Leesburg, VA, founded 2013. DH Technologies holds a SEWP V Group C contract (NNG15SC70B, NAICS 541519, HUBZone) and is an experienced value-added reseller in virtualization, data center, and IT infrastructure. Their SEWP V contract was initially awarded in 2015 with an option period exercised in 2020. DH Technologies's current SEWP V contract page notes it was set to expire October 31, 2025 — creating a potential vehicle continuity question relevant to whether they bid the June 2026 solicitation under SEWP V or a successor vehicle.

A HUBZone SEWP V holder that is a Granicus authorized reseller (directly or through Carahsoft) can legitimately bid this renewal at the Granicus list price plus their margin. The 0.34% SEWP surcharge applies. This is a highly transactional, commodity procurement — the winner typically has the best Granicus pricing and the right vehicle/set-aside certifications.

Teaming Note: Even if Changeis were to team with a SEWP V holder, there is no meaningful professional services component in this contract for Changeis to perform.
