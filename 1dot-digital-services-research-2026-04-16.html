Scope & Technical Areas

1DOT Digital Services is DOT's next-generation department-wide IT services vehicle, establishing a modernized acquisition framework to support the "1DOT IT" strategy announced by Secretary Duffy in August 2025. The vehicle replaces siloed legacy procurement with shared, enterprise-level digital platforms across all DOT Operating Administrations (excluding FAA).

The vehicle provides specialized services across the following domains:

  • Software Engineering & Agile Development — Custom development, application modernization, DevSecOps, CI/CD pipelines
  • Cloud-Native Architecture — Cloud migration, infrastructure-as-code, containerization (Kubernetes, Docker)
  • AI/ML Solutions — Machine learning model development, data science, intelligent automation
  • Digital Platforms & UX — Human-centered design, digital design systems, front-end engineering
  • Mission-Specific Applications — Operating administration-specific app development and maintenance
  • Shared Enterprise Services — Cross-modal platform delivery, enterprise data management, cybersecurity integration
Strategic Context: 1DOT IT Initiative

The "1DoT" initiative launched August 2025 by Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy aims to consolidate DOT's 425+ information systems — many with overlapping roles — into shared enterprise platforms. DOT's CDIO Pavan Pidugu outlined a four-goal strategy:

  • Eliminate technical debt
  • Become customer-centric
  • Build obsessive products
  • Grow talent

The department is establishing four "Digital Factories" to deliver mission-focused software products, alongside centralized shared services (cybersecurity, data, cloud, infrastructure). This vehicle funds the contractor workforce supporting those factories.

In September 2025, DOT signed a $89M, 5-year contract with Google to migrate all 55,000 employees to Google Workspace (Gemini AI included), completing the initial wave within 22 days — signaling the pace of transformation this vehicle must support.
