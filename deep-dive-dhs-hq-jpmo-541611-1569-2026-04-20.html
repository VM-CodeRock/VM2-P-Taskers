1. Executive Summary

Recommendation: CONDITIONAL GO. This is a well-aligned, high-value recompete at a known DHS customer. The RAG engine's top hit is a direct DHS/CBP analog (0.7442), confirming strong past-performance relevance. The incumbent (Cognosante / EIS — now an Accenture Federal Services company) faces a disadvantage from the Accenture Federal acquisition, and the new contract will procured under a BPA Call structure rather than Alliant II, potentially widening the competitive field. Changeis should pursue this aggressively with a teaming strategy that combines its program management and IT advisory credentials with a partner holding DHS or Federal financial systems engineering depth.

The DHS OCFO Financial Systems Modernization Joint Program Management Office (FSM JPMO) is recompeting its portfolio-level support contract after a five-year run under Cognosante CDS (now part of Accenture Federal Services). The follow-on contract spans July 2026 through July 2030 with an estimated value of $50M–$100M on a Labor Hour basis. The JPMO coordinates all FSM activities across DHS Component agencies — including programs such as FSM Trio (TSA, USCG, CWMD) and FSM Cube (ICE, USCIS, CISA, S&T) — modernizing financial, procurement, and asset management systems toward a unified Oracle Federal Financials solution.

The scope demands a broad multi-disciplinary team: IT software engineering, functional/technical testing, business process re-engineering, change management, IT portfolio management, governance/compliance, and administrative support. The BPA Call vehicle structure (FAR Part 8 or 13) indicates the government may leverage an existing Multiple Award Schedule or BPA — this is a different mechanism than the prior Alliant II delivery order, suggesting a new competition path that Changeis must evaluate for vehicle eligibility. The solicitation release is estimated at March 23, 2026, with award in Q4 FY2026.
