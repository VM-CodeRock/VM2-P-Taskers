The TSA Transportation Security Operations Center (TSOC) operates WebEOC as a Mission Essential System (MES) and High Value Asset (HVA), classified FIPS 199 High — the most stringent federal impact category. The system performs incident management, coordination, and situational awareness across all transportation modes. TSA requires migration of the existing on-premises deployment to a WebEOC FedRAMP High SaaS cloud solution compliant with NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5, while preserving all existing features, data, workflows, reports, interconnections, and the mobile application. The new platform must support FIPS 140-2 encryption and maintain the system's ATO status throughout migration.

Vehicle Analysis — NASA SEWP V

NASA SEWP V is one of the federal government's largest governmentwide acquisition contracts (GWACs) for ICT products and product-based services, with a $60 billion ceiling on the upcoming SEWP VI iteration. It is currently extended to September 30, 2026 while SEWP VI evaluation completes (per Nextgov/FCW, March 2026).

In Scope

What SEWP V Allows

  • Commercial ICT products (hardware, software, SaaS subscriptions)
  • Product-based services (installation, migration, configuration, maintenance tied to a product)
  • FedRAMP cloud SaaS licenses + hosting
  • Engineering services tied to an ICT product
  • Help desk and O&M tied to a product
Juvare UCP SaaS license qualifies
Out of Scope

What SEWP V Does NOT Allow

  • General IT support services (no product anchor)
  • Custom software development from scratch
  • General business analysis or process consulting
  • Pure labor-hour staff augmentation
Migration services must be product-anchored

Vehicle implication for Changeis: Changeis must hold a NASA SEWP V contract (Group B or C) to prime this effort, or partner with a SEWP V contract holder as a teaming prime. The Juvare UCP SaaS is the product anchor — all migration, configuration, data migration, and O&M services can ride on SEWP V as "product-based services." Confirm Changeis SEWP V status before investing further BD resources.

WebEOC / Juvare Incumbent Landscape

Incumbent Product Vendor: Juvare LLC (formerly ESi Acquisition, Inc.) / WebEOC

WebEOC is a Commercial Off-the-Shelf (COTS) web-based emergency management system originally developed by ESi Acquisition, which was acquired by Juvare. It is the dominant incident command and EOC management platform in U.S. federal, state, and local government. TSA has used WebEOC since at least 2008 per a 2008 TSA Privacy Impact Assessment filed with EPIC.org.

Sole Source StatusJuvare/ESi is the only authorized provider of WebEOC licenses and UCP development. A 2019 SAM.gov sole source notice confirms no third party can provide the software or maintenance. CBP issued a sole source to Juvare/ESi in 2024 under the same rationale.
Prior TSA ContractsTSA has operated WebEOC on-premises through TSOC. The current migration is a new requirement, not a recompete of an existing contract.
Incumbent IntegratorDev Technology Group holds the TSA FAST 2.5 BPA ($170M, 5-year, awarded April 2024) for agile software development services for TSA Law Enforcement/FAMS. They are the most likely existing systems integrator touching WebEOC workflows.
CBP Parallel PrecedentDHS CBP executed a nearly identical on-premises to FedRAMP High UCP cloud migration in 2024. That contract went sole source to Juvare/ESi, confirming the pattern: licenses + migration services bundled to Juvare or a Juvare-authorized partner.

Sole source risk: Based on the CBP precedent and Juvare's proprietary UCP platform, TSA may award this as a sole source to Juvare/ESi or a Juvare-authorized integrator. The APFS listing as "New Requirement, No Contract" with a competitive designation is encouraging, but the product dependency creates a de facto constraint. Watch for a J&A (Justification & Approval) or pre-solicitation notice on SAM.gov that would confirm or deny competitive intent.
