1. Executive Summary

The FAA is issuing a 5-year, 2-month Task Order under the ITIPSS IDIQ contract vehicle for labor support services backing its FAA Cloud Services (FCS) program — one of the most consequential cloud IT modernization programs in the federal government. The FCS 1.0 contract migrated 200+ applications to AWS and Azure; FCS 2.0 now expands to five cloud service providers. This TO funds the human labor that makes all of that work happen.

OST Global Solutions (OST) — the ITIPSS IDIQ prime contractor and the prime for this team — is assembling a multi-partner team to compete. Paul Huang, VP at OST, has invited Changeis to contribute as a subcontractor and has asked for Changeis's team inputs by April 6, 2026 COB.

The Decision Varun Must Make Should Changeis formally commit to pursue this as a sub to OST? If yes, which labor categories and capabilities should Changeis claim, and who are the key personnel? What differentiators should Changeis put forward in OST's response?

This brief synthesizes all 8 TORP documents, maps Changeis capabilities against the requirements, assesses the competitive landscape, and provides a Go/No-Go recommendation with next steps.

5. Evaluation Strategy — How to Win

Factor 1: Technical Approach (Most Important)

The FAA will evaluate Offerors on three specific subtasks in the Technical factor response: (i) Subtask 2.1 – AIF-410 Technical Support, (ii) Subtask 2.2 – AIF-410 Financial Management Support, and (iii) Subtask 3.1 – AIF-420 Technical Support. Subcontractor contributions are evaluated equally with prime. This is the highest-leverage place for Changeis to add value.

For each subtask, the evaluator wants to see: specific methodologies, management approach, QA procedures, and "innovative solutions or value-added services that set your approach apart." Generic statements will result in Low Confidence.

What OST Needs From Changeis for Factor 1 Demonstrate specific past performance examples for Subtask 2.1 (cloud architecture/ops), 2.2 (financial management with metrics), and 3.1 (operations management). Connect CLMRS financial management to the BPA burn rate tracking requirement. Show tooling or accelerators Changeis can bring.

Factor 2: Key Personnel (Cloud Program Manager)

Only 1 Key Personnel position is required: Cloud Program Manager – SME (IDIQ Labor ID #0#1DCES00L02BG, located FAA HQ Washington DC / Remote). Requirements:

  • Bachelor's in STEM or related (required); Master's or Ph.D. preferred
  • 10 years relevant experience, with at least 5 years providing technical/management leadership on cloud-based programs
  • PMP or equivalent certification (required); PgMP preferred
  • High Confidence = meets all minimum requirements AND exceeds at least one preferred qualification
  • Low Confidence = fails to meet one or more requirements → automatic exclusion

If Changeis is proposing the Cloud Program Manager, the candidate must have PMP (minimum) and 10 years experience including 5 in cloud program leadership. A PgMP certification or Master's degree would push the rating from "Some" to "High Confidence."

Factor 3: Price (Less Important, But Matters)

Price is significantly less important than technical merit, but as technical scores converge, price importance rises. Changeis's competitive labor rates as a small business can strengthen OST's price position vs. large-business competitors. Labor rates must equal or be below IDIQ ceiling rates for each Labor ID Number.

Paul Huang's Ask — Unique Capabilities, Differentiators

Paul needs from Changeis: "1-3 distinct capabilities (unique tools, methods, accelerators, processes), short description of what makes Changeis different, real examples/proof points with metrics."

1. FAA Financial Ecosystem Fluency: Changeis has operated within FAA's financial systems (PRISM, FAST, FAA Financial Manual) on contracts like CLMRS. This directly reduces onboarding risk for Subtask 2.2 — the contractor managing FCS 2.0 BPA finances must understand FAA's procurement and funding lifecycle, not just cloud cost management tools.

2. Proven FAA IT Modernization PMO: CLMRS involved managing a $62M transformation program — complex multi-year IT system integration with monthly financial reporting, stakeholder coordination, and FAA acquisition compliance. This is a direct analog to Task 1 PMO requirements.

3. Agile Program Management with FAA Context: If Changeis has PMP/SAFe-certified PMs with FAA background, this addresses the Project Manager – Senior (Agile) LCAT (Subtask 2.1) — especially the requirement for FAA multi-system software familiarity (Microsoft Office Suite, SharePoint, Adobe).
