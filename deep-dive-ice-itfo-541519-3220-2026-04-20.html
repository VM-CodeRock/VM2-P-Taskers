1. Executive Summary

ICE's Management & Administration / Chief Information Officer (M&A/CIO) component is standing up a brand-new, single-award BPA to supply its Information Technology Field Operations (ITFO) division with Cisco networking gear, Schneider Electric UPS/battery-backup systems, and structured cabling products across every ICE office nationwide — CONUS (including Alaska, Hawaii) plus U.S. territories. The $50M–$100M, five-year FFP vehicle is a new requirement with no incumbent, set aside for small businesses, and is projected to release solicitation June 1, 2026 with award by July 30, 2026.

This is a pure product reseller/logistics BPA — not an IT services engagement. The core differentiator is having authorized reseller status for Cisco, Schneider Electric, and ancillary brands, coupled with the logistics infrastructure to execute simultaneous multi-site deliveries at 400+ ICE field offices. White-glove delivery is required for certain UPS installations.

Changeis's RAG corpus yields a 0.6814 composite score against this opportunity, driven by past performance in IT technology deployment and delivery logistics. However, the score reflects topical adjacency (IT field delivery, hardware logistics) rather than direct past performance as an authorized Cisco/Schneider VAR. The key question for a Go decision is whether Changeis holds or can quickly obtain Cisco Partner / Schneider Electric reseller authorizations at the time of proposal.

Scope Summary

ICE requires a prime reseller contractor to supply and deliver three hardware categories to field offices nationwide:

  • Cisco network products — specific brand-name switches, routers, and networking appliances (exact SKUs per bill of materials)
  • Schneider Electric UPS / battery-backup systems — with some orders requiring white-glove delivery (unboxing, placement, connection)
  • Patch cables and structured cabling products — per ICE specifications

The contractor must be capable of simultaneous deliveries across multiple ICE office locations in CONUS and U.S. territories. A prior RFI (May 2024) also mentioned Juniper, Cradlepoint, Hewlett Packard, Tripp-Lite, and goTenna products — the formal solicitation may expand on the current Cisco/Schneider focus.

Key Procurement Signal: This is a new BPA with no incumbent — ICE published an RFI in May/June 2024 specifically to explore procurement strategy for ITFO hardware. The APFS record published February 26, 2026 represents the formalized forecast following that market research. Competition will be brand-new with no entrenched incumbent to displace.
