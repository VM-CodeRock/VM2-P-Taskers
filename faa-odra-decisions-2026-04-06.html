Notable Case Summaries

ODRA-25-965 — Protest of Science Applications International Corporation (Most Relevant to Changeis)

SAIC protested under Solicitation 693KA9-24-R-00005 — the ATEPS II procurement that also produced Changeis's $114.7M ATEPS II-SDB1 award. The FAA Product Team responded with a revised notice of voluntary corrective action on March 20, 2025, and moved to dismiss the protest as moot. Both the protester's counsel and the intervenor's counsel concurred. ODRA Director John A. Dietrich dismissed the case on March 21, 2025 under 14 C.F.R. § 17.19(c). The subsequent award of an ATEPS II Unrestricted IDIQ to SAIC in July 2025 (Award 693KA9-25-D-00008, ceiling $629.8M) indicates the corrective action was adding SAIC to the unrestricted track — not disturbing the SDB small-business awards including Changeis's contract.

ODRA-24-961 — Contract Dispute of Prow'ess Construction Corp. (Construction Facilities Case)

The most substantively decided recent case involved a construction contractor at the Indianapolis Air Route Traffic Control Center. Prow'ess sought $13,029 for COVID-19 suspension damage to roof board materials; ODRA denied the claim, ruling that the suspension of work notice did not constitute a constructive change order shifting material protection responsibility to the FAA. The FAA counterclaim for $113,395 in workmanship defects was partially granted — but only a nominal $1,000 contract price reduction was awarded because the Product Team failed to provide reliable evidence supporting its quantum. Decision by Director Dietrich December 3, 2024.

ODRA-25-964 — Contract Disputes of Diversified Management Solutions, Inc.

Two consolidated contract disputes (dockets 00972 and 00973) under contracts 6973GH-23-R-00057 and -00060 were jointly dismissed with prejudice by both parties on March 19, 2025. No public findings were issued. The joint dismissal with prejudice indicates a settlement or agreement between Diversified Management Solutions and the FAA Product Team.

ODRA-25-963 — Protest of Spatial Front, Inc. & ODRA-24-962 — Protest of Mythics, Inc. / Oracle America

Both of these protests were resolved without substantive adjudication. Spatial Front's protest (March 4, 2025) was dismissed as moot after the FAA filed a voluntary corrective action — mirroring the SAIC/ATEPS II pattern. The Mythics/Oracle protest (December 23, 2024) was dismissed without prejudice on a joint motion. These cases show a continued ODRA pattern of resolving protests through corrective action rather than contested adjudication.

Trend Analysis — Recent ODRA Activity
  • Corrective Action Is the Dominant Resolution Path. Three of five recent cases were resolved via voluntary corrective action by the FAA Product Team (ODRA-25-965, ODRA-25-963) or joint motions (ODRA-25-964, ODRA-24-962). Only ODRA-24-961 (Prow'ess) resulted in a substantive Findings and Recommendations with a full opinion. ODRA increasingly functions as a settlement catalyst rather than a contested forum.
  • Construction Facilities Disputes Persist. Prow'ess Construction has appeared in multiple recent ODRA cases (ODRA-24-961, ODRA-24-950). These small-dollar construction disputes (under $200K) reflect ongoing FAA facility maintenance litigation from the COVID-era suspension period.
  • Large IDIQ Competitions Draw Protests. The ATEPS II solicitation drew both the SAIC protest (ODRA-25-965) and at least one separate Noblis protest in 2022 under the predecessor SETIS contract. Major IDIQ recompetes attract sustained protest activity. Changeis should anticipate monitoring any future ATEPS II task order protests.
  • Director Dietrich Is the Sole Decision-Maker. All five recent orders were signed by John A. Dietrich, Director and Chief Administrative Judge. This single-DRO pattern on recent orders (vs. the prior multi-judge panel approach) may reflect staff capacity constraints at ODRA.
  • No New Cases Since March 2025 on Public Docket. The five cases listed span December 2024 – March 2025. As of April 2026, the page has not been updated with more recent decisions. This could indicate a quiet period, pending decisions not yet published, or a delay in public posting. Worth monitoring next week to confirm whether any April 2026 orders appear.
