USCG Training Center Cape May Modernization — Design & Construction
$400M
DHS / U.S. Coast Guard NAICS 236220 — Construction Awarded May 7, 2026 Awardee: Whiting Turner Contracting Co.

The U.S. Coast Guard awarded a contract valued at up to $400 million to Whiting Turner Contracting Company for the design and construction of new facilities at TRACEN Cape May, New Jersey — the largest shore construction award in Coast Guard history. Projects include: new recruit barracks (8,000 annually), demolition of abandoned steam trenches and foundations, temporary C5IT utilities, relocation of electrical infrastructure, demolition of James and Healy Halls, a new VIP/grandstand parade complex, a multi-purpose training facility (seamanship, indoor graduation, drill hall, indoor track), a new galley, and a modern fire station replacing 1920s infrastructure. Work is phased to minimize disruption to recruit training operations.

Changeis Relevance (Low-Direct / Watch): Primarily a construction award — not Changeis's primary domain. However, the facilities modernization includes C5IT (Command, Control, Communications, Computers, and Information Technology) connections and transition of 17 towers to electronic systems. Monitor for downstream IT support, network infrastructure, or program management task orders that may follow construction phases.
Source: USCG Press Release, May 7, 2026

DHS Enterprise (Multi-Agency) Equipment + Services — Two Technical Categories Solicitation: Aug 3, 2026 · Award: Q3 CY2026 Announced May 5–6, 2026

DHS is preparing the final solicitation for TACTICS (Tactical Communications and Technical Investigative Comprehensive Solutions), the successor to the $3B TACCOM II contract (awarded 2019, ~$1.8B obligated to date, sunset May 2027). DHS Contractors Face Months-Long Recovery from Record 75-Day Shutdown
Government Executive / Professional Services Council · May 1, 2026

The Professional Services Council warns it could take until year-end for DHS contractors to fully recover from the 75-day government shutdown that ended April 30, 2026. Cybersecurity and disaster-response contracts operated at reduced capacity; several smaller firms faced closure from missed reimbursements. DHS now has compressed time to obligate FY2026 funds. PSC is urging DHS to form a surge team to distribute back payments and interest owed. This is expected to accelerate second-half FY2026 contract activity as DHS rushes to obligate unspent funds before year-end.

Trump Fixed-Price Contracting EO: Implementation Timeline Begins (FAR Amendments Due August 2026)
Federal News Network / PilieroMazza · May 1–7, 2026

President Trump's April 30 Executive Order establishing fixed-price contracts as the federal default is moving into implementation. Agencies must justify non-FFP contracts in writing to agency heads; within 90 days, each agency must review and seek to renegotiate its 10 largest non-fixed-price contracts; FAR amendments are expected within 120 days (by late August 2026). Separately, the most consequential FAR changes in 40 years take effect June 30, 2026: the certified cost/pricing data threshold jumps from $2M to $10M; full CAS coverage threshold doubles from $50M to $100M. Federal contractors should review proposal strategies and pricing structures now.

IBM Security Executive Tom Parker Emerges as CISA Director Frontrunner
Nextgov/FCW · May 3–4, 2026

Tom Parker, a security services lead at IBM with 20+ years in cybersecurity, has emerged as the Trump administration's preferred choice to lead CISA. Parker founded Hubble (acquired 2024), is a World Economic Forum tech pioneer, and is a frequent Black Hat speaker. CISA has been without a permanent director since January 2025; prior nominee Sean Plankey withdrew April 30 after being caught in a dispute over USCG cutter contracts with a GOP senator. Acting Director Nick Andersen continues to lead the agency. A CISA director with strong private-sector cyber credentials could accelerate procurement of commercial cybersecurity tools and shift CISA's contracting posture toward industry-preferred acquisition vehicles.

FAA ATC Modernization Accelerates: AI Integration, 52 Vendors, 2028 Target
FedScoop / Eno Center for Transportation · April 21–24, 2026

The FAA's Brand New Air Traffic Control System (BNATCS) — a $12.5B overhaul with Peraton as $1.5B prime integrator — is showing early progress: 50% of copper wiring replaced with fiber, 270 radio sites converted, surface awareness systems at 54 airports, 17 towers converted to electronic flight strips. Peraton is deploying "agentic AI" to manage 1.5M+ records daily. The FAA is working with 52 vendors and plans customized AI software for traffic flow optimization. FAA Administrator Bedford confirmed a $200M cost reduction vs. initial proposal (enforced by a "No Excuses" clause). The 2028 deadline remains ambitious — creating sustained demand for IT services, program management, and integration subcontractors across the next 24 months.

TSA + CLEAR: Biometric eGates Deployed at 37 Airports; Full Rollout by Year-End 2026
The Points Guy / Biometric Update · February 25, 2026 (ongoing)

CLEAR has expanded biometric eGates to 37 U.S. airports — up from an initial 3-airport pilot — and confirmed full deployment to all CLEAR-enabled security checkpoints by end-2026, ahead of the FIFA World Cup 2026. TSA is simultaneously pursuing AI enhancements to X-ray screening and a DHS OIG review of TSA's facial recognition program. TSA's IT investment is growing rapidly: recent task orders include enterprise app O&M (Deloitte, $4.7M), FAMS modernization (Dev Technology, $4.1M), and Cisco/Fortinet network maintenance. A GAO-requested review by House Homeland Security Committee members of AI and biometric tools is expected to drive additional policy and procurement activity in H2 2026.
