Requirement Description

The TSA/OAPM seeks a contractor to provide all management, labor, personnel, and expertise for Integrated Test Support Services (ITSS) across four functional pillars:

  • Program Management: Resource allocation, reporting, scheduling, planning, data management, test coordination, and test logistics
  • Test Enablement & Software Development: Software development for data capture, software development for test analysis, engineering change request (ECR) preparation and review, and all activities enabling test execution
  • Test Execution & Reporting: Execution, assessing, and reporting of all integrated acceptance testing activities
  • Organizational Management: Establishing and maintaining a formal organization with clear lines of project authority; full responsibility for integration and coordination of all work across subsequent task orders under this IDIQ

This is a recompete of the existing BPA held by Analytical Services & Materials, Inc. (AS&M) under contract 70T04021A7672N003 (ceiling: $81M). TSA expects the new ITSS vehicle to expand scope and value to $50M–$100M. The contract is distinct from — but related to — SAIC's $150M OTSS (Operational Test & Evaluation Support Services) BPA, which focuses on airport screening equipment OT&E rather than integrated acceptance testing.

Incumbent & Competitive Landscape

Incumbent: Analytical Services & Materials, Inc. (AS&M) — a Woman-Owned Small Business (WOSB) holding 70T04021A7672N003, an $81M single-award BPA with TSA for ITSS. AS&M provides integrated testing, site assessment testing (ISAT), software development for data capture/analysis, engineering change request support, and program management. AS&M has cumulative contract history of ~$190M (since 2001) and holds CMMI Level 2 DEV and ISO 9001:2015 certifications.

AS&M is the dominant incumbent threat. They hold the current sole-source BPA, have deep institutional knowledge of TSA testing programs, and are likely to mount a strong recompete defense. The recompete is Full & Open (no set-aside), which means large businesses and teams will compete alongside small businesses.
