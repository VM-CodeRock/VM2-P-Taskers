TSA IAM Context & Regulatory Driver

TSA's Okta deployment is a compliance-driven, infrastructure-layer identity platform — not an IT services engagement. Key context:

  • OMB M-22-09 (Federal Zero Trust Strategy): Mandates that all federal agencies achieve phishing-resistant MFA and zero-trust architecture by FY2024. TSA's Okta Workforce Identity Cloud is the technical implementation of this mandate.
  • PIV Credential Enforcement: Per the requirement description, "all users must use Personal Identity Verification credentials when accessing TSA resources" — Okta provides the integration layer between PIV smart cards and application authentication.
  • Identity-as-a-Service (IDaaS): Okta is a SaaS/cloud subscription product. TSA buys seat licenses (per-user/per-app). There is no custom development, integration labor, or services component in this award — it is a pure license renewal.
  • Annual renewal cadence: The current award runs 09/30/2025–09/29/2026. The APFS forecast targets the next annual cycle (09/30/2026–09/29/2027). This is a predictable, recurring single-year delivery order.
  • SEWP V lock-in: TSA has established SEWP V as the procurement vehicle for this requirement. Without a SEWP V contract and Okta reseller authorization, participation is not possible regardless of company size or capabilities.
