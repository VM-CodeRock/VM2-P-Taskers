Leadership Statements — Day 1

Secretary Sean Duffy — DOT

Secretary Sean P. Duffy U.S. Secretary of Transportation Day 1 — Mar 27 Surprise address, MATS Pro Talks Theater

Secretary Duffy made an unscheduled appearance at MATS on Friday, March 27, joining FMCSA Administrator Barrs after Barrs' scheduled session concluded. Duffy walked the floor, spoke candidly with drivers and media, and delivered an impromptu address to the packed audience — the first sitting Transportation Secretary to appear at MATS in recent memory.

"What we're doing is to roll us back from the Wild Wild West." — Sec. Duffy, MATS Pro Talks Theater, March 27, 2026
"President Trump wants to make trucking great again, 100%. So many Americans who didn't pay attention have now seen all these videos of people being killed or injured on American roads, oftentimes coming from folks who were not well trained." — Sec. Duffy, MATS, March 27, 2026
"The last administration decided it was a good idea to let CDL driving schools self-certify that they're qualified to instruct a new driver in skills and road knowledge. Total bull$#!#. We are rolling back these schools, taking away their certifications. CDL mills are a thing of the past." — Sec. Duffy, MATS, March 27, 2026
"When I have small businesses been in families for generations and going out of business, that's because there's too much fraud and abuse in trucking. We're going to systematically roll back the fraud, prosecute the fraud, and I think what you're going to see is your spot rates are going to go up." — Sec. Duffy, MATS, March 27, 2026
"You have been forgotten too long. With President Trump, with me as your Secretary and Barrs as your FMCSA Administrator, we're focusing on this like a laser." — Sec. Duffy, MATS, March 27, 2026

Key policy announcements from Duffy at MATS:

  • English-only CDL testing: Announced that states will no longer be permitted to offer CDL tests in multiple languages. Administrator Barrs confirmed this by removing bilingual signage from the FMCSA exhibit at MATS.
  • "Ghost office" crackdown: Announced new enforcement to require physical Principal Place of Business (PPB) addresses for all carriers. P.O. boxes and commercial mailbox services used as business addresses will be disallowed. FMCSA has already identified approximately 2,000 carriers operating from such addresses.
  • ELD decertification: Confirmed rollout of decertification for non-compliant ELD providers, echoing Barrs' earlier statements.
  • Operation SafeDRIVE expansion: Confirmed the multi-state roadside enforcement initiative will continue, targeting ELP violators and chameleon carriers.
  • Spot rate prediction: Made the notable economic claim that fraud removal will drive spot rates upward — a direct benefit for legitimate owner-operators.

Source: Overdrive — "DOT's Duffy: 'Spot rates are going to go up'" (March 27, 2026) · Secretary Duffy Facebook (March 27)

Secretary Sean P. Duffy U.S. Secretary of Transportation Pre-MATS Context Feb 6, 2026 — Operation SafeDRIVE Wave 1

  • 8,215 inspections across 26 states + D.C.
  • 704 drivers placed out of service (nearly 500 for English proficiency violations)
  • 1,231 vehicles placed out of service

Leadership Statements — Day 1

Administrator Derek Barrs — FMCSA

Derek D. Barrs FMCSA Administrator Day 1 — Mar 27 Scheduled session, 10–11 AM ET, Pro Talks Theater (B104)

Administrator Barrs opened Friday morning's session by giving a standing ovation to drivers in attendance, acknowledging those who had witnessed fatal or near-fatal crashes, and declaring the dawn of "a new FMCSA." He was joined on the panel by FMCSA officials Ken Riddle and Ankur Saini.

"I want you to hear this clearly from me and our team. I hear you and I listen to you. I may not always agree on every single item, but I'm laboring to make the best possible changes for the trucking industry." — Administrator Barrs, MATS, March 27, 2026
"I told our rulemaking team — make new rules trooper proof. It has to be that simple. We have to make it clear, understandable so you can follow it. If the rules are so difficult for you to comply with and so difficult to enforce, then nothing comes into compliance." — Administrator Barrs, MATS, March 27, 2026 (joking, harkening back to his two decades with Florida Highway Patrol)
"We are not putting our head in the sand and saying carriers circumventing the system just slapping on a DOT number doesn't happen. FMCSA investigators have found some addresses with 400 to 500 registered carriers. How does that happen? I'm coming for them." — Administrator Barrs, MATS, March 27, 2026
"Systems need to reflect how the job actually works." — Administrator Barrs on HOS pilot programs, MATS, March 27, 2026

Key points from Barrs' MATS address:

  • "A New FMCSA": Barrs explicitly declared a shift in the agency's culture and enforcement posture, focused on practical rules that law enforcement can actually apply at roadside.
  • Pro Trucker Package framing: Barrs described FMCSA's 2025 Pro Trucker Package not merely as policy but as "a direct response to drivers."
  • HOS pilot programs: Invited drivers to participate in two active programs — one that expands sleeper berth split options (exploring 6/4 and 5/5 configurations beyond current 8/2 and 7/3), and another allowing drivers to pause the 14-hour clock for up to 3 hours during detention at pickup/delivery.
  • Ghost address enforcement: Confirmed that investigators have found 400–500 carriers registered at single addresses, and that "relentless" enforcement is underway.
  • Nine rulemakings in 2026: Reiterated the pipeline covering CDL mills, Entry-Level Driver Training, ELD certification standards, new-entrant testing for motor carriers, and more.
  • "Driver shortage" reframe: Acknowledged enforcement would remove CDL holders from the market but expressed confidence that "the cream would rise to the top" and American drivers would fill the vacated seats.

Leadership Statements — Day 0 & Day 1

Ankur Saini — FMCSA Chief Product & Technology Officer

Ankur Saini FMCSA Chief Product & Technology Officer (CPTO) Mar 26 & Mar 27 Fraud Panel (Mar 26) + Barrs Panel (Mar 27)

Saini led the Thursday (March 26) fraud and cargo theft panel and was also part of the Friday (March 27) panel with Administrator Barrs and Ken Riddle. He is the FMCSA official most directly responsible for the MOTUS system rollout and the agency's technology-driven approach to carrier registration fraud.

"We need to secure our front door. MOTUS is designed to be a one stop shop for all carrier contacts with the agency. And it was important to balance security with ease of operation, as 99% of carriers are good carriers doing things by the law." — Ankur Saini, FMCSA Fraud Panel, MATS, March 26, 2026

MOTUS System Status: Saini confirmed that MOTUS — the new unified USDOT Registration System — went live for supporting companies in December 2025 and is scheduled to open to all motor carriers, brokers, and freight forwarders in 2026. MOTUS is designed to:

  • Serve as a single, secure, mobile-friendly dashboard for all carrier registration actions
  • Replace the fragmented legacy systems that allowed carriers to exploit registration loopholes
  • Enable real-time fraud detection through improved data accuracy and vetting
  • Require physical address verification — eliminating ghost office registrations

Note: The non-domiciled CDL final rule (effective March 16, 2026) was specifically delayed a full year from its original effective date precisely because the old registration system could not support the new verification requirements. MOTUS resolves that dependency.

Legislative Context

Dalilah's Law — Congressional Action & DOT Support

Dalilah's Law — Status as of March 27, 2026

  • March 18, 2026: House Transportation & Infrastructure Committee approved Dalilah's Law on a bipartisan basis.
  • What it does: Strengthens CDL requirements, mandates English language proficiency for all CDL holders, prevents unqualified or undocumented individuals from obtaining CDLs through lax state enforcement, bans foreign-owned dispatch services, and cracks down on CDL mills.
  • Named after: Dalilah Coleman, a young girl injured in a crash with a non-domiciled CDL driver — Duffy noted at MATS that President Trump had personally invited her to his State of the Union address.
  • DOT support: Secretary Duffy issued a formal statement of support; Administrator Barrs has backed codification of the agency's administrative actions into law.
  • Industry support: ATA, TCA, NTTC and state trucking associations issued a joint statement of support on March 17, 2026.
"By approving Dalilah's Law, T&I Republicans took an important step towards addressing this safety issue and making sure only qualified drivers can obtain a CDL. This is commonsense legislation that requires all truck drivers on our roads to be able to read and speak English." — Rep. Sam Graves (R-MO), T&I Committee Chairman, March 18, 2026

System Context

MOTUS — The New USDOT Registration System

MOTUS is the first major overhaul of FMCSA's carrier registration infrastructure since the Carter administration. After nearly 30 years of failed modernization attempts, FMCSA launched Phase 1 (supporting companies) in December 2025. Phase 2 — for all motor carriers, brokers, and freight forwarders — is expected in 2026.

MOTUS — What It Changes

  • Single dashboard: Replaces fragmented legacy portals with one secure, mobile-friendly system for all registration actions
  • Physical address verification: Closes the "ghost office" loophole; P.O. boxes and commercial mailbox services flagged or rejected
  • Identity vetting: New checks designed to prevent chameleon carriers from simply re-registering under new DOT numbers after enforcement actions
  • ELD integration: Improved vetting of ELD registrations, blocking previously revoked devices from re-entering the system
  • Broker financial responsibility: Supports the broker financial responsibility rule (effective Jan. 16, 2026) requiring suspension of operating authority if available security falls below $75,000 — a rule that had been delayed a full year due to the old system's limitations

Sources: FMCSA — MOTUS Fact Sheet · FreightWaves — FMCSA Delivers on 30-Year Promise (January 2026) · DISA — MOTUS 2026 Changes
