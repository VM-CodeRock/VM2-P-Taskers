The New Round of 32

FIFA 2026 introduces the Round of 32 as a new knockout stage between the group stage and the traditional Round of 16. 32 teams advance from groups (top 2 per group = 24, plus 8 best 3rd-place finishers). They play the R32 June 28–July 3. This is what the seller means by "1/32." The traditional "Round of 16" (the seller's "1/16") runs July 4–7. For Brazil or Argentina, making the R32 requires them to finish in the top 3 of their group — highly likely given their historical strength.

Session Context & Methodology

Original Instructions

Build a buy-decision matrix for YPO-offered Brazil/Argentina FIFA World Cup 2026 tickets. Evaluate 13 match numbers (M7, M49, M76, M79, M86, M87, M91, M95, M97, M99, M100, M101, M102) on date/venue/stage, travel viability from DC, price markup, conditional probability, and provide buy/skip/watch-and-see recommendations with a scoring matrix.

Approach

1) Read all mandatory instruction files. 2) Fetched complete FIFA 2026 schedule from Roadtrips.com (authoritative tabular source with all 104 matches, dates, times, venues). 3) Cross-referenced with MLS Soccer, Fox Sports, Yahoo Sports, and Wikipedia for knockout round match-number assignments. 4) Traced Brazil's Group C bracket path (M7→M29→M49 group; M76→M91→M99→M102 knockout). 5) Traced Argentina's Group J bracket path (M19/M43/M70 group; M86→M95→M100→M102 knockout). 6) Built scoring matrix with 5 dimensions weighted by importance. 7) Applied pre-tournament probability estimates from betting market consensus. 8) Generated tiered recommendations (Buy/Consider/Watch/Skip).

  • M102 (Atlanta SF, Jul 15) is the single most valuable ticket — BOTH Brazil and Argentina feed into this semi's bracket; ~57% chance at least one plays here.
  • M7 (Brazil vs Morocco, MetLife NJ, Jun 13) is the best certainty+travel combination — 3.5 hr drive from DC, guaranteed match.
