Anthropic's most powerful model yet — what it is, what it can do, and how to prepare

TL;DR
Claude Mythos (codenamed Capybara) is Anthropic's next-generation AI model — leaked accidentally on March 26, 2026, then confirmed by Anthropic. It sits in a brand-new tier above Opus, scoring "dramatically higher" than Claude Opus 4.6 on coding, reasoning, and cybersecurity. It's currently in restricted early access with select cyber-defense organizations. No public release date yet; Polymarket gives 45% odds of public launch by June 30, 2026. It is expensive to run and Anthropic describes it as posing "unprecedented cybersecurity risks."
What Is Claude Mythos?

On March 26, 2026, a misconfigured content management system at Anthropic accidentally left roughly 3,000 internal documents — including draft blog posts — in a publicly searchable data store. Two cybersecurity researchers found them independently. The documents described a new AI model called Claude Mythos, also referred to by the working name Capybara.

Anthropic confirmed the model's existence in a statement to Fortune, calling it "a step change" and "the most capable we've built to date." The draft blog post described it as "by far the most powerful AI model we've ever developed."

The name Mythos was chosen to evoke "the deep connective tissue that links together knowledge and ideas." Capybara is the name for the new model tier — positioned above Opus in Anthropic's hierarchy.

The Cybersecurity Risk
Anthropic's own assessment: "Mythos presages an upcoming wave of models that can exploit vulnerabilities in ways that far outpace the efforts of defenders."

This is the primary reason for the cautious rollout. Mythos can find and exploit software vulnerabilities faster than defenders can patch them. This creates a dual-use dilemma:

  • Offensive risk: In the wrong hands, could enable large-scale cyberattacks. A Chinese state-sponsored group already used Claude Code (Opus 4.6) to infiltrate ~30 organizations including tech firms, financial institutions, and government agencies before being detected.
  • Defensive opportunity: Organizations with early access can use it to harden their own codebases before the broader capability becomes widely available.

The market reaction was immediate — cybersecurity stocks like CrowdStrike and Palo Alto dropped on news of the leak, reflecting concern that advanced AI could automate vulnerability discovery in ways that undercut traditional security vendors.

What Else Should You Know
  • The irony of the leak: A model described as posing "unprecedented cybersecurity risks" was revealed because Anthropic misconfigured their own CMS — leaving 3,000 internal documents in a publicly searchable data store. Anthropic called it "human error."
  • It's expensive to run — Anthropic acknowledges Mythos is "very expensive for us to serve, and will be very expensive for our customers to use." Efficiency improvements are ongoing.
  • IPO context: Anthropic is targeting a Q4 2026 IPO at a $60B valuation. Releasing Mythos publicly in Q3 would strengthen the IPO narrative. This creates commercial pressure to accelerate release timing.
  • Chinese state actors already exploiting Claude: Before Mythos even launches publicly, a Chinese state-sponsored group used Claude Code (Opus 4.6) to infiltrate ~30 organizations. Mythos's capabilities will amplify this risk significantly.
  • Competitor reaction: OpenAI and Google are reportedly accelerating their own model releases in response. The AI race is accelerating into dangerous territory.
  • The "Capybara" brand: Chosen for the capybara's nature as a "social bridge" animal — the tier name signals models designed for "connective intelligence" rather than pure task-solving. Whether Mythos keeps this branding in final release is unclear.
