Agency & Program Background

What Is FTA's Technology Transfer (T2) Program?

The Technology Transfer (T2) Program is FTA's mechanism for deploying and disseminating research findings from the Public Transportation Innovation Program (49 U.S.C. § 5312) to the transit industry. FTA manages a research portfolio averaging over $200M in active projects annually. T2 is the "last mile" of that pipeline — moving proven innovations from federally funded research into practice at transit agencies nationwide.

Key program goals: (1) Deploy and promote adoption of FTA innovative research; (2) Facilitate commercialization of U.S.-developed technologies from FTA-funded R&D; (3) Strengthen transit stakeholder capacity to utilize research products. The program aligns with DOT's broader Efficiency goal and supports Executive Order 14104 on domestic manufacturing and technology transfer.

Program History & Trajectory

Historically, T2 activities at FTA were managed through the Volpe National Transportation Systems Center (Cambridge, MA) under OST-R's cross-modal coordination role. FTA published annual T2 reports (FY2018, FY2019) documenting dissemination through publications, webinars, industry events, and Shared-Use Mobility Center partnerships. The FTA cancelled the FY25 T2 NOFO (cooperative agreement, $5M, Feb 2025 deadline) on the day applications were due — a strong signal of a structural pivot.

The current Sources Sought suggests FTA now wants a contractor-managed, FFP/LH structure rather than a cooperative agreement with a nonprofit/academic awardee. This is a meaningful shift — it means a traditional for-profit consulting firm could compete directly (unlike a cooperative agreement which typically goes to nonprofits, labs, or universities).
