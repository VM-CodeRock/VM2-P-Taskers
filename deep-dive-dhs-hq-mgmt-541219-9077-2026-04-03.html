DHS OCFO is re-competing a five-year Multiple-Award BPA (MA-BPA) to sustain ISO 9001 quality management, business process improvement, robotic process automation (RPA), strategic planning, and workforce development support. The incumbent task order (70RDAD21FR0000119, held by Guidehouse Inc.) was valued at approximately $3.8M under the prior award period (Sep 2021 – Mar 2025); the recompete ceiling of $20M–$50M signals a substantially expanded scope under a new MA-BPA structure.

Changeis carries demonstrably relevant past performance in business process re-engineering (HUD BPR via Volpe), strategic planning and communications support (DOT/FAA/Build America Bureau), and ISO-adjacent process optimization work. The RAG engine surfaces 9 past performance contracts across these domains — the top match scoring 0.705 (STRONG tier). However, Changeis does not currently hold DHS-direct financial audit support contracts, and the incumbent Guidehouse (now under DOGE contract pressure) may face a more competitive landscape than in prior cycles.

Key Strategic Consideration

This is a MA-BPA recompete — multiple awards expected. This dramatically increases win probability vs. a single-award competition. Changeis should pursue a teaming arrangement with a firm holding DHS OCFO audit support credentials (e.g., a CPA firm or existing OCFO BPA holder) while leading on ISO QMS, RPA, and process optimization work streams.

  • Timeline urgency: Solicitation estimated May 1, 2026 — approximately 4 weeks away. Capture activity should begin immediately.
  • Set-aside status: TBD — could be full-and-open or small business set-aside. Changeis qualifies as WOSB.
  • DOGE risk factor: Guidehouse lost $376M in federal contracts in 2025 under GSA termination pressure; incumbency advantage may be weakened.
  • GSA Schedule vehicle: Changeis must confirm GSA Schedule eligibility under SIN 541219 for this competition.
