01 · Executive Summary
Bid / No-Bid Recommendation
NO-BID as Prime — Consider Sub/Teaming Role Only
Confidence: High (85%)  |  Decision deadline: April 30, 2026

NHTSA is seeking a successor to the Road to Zero (RTZ) Coalition currently managed by the National Safety Council (NSC), which has held this cooperative agreement continuously since 2016. The requirement calls for coalition management of 1,500+ member organizations, subgrant disbursement (historically ~$750K/year to community grantees), Safe Systems curricula development, AI-enhanced enforcement analytics, and annual cross-sector convening.

Changeis should not bid as prime on this opportunity. The three decisive factors are: (1) NSC is a deeply entrenched incumbent with nine years of coalition history and brand equity that is extremely difficult to displace; (2) Changeis has no demonstrable NHTSA/DOT past performance or cooperative agreement track record — the RAG match shows FMCSA/Volpe adjacency, not NHTSA RTZ direct experience; and (3) the NAICS codes (541611, 611430) and vehicle type (Cooperative Agreement, not FAR contract) are outside Changeis's priority DHS-focused BD pipeline.

A teaming/sub role is worth evaluating — specifically as a data analytics, AI predictive modeling, or curriculum technology sub if NSC or another prime invites teaming. If Varun's goal is market entry into DOT/NHTSA, a sub role here is far lower-risk than a prime bid with a near-zero probability of award.

RFI response (market research only) closes May 4, 2026 at 5:00 PM ET — 6 days from now. An RFI response does not commit to a bid. Decide by April 30 whether to submit a no-cost market research response, which at minimum keeps Changeis visible to NHTSA for future opportunities.

03 · Mission & Scope

The Cooperative Agreement continues NHTSA's flagship traffic safety coalition program. The winner will serve as the operational backbone of the nation's largest traffic safety alliance, performing the following primary workstreams:

WorkstreamDescriptionComplexity
Coalition Management Maintain 1,500+ member organizations; recruit new members; convene expert Steering Group, working groups; manage communications High
Subgrant Program Design, advertise, review, award, and monitor $750K+/year in community grants to nonprofits, universities, local agencies; require grantee pre/post evaluations High
Curriculum Development Develop State/local safety practitioner training materials; eLearning content; knowledge transfer resources; APA-standard scientific reporting Medium
Technology Transfer Disseminate proven countermeasures and evidence-based best practices across the safety network; webinars, newsletters Medium
AI / Predictive Analytics Leverage AI to research traffic safety enforcement strategies; conduct predictive analytics and risk modeling; optimize countermeasure deployment Emerging
Performance Measurement Track crashes by road-user category, project costs, outreach metrics (webinars, newsletters), lessons learned; formative/process/impact/outcome evaluations Medium
Federal Coordination NHTSA substantial involvement — kick-off meetings, annual technical/budget reviews, milestone approvals, liaison with FHWA, FMCSA Ongoing
AI Angle — Key Differentiator

NHTSA's draft SOW explicitly notes interest in AI for traffic enforcement effectiveness, predictive analytics, and risk modeling. This is the highest-differentiation area where Changeis could realistically add value as a teaming partner — but only if paired with an entity that already holds the coalition management and subgrant track record.

The Safe Systems Approach is the governing framework — treating road safety as a shared responsibility between infrastructure, vehicles, road users, and speeds, with a system designed to protect against human error. NHTSA emphasizes science-based evaluation designs, APA-standard reporting, and evidence-based interventions across four focus areas: pedestrian safety, rural road safety, alcohol/drug-impaired driving, and heavy trucks.

07 · Bid / No-Bid Recommendation
Final Recommendation
NO-BID as Prime
Three decisive reasons below — this is not a hedge, it's a clear call.

Reason 1 — Incumbent Entrenchment is Near-Absolute. NSC has managed the Road to Zero Coalition for nine consecutive years, built a 1,500+ member network, and administered grants annually since 2017. NHTSA praised the partnership at its May 2025 Annual Meeting. Displacing this incumbent would require either a structural failure (NSC bid deficiency, cost non-competitiveness, or organizational issue) or a dramatically superior offer. Neither condition appears present.

Reason 2 — Capability Gap is Real and Verifiable. A competitive evaluation for this cooperative agreement will require: (a) demonstrated coalition management at scale, (b) subgrant administration with 2 CFR Part 200 compliance, and (c) NHTSA/traffic safety cooperative agreement past performance. Changeis can document none of these. The best RAG matches are FMCSA/Volpe work with thematic adjacency — evaluators familiar with the traffic safety space will see through a "similar experience" stretch.

Reason 3 — Wrong Vehicle, Wrong NAICS, Wrong Agency for Changeis's BD Strategy. Cooperative agreements, NAICS 541611/611430, and NHTSA behavioral safety programs are all outside Changeis's priority lane. Investing 4–6 days in an RFI response and potentially weeks in a full proposal diverts BD resources from higher-probability DHS opportunities where Changeis has genuine incumbency leverage and established relationships.

Alternative Posture
Pursue Sub/Teaming Role — If NSC or Another Prime Invites It
Recommended only if approached by a prime seeking AI/analytics capability

If NSC (or Cambridge Systematics, ICF) is building a team and seeks an AI analytics or curriculum technology sub, Changeis should evaluate that opportunity seriously. The ask would be limited to the AI predictive analytics workstream (~15–25% of total scope), which aligns with Changeis's genuine capability. This provides NHTSA relationship development, a RTZ citation for future DOT pursuits, and minimal BD overhead — at a fraction of the risk of a prime bid.
