Requirement Summary (APFS)

Program management, advisory, and technical support services to the DHS Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction (CWMD) Office — Information Analysis Directorate (IAD). The effort is to provide programmatic, systems engineering, information analysis, reporting, outreach, and scientific support services to the CWMD IAD in order to enhance the nation's abilities to prevent terrorist and other threat actors from using weapons of mass destruction and support operational partners in closing capability gaps along adversary pathways.

CWMD Office & IAD Mission Context

CWMD Office Overview

The DHS Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction Office (CWMD), established 2017 and authorized by Congress in 2018 (CWMD Act of 2018, P.L. 115-387), serves as the single point of CBRN expertise within DHS. It works to prevent attacks against the United States using chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear (CBRN) weapons through timely, responsive support to operational partners. Budget ~$409.4M; ~286 FTEs.

Information Analysis Directorate (IAD) Functions

IAD within CWMD provides intelligence-facing analysis and science support. Core functions include:

  • WMD/CBRN Threat Intelligence Analysis — Processing intelligence on adversary WMD programs, pathways, and capability gaps
  • Biosurveillance & National Biosurveillance Integration Center (NBIC) — Integrating data from federal, state, and local sources for biodefense early warning
  • Global Nuclear Detection Architecture (GNDA) — Supporting development and maintenance of detection architectures across nuclear/radiological domains
  • Chemical & Biological Defense Architecture Development — Building nascent architectures for CB threats analogous to existing nuclear detection work
  • Reporting & Outreach — Intelligence products and stakeholder communication to SLTT partners and federal agencies
  • Scientific Support — Technical assessments, data categorization, and AI application in cargo screening, biosurveillance, and detection technologies
  • Capability Gap Assessment — Data-driven, defensible analytical activities to identify gaps and resource priorities for FSLTT partners
