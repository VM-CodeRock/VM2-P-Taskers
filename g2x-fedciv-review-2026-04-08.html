C · Industry News & Developments

White House Releases 2026 National Cyber Strategy — Six Pillars Signal Sustained IT Spend

Source: Ryan & Wetmore / Winvale · March 31, 2026
The Trump Administration's first major cyber policy update frames cyberspace as national security. Six pillars: shape adversary behavior, streamline regulation, modernize/secure federal networks (zero-trust + AI-enabled defense), secure critical infrastructure, sustain superiority in emerging tech, and build cyber talent. Pillar 3 explicitly calls for AI-powered cybersecurity tools and cloud migration across federal networks.
Changeis relevance: Directly signals continued investment in zero-trust architecture, cloud migration, and cybersecurity for civilian agencies. Expect new solicitations in NAICS 541512 and 541519 citing this strategy as program context. Position NOW on CISA CDM Phase 4 and DHS zero-trust vehicles.

GSA Proposes AI Clause for All Federal Contractor Contracts (Refresh 31, April 2026)

Source: Pursuit.us / Holland & Knight · March 18, 2026 · GSA Refresh 31 scheduled April 2026
GSA is incorporating a new GSAR clause (552.239-7001, "Basic Safeguarding of AI Systems") into Schedule contracts, initially applying to GSA Schedule orders. Prime contractors become directly liable for downstream AI vendors' compliance. Public comment period closed April 3. Final clause expected in April Schedule refresh.
Changeis relevance: Any AI components in proposals or delivery (AWS SageMaker, Azure AI, etc.) will now carry compliance obligations. Review teaming agreements and subcontract flow-downs before next GSA Schedule task order proposal.

CIO-SP4 Cancelled — 494 CIO-SP3 Recompetes Now Active on OASIS+ and MAS

Source: GovDash GovCon Intel Brief · March 2026
The cancellation of CIO-SP4 threw 494 expiring CIO-SP3 task orders onto OASIS+ and the Multiple Award Schedule overnight. This is the largest vehicle disruption of FY2026 — previously incumbent primes now face open recompetition.
Changeis relevance: If currently subcontracting on any CIO-SP3 task orders, determine recompete status immediately. If not yet on OASIS+, pursue on-ramp. This creates the single largest near-term opening for IT services firms in FY2026.

Trump DEI Executive Order — Immediate Impact on Federal Contractors

Source: PilieroMazza PLLC · April 2, 2026
