01 · Executive Summary
Strategic Moment

On April 10, 2026, the New York Times reported that the FAA is actively recruiting video gamers to fill thousands of air traffic controller vacancies. This is not just a headline — it is the single most powerful contextual hook Changeis can deploy in its FAA APEX RFI response due April 17.

The APEX RFI (APEXRFI032026) asks vendors to propose a scalable, data-driven system that delivers near-real-time visibility into student performance across all phases of ATC Academy training. At its core, APEX is a training intelligence platform. The FAA is simultaneously announcing that its incoming trainee cohort will include non-traditional learners — gamers — with fundamentally different cognitive profiles, learning behaviors, and performance signatures than the career-changer or ex-military controller of the past.

This creates an urgent, credible argument: APEX is not a nice-to-have analytics dashboard. It is a mission-critical infrastructure investment required to train, screen, and graduate a new generation of controllers at a time when the FAA is already 3,500 positions below staffing targets and converting fewer than 2% of applicants into certified controllers.

02 · The Gamer-to-Controller Pipeline Opportunity

Secretary Sean Duffy's statement — "To engage the next generation, we must evolve" — signals a structural shift in how the FAA thinks about its training pipeline. The implications for APEX are direct and specific.

AI
Cognitive Profiling

New Learner Demographics → Predictive Analytics Demand

Gamers bring measurable cognitive assets — hand-eye coordination, rapid decision cycles, sustained screen concentration — but their performance in simulated ATC tasks differs from prior trainee cohorts. APEX's predictive analytics module should model which gaming skill variables correlate with success at each Academy phase, enabling early identification of high-potential non-traditional recruits.

!
Conversion Failure Analysis

200K Applicants · 2% Convert → Error Taxonomy Is Critical

With only 2% of applicants becoming certified controllers and training lasting up to 6 years, the FAA cannot afford to discover failure late. APEX's error taxonomy and trend analytics must identify where non-traditional recruits diverge from historical success patterns — and do it early enough to remediate or redirect, not just document failure.

≡
Data Integration

E-CTI Fast-Track → Enterprise Data Architecture Required

The E-CTI program (11 institutions, 8 added under Duffy) allows graduates to skip the Oklahoma City Academy. This creates a multi-node training pipeline where performance data originates from 11 external institutions and the Academy. APEX's data integration layer must provide a unified enterprise view — exactly the DOT multi-source data architecture Changeis has built at Volpe.

↑
Instructor Adaptation

New Learner Profiles → Instructor Performance Signals Change

Instructors trained to identify controller potential in military-background or career-changer trainees may not immediately recognize the performance signals of a gamer cohort. APEX's instructor analysis module can track whether individual instructors are effectively adapting to new learner demographics — and surface this to leadership before it becomes a throughput problem.

The GAO Finding Amplifies This

The GAO's 2026 report characterizes the ATC shortage as a "product of a rigid system" where training timelines and hiring limits haven't kept pace with demand. Recruiting gamers is a demand-side solution. APEX is the supply-side enabler — the data infrastructure that makes the new pipeline trainable, measurable, and improvable in near-real-time.

04 · Draft RFI Response Themes

The following five themes are recommended for Changeis's concept paper. Each directly maps a gamer-pipeline hook to an APEX RFI requirement, with a Changeis proof point.

1

Gamer-Profile Predictive Modeling

Leverage cognitive assessment and early-phase performance data to build predictive models calibrated for non-traditional recruits. As FAA expands gamer outreach, the Academy will receive cohorts whose performance signatures differ from historical norms. APEX's predictive analytics layer — informed by Changeis's DOT workforce analytics experience — can identify leading indicators of success or failure for this demographic before costly late-stage washouts occur, directly reducing the FAA's 6-year training investment per seat.

2

Unified Data Architecture Across Disparate Sources

Changeis has demonstrated the ability to integrate multi-source DOT data into coherent, interoperable analytics platforms through Volpe task orders (6913G621F400021, 6913G620F400034). APEX requires exactly this capability: consolidating simulator data, classroom assessments, check-ride results, and E-CTI institution records into a single normalized data model. Our approach prioritizes API-first integration, standardized taxonomies, and a data governance layer appropriate for FAA security requirements.

3

Near-Real-Time Dashboard Development

Changeis has delivered interactive, data-dense dashboards for federal performance management programs with drill-down capability to individual metrics. For APEX, we propose role-differentiated views: instructor dashboards surfacing cohort performance trends, Academy leadership views tracking throughput and pass rates by phase, and enterprise dashboards enabling FAA HQ to monitor system-wide controller pipeline health — including cross-institution E-CTI performance comparison.

4

Scalability from Academy to E-CTI Enterprise

With 11 E-CTI institutions (8 added under Secretary Duffy) now part of the controller pipeline, APEX cannot be designed as an Oklahoma City Academy-only system. Changeis proposes a cloud-native, modular architecture that onboards new institutions incrementally — each contributing standardized data to the enterprise view without requiring custom integration work per institution. This directly addresses FAA's Scalability and Future Expansion requirement and supports the Administration's stated goal of accelerating E-CTI expansion.

5

Human-Performance Analytics: Instructor Effectiveness Tied to Learner Outcomes

The introduction of gamer-profile trainees tests instructor adaptability. APEX's instructor performance module should measure not just trainee outcomes by instructor but adaptation signals — whether instructors modify their approach as cohort composition changes. Changeis's experience with workforce performance frameworks in federal training environments allows us to design instructor analytics that are evidence-based, defensible, and actionable without creating punitive surveillance dynamics that reduce instructor morale.
