Executive Summary

TSA's Acceptance Testing Support Services (ATSS) contract is a follow-on recompete of the incumbent Blanket Purchase Agreement held by Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC) (BPA #70T04021A7672N004, awarded 2021, ceiling confirmed via USAspending). The new Indefinite Delivery Contract (IDC) is estimated at over $100M over 5 years (January 2027 – January 2032), with solicitation estimated for release March 1, 2026 — meaning the RFP may already be published or imminent as of this writing (April 17, 2026).


ATSS is one of four simultaneous TSA Testing & Evaluation contract recompetes worth $300M+ combined, creating a unique strategic cluster opportunity for Changeis. SAIC has held TSA's acceptance testing work for multiple contract cycles and has publicly branded its ATSS capability — making them a deeply entrenched incumbent. The scope is demanding: Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT), Site Acceptance Testing (SAT), Operational Readiness Testing (ORT), Verification Testing (VT), test article development and maintenance (8,000+ articles), and logistics support for 5,000+ annual tests across 430+ airports nationwide.


The RAG score of 0.6908 (STRONG) reflects meaningful capability alignment through Changeis's FAA/DOT testing and systems engineering portfolio. Critical gaps exist in direct TSA/DHS past performance and physical test article logistics operations. The recommended posture is GO — Conditional, subject to identifying a qualified teaming partner with TSE field operations experience, given the earliest solicitation timeline in the TSA T&E cluster.

Cluster Strategy Insight: The TSA Sources Sought (July 2025) explicitly noted that TSA has not decided on contract vehicle structure and is evaluating whether to consolidate some of the four BPAs. This suggests TSA may: (a) run four separate competitions (current APFS forecast), (b) consolidate ATSS + DTSS or ATSS + OTSS into a single vehicle, or (c) create a master IDIQ with task order lots. If TSA consolidates ATSS + OTSS, the incumbent (SAIC) would hold both. Changeis should monitor for any consolidation signals.

Cluster Win Strategy: Even a sub-prime position on one TSA T&E vehicle creates the DHS past performance record needed to compete as prime on subsequent vehicles. The optimal approach is to prioritize ATSS as the beachhead (earliest timeline, SAIC incumbent — potentially more vulnerable than Battelle on DTSS), then leverage any award or meaningful performance as a subcontractor to build toward a DTSS or ITSS prime position in the next cycle.

SAIC's ATSS Work Scope (from SAIC public case study):

  • Comprehensive FAT/SAT for checkpoint and baggage screening systems at OEM factories and airports
  • Maintenance and management of 8,000+ test articles (ANSI test kits, ISAT bag sets, threat surrogates)
  • End-to-end logistics support: storage, shipping, 24-hour test article deployment capability
  • Operational Readiness Testing (ORT) support: triggered by software/hardware changes, relocations, or extended downtime
  • ANSI CT Detection Testing per ANSI N42.45-2015 methodology on CT machines
  • Network Acceptance Testing (NAT) for networked screening systems
  • Average: 5,000+ tests annually across 430+ federalized airports
  • Cybersecurity testing of checkpoint systems at multiple domestic locations
  • Canine decoy logistics: 60+ airports (CONUS, Hawaii, Puerto Rico, Alaska)
  • Coordination with DHS OCIO, Network Operations Security Center, and Office of CFO on enterprise systems testing
