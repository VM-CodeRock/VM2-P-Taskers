Requirement Detail

The requirement covers RCRA (Resource Conservation and Recovery Act) permit-required groundwater and environmental monitoring activities across USCG Base Elizabeth City. Specific scope items include:

  • Groundwater sampling and analyses at 10 active Solid Waste Management Units (SWMUs) across the base property
  • Well condition assessment — identifying damaged monitoring wells for future repair or replacement
  • Data summary reports meeting RCRA monitoring requirements (annual compliance reporting)
  • Calcium peroxide injection program implementing enhanced in situ bioremediation (ISB) to address residual dissolved-phase petroleum hydrocarbon contamination at SWMU 58 — JP-4 Pipeline Release site
  • Goal: Bring groundwater contamination at SWMU 58 below RCRA reporting thresholds

This is a recurring annual program. A prior-year version of this same requirement (F2025070427, 2025 RCRA Compliance Groundwater Monitoring Elizabeth City) was forecasted in June 2025 for Q4 FY2025 award at $250K–$500K, coded NAICS 541620 — Environmental Consulting Services and explicitly restricted to holders of the USCG ENV MATOC. The current APFS (F2025072195) represents the 2026–2027 cycle, now appearing under the A&E Services II vehicle with a NAICS shift to 541330.
