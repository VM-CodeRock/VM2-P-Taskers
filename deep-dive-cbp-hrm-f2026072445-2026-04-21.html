1 · Executive Summary

CBP's Office of Human Resources Management (HRM) is recompeting its Professional HR Administrative Support BPA — a Labor Hour contract valued at $50M–$100M — to staff HR operations at two physical Centers: the CBP Hiring Center in Bloomington, MN and the CBP Processing and Servicing Center (PSC) in Indianapolis, IN. Work covers processing personnel actions, running applicant care call centers, electronic records management (eOPF scanning), and general HR staffing support. The vehicle is GSA MAS SIN 561110, the set-aside is SDVOSB, and the incumbent is Vision Centric Inc. (Madison, AL; contract 70B06C23F00000577, ~$12.4M obligated, POP through Sep 30, 2026).

Changeis's RAG corpus returns a MODERATE 0.643 composite score driven primarily by Volpe/DOT task orders with HR management and administrative support content; there is no direct CBP HRM past performance in corpus. The opportunity is fundamentally a location-specific, high-volume HR staffing play — not an advisory or consulting requirement — making NAICS 561110 size standard constraints and SDVOSB set-aside eligibility the decisive qualifying factors. The RAG score reflects thematic overlap, not operational equivalence.

CONDITIONAL GO — Pursue with SDVOSB Teaming Partner: This is a pure HR staffing BPA requiring boots-on-the-ground at CBP centers in Minnesota and Indiana. Changeis's HCPS/advisory capabilities do not directly satisfy the staffing model. Recommend teaming with an SDVOSB-certified HR staffing firm with demonstrated federal HR processing experience and Midwest placement capacity. Without a qualified teaming arrangement, win probability is low.

Scope Summary: CBP HRM requires contractor-provided staff to supplement government HR employees at two Centers experiencing increased applicant volume driven by CBP hiring surges. Work at the Hiring Center (Bloomington, MN) focuses on applicant care, staffing support, and compliance with hiring processes. Work at the Processing and Servicing Center (Indianapolis, IN) covers personnel action processing, electronic records management (scanning documents into the Electronic Official Personnel Folder / eOPF System), and call center operations. Both sites share a requirement for the following task categories:

  • Processing personnel actions — SF-52s, accessions, separations, and status changes
  • Staffing call centers — responding to applicant inquiries, providing hiring process guidance
  • Administrative services support — general office administration, scheduling, correspondence
  • Staffing services assistance — supporting HR specialists with recruitment and selection workflows
  • Electronic records management — eOPF scanning, digitization, quality control of official personnel folders

Acquisition Strategy: SDVOSB Set-Aside placed under the GSA Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) SIN 561110 — Office Administrative Services. The BPA vehicle (FAR Part 8 or 13) means the award will be placed against existing GSA Schedule holders, restricting competition to vendors on GSA Schedule with SIN 561110 coverage and an SDVOSB designation.

Note on dollar range discrepancy: The APFS record (F2026072445) lists the dollar range as $50M–$100M. A separate APFS record (F2025069484, updated Mar 16, 2026) for what appears to be the same recompete lists $20M–$50M. The HigherGov aggregator cites up to $100M. The prior incumbent award was ~$12.4M for a ~3-year base POP. The $50–100M range likely reflects multiple option years and/or scope expansion tied to CBP hiring surges under current administration priorities. Treat the $50M floor as the conservative planning figure.

7 · Key Risks & Next Steps

Size Standard Nuance (NAICS 561110): The current SBA size standard for NAICS 561110 — Office Administrative Services — is $12.5M in annual receipts, with a proposed increase to $15.5M under SBA's August 2025 rulemaking. Vision Centric's ~$23.5M in total contract obligations makes it borderline or potentially above the size standard — however, for SDVOSB set-aside, SBA certification is based on annual receipts averaged over 3–5 years, not total contract obligations. If Vision Centric has grown significantly, they may face a size standard challenge. Any teaming arrangement Changeis pursues must ensure the SDVOSB prime's annual receipts stay below $12.5M (or $15.5M if new rule is finalized) to maintain eligibility.

SAM.gov Monitoring: The estimated solicitation release was February 23, 2026 — monitor SAM.gov for the RFQ/RFP posting. Given that the APFS was updated January 28, 2026, a delay into March–April 2026 is possible. Set up keyword alerts for "CBP Hiring Center," "SDVOSB 561110," and "HRM administrative support."
