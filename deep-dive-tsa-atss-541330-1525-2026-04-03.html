The TSA Acceptance Testing Support Services (ATSS) recompete is the single largest opportunity in this APFS batch — valued at over $100 million across a 5-year IDC (Jan 2027 – Jan 2032). The RAG engine returns a STRONG score (0.691) driven by Changeis past performance on government test and evaluation (T&E) programs, most prominently the FAA CLMRS program where Changeis supported Production Readiness Tests, Government Operational Tests, and Test Readiness Reviews in a complex federal aviation environment.

The critical constraint is scale and domain specificity. ATSS requires deep, hands-on physical acceptance testing at airport locations and OEM factories across the CONUS and OCONUS — performing over 5,000 tests annually on 17,000+ pieces of TSE. This is highly specialized aviation security equipment testing that the current incumbent (SAIC) has performed since at least 2021 under a $110M BPA. Changeis's T&E past performance is in software/systems testing (FAA CLMRS, Volpe analytics), not physical security equipment acceptance testing. A direct prime bid would face steep hurdles on demonstrated experience in TSE-specific acceptance testing.

Recommended action: Identify and approach a prime contractor with TSA testing experience as a teaming opportunity. Changeis can contribute program management, data analysis, logistics coordination, and T&E documentation capabilities as a subcontractor. Monitor the solicitation release (est. Requirement Summary: The Contractor shall provide Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT), Site Acceptance Testing (SAT), Operational Readiness Testing (ORT), and Verification Testing (VT) of Transportation Security Equipment (TSE) at airport and factory locations. ATSS also includes supporting test article development and logistics support. The Contractor shall provide all program management, technical support and execution, labor, personnel, expertise, logistics and facilities for all Acceptance Test and System Acceptance (ATSA) activities.

Based on the prior OATSS RFI and NCMA article on TSA's fusion procurement approach, ATSS scope includes:

  • Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) — At OEM locations, verifying systems meet acquisition requirements before shipment
  • Site Acceptance Testing (SAT) — At airports post-installation, before live screening operations
  • Operational Readiness Testing (ORT) — When hardware/software changes occur or systems return from extended downtime
  • Verification Testing (VT) — Ongoing verification of TSE performance against specifications
  • Test Article Development & Maintenance — Managing 8,000+ ANSI test kits and bag sets across the program
  • Logistics Support — 24-hour test article shipping capability, multi-site CONUS/OCONUS coordination
  • Cybersecurity Testing — Vulnerability testing, OS hardening, network acceptance testing for checkpoint/baggage systems
  • Program Management — Master schedule, documentation, reporting, stakeholder coordination

Scale: 5,000+ acceptance tests annually; 17,000+ TSE units tested in the prior 3 years; 125 in-line baggage systems; simultaneous testing across geographically dispersed airports.
