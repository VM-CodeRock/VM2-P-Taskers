What Is ATLAS?

The Accelerated Transformation of Legacy Applications and Systems (ATLAS) is the FAA's most ambitious IT modernization program in recent history. Announced via SAM.gov on February 17, 2026, it targets the FAA's entire mission-support portfolio — approximately 200 applications and 3,000 databases — for rationalization, consolidation, cloud migration, and AI-enabled modernization. The FAA is forgoing a traditional RFP in favor of a Challenge-Based Acquisition (ChBA) where vendors must demonstrate, not just describe, their modernization capabilities.

Key Insight: The self-scoring matrix in Phase 1 rewards contractors with experience on contracts >$25M, >$50M, and >$100M annually — and subcontractor/teaming partner experience counts for no more than 25% of Factor 1 points. BAH as prime needs Changeis's FAA ATO experience to strengthen its own score.

Program Objectives (8 FAA-Stated Goals)

  • Portfolio modernization & technical debt reduction
  • Improved user experience across mission-support systems
  • High-availability mission operations (99.9% uptime for critical systems)
  • Accelerated value delivery via DevSecOps
  • Enterprise data excellence & intelligent automation (AI/ML)
  • Proactive cybersecurity and compliance
  • Program governance frameworks
  • Seamless service transition

Set-Aside Status & NAICS

Per the SAM.gov notice: No set-aside is currently applied. NAICS: 541512 — Computer Systems Design Services. This is an unrestricted competition, making large prime contractors like BAH fully eligible. Contract value is not officially stated but self-scoring criteria suggest the FAA expects contractors with $25M–$100M+ annual contract experience; the program's 10-year scope likely puts total value in the $500M–$1B+ range based on comparables (e.g., SETIS at $2.3B).

3 · TA Review Framework & Checklist

This checklist covers all critical TA provisions Changeis should verify before signing. Priority levels: Critical · Important · Standard

Scope & Exclusivity Provisions

ProvisionWhat to Look ForPriority
Exclusivity Scope Must be tied to this specific solicitation only (ATLAS ChBA). Any clause purporting to restrict Changeis from teaming on other FAA vehicles (SETIS, CLMRS, ATAPS follow-ons) is unacceptable. Critical
Exclusivity Duration Should expire automatically: (a) if BAH does not submit a Phase 1 response, (b) if BAH is eliminated from any phase, (c) no later than 90 days after contract award or final contract award decision. Critical
Automatic Release Triggers Changeis must be released from exclusivity if BAH: (1) chooses not to submit, (2) is downselected out, (3) fails to award a subcontract within X days of prime award, or (4) materially breaches the TA. Critical
Right to Compete Independently Confirm Changeis retains the right to prime separately or team with other parties on any other FAA contract not specifically named in this TA. Critical
Exclusion of Incumbent Work Ensure the exclusivity clause explicitly carves out Changeis's existing contracts (ATAPS II, ATEPS II) and any pending re-competes. Important

Work Share Provisions

ProvisionWhat to Look ForPriority
Minimum Work Share % Changeis should negotiate a minimum guaranteed work share percentage (not merely "target"). For a meaningful role, aim for ≥15% of total contract value, with specific CLINs or work areas named. Critical
Work Share Tied to CLINs/Functions Work share should be described by specific functional area (e.g., "ATO mission support services, program management support") — not generic language like "as mutually agreed." Critical
Subcontract Award Obligation TA should state BAH shall award a subcontract (not merely "negotiate in good faith"). Good faith negotiation clauses with no subcontract guarantee are a well-known risk (see CGI Federal v. FCi Federal). Critical
Work Share Rebalancing Restrictions on BAH unilaterally reducing Changeis's work share post-award. Any rebalancing should require Changeis's written consent. Important
Option Exercise Commitment BAH must exercise Changeis's subcontract options if BAH's prime options are exercised by the government. Important
Key Personnel Changeis should identify key personnel positions it will fill and have those named in the TA. This makes work share concrete and enforceable. Important
Substitute Work If the government eliminates Changeis's specific CLIN/work area, BAH should be obligated to substitute comparable work of equivalent value. Standard

IP, Data Rights & Confidentiality Provisions

ProvisionWhat to Look ForPriority
Proprietary Information Ownership Each party should retain ownership of its own pre-existing IP, methodologies, and tools. The TA should explicitly state: "Each party retains all rights to its Background IP." No assignment of Changeis's existing frameworks or tools to BAH. Critical
Proposal Materials IP Any content Changeis contributes to the BAH proposal (past performance narratives, technical approach, staffing plans) should remain Changeis's property unless it becomes government-deliverable. BAH should not be able to reuse Changeis's past performance write-ups for other pursuits. Critical
License vs. Assignment If Changeis contributes any tools or methods to the proposal, ensure the agreement grants BAH a limited license for this specific bid — not a perpetual assignment. Critical
Confidentiality / NDA Terms Mutual NDA with clear carve-outs for information already in the public domain, information received from third parties independently, or information independently developed. Important
Residuals Clause Watch for a "residuals clause" allowing BAH to use retained knowledge from Changeis's proposals in future bids. This should be struck or tightly scoped. Important
Government Data Rights Flow-Down Government data rights clauses (FAR 52.227-14, -17) must flow down appropriately; ensure Changeis does not inadvertently grant the government broader rights than required. Important

Non-Compete, Non-Solicitation & Dispute Resolution

ProvisionWhat to Look ForPriority
Non-Solicitation of Employees Standard mutual provision — neither party will solicit the other's employees during the TA term and for 12 months post-termination. Watch for one-sided versions that only restrict Changeis. Critical
Non-Compete Scope Any non-compete should be limited to this specific contract vehicle only. Watch for broad language like "any FAA opportunity" or "any contract for which BAH competes" — these are unacceptable. Critical
Pricing / Rate Transparency At TA stage: ensure the TA does not cap Changeis's labor rates or lock in pricing before the subcontract is negotiated. Rates should be "to be negotiated in good faith" or reference Changeis's published GSA/OASIS+ schedules. Important
Disputes & Governing Law Confirm governing law (Virginia or Maryland are common; avoid unusual jurisdictions). Disputes should require escalation to executive-level before litigation. Alternative dispute resolution clause (mediation/arbitration) before litigation reduces cost. Important
Termination for Convenience BAH's right to terminate Changeis's subcontract "for convenience" should be limited to situations where the government terminates BAH's prime contract. BAH should not have unilateral termination-for-convenience at will against Changeis. Critical
Liability Caps Mutual and proportional limitations on consequential damages. Watch for caps that only limit BAH's liability while leaving Changeis exposed. Important
Indemnification Each party indemnifies the other for its own actions/negligence. Changeis should not indemnify BAH for BAH's prime-level decisions or government interactions. Important
Amendment / Assignment No assignment of this TA without Changeis's written consent. If BAH is acquired or merges, the TA should not automatically transfer to the acquirer. Standard

FAA-Specific Government Flow-Down Requirements

FAA contracts flow down specific requirements that differ from standard FAR/DFARS. Key provisions Changeis should confirm are appropriately (not excessively) flowed down:

RequirementChangeis ActionRisk if Missed
FAA AMS (Acquisition Management System) FAA uses its own AMS, not the standard FAR. Confirm which AMS provisions are being flowed to Changeis and that they are limited to those legally required. Over-broad flow-down of prime-level obligations to sub
Security & Suitability Requirements FAA IT contracts require background investigations. Confirm the process, timeline, and cost responsibility for obtaining clearances/suitability determinations. Delays in personnel start; cost exposure if not accounted for
Ethics & Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) FAA is strict on OCI for IT modernization contracts. Confirm that the TA includes mutual OCI representations and a mitigation plan obligation if OCI is identified. Changeis could be disqualified if undisclosed OCI surfaces post-award
Record Retention & Audit Access Government retains audit rights on subcontractors. Ensure the audit provision is limited to records relevant to this contract and does not expose unrelated Changeis business records. Broad audit clauses could expose Changeis's other client data
Cybersecurity Requirements (NIST SP 800-171) For FAA IT systems, contractors typically must comply with NIST 800-171 or higher. Confirm compliance status and any required System Security Plans (SSPs). Non-compliance = contract performance risk
Prompt Payment Act Flow-Down FAA prime contracts include prompt payment obligations. The TA should state BAH will pay Changeis within 7 days of receiving government payment on work attributable to Changeis. Payment delays; cash flow risk for small business
Buy American / TAA Compliance Confirm any ATLAS-specific domestic preference requirements flow down appropriately and that Changeis has a clear path to compliance. Potential compliance violations at subcontract level

4 · Red Flags — Stop and Negotiate Before Signing
These are deal-breakers or serious concerns. Do not sign if any of these appear without resolution:

  • Broad exclusivity beyond ATLAS ChBA — Any language restricting Changeis from teaming on "any FAA opportunity" or "any opportunity where BAH competes" is an unacceptable overreach. Deal-Breaker
  • "Good faith negotiation" only — no subcontract guarantee — If the TA only commits BAH to "negotiate in good faith" for a subcontract post-award (no mandatory subcontract award), Changeis has no enforceable right to work. Courts (see CGI v. FCi Federal, 2018) will not compel subcontract award. Deal-Breaker
  • Work share percentage is absent or non-binding — Vague language like "Changeis will perform support services" without minimum percentages, CLINs, or defined functional areas provides no protection. Deal-Breaker
  • Assignment of Changeis IP to BAH — Any clause transferring ownership of Changeis methodologies, tools, past performance narratives, or technical approaches to BAH must be struck. Deal-Breaker
  • One-sided non-solicitation or non-compete — If non-solicitation applies only to Changeis (not BAH) or non-compete is broader than this contract, reject it. Deal-Breaker
  • BAH holds all subcontract decision rights unilaterally — "Prime reserves the right to replace subcontractor at its sole discretion" clauses are red flags. BAH can replace only for cause or if the government mandates it. Deal-Breaker
  • No automatic exclusivity release if BAH does not submit — Without this, Changeis could be locked out of other ATLAS teams even if BAH fails to participate. Deal-Breaker
  • Rate caps locking in below-market pricing — If BAH inserts pricing terms at the TA stage (e.g., capped labor rates), this pre-negotiates the subcontract without full information. Rates should be deferred to subcontract negotiation. Watch
  • Unlimited indemnification by Changeis for BAH's actions — Changeis should only indemnify for its own acts/omissions. Cross-indemnification for BAH's prime-level liability is inappropriate. Watch
  • Over-broad confidentiality obligations — Restrictions that prevent Changeis from discussing its own general capabilities or past performance with other potential clients. Watch
  • Governing law in a BAH-favorable jurisdiction — Virginia is common and acceptable; check for unusual jurisdictions. Watch

On Work Share

Comment 3 — Work Share Guarantee (Section [X])
"Section [X] commits only to 'good faith negotiation' of a subcontract. Changeis requests that this be revised to: 'Upon award of the Prime Contract to BAH, BAH shall award Changeis a subcontract for no less than [X]% of total contract value, covering the following functional areas: [list ATO mission support functions]. BAH's obligation to award a subcontract is firm and not subject to further negotiation.'"
Rationale: 'Good faith negotiation' is unenforceable per multiple court decisions. Changeis is contributing significant past performance IP; a firm subcontract commitment is the required quid pro quo.
Comment 4 — Minimum Work Share Percentage
"Please confirm: what minimum work share percentage is BAH committing to for Changeis? Changeis's contribution to the Phase 1 corporate experience scoring (ATO operational knowledge across ATAPS II and ATEPS II) justifies a minimum guaranteed share of [proposed %]. This should be stated as a contractual minimum, not a target."
Rationale: Without a numeric commitment, workshare is theoretical and has no legal weight.
Comment 5 — Option Exercise Obligation
"Please add language confirming: 'BAH shall exercise Changeis's subcontract option periods consistent with the government's exercise of BAH's prime contract options. Changeis's option periods shall not be terminated for convenience while BAH's corresponding prime options remain active.'"
Rationale: Protects Changeis from being cut off mid-contract while BAH continues performing.

On IP & Confidentiality

Comment 6 — Background IP Ownership
"Changeis requires explicit language confirming: 'Each party retains sole ownership of its Background Intellectual Property, including pre-existing methodologies, tools, processes, and frameworks. No Background IP is transferred, assigned, or licensed to the other party except as specifically required for proposal preparation for this specific solicitation, and only for the duration of this Agreement.'"
Rationale: Non-negotiable protection for Changeis's proprietary management consulting and ATO support methodologies.
Comment 7 — Past Performance Narratives
"Changeis's past performance write-ups contributed to the BAH proposal shall remain Changeis's property and may not be used by BAH in any other proposal, bid, or response to any solicitation, without Changeis's prior written consent."
Rationale: Changeis's FAA ATO past performance is a core competitive asset — its reuse by BAH without permission is commercially damaging.

On Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation

Comment 8 — Non-Compete Scope (Section [X])
"Changeis requests that Section [X] be revised to limit any non-compete obligation to: (a) this specific ATLAS ChBA solicitation only; (b) for the duration of this Agreement only; and (c) in the specific functional work areas designated for Changeis under this TA. Changeis cannot accept restrictions on pursuing or accepting work on any other FAA contract vehicle."
Rationale: Standard market practice. Unrestricted non-competes in government contracting TAs are overbroad and may raise antitrust concerns.
Comment 9 — Non-Solicitation Mutuality
"Please confirm the non-solicitation clause applies equally and symmetrically to both parties. If it currently applies only to Changeis, we request it be made mutual: neither BAH nor Changeis shall directly solicit the other's named key personnel during the TA term and for 12 months following termination."
Rationale: One-sided non-solicitation provisions are inequitable and signal bad faith in the partnership.

On Termination & Pricing

Comment 10 — Termination for Convenience Limitation
"Changeis requests that BAH's right to terminate Changeis's subcontract 'for convenience' be limited to circumstances where: (a) the government terminates BAH's prime contract or the relevant portion thereof; (b) the government's requirements change such that Changeis's scope is eliminated; or (c) a government-directed modification makes Changeis's work technically infeasible. BAH should not have unilateral termination-for-convenience authority independent of government direction."
Rationale: Protects Changeis from being removed after contributing proposal work, particularly if a competing subcontractor offers BAH a better commercial deal.
Comment 11 — Pricing / Rate Deferral
"Changeis notes that Section [X] references [pricing/rate terms]. Changeis requests that all rate, pricing, and compensation terms be deferred to subcontract negotiation following prime contract award, when scope is better defined. Any rates included in this TA should be non-binding estimates only, with final rates to be negotiated consistent with Changeis's published GSA or OASIS+ rates."
Rationale: Locking in rates at TA stage, before full scope is known, can result in below-market pricing that disadvantages Changeis over a 10-year performance period.

General / Administrative

Comment 12 — OCI Representations
"Please add mutual OCI representations: 'Each party represents that, to the best of its knowledge, its participation in this team arrangement does not create an Organizational Conflict of Interest with respect to the ATLAS solicitation. Each party agrees to promptly notify the other if any potential OCI is identified and to cooperate in good faith on any required mitigation plan.'"
Rationale: FAA is particularly OCI-sensitive on IT modernization contracts. Proactive OCI management protects the team's eligibility.

Arguments FOR Teaming with BAH on ATLAS

  • BAH is a credible prime — One of 5 large primes on FAA SETIS; deep FAA SE2020/SE2025 history; strong institutional relationships with FAA leadership.
  • Changeis is highly relevant to ATLAS — ATO mission support, technical analysis, and program management expertise (ATAPS II, ATEPS II) maps directly to ATLAS's mission-support modernization objectives.
  • Access to a 10-year, large-scale contract — ATLAS's scope (200+ apps, 3,000 databases) and duration (10 years) represents a long-term revenue anchor that would be very difficult for Changeis to compete for as a prime.
  • Phase 1 already closed (March 10) — BAH has already submitted or is in process; Changeis's inclusion post-Phase 1 may be as additional teaming depth for later phases (Phase 2/3 demonstration). Clarify which phase this TA is for.
  • WOSB diversity value — On an unrestricted contract, BAH may need to show small/diverse business participation for scoring purposes. Changeis as a WOSB strengthens BAH's subcontracting plan.

Arguments Against / Risks to Manage

  • ATLAS is unrestricted — Changeis gets no set-aside protection. As a sub, Changeis's work share depends entirely on BAH's discretion. Contract protections in the TA are the only safeguard.
  • BAH has a full in-house bench — BAH may not need Changeis for technical execution, only for proposal scoring (past performance, small business credit). Post-award workshare reduction is a real risk without firm commitments.
  • Phase 1 deadline has passed — If BAH's Phase 1 submission is already in, Changeis's leverage to negotiate TA terms is lower (the value Changeis's past performance added is already captured in the proposal). BAH has less incentive to offer strong workshare commitments now.
  • Exclusivity risk to CLMRS — If ATLAS TA exclusivity language is broad, it could freeze Changeis out of the CLMRS re-compete (~2027), which is a high-fit, natural follow-on opportunity.
  • Challenge-Based Acquisition = long timeline — 4 phases, with Phase 3 requiring live demonstrations. BAH could be eliminated mid-process, leaving Changeis in limbo if exclusivity isn't properly scoped.

Strategic Recommendation

Proceed with ATLAS teaming, but only with tight TA protections. The opportunity is strategically sound — the contract size and duration are compelling, and Changeis's FAA ATO expertise is a genuine differentiator for BAH's team. However, Changeis must insist on: (1) binding work share commitment (not just good faith negotiation), (2) narrow exclusivity scoped to ATLAS only with automatic release triggers, and (3) explicit CLMRS carve-out. Do not sign a TA that trades Changeis's proposal contribution for vague future workshare promises.

Priority: Resolve Comments 1–5 (Exclusivity + Work Share) before any other negotiation. These are the load-bearing provisions.
