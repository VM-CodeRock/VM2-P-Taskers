What This Does

Every Sunday at 8:00 AM ET, VM2 checks the CCC Tennis Events calendar for next Sunday's Cardio Tennis session, reviews your Outlook calendar for conflicts, and emails you with session details and your schedule. You reply YES to sign up or NO to skip. VM2 never auto-registers — you always have the final say.

Step 1 — Check CCC Tennis Events Calendar

VM2 browses the CCC Tennis Events calendar (ssid=323474) at ccclub.org to find the Cardio Tennis session for the following Sunday. Looks for event name containing "Cardio Tennis," typically scheduled 9:30 AM – 11:00 AM.

Step 2 — Check Outlook for Conflicts

VM2 queries your Microsoft 365 Outlook calendar for the target Sunday. Lists all events to surface potential conflicts.

Step 3 — Send Confirmation Email

VM2 emails varunm24@gmail.com with:

  • Cardio Tennis session details (date, time, location, available spots if visible)
  • Your full calendar for that Sunday
  • A clear YES/NO prompt: "Reply YES to sign up, or NO to skip"

Step 4 — Registration (On YES Reply)

If you reply YES, VM2 logs into ccclub.org using stored credentials and completes the Cardio Tennis registration. Captures the confirmation number.

Step 5 — Calendar & Confirmation

On successful registration, VM2 adds the event to your Outlook calendar and sends a confirmation reply with the reservation number.

Design Decisions
Why No Auto Sign-Up?

Originally designed to auto-register, the workflow was revised at Varun's request on March 22, 2026. Reason: Varun wants to check his Sunday schedule for conflicts before committing. The email confirmation step gives him full control while VM2 still handles the grunt work of checking availability and surfacing calendar conflicts.

Duplicate Prevention

During the initial sign-up attempt, VM2 registered for March 29 Cardio Tennis (Confirmation #3823890-2538802) but the CCC website indicated Varun was already part of an existing reservation, suggesting a possible duplicate. This experience informed the decision to always confirm before registering.

Todoist Integration

A recurring "CCC Cardio Calendar" task in Todoist serves as a backup trigger. The weekly cron is the primary mechanism; the Todoist task provides visibility in the task management system.
