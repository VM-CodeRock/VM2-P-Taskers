Five Key Changes — Impact on Changeis

1. Award Structure Expanded — New Emerging Large Business Lane Mildly Positive

Awards per domain increased from 50 to 70, with SB slots rising to 25. A new Emerging Large Business (ELB) lane reserves 15 of 30 large business slots. Socio-economic subcategories (WOSB, SDVOSB, 8(a), HUBZone) have been removed from the SB lane.

Changeis Impact

The expansion to 25 SB awards per domain improves Changeis's odds. However, the removal of socio-economic subcategories means all small businesses compete head-to-head without set-aside advantages. Changeis should verify whether it qualifies as Emerging Large Business (if revenues have crossed NAICS thresholds) for additional opportunities in that lane.

2. Domain Renamed — Eligibility Reframed Low Impact

"Technical Services" has been renamed to "Engineering, Logistics and Operational Services" (NAICS 541330 unchanged). Joint venture language has been added.

Changeis Impact

Minimal direct impact—541330 is not a Changeis NAICS code. The JV language is relevant only if Changeis considers teaming with an engineering firm, but the primary pursuit should remain in 541512.

3. Qualifying Project Rules Revised Highly Positive

Draft 5 introduces formal LOE vs. Outcome-Based qualifying project distinctions. Subcontract-based QPs are now permitted for SBs, ELBs, and CSVs. The prior 50% bid-rate off-ramp requirement has been removed.

Most Favorable Change for Changeis

CLMRS ($62M ceiling) and ATAPS II ($103M ceiling) both easily exceed the $2.5M minimum and $10M max-point thresholds for SBs. Changeis's work includes both LOE and Outcome-Based delivery. If any Army subcontract experience exists, it now qualifies as a QP under Draft 5—this should be assessed immediately.

4. Off-Ramp Criteria Expanded Mixed

The 50% bid-rate threshold has been removed. New triggers include: contractor-induced cost overruns, marginal/unsatisfactory CPARS, vacancy rates ≥10%, time-to-fill ≥60 days, and CMMC compliance lapses.

Changeis Impact

Removing the bid-rate requirement is positive—Changeis would struggle to bid on every Army task order given its DOT-focused pipeline. However, the operational thresholds (vacancy rate, time-to-fill, CMMC) are continuous 10-year obligations requiring dedicated compliance infrastructure.

5. Scorecard and Price Evaluation Revised Positive

Approved Rate Agreements and EVMS certifications removed. New elements include updated point allocations, business-size-specific dollar thresholds ($10M for SBs), and price as an evaluated factor for the first time.

Changeis Impact

Removal of Approved Rate Agreements levels the playing field for Changeis. The $10M SB threshold means CLMRS and ATAPS II both earn maximum dollar-value points (4,000 per QP). However, the addition of price as an evaluated factor introduces a new variable that will require competitive pricing strategy. ISO 9001, ISO 27001, and CMMI Level 3 remain strong scoring assets.

Part 2: DOT SWES 2.0 — $548M Software Engineering BPA

SWES 2.0 (Software Engineering Services 2.0) is the Department of Transportation's successor to the original SWES BPA, expanding from FHWA-only to all DOT Operating Administrations except FAA. It covers the full spectrum of application software engineering support—development, modernization, DevOps, cloud-native apps, testing, and maintenance.

High Priority — Strong Natural Fit

SWES 2.0 directly aligns to Changeis's core delivery model: Agile/SAFe software engineering, DevSecOps, cloud-native development, application modernization, and DOT domain expertise. Strategic Context

Portfolio Diversification Opportunity

SWES 2.0 represents a strategic vehicle to reduce Changeis's FAA revenue concentration. Winning a SWES 2.0 BPA would open doors to FHWA, FMCSA, FRA, FTA, and other OAs—diversifying the DOT portfolio beyond CLMRS. Combined with the CLMRS re-compete (expected 2027), this creates a two-prong DOT growth strategy.

MAPS, while conditional, offers entry into the Army market—a significant expansion beyond DOT. The new Emerging Large Business lane and subcontract QP rules make this more accessible to DOT-focused firms than previous iterations. However, the gate requirements (FCL, CMMC) are binary constraints that must be resolved before committing proposal resources.
