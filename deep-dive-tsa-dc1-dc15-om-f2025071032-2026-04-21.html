1 · Executive Summary

TSA's Office of the Chief Information Officer is recompeting a Firm Fixed Price task order on the DHS Data Center and Cloud Optimization (DCCO) IDIQ (contract 70RTAC21D00000006) to continue Operations and Maintenance support for its two primary data center environments: DC1 (Government-owned, contractor-operated facility at NASA Stennis Space Center, Stennis, MS) and DC15 (a separate colocation or secondary facility). The O&M fees cover space, connectivity, and power for TSA's production, test, and development infrastructure — essentially facility-level colocation fees and managed hosting charges, not IT application support.

The DCCO IDIQ is a single-award, single-contractor vehicle — awarded exclusively to Peraton (via Perspecta Engineering Inc.) with a $2.685B ceiling. This is not a multi-award vehicle where Changeis could compete for task orders. Only Peraton can be prime on DCCO task orders.

The RAG score of 0.6536 (STRONG) is a classification artifact from NAICS 541519 and general IT O&M terminology overlapping with Changeis's Volpe/DOT portfolio — it does not reflect genuine data center infrastructure O&M capability alignment. The top RAG match (FMCSA Safety Programs, V3272120) is a website and SharePoint development task order, which bears no functional resemblance to physical data center colocation O&M.

NO-GO — Vehicle-Gated: The DCCO IDIQ is a single-award contract held exclusively by Peraton. Changeis cannot compete as prime or sub on DCCO task orders. This is a structural exclusion, not a capability gap. Archive and monitor for future vehicle recompete.

What Is Being Procured: This task order pays for the space, connectivity, and power that TSA's mission-critical equipment occupies in DC1 and DC15. These are colocation/co-hosting fees — the facilities management and utility costs of running physical servers in DHS-managed data center facilities. The APFS description is explicit: "O&M fees are paid to the data center service provider" and "Operations and Maintenance are provided by an external services Task Order IDIQ awarded by DHS." This is fundamentally a facilities and managed hosting contract, not an IT services or consulting contract.

DC1 Facility Context: DHS Data Center 1 (DC1) is a Government-owned, contractor-operated data center at NASA's Stennis Space Center in Mississippi. DHS leases approximately 36,000 sq ft of raised floor space within this facility, which also hosts other federal agencies. NASA owns and manages the physical structure; Peraton operates the computing and hosting environment under the DCCO IDIQ.

DC15 Context: DC15 is a secondary or colocation facility. USAspending records show that DHS also procures colocation services at third-party facilities (e.g., DHS SV10 in San Jose) through the DCCO IDIQ, suggesting DC15 may be a leased colocation site. Both facilities together support TSA's production, test, and development environments.

Solicitation Status Alert: The estimated solicitation release was April 1, 2026 — already passed as of this analysis date (April 21, 2026). Check SAM.gov for any active solicitation posted under DCCO IDIQ or TSA CIO. The task order may already be in active procurement as a directed modification to Peraton under the existing vehicle.

Is This a Colo-Provider Recompete? This is not a commercial colocation provider recompete (e.g., Equinix, Digital Realty, CyrusOne, QTS). DHS DC1 is Government-owned infrastructure at NASA Stennis — not a commercial colo. The O&M fees are paid by DHS HQ to Peraton for managed hosting services within a Government facility, not to a commercial colo vendor. DC15 may involve third-party colo (as evidenced by DCCO task orders for facilities in San Jose), but even those flow through the Peraton DCCO vehicle, not directly to the commercial provider.
