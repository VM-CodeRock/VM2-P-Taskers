About Drone Analyst

Drone Analyst (droneanalyst.com), founded as Skylogic Research by Colin Snow, was the commercial drone industry's longest-running independent research firm. Its annual Drone Market Sector Reports (2017-2021) were the industry benchmark for UAS adoption data. The blog has been dormant since 2022, so this brief supplements its historical analysis with current FAA, DOT, and White House primary sources from 2025-2026.

Key Industry Trends

1. BVLOS Regulatory Breakthrough: FAA Part 108 / Part 146 HIGH

The FAA published its landmark NPRM for Part 108 "Normalizing UAS BVLOS Operations" on August 7, 2025 -- the most significant drone regulation since Part 107 in 2016. This 700+ page proposed rule enables routine commercial BVLOS operations without individual waivers. The companion Part 146 creates a certification framework for Automated Data Service Providers (ADSPs) providing UTM services. Comment periods closed October 2025 and February 2026; final rule expected mid-2026.

Changeis Implication: Part 146's ADSP certification creates a new class of FAA-regulated digital service providers who must implement Safety Management Systems, cybersecurity programs, quality management frameworks, and interoperable data exchange -- all areas where Changeis has demonstrated capability through CLMRS. The regulatory infrastructure buildout will require IT support contracts for compliance systems, data pipeline development, and certification tooling.

2. FAA Reorganization: New Office of Advanced Aviation Technologies HIGH

In January 2026, DOT Secretary Duffy and FAA Administrator Bedford announced the largest FAA organizational restructuring in agency history under "Flight Plan 2026." Key changes: a new Office of Advanced Aviation Technologies (OAAT) consolidating UAS/eVTOL/supersonic integration; a new Airspace Modernization Office for NAS modernization; and an Aviation Safety Office under a new Associate Administrator.

Changeis Implication: The creation of OAAT establishes a new, consolidated FAA client office with significant technology needs. Changeis's established relationships within FAA ATO (via CLMRS and ATAPS II) position them to support this new office's program analysis, technology assessment, and IT support requirements. ATAPS II task orders could expand into OAAT scope.

3. FAA AI Deployment for UAS Waiver Processing HIGH

Executive Order 14307 (June 2025) mandated FAA deploy AI tools within 120 days to streamline UAS waiver reviews under Part 107. The AI tools are required to support performance- and risk-based evaluation, precedent identification, mitigation recommendation, and category pattern recognition. This is a direct, EO-mandated IT modernization initiative.

Changeis Implication: This is one of Changeis's highest-opportunity areas. The EO-mandated AI deployment for UAS waiver processing is essentially an AI/ML engineering and data analytics contract -- Changeis's core Digital Factory capabilities (AWS/ROSA cloud, DevSecOps, Agile/SAFe) are directly applicable. Pursue via ATAPS II task order or new FAA IT procurement.

4. UTM Ecosystem Buildout and ADSP Market Creation HIGH

The FAA is building the digital backbone for routine low-altitude drone operations through UAS Traffic Management (UTM). Components include LAANC (already operational), ADSP certification under Part 146, real-time surveillance data exchange, and integration pathways to legacy ATC systems. This creates an entirely new digital infrastructure layer in the NAS.

Changeis Implication: UTM/ADSP infrastructure requires the same technology stack Changeis deploys for FAA's Digital Factory: cloud-based microservices (AWS/ROSA), APIs for data interoperability, DevSecOps pipelines, cybersecurity frameworks, and continuous compliance monitoring. This is a multi-year growth area for FAA IT support contracts.

5. Domestic UAS Supply Chain Transformation / ASDA Compliance HIGH

The Trump administration has launched the most aggressive policy campaign to eliminate Chinese-made drones from US government and commercial markets. The American Security Drone Act (ASDA) prohibitions took full effect December 22, 2025. OMB Memorandum M-26-02 established government-wide policy. All federal agencies must audit and replace non-compliant UAS, including DOT modal agencies (FHWA, FMCSA).

Changeis Implication: FAA must audit and replace all non-ASDA-compliant UAS in its fleet. These assets must be tracked, decommissioned, and replaced through FAA's supply chain systems -- which Changeis manages via CLMRS. This is a natural CLMRS scope expansion for UAS equipment tracking and ASDA compliance. Additionally, all DOT modal agencies covered by SWES 2.0 will need similar compliance support.

6. Counter-UAS Technology Surge MEDIUM

Counter-UAS has become a multi-billion-dollar federal priority. DHS launched its Program Executive Office for UAS/C-UAS (January 2026) with $115M for FIFA World Cup 2026 / Americas 250 celebrations. DoD launched a Counter-UAS Marketplace for rapid acquisition. $2B+ in combined federal/SLTT spending expected through FY2028.

Changeis Implication: C-UAS systems require data analytics and IT integration infrastructure: drone detection feeds, threat assessment algorithms, interagency data sharing, command-and-control dashboards, and audit trails. This is an adjacent market where Changeis's cybersecurity and data engineering capabilities apply, though it requires DHS/DoD client development.

7. Advanced Air Mobility / eVTOL Integration MEDIUM

The DOT released the Advanced Air Mobility National Strategy in December 2025 -- a 10-year roadmap (2027-2035) for integrating eVTOL, powered-lift aircraft, and high-autonomy UAS. In March 2026, FAA selected 8 eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP) projects across 26 states, involving Archer, BETA, Electra, and Joby, with operations beginning summer 2026.

Changeis Implication: The AAM National Strategy calls for FAA's ATC modernization to incorporate service-based architecture for low-altitude traffic management -- a multi-year IT transformation. Longer-horizon opportunity but positions Changeis for next-gen NAS modernization work. The strategy also calls for workforce development and digital infrastructure, both within Changeis's capability.

8. FAA UAS Test Site Program Expansion MEDIUM

FAA expanded its UAS Test Site network from 7 to 9 sites through the UASTS 2.0 competitive selection (SIR 697DCK-26-R-00019). Sites support BVLOS technology development, counter-UAS detection research, autonomous operations, and AAM test infrastructure development using Other Transaction Agreements.

Changeis Implication: The test site BAA mechanism offers a direct, lower-barrier entry point for Changeis to support UAS technology validation -- particularly in data analytics, UTM system testing, and safety case development. Teaming with an established test site operator would provide access to FAA R&D funding.

Strategic Takeaway

Bottom Line for Changeis

The UAS/drone sector is generating the largest wave of new FAA IT procurement in a decade. The convergence of BVLOS rulemaking, FAA reorganization, ASDA compliance mandates, and the AI/automation executive order creates a window of opportunity that maps directly to Changeis's existing capabilities and client relationships. The three immediate actions require no new contract vehicles -- they leverage CLMRS and ATAPS II.

Longer term, the UTM ecosystem buildout and ATC modernization represent multi-year, multi-billion-dollar IT programs where Changeis's FAA Digital Factory positioning is a genuine competitive advantage that few small businesses can match.
