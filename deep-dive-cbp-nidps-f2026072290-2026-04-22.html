The requirement covers contractor support to streamline and automate the entire life cycle of NIDPS and CMIR documents: receiving physical forms, digitizing/scanning, entering data electronically, and transmitting to CBP — while minimizing labor costs and maximizing technological efficiencies. This is a labor-intensive, high-volume document digitization operation, not an IT development contract.

NIDPS & CMIR Background — What Is This System?

NIDPS (Non-Immigrant Data Processing System) is CBP's legacy system for processing paper arrival/departure forms submitted by non-citizens at U.S. ports of entry — principally Form I-94 (Arrival/Departure Record) at land border crossings. Air and sea ports of entry shifted to fully electronic I-94 issuance in 2013, but land border crossings still generate paper I-94 forms that must be manually digitized and entered into CBP's passenger vetting systems.

CMIR (Currency and Monetary Instruments Report) is FinCEN Form 105, required of travelers who bring more than $10,000 in currency or monetary instruments into or out of the U.S. CBP collects these paper forms at ports of entry; they must be digitized, entered, and transmitted to Treasury/FinCEN for anti-money-laundering and anti-trafficking enforcement.

Together, NIDPS and CMIR 2.0 represent the paper document digitization backlog that still flows from physical ports of entry. The Passenger Systems Program Office (PSPO) within CBP's Office of Information Technology (OIT) owns and operates these systems and manages the data entry service contract.

Operational Context
Per SourceAmerica, Coleman Data Solutions (operating as Coleman Professional Services) receives daily shipments of physical documents from CBP. Workers — over 30 people with disabilities — sort documents into 20 categories, operate high-speed scanners, and manually key handwritten form data into CBP systems. The operation runs continuously to prevent backlog accumulation. This is mission-critical, high-accuracy work tightly integrated into CBP's passenger vetting and financial intelligence pipelines.
Vehicle & Contract Analysis
DISQUALIFYING FACTOR: AbilityOne Mandatory Source (FAR 8.2)
The AbilityOne Program (Javits-Wagner-O'Day Act, 41 U.S.C. §§ 8501–8506) creates a statutory mandatory source requirement. Federal agencies must purchase services on the Procurement List from designated nonprofit agencies employing people with disabilities. CBP has no discretion to compete this requirement while it remains on the AbilityOne Procurement List. There is no mechanism for a commercial firm to compete for or team on an AbilityOne direct award.
