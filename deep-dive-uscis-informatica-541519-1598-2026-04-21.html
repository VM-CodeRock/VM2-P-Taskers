USCIS Informatica Software Maintenance Renewal

APFS F2025071598 · NAICS 541519 · NASA SEWP V · 8(a)
Created: 2026-04-21
1. Executive Summary
Opportunity at a Glance: USCIS is recompeting its annual Informatica software license and maintenance renewal — a sole-source follow-on that has been held by Govsmart, Inc. (HUBZone, CIO-CS) for at least two consecutive years. The FY2026 award is expected via NASA SEWP V, set aside for 8(a) competition, at an estimated value of $1M–$2M (prior year award: $847,984). Solicitation is expected September 7, 2026 with award by September 21, 2026.

Informatica's IICS (Intelligent Cloud Services) platform powers USCIS's enterprise data integration, ETL/ELT pipelines, data catalog, and business intelligence foundation — including the broader PLANXS/DBIS data warehouse ecosystem. The software is mission-embedded, making displacement unlikely. This is a pure software reseller/VAR opportunity: no professional services, no labor — margin lives in the reseller discount and vehicle positioning.

The RAG score of 0.59 (MODERATE) reflects Changeis's past performance in adjacent maintenance and software support domains but no direct Informatica reseller history on record. The Go/No-Go hinges entirely on whether Changeis holds — or can quickly establish — an Informatica reseller authorization and a SEWP V Group B (8(a)) vehicle or teaming arrangement.

What Is Informatica at USCIS?

Informatica (specifically its Intelligent Cloud Services / IICS platform and legacy PowerCenter ETL suite) serves as the backbone of USCIS's enterprise data pipeline — feeding the eCISCOR data warehouse, supporting the PLANXS/DBIS analytics ecosystem, and enabling data governance across 100+ mission applications. Informatica holds a FedRAMP Moderate authorization and is explicitly cited as a USCIS reference customer for its cloud data foundation. The software supports data and BI services across USCIS OIT's Camp Springs operations.

4. Competitive Landscape

This is a narrow, specialized market: winning requires (1) an active Informatica reseller authorization, (2) an eligible 8(a) certification (or teaming with an 8(a) prime), and (3) a SEWP V Group B slot. The pool of qualified 8(a) Informatica resellers on SEWP V is small. Past awards confirm single-bidder responses, indicating a sticky incumbent dynamic.

6. Strategic Considerations & Market Context
Opportunity Dynamics

Vehicle Switch = Opening

  • Prior awards (FY2024, FY2025) were on NIH CIO-CS; the forecast now specifies NASA SEWP V — this may reflect USCIS's broader vehicle preference shift
  • Govsmart holds SEWP V HUBZone, not 8(a); the 8(a) set-aside creates a crack
  • Both prior awards had only 1 bidder — strong incumbent lock, but openness to competition via 8(a) requirement
  • The $847K FY2025 award was notably lower than $1.44M FY2024 — possible license reduction or renegotiation
Software Context

IICS is Sticky & Growing

  • Informatica is deeply embedded in USCIS's PLANXS/DBIS data warehouse — a $100M+ professional services ecosystem depends on this layer
  • USCIS is actively migrating to cloud (IICS FedRAMP Moderate); license value likely to grow
  • Adjacent ICE contract (FCN, $591K, FY2025) confirms DHS-wide Informatica footprint
  • No displacement risk from open-source alternatives on this timeline — pure renewal
Margin Profile

Reseller Economics

  • Typical federal software reseller margins: 3–12% depending on partner tier and volume discount
  • On a $1M award, gross margin ~$30K–$120K — low effort, high predictability
  • FFP structure eliminates labor risk; performance risk is near-zero (just pass-through licenses)
  • Annual renewal creates a recurring revenue stream if won
