Critical Finding: Q&A Backlog Validates Turner's Claim 1

The CHESS portal document confirms: 1,373 questions submitted, only 170 answered (12.4% response rate). This validates Robert Turner's open letter claim that the Q&A backlog is a material unfairness issue. The government response window closes April 17 — with 1,203 questions unanswered at that point.

Document Overview: The 312-page CHESS portal Q&A document covers all questions submitted by offerors to the MAPS IDIQ solicitation (W15P7T-26-R-A006) between approximately March 30 and April 16, 2026. Questions span all five MAPS domains, multiple business size categories (Large, Emerging Large, Small Business), and every major component of the self-scoring evaluation framework.

Key Findings

  • ISO requirement is firm. The government explicitly declined to substitute CMMC Level 2 for ISO certifications. ISO 27001:2022 (and ISO 9001 for Management domain) remain mandatory gate criteria. This is the decisive answer for Changeis's ELB vs. SB lane decision.
  • No subcontractors permitted — at all. The government confirmed multiple times that offerors shall not propose subcontractors for this solicitation. Prime must independently meet ALL screening criteria (CMMC, ISO, facility clearance, accounting system). This overrides prior assumptions about teaming strategies.
  • JV ISO reliance confirmed. Mentor-Protégé JVs CAN rely on the managing/mentor partner's ISO 9001:2013 or ISO 9001:2015 certification. This is a significant positive clarification for ArchZen.
  • Total Contract Value = includes unexercised options. "Total Contract Value" for QP purposes includes ALL options, exercised or not. Not limited to obligations. Critical for QP dollar threshold calculations.
  • CMMC conditional not acceptable. Final CMMC Level 2 (Self) certification required. Conditional assessments do not pass the screening gate.
  • CPARS pulled by government — except for QPs completing May 1–30. Offerors do NOT submit CPARS. The government pulls them. Exception: QPs expected to complete between May 1–30, 2026 must have CPARS submitted.
  • Tie-breaker excludes "Regulatory Compliance" and (for ELB/SB) "Small Business Subcontracting" CPAR elements. These are excluded from both the 10% marginal screening and the tie-breaker percentage calculation.
  • Pricing: no cost volume required. Only the $50–$100 FFP postaward conference price in Attachment 0001 is required. No additional pricing, cost breakdown, or pricing volume.
  • Deadline extension request denied. The government declined a two-week extension request, stating it "will not be changing the requirements" and the solicitation "maximizes competition." May 1, 2026 5:00 PM EDT remains the deadline.
  • Q&A answers posted live, not necessarily in order. Government confirmed answers are posted as available, not sequentially. Monitor CHESS portal continuously through April 17.

Subcontracting Rules — No Subcontractors Permitted (23 answered)

Definitive Government Position

The government confirmed repeatedly and unambiguously: "Offerors shall not propose subcontractors for this Solicitation." This applies to:

  • CMMC / facility clearance certifications (prime must independently hold all)
  • Past performance / QPs (subcontractor QPs are NOT eligible)
  • Large Business primes cannot use subcontractor certifications to meet screening criteria
Q — Submitted 4/3/2026
Is a firm permitted to submit a solo proposal as a Prime Contractor while also participating as a Subcontractor on another proposal submitted by a Large Business?
A — Govt. Response (4/6/2026) No Subcontractors
"Please refer to Solicitation Section L.1.1 — 'One Contract Per Offeror'. Also, please note Offerors shall not propose subcontractors for this Solicitation."
Q — Submitted 4/3/2026
How many Qualified Projects (QPs) may a Prime Contractor utilize from its proposed Subcontractor(s)?
A — Govt. Response (4/6/2026) No Subcontractors
"Please see the answer above regarding subcontractors." (Zero — subcontractors are not permitted.)
Q — Submitted 4/2/2026
If a subcontract is used as a QP, how does one respond to M.5.6 "passthrough rate" since the offeror would not be the prime?
A — Govt. Response (4/6/2026) Clarified — QP as Sub
"For QPs where the Offeror performed as a Subcontractor, the Total Incurred Labor Dollars reflects the total labor performed by the Offeror and any second tier subcontracts they let under its subcontract." (Note: the offeror may USE a contract where they performed as a sub as a QP — but may not propose subcontractors going forward.)

Is Attachment 003 accepted in lieu of all supporting documentation, or are firms required to submit both Attachment 003 and separate supporting documents?

Contract Value / Dollar Thresholds (3 answered)

Q — Submitted 4/2/2026

ArchZen (MPJV) — Q&A Confirmed Advantages

  • ISO 9001 reliance confirmed: ArchZen can rely on Kaizen Federal Systems' (mentor partner's) ISO 9001:2013/2015 certification for the Management domain QPs.
  • 13 CFR 125.2(g) analysis: The subcontractor exclusion rule applies to new proposal teaming. However, ArchZen as a MPJV may separately argue that its mentor's past performance must be considered per 13 CFR 125.2(g) — this regulatory conflict has NOT been resolved by any Q&A answer (the government did not address it directly). This remains a protest vector if excluded.
  • QP as subcontractor: ArchZen/Kaizen can use contracts where they performed as a subcontractor as QPs (not as new teaming arrangements). Passthrough rate calculation also clarified.

Recommended Lane Decision Logic

  • If Changeis holds ISO 27001:2022 (corporate) AND ISO 9001 (for Management domain) → proceed as ELB prime under domains where Changeis independently qualifies.
  • If ISO issue is unresolved → ArchZen MPJV (SB lane) is the safer path. Mentor's ISO 9001 is confirmed available for JV reliance. The 2,000-point JV penalty in SB lane remains, but the JV's combined QP strength may more than offset this.
  • Note: The no-subcontractor rule means Changeis cannot use any partner entity's standalone certifications for an ELB proposal — the prime entity must hold all certs independently.
