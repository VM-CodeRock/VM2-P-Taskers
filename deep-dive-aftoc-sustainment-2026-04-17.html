AFTOC System — Program Intelligence

AFTOC (Air Force Total Ownership Cost) is the Air Force's authoritative data system for collecting, compiling, storing, and standardizing cost, logistics, and programmatic data from over 26 disparate sources within the Air Force. It feeds into the DoD-wide Enterprise VAMOSC (EVAMOSC) system overseen by OSD CAPE and is a statutory requirement for Visibility and Management of Operating and Support Costs (VAMOSC) reporting.


Mission: Produce and report VAMOSC data by Air Force weapon system according to the OSD CAPE O&S Cost Guide. This data underpins Independent Cost Estimates (ICEs), Component Cost Analyses (CCAs), Defense Acquisition Board reviews, and annual O&S budget justifications across Air Force programs.


Functional Area Description Complexity
VAMOSC Data Production Collect, process, and publish O&S cost data by weapon system per OSD CAPE O&S Cost Guide structure High
Data Integration Integrate 26+ DoD data sources (finance, logistics, programmatic) into unified AFTOC data model High
Quality Control Implement business rules to ensure data processing accuracy; validate against expected results Medium
IT / ATO Compliance Maintain AFTOC Authority to Operate (ATO) under RMF; ensure CUI, SFIS/SLOA compliance; milCloud 2.0 / DISA environments High
Indirect Cost Management Manage indirect cost processing and maintain AFTOC Metrics Tool Medium
Community Engagement Establish and maintain relationships with AFTOC user community; feedback loops and data usage support Low-Med
Archive Management Maintain data archives; support reprocessing based on DAF rule changes Medium
Database Administration Database management on DISA milCloud 2.0 and Hill AFB UT enclave servers High

Key Compliance Framework: CUI (Controlled Unclassified Information) · SFIS (Standard Financial Information Structure) / SLOA · RMF (Risk Management Framework) · OSD CAPE O&S Cost Guide · EVAMOSC interface standards · DISA milCloud 2.0 AO requirements
