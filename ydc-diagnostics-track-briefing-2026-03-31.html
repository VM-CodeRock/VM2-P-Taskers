Zori AI — The Intelligence Layer

Zori is Fountain Life's proprietary AI medical expert, launched in 2025. It synthesizes each member's clinical data (MRI results, labs, biomarkers, genetics) into a unified health profile. Zori functions as a shared "Google Doc" between physician and patient — both can query it. Members can ask Zori about MRI results, nutrition, medication interactions, or longevity optimization at any time. Clinicians use Zori to track data year-over-year and surface risks that could be missed manually. Per Dr. Kapp: "Up until now, your body and all of these tests are really kind of locked behind the door in medicine. Zori gives you an AI agent that helps you interpret that data."

04 · Suggested Panel Questions for Dr. Kapp (YPO Audience)

Questions are designed to surface the most compelling content for a YPO/C-suite audience focused on diagnostics and longevity. Listed in suggested sequence: opens with personal hook, builds to corporate ROI, closes with forward-looking.

Opening Question · Q1
"Dr. Kapp, you built the predecessor to Fountain Life after attending a single medical conference that changed your thinking. What did you see that most physicians were missing — and why did it take an outsider-entrepreneur to actually build this?"
Why it works: Invites a personal origin story. Gets him into storytelling mode. Sets up the reactive-vs-proactive healthcare framing that anchors everything else.
Core Substance · Q2
"Your data says 14% of asymptomatic, 'healthy' Fountain Life members had life-threatening conditions found at first screening. Walk us through what that actually looks like — what are these findings, and what happens in the first 90 days when something is discovered?"
Why it works: Makes statistics real. Executives are data-driven; a concrete case example from Dr. Kapp will be memorable. Opens door for the Nazarian aneurysm story.
AI Deep-Dive · Q3
"Zori is your AI that synthesizes every member's diagnostic data. How is Zori changing what a doctor can actually see — and what's the one clinical finding in the past year that Zori caught that a human likely would have missed?"
Why it works: Cuts through AI hype with a specific, concrete answer. YPO members are sophisticated about AI — they want real examples, not marketing language.
Executive/CEO Angle · Q4
"Many people in this room run companies. You've published data showing $20 million in annual benefit per 1,000 members and a 13:1 return on investment for executive health programs. Make the case to a CEO in this audience: why is this a better capital allocation than, say, a new enterprise software system?"
Why it works: Directly addresses the YPO audience as business operators. Gets Dr. Kapp onto ROI terrain where Fountain Life's data is strongest. May also prompt companies to inquire about corporate programs.
Cancer Detection · Q5
"The GRAIL Galleri test can detect 50+ types of cancer from a single blood draw — before symptoms appear. For someone in this room who has never heard of liquid biopsy, explain what this test is, what it costs, and who should be getting it right now."
Why it works: Galleri is a breakthrough that most executives haven't yet encountered. This question positions Dr. Kapp as an educator, not a salesperson. Highly actionable.
Access / Equity · Q6
"Your current memberships are $10,000–$30,000 a year — necessary to build the business, but not accessible to most. What's your honest roadmap to making this level of diagnostics affordable — $5,000? $1,000? — and how long does that take?"
Why it works: Addresses the elephant in the room. YPO audiences appreciate candor on equity. Dr. Kapp has a real answer (hotel/resort partnerships, insurance integrations, tech cost deflation) — give him space to share it.
Forward-Looking Close · Q7
"Ten years from now, what does a routine physical look like? Is the annual doctor visit dead — or does it evolve into something entirely different because of companies like Fountain Life?"
Why it works: Strong closing question. Invites a visionary answer. YPO members like thinking 10 years out. Dr. Kapp has a well-rehearsed answer here (he believes the first people to live to a healthy 120 are already alive). Great panel ending moment.

07 · YPO Diagnostics Panel — Format & Moderator Tips

Based on hybrid event best practices and YPO wellness event conventions.

1
Keep Panel to 3 Panelists Max

With 2 in-person + 1 virtual (Dr. Kapp), you're already at the recommended maximum. Three panelists allows each sufficient "screen time" without the moderator being stretched thin across formats. Do not add a fourth.

2
Designate a Virtual Producer

A dedicated person on the Zoom/Teams end coordinates with Dr. Kapp before and during the event. They monitor his audio, handle connectivity issues silently, and relay time signals. The moderator should not manage tech logistics live.

3
Large Screen Placement — Non-Negotiable

Dr. Kapp's video feed should be on a screen at stage level, visible to both panelists and the audience. He should feel present in the room, not relegated to a corner monitor. A camera pointed at the floor mic lets him see audience questioners too.

4
Pre-Event Panelist Check-In (Day Before)

30-minute Zoom with all three panelists + moderator. Assign 2–3 topic areas to each panelist so there's no overlap. Confirm the AV setup. Let Dr. Kapp flag any topics he wants to particularly address — he will likely bring strong data on the corporate ROI angle.

5
Moderator: Actively Bridge Virtual to In-Room

After in-person panelists speak, explicitly direct: "Dr. Kapp, from the Fountain Life lens — what does your data show on this?" Never let him go more than 5–6 minutes without an active prompt. YPO audiences respond well to visible cross-panelist debate.

6
Differentiate the Panelists Early

Open with a 60-second intro framing for each panelist so the audience knows who brings what: e.g., "Dr. Kapp operates the country's most comprehensive AI diagnostics platform and has data on 10,000+ executive health scans" vs. the other two panelists' distinct angles. Prevents topic redundancy.

7
Suggested Panel Arc

Segment 1 (15 min): What the diagnostics revolution actually looks like today — use the 14% asymptomatic finding to anchor. Segment 2 (20 min): AI's role — Zori, data synthesis, clinical decision support. Segment 3 (15 min): The executive / corporate lens — ROI, productivity, why this matters for business leaders. Q&A (15 min): Take from both floor and online/virtual audience.

8
YPO Culture Considerations

YPO audiences have high BS detectors and value peer-level exchange over keynote-style presentations. Dr. Kapp should be prompted to share specific patient stories (aneurysm, cancer detection), not just statistics. Consider a "show of hands" moment: "How many of you have had a full-body MRI?" — typically fewer than 10% — then let Dr. Kapp react to that. Highly effective for YPO rooms.
