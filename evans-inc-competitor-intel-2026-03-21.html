3) What could be happening in their business (reasoned interpretation)

SignalWhat it likely meansWhy it matters to Changeis
ATEPS II win is framed as a growth milestone They’re scaling delivery capacity and hiring to serve a long runway, likely aiming to defend the base and expand adjacency work. Incumbent strength in FAA, especially program enablement + change, can raise the bar for differentiation in aviation pursuits.
“Tech modernization + adoption” wedge They’re competing against pure IT shops by claiming adoption/enablement is built-in, and against pure change shops by claiming real technology implementation. Changeis should counter-position with sharper “outcomes proof” and distinct accelerators (methods, tooling, AI enablement).
OASIS+ emphasis They want easier cross-agency entry; GWAC presence becomes a growth engine beyond FAA. Expect more head-to-head overlap in non-FAA buys where the vehicle advantage is decisive.
Compliance signaling (CMMC L2, ISO 9001) They are using certifications as “risk reduction” brand assets, especially for DoD-adjacent work. If Changeis is still “CMMC L2 in progress,” messaging must carefully frame timeline and compensating controls (ISO 27001, etc.).

4) Recommended actions (practical, near-term)

  1. Map overlap: list FAA/DoT programs and typical support categories where Evans claims strength (PMO, workforce optimization, transformation) and identify 2–3 “Changeis wedge” offerings.
  2. Message counter: create a one-page "Why Changeis" that answers Evans’ core claim (“we make adoption a given”) with stronger proof points, and a clearer AI-enabled delivery angle.
  3. Vehicle strategy: ensure Changeis has a simple buyer-path story (prime/sub, partner vehicles) where Evans leans on OASIS+/ATEPS II.
