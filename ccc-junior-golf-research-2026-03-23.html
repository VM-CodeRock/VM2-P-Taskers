Question 1

What is the CCC Junior Golf Program?

The Congressional Country Club Junior Golf Program is a comprehensive, multi-tiered golf development program available exclusively to children of CCC member families. It is led by Stacy Miller-Arndt, LPGA Class A Professional and Junior Golf Director, a position she has held since 1999. Miller-Arndt is a GRAA Top 100 Growth of the Game Teaching Professional, a US Kids Golf Master Top 50 Instructor, and the 2016 LPGA National Junior Golf Leader — among the most credentialed junior golf educators in the Mid-Atlantic region.

Program Philosophy

"We believe in athletes first and golfers second. If we can build an athlete, then we will have a golfer for his or her whole life." — Stacy Miller-Arndt, LPGA, Junior Golf Director, CCC

Certification Program

Junior golfers (ages 17 and under) can earn "Certified Junior Golfer" status by passing a written test on basic rules (water hazards, lateral hazards, out of bounds, cart paths). Certified juniors receive a bag tag and gain expanded course access without requiring a parent on the course. Contact the Junior Golf Office at (301) 469-2308 to schedule the test. Non-certified juniors must always play with an adult.

Facilities

Per the CCC Golf Department profile, junior golfers have access to:

  • The dedicated Junior Indoor Studio: 5 fitting bays (3 with full simulators) and a putting green — a separate facility from the adult Golf Performance Center
  • The Golf Performance Center (opened 2022): 7 bays, 2 simulators, 1,000 sq ft putting green, Trackman, Swing Catalyst
  • Two 18-hole championship courses: the Blue Course (recently renovated, host of 3 US Opens) and the Gold Course
  • Double-sided driving range with 40+ stations, 4 practice putting greens, 2 short game/bunker areas

Age Groups Served — Is 8.5 the Right Age?

The CCC Junior Golf Program spans ages 3 to 17, structured by skill level rather than strict age bracket. At 8.5 years old, Varun's son falls squarely into the program's core developmental target demographic.

Verdict: Excellent Fit

8.5 is one of the best ages to enter a structured junior golf program. Industry consensus — from golf educators and the First Tee organization — identifies 7-9 as the optimal window for formalizing golf instruction. The child's existing love of golf is the single strongest predictor of program success.

Question 4

What Will the Child Learn?

Golf Skills — Progression by Division

Based on CCC's documented clinic curriculum, skill instruction follows a systematic progression:

  • Fundamentals (entry level): Grip, stance, posture, alignment, basic swing mechanics. Putting. Taught via games and challenges to maintain engagement.
  • Short game: Chipping, pitching, sand/bunker play, putting distance control. Dedicated Short Game Clinics cover each discipline separately.
  • Full swing: Ball flight principles (high/low, draw/fade), contact quality, distance development appropriate to age and physique.
  • Advanced (9-hole and up): Course management, shot selection by lie, launch angle optimization, tournament preparation. Video analysis and Trackman data used at the Indoor Studio.

Rules and Etiquette

  • Golf course etiquette: pace of play, silence during others' shots, repairing divots and ball marks, raking bunkers
  • Rules of golf: water/lateral hazards, out of bounds, cart paths (required for Junior Certification)
  • Scoring accuracy and self-reporting of penalties — a foundational element of golf's integrity culture
  • Private club dress code and social expectations — relevant given CCC's formal environment

Life Skills

Research from junior golf educators consistently identifies these developmental benefits at ages 7-9:

  • Emotional regulation: Handling frustration after bad shots without outbursts — critical social-emotional skill for this age
  • Individual accountability: No teammates to deflect to; each shot is the child's own responsibility
  • Patience and persistence: Gradual improvement via deliberate practice, not instant results
  • Confidence: Measurable, visible improvement — advancing divisions provides concrete milestones
  • Social skills: Playing alongside peers in a structured, adult-supervised environment; etiquette of congratulating others
  • Strategic thinking: Club selection, target selection, and reading the course all require real-time problem-solving

Tournament Experience

Per Miller-Arndt's published program philosophy, CCC actively prepares juniors for external competitions:

  • Drive, Chip and Putt: Free USGA/Masters competition, ages 7-15. Local qualifiers in Maryland (University of Maryland Golf Course among sites). At 8.5, the child enters the 7-9 age bracket. Registration open now for 2026 season.
  • US Kids Golf Local Tour: CCC is a registered US Kids Golf family course. Central Maryland Local Tour runs fall (September-October).
  • Middle Atlantic PGA Section events and the Peggy Kirk Bell Girls Golf Tour (if applicable)
  • Internal: weekly Playday competitions, interclub matches against other private clubs
Sources: GRAA Profile: Stacy Miller-Arndt (2025) • CCC Newsletter (May 2010) • First Tee Colorado: Life Skills from Golf (2024) • Drive, Chip and Putt official site
Question 5

Is This a Good Idea for an 8.5-Year-Old Who Loves Golf?

Overall Assessment

Yes — highly recommended. For a child who already loves golf, a structured program at one of the premier private-club junior golf programs in the DC area is an outstanding use of this developmental window. The caveats are practical (membership cost, parent time, equipment), not programmatic.

Reasons to Enroll
  • Ideal developmental window. Ages 7-9 are optimal for ingraining correct fundamentals. Habits formed now persist for decades.
  • Intrinsic motivation. The child already loves golf — the single most important predictor of success and enjoyment in any junior program.
  • Elite, career-dedicated instruction. Stacy Miller-Arndt has 27+ years at CCC and is one of the country's top-ranked junior golf educators.
  • World-class facilities. A dedicated Junior Indoor Studio, championship courses, and launch monitor technology at age 8 is extraordinary.
  • Structured progression. Skill-based divisions mean no pressure to advance before ready — the child moves at their own pace.
  • Peer community. Lasting friendships built through a shared passion within the club's member community.
  • Tournament pathway. Drive, Chip and Putt and US Kids Golf provide age-appropriate external competition milestones.
  • Life skills. Accountability, emotional regulation, integrity, and patience — reinforced weekly in a structured setting.
  • Family sport. Golf is one of the few sports where parent and child can play together competitively for decades.
Considerations and Caveats
  • Members-only program. Requires active CCC family membership, which carries a substantial initiation fee and monthly dues (among the highest in the DC area).
  • Parental time commitment. Non-certified juniors require adult accompaniment on course — a weekly time obligation during the summer season.
  • Summer-centric calendar. Peak program runs June-August. Year-round engagement requires utilizing the Indoor Studio, which is optional but carries separate costs.
  • Equipment investment. Age-appropriate clubs are necessary (US Kids Golf sizing recommended). Quality junior sets cost $200-$400+.
  • Program documentation limited. The most detailed publicly-available program data is from 2010 documentation. Current fees, exact schedule, and 2026 structure should be confirmed directly with Stacy Miller-Arndt.
Sources: PGA of America: Parent's Guide to Youth Golf (2023) • Golf Blue Sky: 8 Reasons to Get Kids Playing Golf (2025) • Hurricane Junior Golf Tour: Junior Golf as Life Training (2025)
