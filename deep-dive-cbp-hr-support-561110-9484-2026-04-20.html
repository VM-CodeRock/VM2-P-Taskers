1. Executive Summary
⚠
Verdict
Conditional Pursue — Teaming Required
This is a follow-on recompete for administrative HR staff augmentation at two CBP centers (Bloomington, MN and Indianapolis, IN). The work is labor-intensive, location-specific, and operationally routine — not a natural fit for Changeis' technology and management consulting core. However, the $20M–$50M SDVOSB-restricted value, long period of performance, and known incumbent weakness (Vision Centric has only $12.4M current contract value vs. a potential $50–100M new ceiling) create a genuine opening. Changeis should pursue only as the prime on a teaming arrangement with a staffing-focused SDVOSB subcontractor who holds existing MN/IN labor pools. RAG composite score: 0.628 (Moderate).

CBP's Office of Human Resources Management (OHRM) requires contractor staff to supplement its HR workforce at two processing centers handling high-volume applicant recruiting and hiring. Services span personnel action processing, call center staffing, administrative support, staffing services, and eOPF records management. The acquisition flows through GSA MAS SIN 561110 as a Labor Hour BPA under SDVOSB set-aside — meaning only Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Businesses on GSA Schedule may compete.

Scope: The CBP Hiring Center (Bloomington, MN) and CBP Processing and Servicing Center (Indianapolis, IN) both require staff augmentation due to sharply increased applicant volumes for CBP hiring. Contractor personnel will: process personnel actions, staff applicant care call centers, provide administrative services support, assist with staffing services, and perform eOPF electronic records management (scanning documents into OPM's Electronic Official Personnel Folder system). The work is ongoing, high-volume, and directly tied to CBP's frontline recruitment pipeline.

Note on Two Forecasts: Two related APFS records exist for this requirement — F2025069484 ($20M–$50M, est. solicitation 3/31/26) and F2026072445 ($50M–$100M, est. solicitation 2/23/26, same incumbent). This may indicate CBP is running parallel acquisition strategies or has upwardly revised the ceiling. The larger ceiling record references a more specific strategy: "SDVOSB Set aside under the GSA MAS SIN 561110." Offerors should monitor both SAM.gov and the DHS APFS system for the actual solicitation.
