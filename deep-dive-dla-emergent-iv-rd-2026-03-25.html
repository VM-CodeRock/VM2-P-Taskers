Opportunity Overview

BAA Structure & Purpose

The DLA Emergent IV BAA (BAA0001-22) is the fourth iteration of DLA's flagship R&D broad agency announcement, issued under FAR 35.016. The BAA addresses DLA's need for scientific study and experimentation directed toward advancing the state-of-the-art or increasing knowledge in areas of interest not anticipated by DLA's annual R&D program planning processes. This is a rolling, multi-year vehicle with 37+ amendments issued since original posting in June 2022.

The BAA was originally capped at $50M. Amendment 06 raised the ceiling to $125,000,000, signaling DLA's expanded appetite for emergent technology solutions. DLA intends to award multiple contracts — both definitive one-time contracts and IDIQ vehicles — as a result of this BAA.

Key Submission Path
Step 1 — White Paper: Submit an initial summary (white paper) describing a specific project tied to a listed Area of Interest. White papers are submitted to RandD.DCSO@dla.mil and should use the template from Amendment 31. Step 2 — Technical & Cost Proposal: If DLA finds the white paper of sufficient interest, they will request a full technical and cost proposal. No full proposal should be submitted without prior white paper submission.

White Paper Submission Requirements

  • Submit an initial summary ("white paper") describing a specific project related to one of the Areas of Interest in Attachment B
  • Use the revised White Paper Template from Amendment 31
  • Subject line for questions follows requirements in Amendment 31
  • Submit to: RandD.DCSO@dla.mil
  • If white paper is of sufficient interest, DLA will request technical and cost proposals
  • Eligibility rules updated in Amendment 13 (revises B.1 and B.4)

  • Amendment 37 adds solicitation clauses applicable to all future awards

Competitive Landscape

DLA Emergent IV is a broad, multi-award BAA with no set-aside. Likely competitors span four categories:

Large Defense Integrators (High Resource, All AOIs)

Companies like SAIC, Leidos, Booz Allen Hamilton, CACI, ManTech have the resources to pursue multiple AOIs simultaneously with dedicated BD teams. They will likely focus on AI/ML, Advanced Manufacturing, and Critical Emerging Technologies AOIs with large-team white papers. Changeis advantage: Agility, lower overhead, deep subject matter expertise in specific AOIs without large overhead cost structure.

Mid-Market Defense IT / Analytics Firms

Empower AI, LMI, Engility (SAIC), PAE, Peraton — these companies actively pursue DLA BAA vehicles. Empower AI has already landed the AMTR BAA showing DLA vendor maturity. LMI has deep logistics research DNA. Changeis advantage: More flexible white paper positioning across less-contested AOIs; teaming opportunity with these firms on specific topics.

Small Businesses & University Research Teams

DLA specifically encourages HBCUs, MIs, and small businesses. DLA's Collider Day sourcing shows deliberate small business engagement across manufacturing and materials AOIs. Changeis angle: Consider partnerships with university research labs (e.g., Georgia Tech Supply Chain, MIT Lincoln Lab) for research credibility in specific AOIs. White paper teaming with universities is common and competitively advantageous.

Specialized Technology Firms (AOI-Specific)

For AOIs like Blockchain Logistics, Lithium Battery Deactivation, and Edge Computing — niche tech firms and startups are the primary competition. These include blockchain supply chain startups, battery technology companies, and edge computing vendors. Changeis positioning: Focus on AOIs where business/analytical support is the core requirement (AI/ML applications, data analytics, supply chain data) rather than hardware or materials R&D.
