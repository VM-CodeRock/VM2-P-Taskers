Executive Summary
STRONG opportunity for Changeis. CBP is standing up a brand-new WOSB-set-aside financial management and accounting operations contract at its Indianapolis Office of Finance. The RAG engine scores this 0.6625 — highest in the match set — driven by Changeis's active CBP/OFAM Human Capital Program Support (HCPS) contract and prior DOT/Volpe financial operations work. The opportunity is a logical adjacency to Changeis's existing CBP footprint. Critical path issue: the requirement is posted under NAICS 541219 (Other Accounting Services), while Changeis's primary profile sits in 541611 and 541511 — a gap that can be closed via teaming with an accounting-focused WOSB sub (e.g., GAP Solutions or Dextera sub arrangement) while Changeis primes.

CBP's Office of Finance, Financial Operations Directorate (FOD) — headquartered at 8899 E. 56th Street, Indianapolis, IN 46249 — manages the government's second-largest revenue collection function, processing over $40 billion in annual duties, fees, and taxes. The prospective requirement consolidates multiple financial service functions: payroll processing, commercial accounts payable, travel voucher adjudication, government travel card administration, field revenue support, revenue collections, revenue protection, and reimbursable service agreement (RSA) management. This is a new contract vehicle with no incumbent, creating a clean competitive field for a well-positioned WOSB.

Solicitation is projected to release April 24, 2026 — approximately one week from the date of this analysis — placing Changeis on immediate pre-solicitation footing. The award date of July 2, 2026, and performance start of September 1, 2026 create an aggressive nine-week evaluation timeline. Changeis must move now on teaming, NAICS eligibility alignment, and capture strategy.

Competitive Landscape

This is a new WOSB-restricted competition with no incumbent. However, the incumbent-adjacent players and the CBP WOSB ecosystem are well-defined. The most dangerous competitors are those with existing CBP FOD relationships and 541219 credentials.

Dextera Corporation
WOSB Highest Threat
The de facto incumbent. Has operated CBP FOD accounting operations in Indianapolis since at least 2020. Holds NAICS 541611 via GSA MAS. Has 44 FTE in place, knows the processes, has CBP past performance. Will absolutely re-compete. Their risk: NAICS changed from 541611 to 541219 — they may face same gap issue, or they add that SIN. Primary competitor.
GAP Solutions, Inc.
SB High Threat / Partner Option
Was a subcontractor on the Dextera contract. Has direct CBP National Finance Center revenue collection experience, including CBP's $40B duty/fee collection function. Holds 541219 directly. If not partnered with Dextera, they may prime or sub to another bidder — Changeis should approach them for teaming immediately.
Amivero
WOSB Moderate Threat
Top CBP-focused WOSB (OrangeSlices watchlist). Strong DHS/CBP IT and operations presence. Has broad DHS footprint. Would likely compete on CBP mission-support work of this scale. Teaming or direct competitor.
Indigo IT
WOSB Moderate Threat
CBP-focused WOSB on OrangeSlices watchlist. Primarily IT-services, less direct financial ops history. Potential competitor if they partner with an accounting sub.
Chevo Consulting
WOSB Moderate Threat

Educology / Aalis / Liona

DHS FMO BPA Context: DHS awarded a $100M Financial Management and Operations Support BPA (unrestricted) to Deloitte, EY, Guidehouse, Kearney, and KPMG. This covers DHS OCFO enterprise-level financial management consulting — a separate, more strategic function from the CBP FOD transactional accounting operations here. No overlap expected with the CBP FOD requirement.

Most critical competitive risk: Dextera + GAP Solutions prime/sub or JV. They have the incumbency advantage (even as a "new contract"), the Indianapolis workforce in place, and together cover all NAICS codes. Changeis must counter with its unique CBP institutional advantage (active HCPS contract = organizational trust), superior program management, and the ability to demonstrate a more comprehensive solution — not just the clerk function but full financial operations management.
