2) Opportunity Details

What FTA is asking for (capability statement)

  • Provide anticipated labor categories/staffing expectations for: Program Manager; Senior Meeting Planner/Event Facilitator; Event Planner; Meeting Registrar/Admin Support Specialist.
  • Explain how you would accommodate uncertainty in number of meetings/conferences.
  • Comment on gaps in the SOO / what additional detail would be helpful in a forthcoming RFP.
  • Provide legal name, NAICS, CAGE, business size; limit to 5 pages; Word, Times New Roman 12.
  • Submit by email to crystal.zorich@dot.gov and teddy.cosia.ctr@dot.gov (per SAM posting).

Operational implications

  • FTA appears to be shaping labor categories around event/meeting planning operations rather than IT delivery.
  • This is a strong adjacency to Changeis’ DOT/FAA experience (Volpe Center history) even though the work is not ERP/DevSecOps-focused.
  • Likely downstream solicitation may include hybrid event support, registration systems, reporting, and policy-compliant travel management.

4) Competitive Landscape

This is a Sources Sought, so SAM does not identify an incumbent. Based on typical federal event/logistics support buys (DOT/Volpe/FTA), likely competition includes:

  • Small business event logistics and meeting management firms with federal travel/meeting compliance experience.
  • Consulting firms already supporting DOT/Volpe contracts with “conference planning and logistics” CLINs.
  • Vendors with registration platforms + on-site staffing (hybrid events).

Recommended intel follow-up: identify prior FTA logistical support awards and conference-support task orders via FPDS/USASpending once a solicitation number/vehicle is known.

5) Win Strategy Recommendations

Positioning themes

  • FTA-ready logistics playbook: repeatable processes for venue sourcing, registration, speaker management, accessibility, and on-site execution.
  • Compliance by design: federal invitational travel, records, privacy, and procurement guardrails built into event operations.
  • Hybrid enablement: webinar support, dry runs, A/V coordination, and post-event reporting.
  • Surge-ready staffing model: clear approach to uncertain event volume (bench, subcontractor network, scalable registrar support).

Teaming suggestions

  • If Changeis primes: consider a niche event production/logistics small business as a subcontractor for venue + A/V operations, while Changeis leads program management, compliance, reporting, and stakeholder coordination.
  • If Changeis subs: partner with an established federal event/logistics prime and supply program management, analytics/reporting, and DOT-domain expertise.
