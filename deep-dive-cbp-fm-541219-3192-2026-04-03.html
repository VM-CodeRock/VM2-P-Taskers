CBP is forecasting a new, multi-year (5-year base, FY2026–2031) financial management and accounting operations contract at its Indianapolis National Finance Center. The acquisition will be set aside for Women-Owned Small Businesses (WOSB) and placed on the GSA MAS Schedule (NAICS 541219) at a Firm Fixed Price. At $20M–$50M total, this is among the larger WOSB-set-aside accounting services procurements in the DHS portfolio.

The requirement spans six distinct financial operations functional areas — payroll, commercial accounts, travel vouchers, government cards, field revenue support, and revenue collections — indicating a large, multi-function operations center engagement rather than a narrowly defined advisory or audit role.

Changeis RAG Engine: STRONG classification (top score 0.662), with 10 matching past-performance records, all anchored in DOT/FAA/Volpe financial and administrative program support. The match quality reflects financial program management and administrative financial support experience, which partially — but not fully — aligns with the transaction-processing and operations center intensity of this requirement.

Key Risk Factor
This is an operations contract — payroll processing, voucher management, government card reconciliation — not a financial advisory or audit contract. Standalone pursuit requires demonstrable financial operations at comparable transaction volume. A WOSB prime with CBP/DHS financial operations track record should be identified immediately.
