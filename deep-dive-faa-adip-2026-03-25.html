System Description & Scope

ADIP is the FAA's authoritative hub for all airport and aeronautical data. Mandated by the FAA Reauthorization Act of 2018 (Section 314), it replaced the legacy disparate systems (AGIS, various paper/digital forms) with a unified, web-based platform. The system currently operates at version 10.9.3 (released August 2025) and serves airport operators, contract survey companies, FAA regional staff, and airline stakeholders.

Core Modules:

  • AGIS (Airport Geographic Information System) — Advanced GIS platform collecting/validating airport survey data; built on ESRI/open data standards; AIXM/XML data model; linked to NASR
  • Airport Master Record (5010) — Replaced FAA Forms 5010-1/2/3/5; digital submission and tracking; Form cancelled July 2024 with ADIP as replacement
  • Modification of Standards (MOS) — Mandatory as of March 2018; manages deviations from FAA airport design standards
  • Runway Safety Area (RSA) — Runway safety analysis and mitigation reporting module
  • Runway Incursion Mitigation (RIM) — Workflow support for runway safety programs
  • Airport Obstacle Data Search — Consolidated obstacle database (eTOD), formerly fragmented across multiple FAA systems
  • Issue Tracking Module — Integrated support desk within portal (post-login)

Technical Environment

Based on available public documentation, ADIP operates as a web-based SaaS/portal with the following characteristics:

  • Angular/web-based front-end (confirmed via URL structure: /agis/public/#/airportSearch/advanced)
  • ESRI GIS integration for spatial analysis and visualization
  • AIXM/XML aeronautical data standards
  • PIV/CAC multi-factor authentication (FAA MyAccess for internal; ADIP portal for external)
  • Oracle/SQL backend databases (consistent with DOT DAMIS/SAI architecture pattern)
  • Integration with NASR (National Airspace System Resources), NFDC, and eTOD systems
  • API and machine-interface support; data CSV extract and PDF generation
  • High-resolution satellite imagery integration
