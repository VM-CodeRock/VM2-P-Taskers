Research Brief

AI Impact on US Jobs

The Numbers, Sectors, Timeline & Changeis Implications
April 8, 2026 · Prepared for Varun Malhotra, President — Changeis Inc.
Executive Summary

AI is reshaping the US labor market faster than any prior technological disruption — but the picture is more nuanced than headlines suggest. Displacement is real, concentrated, and accelerating; yet net job creation projections remain positive over a 10-year horizon. The inflection point is right now: 2026 is the year Wall Street firms, federal agencies, and major employers all cite as the breakover moment.

5 Key Findings

  • Displacement is measurable but understated. Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley estimate AI raised the US unemployment rate by only ~0.1 percentage points so far — but independent analysis puts total US jobs displaced or foregone in 2025 at 200,000–300,000, with the real number 4–6× higher than what companies officially attribute to AI. (Axios, Apr 2026; David Shapiro, Feb 2026)
  • Entry-level and junior roles are the first casualties. Gen Z and workers under 30 are bearing disproportionate impact. Employment for 22–25-year-olds in AI-exposed roles fell 16% from late 2022 to mid-2025. Entry-level job postings declined ~35% since January 2023. (Fortune, Apr 2026)
  • 50–55% of US jobs will be reshaped within 2–3 years. BCG's April 2026 microeconomic modeling finds most jobs won't be eliminated — they'll be fundamentally changed. Full substitution (10–15% of jobs) will take 5+ years. (BCG, Apr 2026)
  • Federal IT is uniquely vulnerable AND opportunistic. DOGE's workforce culling removed the very people needed for AI adoption, creating a massive talent vacuum in federal tech. This is Changeis's strategic opening: agencies desperately need experienced IT services partners to fill this gap.
  • Policy is reactive, not proactive. The White House, DOL, and Congress are all moving to address AI workforce impacts — but reskilling capacity (60,000/year) is far below displacement pace (200,000–300,000/year). The gap is a structural business opportunity.

Key Insight: The Invisible Displacement Problem

Only ~4.5% of 2025 layoffs were officially attributed to AI. However, the much larger displacement channel is quiet attrition — companies deploy AI, achieve productivity gains, and simply don't replace workers who leave. This "hiring freeze" effect accounts for the majority of AI's labor market impact and is nearly invisible in traditional statistics. Goldman Sachs estimates net -16,000 jobs/month (25,000 substituted - 9,000 augmented), but Morgan Stanley and Goldman both note true aggregate impact may be even smaller due to offsetting AI infrastructure demand. (Fortune, Apr 2026)

Federal IT Sector: Deep Dive

The federal government IT sector is Changeis's primary market and deserves detailed analysis. It presents a paradox: massive AI adoption mandates from the White House, combined with a self-inflicted talent crisis that makes external IT services partners essential.

Federal AI Talent Vacuum
  • DOGE eliminated ~250,000–264,000 federal workers (net), including recent AI/tech hires who were probationary
  • 18F digital services agency "deleted"; US Digital Service in turmoil; modernization projects halted
  • Technical jobs specifying AI capabilities had risen from 0% (2016) to 9% of all federal technical jobs (2024) — then gutted
  • 750 projected AI-capable hires by Sep 2025 (Biden plan) never materialized — mass fired as probationary employees
  • Federal employees now view AI as existential threat, resisting adoption — cultural damage from DOGE framing
Federal AI Adoption Mandates
  • White House AI Action Plan (Jul 2025): AI adoption is a national priority; every federal employee whose job could benefit must have access
  • OMB M-26-05: Agencies get flexibility to approve AI tools faster, without blanket approvals
  • GSA USAi: 43 agencies enrolled, AI tools at $1/license — access is no longer the bottleneck
  • FedRAMP 20x: Streamlined cloud/AI provider approval
  • US Tech Force: 1,000 AI/tech specialists hired for 2-year tours in agencies
  • $50B+ Amazon AWS investment in dedicated government AI cloud regions

Bottom line for federal IT contractors: The US government has world-class AI mandates, unlimited procurement access, and virtually no internal capacity to execute. The DOGE talent purge removed the people who could have built internal capability. This is a structural outsourcing opportunity of historic magnitude for firms like Changeis that combine IT services expertise with AI knowledge.

Critical Timing Asymmetry

AI destroys jobs faster than it creates them. Destruction happens in 12–18 months (speed of enterprise AI adoption). Job creation from new industries takes 5–10 years to materialize at scale. This gap creates a structural unemployment risk window of 2025–2030. Policy retraining capacity (60,000/year) is far below displacement pace (200,000–300,000+/year). (DWU Consulting, Mar 2026)

Changeis Angle — Strategic Implications

Changeis operates at the intersection of three major forces: federal agencies undergoing mandated AI transformation, a gutted federal IT workforce unable to execute that transformation, and an AI wave reshaping IT professional services delivery itself. The net assessment: significant strategic opportunity, with real workforce vulnerabilities to manage proactively.

Opportunities for Changeis
  • Federal AI Integration Services: 43 agencies on GSA USAi with AI tool access but no implementation capacity. Every agency needs an IT services partner to actually deploy and integrate these tools. Changeis can lead.
  • Workforce Transformation Advisory: Agencies need help reskilling existing federal employees for AI-augmented roles. DOL launched "Make America AI-Ready"; DOL is contracting for AI apprenticeship integration. This is a direct contract vehicle.
  • AI Governance & Oversight: OMB M-26-05 requires agencies to take responsibility for AI tool decisions. Changeis can provide the governance frameworks, ATO support, and FedRAMP-aligned AI deployment expertise.
  • Legacy System Modernization with AI: Agencies are retiring decades-old systems and replacing with AI-ready infrastructure. This is core Changeis territory — IT modernization plus AI integration.
  • Agentic AI Deployment: The White House explicitly mandates agentic AI for benefits processing, procurement, permit review. Changeis can build and deploy these solutions.
  • Post-DOGE Rebuilding: Agencies that lost institutional IT knowledge through DOGE cuts need experienced partners to reconstitute capability. Changeis's continuity and federal experience is a differentiator.
Risks to Manage
  • Junior/Entry-Level Role Compression: Changeis's IT delivery model likely includes entry-level staff (helpdesk, junior developers, analysts). These are the roles most vulnerable to AI substitution by clients — scope may shrink or migrate to AI oversight roles.
  • AI Writing Code Reduces Headcount: If Microsoft's 30% AI-written code becomes 60–70% industry standard, client expectations for team sizes will compress. Fixed-price contracts may face margin pressure.
  • Reskilling Lag: Changeis staff must be upskilled before clients demand it. Workers without AI skills face job insecurity; workers with AI skills command 56% wage premium — creating retention risk for top performers.
  • Contract Scope Shifts: Agencies automating routine IT functions (helpdesk, data entry, basic reporting) will reduce certain contract scopes. Changeis must migrate up the value chain toward AI strategy and implementation.
  • Competition from Pure-Play AI Firms: New entrants (AI-native consultancies, hyperscaler professional services arms) will compete aggressively for the federal AI integration market. Changeis's incumbent relationships and clearances are moats, but not indefinitely.

Recommended Positioning for Changeis

Reframe the value proposition: From "IT services and staffing" to "Federal AI Transformation Partner." Position Changeis not as a provider of headcount, but as the firm that helps federal agencies actually execute their AI Action Plan mandates — strategy, implementation, governance, and workforce transformation.

  • Pursue AI Integration Task Orders: Target GSA USAi, CIO-SP4, and agency-specific IDIQs with AI-forward capability statements. Lead with agentic AI deployment and LLM integration capabilities.
  • Build an Internal AI Center of Excellence: Establish internal AI CoE to (1) upskill Changeis staff, (2) develop repeatable AI integration playbooks for common federal use cases, (3) generate thought leadership for business development.
  • Protect Incumbent Positions Proactively: For existing contracts, proactively propose AI augmentation add-ons. Frame as "we can do more for you with AI" — not reactive to client requests, but leading the transformation conversation.
  • Partner Strategically: Identify GSA USAi-listed AI tool vendors (ServiceNow, Microsoft, etc.) as teaming partners. Use their commercial AI tools to underpin Changeis-delivered federal solutions.
  • Workforce Reskilling Investment: Mandate AI literacy training for all Changeis staff in 2026. Identify 10–15 AI champions across practice areas. Create an AI apprenticeship track for junior staff who would otherwise be displaced.
  • Watch the DOGE Rebuild: Agencies that lost technical talent are actively rebuilding (OPM Early Career Network, April 2026). Changeis can serve as the bridge — providing experienced IT professionals while agencies rebuild permanent staff.
Policy Landscape — Government Response

White House AI Action Plan (Jul 2025)

Mandates AI access for all federal employees. Establishes GSA AI procurement toolbox. Directs agencies to cut approval red tape. Key: AI adoption is no longer optional in federal government. (Blitz AI)

National AI Legislative Framework (Mar 20, 2026)

White House published legislative recommendations: AI training in vocational programs, federal research on AI-driven workforce shifts, expanded land-grant AI education. No new legal obligations yet — aspirational. (Employer Report)

DOL "Make America AI-Ready" (Mar 24, 2026)

Free AI literacy course via SMS ("text READY to 20202"). Five foundational AI competency areas. Public-private partnership with Arist. (DOL)

DOL AI Apprenticeship Initiative (Apr 1, 2026)

National contracting opportunity: integrate AI skills into Registered Apprenticeship programs. Focus on data centers, telecom, advanced manufacturing. 1-year base + 4 option years. (DOL.gov)

OPM AI Workforce Programs (Dec 2025)

US Tech Force: 1,000 AI/tech specialists in 2-year federal tours. Data Science Fellows Program (Spring 2026): 250 fellows. Project Management Fellows: 250 fellows. Critical skills gap acknowledgment. (OPM Memo)

Senate Bipartisan Workforce Data Letter (Mar 6, 2026)

9 senators (bipartisan) urge DOL, BLS, Census to update surveys to measure AI's workforce impact. Calls for CPS, JOLTS, and National Longitudinal Survey to incorporate AI questions. (NextGov/FCW)

DOGE: Intended vs. Actual Impact on Federal AI

DOGE was sold as a technology modernization initiative but paradoxically undermined federal AI adoption. Key findings from Brookings Institution (Jan 2026):

  • Net federal workforce reduction: ~264,000 (Jan 2025–Jan 2026); 386,826 departed, 122,000 hired (55% less than 2024)
  • Mass firings targeted probationary employees — which included most recent AI-specialized hires (hired under Biden AI hiring surge)
  • 18F (GSA digital services agency) deleted; USDS in turmoil; long-term modernization projects halted
  • DOGE framing of AI as "efficiency/workforce reduction" caused federal employees to perceive AI as existential threat — creating resistance to adoption
  • By October 2025, agencies were already rehiring to reconstitute capability — adding cost on top of severance/deferred resignation payments
  • Claimed DOGE savings of $200B vs. AEI estimate of ~$10B actual savings; Senate PSI found $21.7B in DOGE-generated waste
Policy Gap Analysis

Current government reskilling capacity: ~60,000 workers/year. Current AI displacement pace: 200,000–300,000 workers/year (and accelerating). The 240,000+ annual gap between displaced workers and retraining capacity means the US workforce is running a structural deficit. This gap is a market opportunity for workforce transformation services firms — and a major policy risk if unaddressed.
