Executive Summary

April 22 is a high-signal day for the DHS/federal civilian pipeline. Three major structural shifts broke simultaneously: (1) DHS officially kicked off Cumulus cloud contracts with all four hyperscalers, opening a downstream competitive vehicle for professional services; (2) USCIS named a new CIO — Andrew Vanjani — ending a nearly year-long vacancy, with declared priorities in fraud detection, infrastructure modernization, and digital architecture; and (3) OMB issued M-26-12 mandating commercial-first procurement, requiring agencies to report every non-commercial award from the last six months by May 4. On the opportunities side, the DOT $1.9B 1DOT Digital Services BPA (solicitation August 2026) and the DHS APFS FEMA IT contract are notable near-term actions. The competitive environment for DHS IT integrators just got more complex — Cumulus professional services will be a major prize.

USCIS Names Andrew Vanjani as New CIO — First Day April 22 Leadership
Nextgov/FCW · April 22, 2026 · Source
Andrew Vanjani began his first day as USCIS CIO on April 22, filling a vacancy left by Bill McElhaney who departed in May 2025. Vanjani brings experience from GSA, Maryland DoIT, the Organization of American States, and IT firms (HP, DMI). He took his SES oath before Executive Director Joseph Edlow. Stated CIO priorities: (1) leveraging emerging technology to combat fraud/identity risks; (2) designing future-ready digital infrastructure; (3) delivering timely, fair outcomes. USCIS has been pursuing IT backlog reduction and electronic case processing modernization for years.
Changeis Impact: New CIO = new relationship-building window at USCIS OIT. Vanjani's explicit fraud/identity and infrastructure modernization priorities align with Changeis capabilities in IT O&M and digital transformation. Attend the April 28 USCIS Industry Day to establish early face time.
DHS Cumulus Cloud: Block 1 Awards to AWS, Oracle, Google, Microsoft — Competitive Vehicle Coming Cloud / Contract
Washington Technology · April 22, 2026 · Source
DHS announced Block 1 non-competitive awards for the Cumulus enterprise cloud contract — one-year base plus four options each — to AWS (finalized by end of June), Oracle, Google Cloud, and Microsoft (targeted by end of Q3 FY26). Coverage includes IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, AI, cybersecurity, logging, and monitoring. Financial values are redacted. A separate competitive multiple-award IDIQ will be announced for professional services, marketplace solutions, and managed services through March 2031.
Changeis Impact: The competitive Cumulus vehicle is the most consequential DHS cloud opportunity in years — covering professional services, cloud migration, and managed services. Changeis should identify the DHS Cumulus program office POC (Gregory Blaszko, gregory.blaszko@hq.dhs.gov) and position for the forthcoming multiple-award competition.
OMB M-26-12: Agencies Must Report All Non-Commercial Awards by May 4 — Commercial-First Mandate Policy
Washington Technology / GovExec · April 22, 2026 · Source
OMB Director Russ Vought issued M-26-12, implementing Trump's commercial-first procurement executive order. Agencies must report every non-commercial award from April–September 2025 by May 4. Awards over $10M must explain why a commercial offering wasn't used. New approval chain: non-commercial contracts now require sign-off from a political appointee Chief Acquisition Officer plus OMB. In FY2024, over two-thirds of federal contract spend — including $130B in cost-reimbursement contracts — was non-commercial.
Changeis Impact: Commercial-first mandate favors firms that can offer commercially priced, off-the-shelf service delivery models. Review any active proposals or pipeline bids to ensure you have a commercial product/service narrative. Expect increased use of GSA Schedules, BPAs, and IDIQs over standalone cost-reimbursement vehicles.
OMB M-26-10: CIO Monthly IT Contract Reporting Begins May 2026 Policy
Government Contracts Legal Forum · April 9, 2026 · Source
OMB M-26-10 (March 31, 2026) requires agency CIOs to submit monthly IT contract reports to OMB beginning May 2026 through October 2026, covering all approved IT contracts — especially those enabling public-facing digital services. IT vendors must now disclose utilization and pricing data that can be shared across agencies. This directly increases CIO-level accountability for every IT contract and its ROI.
Changeis Impact: DHS/USCIS CIOs will be under heightened scrutiny of IT spending, favoring vendors with clear ROI narratives and transparent pricing. Changeis should prepare "business value" metrics for any active or upcoming DHS IT engagements — outcome data is now critical for contract renewals.
GSA Deputy Administrator Outlines "Million Hours Challenge" — Scaling AI Automation Across Agencies AI Policy
Nextgov/FCW · April 22, 2026 · Nextgov
GSA Deputy Administrator Michael Lynch detailed the agency's "million hours challenge" — an internal goal to automate one million hours of government work through AI tools by end of FY26. Lynch also outlined plans for GSA's shared services programs and new internal AI automation infrastructure that could be extended to other civilian agencies through USAi.
Changeis Impact: GSA-led AI automation push creates services opportunities for firms that can implement AI-driven workflow automation within civilian agencies. Changeis should explore teaming with GSA USAi-enabled partners for upcoming civilian digital transformation engagements.
House FY27 VA Funding Bill Allocates $3.4B for EHR Modernization — 25% Withheld Budget
Nextgov/FCW / Washington Technology · April 22, 2026 · Nextgov
The House Appropriations Committee voted out an FY27 VA spending bill allocating $3.4B for the Oracle Cerner EHR modernization project, but withholding 25% until July 1, 2027, contingent on VA providing additional information and hitting performance requirements. This signals continued Congressional skepticism of large federal IT modernization programs despite sustained funding.
Changeis Impact: VA EHR modernization remains a large and visible federal IT program. Watch for program support and PMO opportunities as VA and Oracle Cerner navigate the conditional funding restrictions. The accountability model (performance-gated funds) is a signal for how future large IT modernization awards may be structured.
