Executive Summary

Google released Gemma 4 on April 2, 2026 under a pure Apache 2.0 license — the first Gemmaverse family without custom usage restrictions. Four model sizes (E2B, E4B, 26B MoE, 31B Dense) support text, image, audio, and video inputs with context windows up to 256K tokens. Changeis is positioned to be the federal integrator that turns this technology window into contracted program delivery.

Gemma 4 Overview
Model Architecture & Sizes

Gemma 4 ships in two deployment tiers. The Edge Tier targets mobile, embedded, and bandwidth-constrained environments. The Workstation Tier targets servers with GPU acceleration — directly applicable to federal on-premise deployments.

Model Active Params Total Params Context Modalities Min Hardware Federal Tier Fit
Gemma 4 E2B 2.3B 5.1B 128K Text · Image · Audio 1.5GB RAM (quantized) Edge / IoT
Gemma 4 E4B 4.5B 8B 128K Text · Image · Audio 8GB RAM (quantized) Endpoint / Laptop
Gemma 4 26B MoE 3.8B active 26B 256K Text · Image · Video 24–48GB VRAM Server / FedRAMP
Gemma 4 31B Dense 31B 31B 256K Text · Image · Video 1× H100 (80GB) IL4/IL5 Server

The 26B MoE uses 128 experts with only 8 active per token — delivering 26B-class intelligence at ~4B inference cost. This is the primary recommendation for cost-constrained federal deployments.

Apache 2.0 License — What It Means for Federal Clients
Apache 2.0 Is the Gold Standard for Government Deployment

Apache 2.0 is the first OSI-approved license applied to the Gemmaverse. It grants the right to use, modify, redistribute, and deploy commercially with no user limits, no competing model clauses, and no mandatory source code disclosure. Federal agencies and their contractors can treat Gemma 4 weights as owned government data assets.

Right Granted

Full Modification Rights

Agencies can fine-tune, LoRA-adapt, or domain-specialize Gemma 4 on classified or sensitive data without disclosing changes to Google or any third party.

Right Granted

No Telemetry Obligation

Air-gapped deployments with zero outbound connectivity are fully compliant. No "phone home" requirements, no usage reporting, no API key dependency.

Right Granted

Redistribution & Embedding

Changeis can embed Gemma 4 in agency-specific platforms, RAG systems, or workflow tools and deliver them under existing contract vehicles without additional IP licensing.

Contrast: Meta's Llama Community License prohibits training competing models and has a 700M MAU cap. Mistral's large models require a commercial license. Gemma 4's Apache 2.0 has zero such restrictions. Source: Google Open Source Blog (April 2026)

FedRAMP Note

FedRAMP High (421 NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 controls) applies to cloud service providers, not on-premise deployments. An agency running Gemma 4 on government-owned hardware within a FedRAMP High authorized boundary does not require a separate FedRAMP authorization for the model itself — only the underlying infrastructure needs to be authorized.

Architecture 3: Air-Gapped SCIF Deployment

The most security-intensive scenario — full physical and network isolation with no connectivity to any external network. This architecture applies to classified intelligence analysis, ITAR-controlled research environments, and SAP programs.

Air-Gap Compliance Advantage

Air-gapped deployment eliminates entire categories of FedRAMP/RMF control requirements: network boundary defense, external system interfaces, cloud service provider trust chains, and data transmission encryption. The control surface shrinks dramatically, accelerating ATO timelines.

AI-Specific Risk Controls
Risk

Prompt Injection / Jailbreak

Gemma 4 includes configurable system prompts and instruction-following safeguards. Additional guardrails via Llama Guard or custom input validation at the API gateway layer are recommended for agency deployments.

Risk

Model Hallucination

RAG architecture grounds responses in verified agency document corpora. All outputs must carry human-in-the-loop review requirements for high-stakes decisions. Confidence scoring and retrieval attribution required in SSP.

Risk

Supply Chain — Weight Integrity

Verify SHA-256 hash of downloaded weights against Google's published checksums before transfer to classified environments. Maintain chain-of-custody documentation as part of CMP for ISSO review.

Federal Recommendation

Gemma 4 26B MoE is the primary recommendation for most federal deployments due to its combination of Apache 2.0 licensing, 256K context, multimodal capabilities, and efficient inference cost. Llama 3.3 70B remains a viable alternative for workloads requiring maximum English-language benchmark performance, but its Community License requires legal review for government use. Phi-4 is a strong option for math-intensive workloads on constrained hardware.
