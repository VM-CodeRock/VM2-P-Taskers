Operation SafeDRIVE (wave 1) USDOT/FMCSA reported the first wave of Operation SafeDRIVE (Jan 13–15, 2026) across 26 states + DC: 8,215 inspections, 704 drivers OOS (incl. nearly 500 for English proficiency violations), 1,231 vehicles OOS, 56 arrests; “nearly 2,000” drivers/vehicles removed from roads. (FMCSA newsroom) Sets enforcement posture for 2026 (high-visibility blitzes + driver qualification focus) and likely drives increased compliance scrutiny by fleets and insurers. (FMCSA newsroom)
ELD enforcement FMCSA removed fourteen ELD devices from the registered list; motor carriers have 60 days to replace them, and using revoked devices after May 4, 2026 will be treated as operating without an ELD (drivers can be placed OOS). (FMCSA bulletin) Creates immediate vendor churn risk and compliance deadlines; reinforces the agency’s shift toward tighter ELD vetting and enforcement. (FMCSA bulletin)
