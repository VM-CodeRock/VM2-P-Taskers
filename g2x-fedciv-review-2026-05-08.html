Industry News & Developments 4
Executive Order: Fixed-Price Contracting Now the Federal Default
President Trump signed EO "Promoting Efficiency, Accountability, and Performance in Federal Contracting" on April 30, 2026. Establishes firm-fixed-price (FFP) and fixed-price incentive contracts as the default and preferred procurement method across all agencies. Contracting officers must now justify in writing and obtain senior-level approval to use cost-reimbursement or T&M vehicles above specified thresholds. Agencies must review their 10 largest non-fixed-price contracts within 90 days and seek conversion where practicable. OMB guidance due June 14; FAR amendments due August 28. Direct Impact on Changeis: Expect more FFP solicitations in FY2026 recompetes. Proposal pricing strategies must price more precisely. Time-and-materials vehicle holders (including at DHS/FAA) face renegotiation risk. Changeis should update proposal templates to emphasize FFP-readiness and defined deliverable structures.
Sources: White House EO · JD Supra Analysis · Mayer Brown · May 7, 2026
