Requirement Summary

CBP OIT requires 24×7×365 Technology Service Desk operations as the primary single point of contact for all CBP IT issues. Services include answering inbound calls, emails, and self-service requests; documenting, diagnosing, and tracking incidents across all CBP systems; supporting the ACE (Automated Commercial Environment) Account Service Desk (ASD) for the trade community; and ensuring both internal CBP staff and authorized enforcement agencies have continuous access to mission-critical systems.

Scope spans Tier 1 and Tier 2 support, program management, customer-focused operations, and Advanced Support Operations. The TSD supports CBP users globally, including the Office of Trade (OT). Work is primarily phone, email, chat, and web-based self-service with limited in-person support, performed at the Ashburn, VA facility.

Why a Bridge? The incumbent contract (70B04C20F00001438) expires December 15, 2025, but the main recompete — a new 6-year, $100M+ task order via STARS III — is being awarded separately. This bridge covers the gap period (June 2026–June 2027) until the new STARS III contract is fully operational. Bridge structures typically award to the incumbent or a closely aligned team to ensure continuity of operations with no service interruption.

4. Bridge Contract Context

What is a Bridge Contract?

A bridge contract is a short-term, interim award issued to maintain continuity of services while an agency completes the recompetition of the primary contract. Bridges are common in IT services when the main recompete timeline slips (protest delays, evaluation time). They are typically awarded to the incumbent — or a closely related team — under streamlined procedures to avoid service disruption.

This Bridge's Context

The incumbent Patriot LLC contract (70B04C20F00001438) ran September 2020 – December 2025 at a $45.5M ceiling. The main TSD recompete was announced in February 2025 at $100M+ via STARS III (8(a)), with an anticipated award date of Q4 FY2025. That timeline slipped — evidenced by this bridge appearing in the APFS. The bridge bridges from June 2026 through June 2027, giving CBP continuity while the STARS III winner mobilizes.

Bridge Procurement Dynamics Bridge contracts heavily favor incumbents. Patriot LLC ran TSD for 5+ years, knows CBP's systems, has cleared personnel (CBP Tier IV BI), and has proven operations. A bridge procurement on a tight timeline (award April 29, 2026) is effectively a sole-source or incumbent-preferred action. New entrants would need an extraordinarily compelling reason to displace a performing incumbent on a bridge.
