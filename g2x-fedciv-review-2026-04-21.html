Section 3 — Industry News & Policy Developments
OPM Director Kupor: AI in Federal Services — Retirement Services Pilot, Tech Force Program AI Policy
FedScoop · April 21, 2026 · Source
OPM Director Scott Kupor highlighted a retirement services call center AI pilot and identified two primary blockers to federal AI adoption: attracting modern software talent and changing government's risk-averse culture. OPM's Tech Force program targets early-career engineers for time-limited placements focused on AI and modernization.
Changeis Impact: Growing OPM/agency demand for AI-ready IT talent and change management services. Aligns with Changeis capabilities in digital transformation and workforce enablement.
CISA Resources "More Limited Than I Would Like" Amid DHS Shutdown — Top Official Budget Risk
Nextgov/FCW · April 21, 2026 · Nextgov
CISA Deputy Executive Director Nick Andersen told lawmakers that the ongoing DHS partial shutdown has limited "preparatory activities" and typical outreach. CISA is operating with nearly 1,000 fewer staff than January 2025, and the FY27 Trump budget proposes an additional $707M cut (from a current ~$491M cut in FY26).
Changeis Impact: CISA contract pipeline faces near-term headwinds; however, reorientation toward core cyber mission (network defense, critical infrastructure) could unlock new targeted procurement. Monitor CISA CEEOSS recompete (Q3 FY26).
Trump FY27 Budget: DHS Gets $63B, TSA Privatization, CISA −$707M, CBP +$18.5B Budget
MeriTalk / Security Magazine · April 3–7, 2026 · MeriTalk
FY27 budget proposes $63B DHS discretionary authority (−3.3% from FY26 CR). Key shifts: CBP +$18.5B (border enforcement surge), ICE +$10B, USCG +$12.5B operations. TSA faces privatization of airport screeners (−$52M). CISA cut by $707M. DHS HQ consolidation proposed (−$53M). No full-year FY26 DHS appropriations bill yet in place.
Changeis Impact: CBP/ICE/USCG investment surge = sustained IT modernization demand. TSA privatization creates workforce transition IT needs. CISA cuts may redirect funds to CBP/ICE cyber programs.
OMB M-26-05: Agencies Accelerating AI Deployment — "Past the Should We?" Phase AI Policy
FedScoop · March 3, 2026 · FedScoop
OMB M-26-05 calls for agile, risk-based AI technology adoption. 43 agencies signed up for GSA USAi enterprise AI licenses (as low as $1). OneGov/Broadcom agreement expands AI software access. U.S. Tech Force initiative recruiting 1,000 technologists for AI-focused placements.
Changeis Impact: M-26-05 creates direct procurement pressure for AI integration services at DHS components. Changeis should position AI-augmented IT service delivery as a differentiator in active bids (TSA FOCUS, USCIS SOAR).
GAO Report: Agencies Missing Step on AI Acquisition Lessons — Policy Updates Required by Dec 2025 Oversight
Nextgov/FCW · April 13, 2026 · Nextgov
GAO sampled four agencies finding inadequate policies for collecting AI acquisition lessons learned. All four recommendations center on updating internal policy to submit lessons to GSA's AI acquisition repository per OMB M-25-22. Agencies were required to update AI policies by December 29, 2025.
Changeis Impact: Creates advisory services opportunity — helping DHS components build AI governance frameworks and comply with M-25-22 agency policy updates.
