Executive Summary

The DOT Office of Financial Management and Transit Benefit Programs (M-70) is seeking contractors to modernize the Department's Working Capital Fund (WCF) — a ~$764M annual shared-services fund that consolidated IT resource management across 11 Operating Administrations in FY25, with HR and Procurement slated for FY27.

The BD-RAG engine scored this opportunity at 0.671 (STRONG), driven by Changeis's deep DOT/Volpe administrative and financial support portfolio. However, the SOW includes significant technical components (cloud-native architecture, Delphi/G-Invoicing integration, ServiceNow connectors) that exceed Changeis's solo capabilities.

Strategic Recommendation

Pursue as Changeis (prime) + EY (sub) via the IPSS-3 BPA (693JJ126A000001). Changeis leads Task Areas 1, 2, and 5 (PMO, Change Management, Contract Transition). EY leads Task Areas 3 and 4 (IT Modernization, Financial Systems Integration). Shape the RFI response to recommend IPSS-3 as the acquisition vehicle.

Opportunity Context & Market Intelligence

Why This Opportunity Exists

A September 2025 DOT Office of Inspector General audit found critical deficiencies in OST's WCF management:

  • Billing errors — WCF customer bills contain errors preventing accurate portrayal of fund usage
  • Vague service descriptions — Customers cannot understand what OST provides for advance payments
  • Employee accounting issues — Employees in wrong positions, grades, or no longer in OST still listed
  • Lack of transparency — Descriptions not updated annually to reflect changes in operations

This contract is a direct remediation response. The FY25 IT consolidation amplified these problems, and the FY27 HR/Procurement expansion makes modernization urgent.
